<p><strong>Millsaps College</strong> is a private <a>liberal arts college</a> located in <a>Jackson, Mississippi</a>.
<p>The college was founded by a <a>Confederate</a> veteran, Major <a>Reuben Webster Millsaps</a> in 1889-90 by the donation of the college's land and $50,000.
Dr. <a>William Belton Murrah</a> was the college's first president, and Bishop <a>Charles Betts Galloway</a> of the United Methodist Church organized the college's early fund-raising efforts.
Candidates for an undergraduate degree must also pass oral and written <a>comprehensive exams</a> in their major field of study.
Unacceptable performance on comprehensive exams will prevent a candidate from receiving a degree, even if all course work has been completed.
<p>Millsaps offers <a>B.S.</a>, <a>B.A.</a>, <a>B.B.A.</a>, <a>M.B.A.</a> and MAcc degrees and corresponding programs.
Millsaps sends large numbers of graduates to <a>graduate school</a>s, <a>law school</a>, and <a>medical school</a>.
<p>The current undergraduate population is around 1100 students on a 103 <a>acre</a> (417,000 m&#x00b2;) campus near downtown <a>Jackson, Mississippi</a>.
Millsaps offers 33 majors and 34 minors, including the option of a self-designed major, along with a multitude of study abroad and internship opportunities.
The college offers research partnerships for undergraduate students, and a variety of study abroad programs.
The college also offers a <a>Continuing Education</a> program and the Community Enrichment Series for adults in the Jackson area.</p>
<p><a>thumb|left|Millsaps College campus.</a>
<p>Millsaps fell from 81 to 89 in the 2010 <a>U.S. News &amp; World Report</a>'s list of &quot;Best Liberal Arts Colleges&quot;.[<a>http://rankings.usnews.com/best-colleges/jackson-ms/millsaps-college-2414</a>]
<p>Millsaps was one of 40 schools in <a>Loren Pope</a>'s <em><a>Colleges That Change Lives</a></em>.</p>
<p>The Majors had a fierce football and basketball rivalry with <a>Mississippi College</a> in nearby <a>Clinton</a> through the 1950s before competition was suspended after an infamous student brawl at a basketball game.
<p>Millsaps was the summer training camp home for the <a>NFL's</a> <a>New Orleans Saints</a> in 2006, 2007, and 2008.</p>
<p>Millsaps was also home to the famous game-ending play in the <a>2007 Trinity vs. Millsaps football game</a>, in which <a>Trinity University</a> defeated Millsaps by a score of 28-24 after the miraculous play that later won the Pontiac Game-Changing Performance of the Year award, which had never before been bestowed upon a play outside of the <a>NCAA</a>'s <a>Bowl Subdivision</a>.</p>
<p>In 2008, Millsaps quarterback <a>Juan Joseph</a> was awarded the <a>Conerly Trophy</a>, which goes to the best football player in the state of <a>Mississippi</a>.</p>
<p>The school is home to six different <a>fraternities</a>:  <a>Alpha Phi Alpha</a>, <a>Kappa Alpha Order</a>, <a>Sigma Alpha Epsilon</a>, <a>Pi Kappa Alpha</a>, <a>Lambda Chi Alpha</a>, and <a>Kappa Sigma</a>; as well as six <a>sororities</a>:  <a>Delta Delta Delta</a>, <a>Kappa Delta</a>, <a>Phi Mu</a>, <a>Chi Omega</a>, <a>Alpha Kappa Alpha</a>, and <a>Delta Sigma Theta</a>.</p>
