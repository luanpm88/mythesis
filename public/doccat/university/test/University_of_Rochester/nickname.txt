<p>The <strong>University of Rochester</strong> (<strong>U of R</strong>, <strong>UR</strong>, or URoc) is a <a>private</a>, nonsectarian, research university located in <a>Rochester, New York</a>.
The University grants undergraduate, graduate, doctoral, and professional degrees through six schools and various interdisciplinary programs.

The university is headed by a board of trustees, with Edmund A. Hajim being the chairman.
The board appoints the president of the university, currently <a>Joel Seligman</a>, who replaced <a>Thomas H. Jackson</a> on July 1, 2005.</p>
<p>The original buildings of the campus were dedicated in 1930.
The main academic buildings, designed in the Greek revival style, are centered around the <a>Eastman Quadrangle</a> (generally referred to as the academic quad) which is formed by <a>Rush Rhees Library</a> and Dewey, Bausch &amp; Lomb, Morey, and Lattimore Halls.

<p>The mascot is the <a>Yellowjacket</a>.
<p>The university uses Dandelion Yellow and a shade of blue (Rochester blue) as its official colors, which are the prominent colors on the official regalia.</p>
<p>The <a>motto</a> of UR is <a>Meliora</a>, which loosely translates to &quot;better&quot; with the connotation of &quot;ever better&quot;, the meaning adopted by the university.</p>
<p>The image of <a>Rush Rhees Library</a>'s main dome serves as an additional icon for the University of Rochester.</p>
Although less frequently used, the university also has an official <a>Alma Mater</a>, <em>The Dandelion Yellow</em>.</p>
<p>The student body at the University of Rochester is both ethnically and socioeconomically diverse.
There are over 200 active <strong>Students' Association</strong> recognized groups on campus, which range from cultural dance groups to U of R's comedy improv troupe <a>In Between the Lines</a>.
The campus also hosts a volunteer EMT squad (The University of Rochester MERT) which provides free New York State certified Basic Life Support services to the River Campus community.
