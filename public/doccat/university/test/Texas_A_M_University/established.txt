The Texas A&amp;M culture is a product of the university's founding as a rural military and agricultural school.
Although the school and surrounding community have grown, and military training is no longer required, the <a>school's history</a> has instilled in students &quot;the idealized elements of a small-town life: community, tradition, loyalty, optimism, and unabashed sentimentality.&quot;
Some of the school traditions date to the 1890s, while others have been introduced more recently.
They dictate many aspects of student life, including how to greet others (using the official school greeting &quot;Howdy!&quot;),
how to act at an A&amp;M sporting event, and, in many cases, what words a student may use in conversation.</p>
