The university is located on an  campus in northwestern <a>Santa Clara Valley</a> approximately  northwest of <a>San Jose</a> and  southeast of <a>San Francisco</a>.</p>
<p><a>Leland Stanford</a>, a Californian railroad tycoon and politician, founded the university in 1891 in honor of his son, <a>Leland Stanford, Jr.</a> who died of <a>typhoid</a> at the age of 16.
Following World War II, Provost <a>Frederick Terman</a> supported faculty and graduates' entrepreneurialism to build self-sufficient local industry in what would become known as <a>Silicon Valley</a>.
By 1970, Stanford was home to a <a>linear accelerator</a>, one of the original four <a>ARPANET</a> nodes, and had transformed itself into a major research university in <a>computer science</a>, <a>mathematics</a>, <a>natural sciences</a>, and <a>social sciences</a>.
More than 50 Stanford faculty, staff and alumni have won the <a>Nobel Prize</a> (<a>list of Nobel Laureates by university affiliation</a>).
Stanford also boasts the largest number of <a>Turing award</a> winners for a single institution.
Stanford faculty and alumni have founded many prominent technology companies, including <a>Cisco Systems</a>, <a>Electronic Arts</a>, <a>Google</a>, <a>Hewlett-Packard</a>, <a>LinkedIn</a>, <a>Sun Microsystems</a>, and <a>Yahoo!</a>.</p>
<p>The university is organized into seven schools including academic schools of <a>Humanities and Sciences</a> and <a>Earth Sciences</a> as well as professional schools of <a>Business</a>, <a>Education</a>, <a>Engineering</a>, <a>Law</a>, and <a>Medicine</a>.
Stanford has a student body of approximately 6,900 undergraduate</p>
<p>Stanford competes in 34 varsity sports and is one of two private universities in the <a>NCAA Division I-A</a> <a>Pacific-10 Conference</a>.
Stanford's athletic program has won the <a>NACDA Directors' Cup</a> every year since 1995.
In the <a>2008 Summer Olympics</a> in <a>Beijing</a>, Stanford athletes won 25 medals, including 8 gold medals, more than any other university in the United States.</p>

<p>On the founding grant but away from the main campus, <a>Jasper Ridge Biological Preserve</a> is a nature reserve owned by the university and used by wildlife biologists for research.
<a>Hopkins Marine Station</a>, located in <a>Pacific Grove, California</a>, is a <a>marine biology</a> research center owned by the university since 1892.
The university also has its own golf course and a seasonal lake (<a>Lake Lagunita</a>, actually an irrigation reservoir), both home to the endangered <a>California Tiger Salamander</a>.
<p>Stanford offers a free shuttle bus service named <a>Marguerite</a> and offers monetary incentives to its employees for carpooling.
Stanford is a member of the Association for the Advancement of Sustainability in Higher Education.
The <a>Aspen Institute</a> has ranked the <a>Stanford Graduate School of Business</a> as the number one MBA program for incorporating social and environmental issues into the training of future business leaders, out of 590 schools worldwide.
<p>Stanford University is a tax-exempt <a>corporate trust</a> owned and governed by a privately appointed 35-member <a>Board of Trustees</a>.
The Stanford trustees also oversee the <a>Stanford Research Park</a>, the <a>Stanford Shopping Center</a>, the <a>Cantor Center for Visual Arts</a>, <a>Stanford University Medical Center</a>, and many associated medical facilities (including the <a>Lucile Packard Children's Hospital</a>).</p>
<a>John Etchemendy</a> was named the 12th Provost in September 2000.</p>
<p>The university is organized into seven schools: <a>School of Humanities and Sciences</a>, <a>School of Engineering</a>, <a>School of Earth Sciences</a>, <a>School of Education</a>, <a>Graduate School of Business</a>, <a>Stanford Law School</a> and the <a>Stanford University School of Medicine</a>.</p>
Full-time undergraduate tuition was $36,030 for 2008&#x2013;2009.</p>

Other Stanford-affiliated institutions include the <a>SLAC National Accelerator Laboratory</a> (originally the Stanford Linear Accelerator Center) and the <a>Stanford Research Institute</a>, a now independent institution which originated at the university, in addition to the <a>Stanford Humanities Center</a>.</p>
<p>Stanford also houses the <a>Hoover Institution on War, Revolution and Peace</a>, a major <a>public policy</a> <a>think tank</a> that attracts visiting scholars from around the world, and the <a>Freeman Spogli Institute for International Studies</a>, which is dedicated to the more specific study of <a>international relations</a>.
<a>Meyer Library</a> holds the vast <a>East Asia</a> collection and the student-accessible media resources.
<p>Digital libraries and text services include <a>digital image collections</a>, <a>HighWire Press</a>, the Humanities Digital Information Services group, and the Media Microtext Center.
<p>Stanford is a founding and charter member of <a>CENIC</a>, the Corporation for Education Network Initiatives in California, the nonprofit organization that provides extremely high-performance Internet-based networking to California's K-20 research and education community.</p>
92% of students graduated in the top tenth of their high-school class and the inter-quartile ranges for the <a>SAT</a> were 680&#x2013;780 for math, 670&#x2013;760 for writing, and 650&#x2013;760 for reading.</p>

<p>Stanford has a thriving artistic and musical community.
Extracurricular activities include theater groups such as Ram's Head Theatrical Society and the Stanford Shakespeare Society, award-winning <a>a cappella</a> music groups such as the <a>Mendicants</a>, Counterpoint, the Stanford Fleet Street Singers, <a>Harmonics</a>, Mixed Company, Testimony, <a>Talisman</a>, Everyday People, Raagapella, and a group dedicated to performing the works of <a>Gilbert and Sullivan</a>, the Stanford Savoyards.
Beyond these, the <a>music department</a> sponsors many ensembles including five choirs, the <a>Stanford Symphony Orchestra</a>, <a>Stanford Taiko</a>, and the Stanford Wind Ensemble.</p>
<p>Perhaps most distinctive of all is its <a>social</a> and <a>vintage dance</a> community, cultivated by dance historian <a>Richard Powers</a> and enjoyed by hundreds of students and thousands of alumni.
Stanford also boasts a student-run swing performance troupe called <a>Swingtime</a> and several alumni performance groups, including Decadance and the Academy of Danse Libre.</p>
<p>The <a>creative writing</a> program brings young writers to campus via the <a>Stegner Fellowship</a>s and other graduate scholarship programs.

<p>Stanford also hosts various publishing courses for professionals.
