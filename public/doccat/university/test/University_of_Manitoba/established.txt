<p>In 1901 the <a>Legislative Assembly of Manitoba</a> changed the University Act so that the university could do its own teaching, and in 1905 a building in downtown Winnipeg became its first teaching facility with a staff of six science professors.
The governance was modelled on the provincial University of Toronto Act of 1906 which established a <a>bicameral</a> system of university government consisting of a senate (faculty), responsible for academic policy, and a board of governors (citizens) exercising exclusive control over financial policy and having formal authority in all other matters.
The president, appointed by the board, was to provide a link between the 2 bodies and to perform institutional leadership.&lt;ref
Graduate training based on the German-inspired American model of specialized course work and the completion of a <a>research thesis</a> was introduced.&lt;ref
<p>The first school of <a>architecture</a> in western Canada was founded in 1919 at the University of Manitoba.&lt;ref&gt;PgNm=TCE&amp;Params=A1ARTA0009565&lt;/ref&gt;</p>
<p>By 1920, the University of Manitoba was the largest university in the <a>Canadian Prairies</a> and the fifth largest in Canada.

<p>The University was originally located on Broadway.
In 1929, following the addition of more programs, schools, and faculties, the University moved to its permanent site in <a>Fort Garry, Manitoba</a>.
The University maintained the Broadway facilities for many years.
<p>The University established an Evening institute in 1936.</p>

name=&quot;thecanadianencyclopedia2&quot;/&gt; In 1967, two of the colleges that had been part of the University of Manitoba were given university status by the provincial government.
United College, which had been formed by the merging of Wesley College and Manitoba College, became the <a>University of Winnipeg</a>, and Brandon College became <a>Brandon University</a>.</p>
Boniface College and St. John's College, two of the founding colleges of the University, are still part of the University of Manitoba.
St. Boniface College is the University's only <a>French language</a> college; it offers instruction in French and facilities for the training of teachers who expect to teach in the French language.
St. John's College, which dates back to 1820, offers instruction in Arts and Science and, among other special programs, prepares men and women for the ordained ministry of the Anglican Church.</p>
