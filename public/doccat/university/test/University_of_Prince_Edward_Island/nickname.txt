<p>The <strong>University of Prince Edward Island</strong> (<strong>UPEI</strong>) is a <a>public</a> <a>liberal arts</a> university in <a>Charlottetown</a>, <a>Prince Edward Island</a>, <a>Canada</a>, and the sole university in the province.
The enabling legislation is the <em>University Act, R.S.P.E.I 2000.</em>
The Confederation Trail runs alongside its eastern edge.</p>
<p><a>Bachelor's degree</a> programs, in many cases including &quot;honours&quot; options, are available in <a>Arts</a>, <a>Science</a>, <a>Business Administration</a>, <a>Education</a>, and <a>Nursing</a>.
The university also now offers an MA in Island Studies.
Since 1998, The Centre for Conflict Resolution Studies has been offering courses leading to a Certificate in Conflict Resolution Studies.
<p>Significant government investment in UPEI since the mid-1990s has begun to see the university improve in national rankings.
<p>The UPEI campus provides its students with many athletics amenities typically found on university campuses.
The [CARI Complex|<a>http://www.caripei.ca</a>]
The campus is well-known to Islanders of all ages, from children who take part in <a>Panther day camps</a> or come to <a>varsity games</a>, to adults who use the <a>Robertson Library</a> or take classes through <a>Seniors College</a>.
