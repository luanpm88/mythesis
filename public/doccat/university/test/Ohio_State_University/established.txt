Ohio State is currently ranked by ''[[U.S. News &amp; World Report]]'' as the best public university in Ohio, among the top 130 universities in the world, among the top 60 universities in the United States, and among the top 20 public universities in the United States.
Ohio State has been officially designated as the [[Flagship#University campuses|flagship]] institution of Ohio's public universities by the newly centralized [[University System of Ohio]].
It also includes regional campuses in [[Ohio State University, Lima Campus|Lima]], [[Ohio State University, Mansfield Campus|Mansfield]], [[Ohio State University, Marion Campus|Marion]], [[Ohio State University, Newark Campus|Newark]], and [[Ohio State University Agricultural Technical Institute|Wooster]].</pre>
<p> Ohio State University was founded in 1870 as a <a>land-grant university</a> in accordance with the <a>Morrill Act of 1862</a> under the name of Ohio Agricultural and Mechanical College.
While some interests in the state had hoped that the new university would focus on matriculating students of various agricultural and mechanical disciplines, Governor <a>Rutherford B. Hayes</a> foresaw a more classic, comprehensive university, and manipulated both the university's location and its initial board of trustees towards that end.
In 1878, and in light of its expanded focus, the college permanently changed its name to the now-familiar &quot;Ohio State University&quot;, with &quot;The&quot; as part of its official name, although the state code that declares the &quot;The&quot; neglects it in sections including in the title of the section that originally declares it.</p>
<p>Internationally, in 2010 <a>QS World University Rankings</a> ranked Ohio State University 124th in the world, and 40th in the United States.
It scored 88th in Social Sciences in the international subject rankings.
Three years back, China's <a>Shanghai Jiao Tong University</a> placed Ohio State as the 61st ranked university in the world in their 2007 <a>Academic Ranking of World Universities</a>.</p>
<p>The Lombardi Program on Measuring University Performance at <a>Arizona State University</a> detailed analysis and rankings of American universities currently places Ohio State as the 24th ranked university in America, the 10th ranked public university in the country and the top overall university in Ohio.
Of their nine ranking criteria, Ohio State ranked in the top-25 in four categories and between 26-50 in an additional four categories.
The <a>Washington Monthly</a> college rankings, which seek to evaluate colleges' contributions to American society based on factors of social mobility, cutting edge research, and service to the country by their graduates, currently place Ohio State as 12th in the nation and 10th among public universities.</p>
<p>Ohio State is also the only public university in Ohio to which the <a>Carnegie Foundation for the Advancement of Teaching</a> has given both its highest overall classification of <em>Doctoral/Very High Research Activity</em> and highest undergraduate admissions classification of <em>more selective</em>.</p>
<p><em>US News</em> ranks the undergraduate program at Ohio State's <a>Fisher College of Business</a> 12th in America and the top undergraduate business school in Ohio.
The graduate program of the Fisher College is ranked 21st in America and is the top graduate school of business in Ohio.
<p>Ohio State law school is ranked by <em>US News</em> as top of the nine law schools in Ohio and 31st overall in America.
Ohio State's medical school is ranked as the top public medical school in Ohio and 31st for research and 38th for primary care.
<em>US News</em> ranks Ohio State's undergraduate engineering program as the 25th best program in America and the top undergraduate engineering program in Ohio.
Its graduate program in engineering is ranked 26th in the country and highest in Ohio.
Ohio State's College of Education was ranked 17th in America by US News and the highest in Ohio.
<p>Ohio State's <a>political science</a> department is ranked 13th in the country by <em>US News &amp; World Report</em>, with the American politics section fifth, international politics 12th and political methodology 10th.
The most notable of these is the Center for Slavic and East European Studies founded in 1965 by Professor Leon Twarog.
<p>In a study by industry publication <em>Dance Teacher</em>, a survey of 100 dance department chairs in the United States and Canada ranked Ohio State's Department of <a>Dance</a> as the top ranked graduate program and the second ranked undergraduate program in North America.</p>
<p><a>U.S. News &amp; World Report</a> ranked the graduate program in Design at #5 in the nation in their 2009 rankings.
In its 2008 edition of &quot;America's Best Architecture &amp; Design Schools, the journal <em>DesignIntelligence</em> ranked the undergraduate <a>Industrial Design</a> program #3 nationwide, and the graduate program in Design #10 nationwide.

Each year since 2002, Ohio State has either led or been second among all American universities in the number of their faculty elected as fellows to the AAAS.
In 2008, three Ohio State faculty were awarded Guggenheim Fellowships, placing Ohio State among the top 15 universities in the nation.
Since the 2000-2001 award year, 55 Ohio State faculty members have been named as <a>Fulbright Fellows</a>, the highest of any Ohio university.</p>
Ohio State also announced in 2006, that it would be designating at least $110 million of its research efforts to what it termed &quot;fundamental concerns&quot; such as research towards a cure for cancer, renewable energy sources and sustainable drinking water supplies.</p>
The 2009 freshman class had an acceptance rate of 52%, with 54% of students graduating in the top 10% of their high school class and 87% in the top 25% of their class.

<p>Ohio State was among the first group of public universities to raise a $1 billion <a>endowment</a> when it passed the $1 billion mark in 1999.

<p>Ohio State is one of only three universities (the <a>University of Michigan</a> and the <a>University of California at Berkeley</a> being the others) to have won national championships in all three major men's sports (baseball, men's basketball, and football).
