The school was originally situated within a farming community located on the northern edge of Columbus.
<em><a>The Economist</a></em> ranked The Fisher College as the 29th ranked MBA program in the world in their 2005 <em>&quot;Which MBA?&quot;</em> issue.
A study by Simon Hix of The <a>London School of Economics</a> ranked it as the fourth best political science department in the world, based on publications.
<p>Undergraduate admissions to Ohio State are classified as &#x201c;more selective&#x201d; by <em><a>US News &amp; World Report</a></em> and <em><a>The Princeton Review</a></em> and according to the data are the most selective for any public university in Ohio.
<p>The <a>William Oxley Thompson Memorial Library</a>, anchoring the western end of The Oval, is Ohio State library's main branch and single largest repository.
An additional eight branches are located at off-campus research facilities, regional campuses, and a book storage depository near campus.
There are two student-run radio stations on campus.
Students also operate a local cable channel known as <a>Buckeye TV</a>, which airs primarily on the campus cable system operated by the Office of Information Technology (OIT).</p>
The traditional school songs from &quot;<a>Carmen Ohio</a>&quot; to &quot;<a>Hang on Sloopy</a>&quot; to &quot;Fight the Team Across the Field&quot;, are arranged to fit this unique instrumentation.
Upperclass finance students taking Business Finance 724 are given the opportunity to manage a twenty million dollar investment fund.
<p>The Residence Hall Advisory Council (RHAC), which is a representative body of all students living in the University's dormitory facilities, helps evaluate and improve the living conditions for dormitory residents.</p>
The 2007 freshman class contained 22.7% of first generation college students which far exceeded the national norm on American campuses of 15.9%.</p>
<p><a>thumb|200px|South Campus Gateway</a> Ohio State operates 31 on-campus residence halls divided into three geographic clusters: South Campus (site of the university's original dormitories), North Campus (largely constructed during the post-war enrollment boom) and Olentangy Area or &quot;The Towers.&quot;
Within the residence hall system are 40 smaller living and learning environments defined by social or academic considerations.
Students in the Scholars program are expected to live and take select classes with other members of the program.
The first of these, The Young Scholars Program, was initiated in 1988.
The program offers a series of academic camps each summer and counseling throughout the students' high school careers.
Four dormitories are designated all or in part as honors residences: Taylor, Bradley, Siebert, and Lincoln.</p>
<p>At Ohio State University, there are three recognized student governments that represent their constituents.</p>
The school colors are scarlet and gray.
