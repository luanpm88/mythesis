The 1.7&nbsp;km&lt;sup&gt;2&lt;/sup&gt; main campus in Burnaby, located 20&nbsp;km from downtown Vancouver, was established in 1965 and presently has more than 34,000 students and 950 faculty members.
<p>SFU was ranked 1st among Canada&#x2019;s Comprehensive Universities in 2009 and 2010 by <a>Macleans Magazine</a> , 57th in the world and 3rd in Canada in 2009 by Webometrics Ranking of World Universities.</p>
<p>Simon Fraser University was founded upon the recommendation of a 1958 report entitled <em>Higher Education in British Columbia and a Plan for the Future</em>, by <a>Dr.
J.B. Macdonald</a>, who recommended the creation of a new university in the Lower Mainland.
In May of the same year Dr. Gordon M. Shrum was appointed as the university's first Chancellor.
Architects <a>Arthur Erickson</a> and Geoffrey Massey won a competition to design the university, and construction began in the spring of 1964.
<p> The campus was noted in the 1960s and early 1970s as a hotbed of political activism, culminating in a crisis in the Department of Political Science, Sociology, and Anthropology in a dispute involving ideological differences among faculty.
<p>The school's original <a>coat of arms</a> was used from the university's inception until 2006, at which point the Board of Governors voted to adapt the old coat of arms and thereby register a second coat of arms.
<p>In 2009, SFU became the first Canadian university to be accepted into the <a>National Collegiate Athletic Association</a> (NCAA).
Starting in the 2011-2012 season, SFU will be competing in the NCAA&#x2019;s Division II Great Northwest Athletic Conference (GNAC).
In 2007, the University began offering <a>dual and double degrees</a> by partnering with international universities.
<p>SFU has been rated as Canada's best comprehensive university (in 1993, 1996, 1997, 1998, 2000, 2008, 2009 and 2010) in the annual rankings of Canadian universities in <em><a>Maclean's</a></em> magazine since 1991.
Research Infosource, Canada&#x2019;s leading provider of research intelligence evaluation, named SFU the top comprehensive university in Canada for &#x201c;publication effectiveness&#x201d; in 2006.
