<p><strong>Ume&#x00e5; University</strong> () is a <a>university</a> in <a>Ume&#x00e5;</a> in the mid-northern region of <a>Sweden</a>.
<p>Internationally, the university is known for research relating to the genome of the <a>Populus</a> tree (Life sciences), contributions to the <a>Gleason problem</a> and <a>function space</a>s on <a>fractals</a> (mathematics) and its school of industrial design which gives degree programs in English open to students from all of the world.
<p>Ume&#x00e5; University has four <a>faculties</a> and nine campus schools, with additional campuses in the cities of <a>Skellefte&#x00e5;</a> and <a>&#x00d6;rnsk&#x00f6;ldsvik</a>.
<li><a>Ume&#x00e5; School of Business</a> - aka: USBE, is one of Sweden's four <a>EQUIS</a> accredited business schools, about 2,000 students currently studies at USBE.
<li><a>Ume&#x00e5; School of Fine Arts</a> - was started in 1987 in a former factory next to the <a>Ume&#x00e4;lven</a> river.
