The university is known for innovation in medical research, public service, and patient care.
<p>Overall, the campus ranked third in the nation in annual <a>NIH</a> funding with $439&nbsp;million in 2007.</p>
<p>The Academic Ranking of World Universities, published annually by Shanghai Jiaotong University, in 2008 ranks UCSF 3rd in the world for Life and Agricultural Sciences and 2nd in the world for Clinical Medicine and Pharmacy.
In addition, the UCSF Medical Center in 2007 was ranked by <em>US News and World Report</em> the 7th-best hospital in the nation, making it the highest-ranked medical center in northern California.</p>
<p>In 2010, it ranked 4th overall among research-based medical schools by <em>US News and World Report</em>; the highest in the western United States.
It is one of only three medical schools that ranked top 10 in both research and primary care categories, the others being the <a>University of Washington School of Medicine</a> and the <a>University of Pennsylvania School of Medicine</a>.
In addition, the magazine ranked UCSF in the top 10 in seven of the eight medical school specialty programs assessed, including first in AIDS medical care, second in women's health, and third in internal medicine.
This figure rose from 2008 when the School of Medicine received a total of $394.9&nbsp;million in NIH funds, but was still the second largest recipient.</p>
<p>In 2008, <em>US News and World Report</em> ranked the UCSF graduate programs in <a>nursing</a> as second in the nation.
<p>In <em>San Francisco Magazine's</em> 2003 survey of the &quot;Best Doctors&quot; in the Bay Area, 55 percent of those honored were UCSF faculty.</p>
