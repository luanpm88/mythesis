<p>  The <strong>University of California, San Francisco</strong> (<strong>UCSF</strong>) is one of the world's leading centers of health sciences research, patient care, and education.
UCSF's medical, pharmacy, dentistry, nursing, and graduate schools are among the top health science professional schools in the world.
The UCSF Medical Center is consistently ranked among the top 10 hospitals in the United States by <em><a>U.S. News &amp; World Report</a></em>.
The Schools of Dentistry, Medicine, Nursing, and Pharmacy and the Biomedical Sciences graduate program are also located at Parnassus.
The building is named after venture capitalist <a>Brook Byers</a>, co-chair of UCSF's capital campaign that concluded in 2005 and raised over $1.6&nbsp;billion.
The building is named in honor of William J. Rutter, former <a>chairman</a> of the university's Department of Biochemistry &amp; Biophysics and co-founder of <a>Chiron Corporation</a>.
The headquarters of the new <a>California Institute for Regenerative Medicine</a> are also located nearby in the Mission Bay neighborhood.</p>
The professional schools of the University of California, San Francisco are among the top in the nation, according to current (2006) <em>US News and World Report</em> graduate school and other rankings.
<p>In 2009, the School of Medicine was the second largest recipient of <a>National Institutes of Health</a> research funds among all US medical schools, receiving awards totaling $417.7&nbsp;million.
The pediatric nurse practitioner specialty ranked fifth nationally, while the gerontology/geriatrics and nursing service administration programs ranked seventh.</p>
<p>The School of Nursing in 2007 ranked first nationally in total <a>NIH</a> research funds with $13.8&nbsp;million.</p>
