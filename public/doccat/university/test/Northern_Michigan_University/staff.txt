The USOEC has more residential athletes than the <a>Lake Placid</a> and <a>Chula Vista</a> sites combined.
<p>USOEC athletes attend NMU or <a>Marquette Senior High School, Marquette, Michigan</a> while training in their respective sports.
<p>On-campus USOEC athletes live in  NMU&#x2019;s  Meyland Hall, eat in campus dining halls, and train at the university&#x2019;s <a>Berry Events Center</a> and <a>Superior Dome</a>.
<p>The USOEC also offers a variety of short-term training camps; regional, national, and international competitions; coaches and officials education clinics; and an educational program for retired Olympians.</p>
Roughly 50 <a>Cadets</a> train year round to earn their commissions as <a>United States Army</a> Officers in both the Active Duty and Reserve components.
NMU <a>ROTC</a> also trains a specially selected group of Cadets to compete in the annual Ranger Challenge competition held in <a>Fort McCoy, Wisconsin</a>.
