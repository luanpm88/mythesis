<p>The <strong>University of Oslo</strong> (), is the <a>oldest</a> and largest <a>university in Norway</a>, situated in the Norwegian capital of <a>Oslo</a>.
<p>The university has faculties of (<a>Lutheran</a>) <a>Theology</a>, <a>Law</a>, <a>Medicine</a>, <a>Humanities</a>, <a>Mathematics</a> and <a>Natural Sciences</a>, <a>Dentistry</a>, <a>Social Sciences</a>, and <a>Education</a>.
The Faculty of <a>Law</a> is still located at the old campus on <a>Karl Johans gate</a>, near the <a>National Theatre</a>, the <a>Royal Palace</a>, and the <a>Parliament</a>, while most of the other faculties are located at a modern campus area called <a>Blindern</a>, erected from the 1930s.

<p>The <a>Times Higher Education World University Rankings</a> ranked UiO 186th worldwide in 2010 (2nd in Norway behind the <a>University of Bergen</a>), while the <a>Shanghai Jiao Tong University</a>'s <a>Academic Ranking of World Universities</a> ranked UiO 75nd worldwide (and best in Norway).</p>
<p>Also in 2010, the <a>QS World University Rankings</a> ranked UiO 100th worldwide, while the <a>Webometrics Ranking of World Universities</a> ranked UiO 53rd worldwide.</p>
