<p><strong>Occidental College</strong> is a selective, <a>private</a>, <a>coeducational</a> <a>liberal arts college</a> located in the <a>Eagle Rock</a> neighborhood of <a>Los Angeles, California</a>.
Occidental students have won 10 <a>Rhodes Scholarships</a>, 12 <a>Truman Scholarships</a>, 55 <a>Watson Fellowships</a> and, since 2003, 51 <a>Fulbright Scholarships</a>.
The <a>Carnegie Foundation for the Advancement of Teaching</a> selected Occidental as a community engagement institution, becoming one of a handful of liberal arts colleges to be so designated for its commitment to mutually beneficial collaboration with its surrounding communities.</p>
<p><a>left|thumb|250px|Highland Park campus, 1904.</a></p>
<p>Occidental College was founded on April 20, 1887, by a group of <a>Presbyterian</a> clergy and laymen, including <a>James George Bell</a>.
Initially located in <a>Boyle Heights</a>, the college moved to a new campus in Los Angeles&#x2019; <a>Highland Park</a> neighborhood in 1898.
That same year, Occidental President <a>John Willis Baer</a> announced the trustees&#x2019; decision to convert Oxy into an all-men&#x2019;s institution.
<p>In 1962, <em><a>Time Magazine</a></em> described Occidental as a little giant in a story about the college&#x2019;s rise to national prominence.
In 1969, the school opened its first two co-ed <a>dormitories</a>, and two more followed a year later.
<p>In 1979, Occidental installed <em>Water Forms II</em> (see <a>image below</a>), a kinetic fountain designed by professor George Baker.
By 1986, for the first time since <a>World War II</a>, women students outnumbered men.
On July 1, 2009, <a>Jonathan Veitch</a>, formerly dean of <a>The New School</a>'s <a>Eugene Lang College</a>, became Occidental's 15th president, and the first to be a native <a>Angeleno</a>.</p>

<p>Many Occidental students participate in off-campus programs in the <a>United States</a> and in <a>Europe</a>, <a>Asia</a>, <a>Africa</a>, the <a>Middle East</a>, <a>Latin America</a>, and <a>Oceania</a>.
<p>Occidental offers a unique Occidental-at-the-<a>United Nations</a> Program in <a>New York</a>.
When selected, students intern in the <a>United Nations Secretariat</a> or with a related institution, such as the <a>US State Department</a> or an international <a>NGO</a>.
Some students also study in <a>Washington, D.C.</a> through <a>American University</a>.</p>
<p>Occidental is among a handful of American colleges that participates in the Richter Summer Research Program, in which students compete for a chance to pursue independent research or creative work anywhere in the world.
<p>With a competitive GPA and LSAT scores, <a>Columbia Law School</a> admits students upon completion of their junior year at Occidental into its Accelerated Interdisciplinary Program in Legal Education.
<p>Students who are interested in <a>biotechnology</a> and who become a <a>biochemistry</a> major maintaining a 3.2 GPA in the necessary courses, will be guaranteed admission to the Keck master&#x2019;s in bioscience program.

Occidental features 21 varsity sports teams and a program of club sports and intramural competition.
<p>During the 2006&#x2013;2007 athletic season, the Tiger&#x2019;s <a>cross country</a>, <a>American football</a> and <a>basketball</a> teams were <a>Southern California Intercollegiate Athletic Conference</a> champions.
The school&#x2019;s Blackshirts <a>Rugby union</a> team was also league champion for the first time in five years.
