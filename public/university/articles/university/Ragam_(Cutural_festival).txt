<p>    <strong>RAGAM</strong> is the annual inter-collegiate cultural festival organized by the <a>National Institute of Technology, Calicut</a> (NITC), <a>Kerala</a>, <a>India</a>. Ragam is organized during the month of February or March and lasts for four or five days. Colleges and universities all over Kerala and outside the state compete with each other in events as wide ranging as trivia quizzes and dance competitions to be crowned champions of the festival.</p>
<p>Ragam was started in the late Seventies in the memory of a former student <a>P. Rajan</a>. He died after being held (ostensibly for being a <a>Naxalite</a>) in police custody.</p>
<h2>History</h2>
<p>Ragam, a fest which has spanned 2 decades, is undoubtedly one of India's finest and Kerala&#x2019;s foremost cultural festival. The humble yet controversial beginnings of this festival are well known to every college student worth his salt.</p>
<p>The Festival has seen many famous musicians and bands perform. Among the famous names that performed for Ragam over the years are <a>Shankar Mahadevan</a>, <a>Shaan</a>, <a>Karthik</a>, <a>Balabhaskar</a>, <a>Benny Dayal</a>, <a>Parikrama</a>, <a>Silk Route</a>, <a>Motherjane</a>, Breathe The Floyd ( A pink floyd tribute band from UK). </p>
<p>Every Ragam promises to be bigger and better than the previous one and keeps the promise.</p>

<h3>Ragam '09 -<em>A Cult, A Culture</em></h3>
<p>Ragam &#x2019;09 saw NITC play host to 5000 people representing colleges from all parts of Kerala and the rest of the country. During the three days, one could see a very colorful sight on the Rajpath accompanied by the sounds of people having the time of their lives. The presence of people from all over the country was indeed a testimony to the transformation of Ragam into a fest of national eminence.</p>
<p>At Ragam &#x2019;09, three new events were introduced to add to the already delectable palette of cultural offerings, namely couple dance, the Chakravyuham and the Kathaprasangam.Music events like Amplified and Raaga showcased talents in the western and eastern styles respectively. The literary events like JAM, extempore, etc. which saw participation in excess of 300, proved to be competitive for the literary buffs while dance events like the Choreo Nite captivated the crowds with a rich display of graceful steps and tantalizing movements. </p>
<p>The highlights of Ragam &#x2019;09 were the three pro shows. The first one was by renowned south Indian playback singer Benny Dayal who kept the crowd mesmerized and off their seats with his performances of super hit songs like &#x2018;adiyae kolluthaey&#x2019;,&#x2019;taxi taxi&#x2019; and &#x2018;Pappu cant dance&#x2019; among others. The second day was NITC&#x2019;s appointment with the phenomenal Shankar Mahadhevan. His electric performance set the stage on fire and his masterfully rendered hit songs threw the crowd into rapture. The last performance was by Pink Floyd tribute band, &#x2019;Breathe the Floyd&#x2019;. NITC being their first stop on an all India tour, they transported the crowd into the depths of psychedelic rock and played some all time hits of the legendary band.</p>
<h3>Ragam '08 -<em>My Muse,My Canvas</em></h3>
<p>Over 4000 students of 130 colleges participated in Ragam 08. The 26th to 29 March 2008, saw the sprawling campus come alive with the sounds of music and dance and the oratory skills of the future world leaders. The events included group dances, western and eastern music competitions both group and solo, dumb charades and all kinds of literary events , enough to set any college students imagination alight!</p>
<p>Staying true to the grand scale on which it is organized, Ragam 08 was backed up by the sponsorships of bigwigs such as tata indicom and sony.</p>
<p>The highlight of the event was probably the Pro Shows again, this time featuring popular playback singer Karthik, the hip indian rock band, the Raghu Dixit Project and the white metal band, Whitenoise.</p>
<h3>Ragam '07 -<em>Where the die rolls 7</em></h3>
<p>With about 4000 students from over 110 colleges participating in various events ,Ragam 07 was a huge success.Students from across the country vied for the top honours to bring glory to their alma mater.</p>
<p>The fest start off with a musical event followed by events which portrayed out each participants creativity and talents. The participation for &#x2018;on the spot&#x2019; events was high, pencil -sketching event was a crowd-puller. Western orchestras electrified the audience with some hot numbers.  The main dancing event , which over the years has been arguably the major attraction at Ragam , saw 14 teams from various colleges this year.</p>
<p>Shaan,the young singing sensation of Bollywood ,took the proshows at Ragam to an unprecedented level with his benchmark  performance.</p>

<h2>Events</h2>
<p>The traditional events are categorised under literary, dramatics, fine arts and speaking events. The literary events include Dumb Charades, What's The Good Word, Pictionary, Triple Mash and Creative Writing, while the speaking events consists of Extempore, Just A Minute (JAM), Block and Tackle, debate and mock press. The fine arts events include clay modelling, collage, pot painting, face painting, cartooning, Rangoli and pencil sketching.</p>
<p>The music and dance programmes include Western orchestra, Eastern orchestra, light music, group dance and classical dance. The dramatics competitions planned are mime, mimicry, mono act, street play and tableau. There are also crowd-pullers such as the Ragam Quizzes, pot pourri, movie spoof and Ad-Apt.</p>
