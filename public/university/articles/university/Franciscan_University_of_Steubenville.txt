<p><strong>Franciscan University of Steubenville</strong> is a <a>Catholic</a> institution located in <a>Steubenville, Ohio</a>, 40 miles west of <a>Pittsburgh, Pennsylvania</a>.  The school (originally named the &quot;College of Steubenville&quot;) was founded in 1946 by the Franciscan Friars of the Third Order Regular.  In 1974, <a>Fr. Michael Scanlan, TOR</a>, became president and began a series of major reforms to restore the school to its Catholic heritage.  In 1985, the University changed its name from &quot;College of Steubenville&quot; to &quot;University of Steubenville,&quot; and in 1985 to &quot;Franciscan University of Steubenville.&quot;</p>
<h2>Academics</h2>
<p>The school offers five associate degree programs, 36 bachelor degree majors (seven pre-professional programs), 34 minors, and seven graduate degree programs. The University maintains a 15:1 student-faculty ratio.   </p>
<p>Students need a minimum of 124 credits for graduation. The number of electives varies with each major program. The University operates on the American <a>semester</a> system. Three summer sessions also are available.</p>
<p>Franciscan University of Steubenville participates in the <a>Advanced Placement (AP) Program</a>, the <a>College Level Examination Program (CLEP)</a>, and <a>International Baccalaureate (IB)</a> and gives credit by examination in a number of subjects.</p>
<h3>Associate degree programs</h3>
<p>Associate degrees are awarded in accounting, business administration, child development, general studies, and theology. </p>
<h3>Undergraduate programs</h3>
<p>Bachelor of Arts degrees are awarded in biology, catechetics (formerly known as religious education), chemistry, classics, communication arts (multimedia, journalism, and TV/radio), drama, economics, English (drama, British and American literature, Western and world literature, and writing), French, German, history, humanities and Catholic culture, legal studies, philosophy, political science, psychology,sacred music, sociology, Spanish, and theology. </p>
<p>Bachelor of Science degrees are awarded in accounting, anthropology, biology, chemistry, international business, economics, finance, management, marketing, computer information science, computer science, education (with 14 different licensure programs), mathematical science, mental health and human services, nursing, and social work.</p>
<h3>Special programs</h3>
<p>The Army ROTC program was approved in 2010, and currently has 13 cadets. Air Force ROTC classes are offered through the University of Pittsburgh.</p>
<p>The University offers the following pre-professional programs: dentistry, law, medicine, optometry, pharmacy, physical therapy, and veterinary medicine. For undergraduate business majors, a 4+1 program allows for accelerated completion of an MBA.</p>
<p>There is also an honors program in the <a>Great Books of Western Civilization</a>, offered to highly qualified undergraduate candidates.    </p>
<p>The Pre-Theologate program offers human, academic, spiritual, pastoral and fraternal formation for men discerning the priesthood.</p>
<h3>Graduate programs</h3>
<p>In addition to its undergraduate programs, the University also offers a Master of Arts in Counseling, Master of Arts in Philosophy, Master of Arts in Theology and Christian Ministry, <a>Master of Business Administration (MBA)</a>, Master of Science in Education, Master of Science in Educational Administration, and Master of Science in Nursing. The Master of Arts in Theology and Christian Ministry can be earned through the Distance Learning Program, with most courses available via audiotaped lectures.</p>
<h2>Austrian program</h2>
<p>Since 1991, up to 150 students per semester study at the University&#x2019;s program in <a>Gaming</a>,  <a>Austria</a>.  The campus is located in a renovated fourteenth-century <a>Carthusian</a> monastery, known as the Kartause Maria Thron or the <a>Gaming Charterhouse</a>, in the foothills of the Austrian <a>Alps</a>.    </p>
<p>The Austrian Program features a four-day class schedule, so students may spend extended time visiting religious, cultural, and historical sites throughout Europe. The program sponsors trips throughout Europe including places such as: Vienna, Salzburg, and Munich. Along with the Poland pilgrimage, the program also includes a ten-day pilgrimage to Rome and Assisi.</p>
<h2>Student life</h2>
<p>Students are encouraged to join in faith households, small groups of students whose members study, recreate, and pray with one another. Typically, these student groups are attached to a particular dormitory on campus and are centered around particular devotions or charisms.   </p>
<p>The campus is also known for its liturgies, retreats and spiritual talks. Hundreds of students make a weekly commitment to <a>Eucharistic adoration</a>, and most Masses have standing room only, even on weekdays. The Works of Mercy Program places students shoulder-to-shoulder with the poor and marginalized in inner city and rural communities. Over summer, winter, and spring breaks, students volunteer to help others and preach in the United States and in countries such as Ecuador, Haiti, Jamaica, and Thailand. Students join the pro-life group, Students for Life, while others sign up for evangelization and Christian outreach activities spearheaded by the Student Life Office, households, and other campus groups.</p>
<p>There is a 28-member student government.</p>
<p>Student-run clubs and academic organizations include College Republicans, Democrats for Life, Computer Science Club, Tennis Club, Students for Life, Philosophy Club, St. Jerome Debate Society, Ut Unim Sint club for ecumenism, and Biology Club. </p>
<h2>Athletics</h2>
<h3>Varsity</h3>
<p>Franciscan University's athletics teams, nicknamed the Barons, primarily compete in NCAA Division III, in the Allegheny Mountain Collegiate Conference. The men's rugby team competes in Division II with the Allegheny Rugby Union.</p>
<p>Franciscan University fields men's teams in basketball, baseball, cross-country, tennis, soccer, track and field, and rugby. Women's teams are: basketball, softball, cross-country, tennis, soccer, track and field, and volleyball.</p>
<h3>Intramurals</h3>
<p>Franciscan University offers a variety of intramural sports throughout the academic year. Flag football and volleyball are held during the fall semester, and basketball and ultimate Frisbee in the spring. A co-ed Sunday futsal soccer league runs through both semesters, and there are several one-day tournaments held periodically, such as co-ed softball, sand volleyball, and 3-on-3 basketball.</p>
<h2>The campus</h2>
<p>The academic buildings on campus include Egan Hall, Stafford Hall, <a>Saints Cosmas and Damian</a> Hall, and the <a>Saint Joseph</a> Center.</p>
<p>Egan Hall houses: classrooms, a theater, television and radio studios, special laboratories for the education, and psychology departments, and computer workstations on each floor.</p>
<p>In the newly remodeled Stafford Hall, a simulated clinic gives nursing students the opportunity to practice their skills.  Stafford Hall also contains classrooms and offices.</p>
<p><a>Saints Cosmas and Damian</a> Hall is the main science building.  It houses biology and chemistry laboratories, classrooms, and the campus' largest lecture hall.  It also serves as additional laboratory space for the <a>Nursing</a> Department, and contains two computer science labs with advanced software for programming.</p>
<p>Starvaggi Hall is the main administrative building on campus. It also houses Admissions, Financial Aid, Career Services, and the Registrar.</p>
<p>The John Paul II Library&#x2019;s collection includes more than 230,000 books and bound periodicals, and more than 390 current periodicals. The OPAL Catalog and <a>OhioLINK Network</a> provide access to many research databases and more than 7 million books and journals.    </p>
<p>There are 12 residence buildings on campus: <a>Saint Francis</a> Hall, <a>Trinity</a> Hall, <a>Marian</a> Hall, <a>Saint Thomas More</a> Hall, <a>Saint Louis</a> Hall, <a>Saint Elizabeth</a> Hall, <a>Kolbe</a> Hall, <a>Clare</a> Hall, <a>Padua</a> Hall, <a>Saint Bonaventure</a> Hall, <a>Vianney</a> Hall, and <a>Scotus</a> Hall. Assisi Heights, a small neighborhood of apartments, is also available for upperclassman and graduate student housing.</p>
<p>Franciscan University of Steubenville has two <a>soccer</a> fields, a <a>rugby</a> field, a <a>baseball</a> field, and a field designated primarily for intramural sports.  In 2007, the university purchased the golf course which borders the main campus from the city of Steubenville for future development. It is currently used by the cross country team for practice.  </p>
<p>Christ the King Chapel is the center of the spiritual life of the campus.  There are three Masses every weekday while classes are in session, four Masses on Sundays, vespers on Sunday evening, praise &amp; worship every Tuesday, and confessions held at least four times per week. Weekday Masses are routinely standing room only, while Sunday Masses during the school year require extra chairs to be arranged in the foyer and the Eucharistic chapel.</p>
<p>The <a>Portiuncula</a> chapel, a replica of St. Francis' original chapel, sits on the edge of the main campus. It is home to <a>perpetual adoration</a> (at least two students volunteer to be present and adore <a>the Blessed Sacrament</a> during every hour of the week throughout the fall and spring semesters).  Outside of this chapel are the Tomb of the Unborn Child, which contains the remains of seven aborted infants, a <a>Creche</a>, <a>Stations of the Cross</a>, and Marian Grotto. In 2009 the Vatican designated the Portiuncula as a place of pilgrimage where the faithful can obtain a plenary <a>indulgence</a> on five certain days through the year and under certain conditions of prayer and a detachment from sin.</p>
<p>The J.C. Willams Center is the student center on campus.  It contains a coffee shop and eatery, the campus bookstore, student mailboxes, several meeting rooms and four lecture halls, and areas for studying or socializing. It also houses the Tom and Nina Gentile Gallery, which contains numerous works of art donated to the University.</p>
<p>The Finnegan Fieldhouse is home to a <a>basketball</a> court, two <a>racquetball</a> courts, a weight room, one room for aerobic classes, a cardio-vascular room, and the campus health and counseling center, as well as classrooms.</p>
<p>At the far north end of campus is the Steel Cross.  This cross, made of two steel <a>I-beam</a>s, is 35 feet tall and visible from afar.</p>
<h2>Reputation</h2>
<p>The University is ranked in the top tier in its category (Masters Colleges in the Midwest) in the 2011 U.S. News &amp; World Report&#x2019;s list of America&#x2019;s Best Colleges.  Young America's Foundation rates Franciscan one of the top 10 conservative colleges in the nation, and the Cardinal Newman Society ranks it as one of the 21 top Catholic colleges and universities in <em>The Newman Guide to Choosing a Catholic College</em>.</p>
<p>Franciscan University of Steubenville graduates have gained admission to such post-graduate institutions as <a>Yale Law School</a>, <a>Notre Dame Law School</a>, <a>UCLA Medical Center</a>, and the <a>Mendoza College of Business</a> at Notre Dame.</p>
