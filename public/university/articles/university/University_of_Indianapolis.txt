<p>The <strong>University of Indianapolis</strong> is a <a>university</a> located in <a>Indianapolis</a>, <a>Indiana</a>, and affiliated with the <a>United Methodist Church</a>. The shortened name it uses is <strong>UIndy</strong>. With a branch campus in Athens, <a>Greece</a>, and affiliations with campuses in <a>Belize</a> (<a>Galen University</a>), the <a>People's Republic of China</a>, <a>Cyprus</a>, and <a>Israel</a>, the campus embraces a very diverse population of students and opportunities. Established in 1902, the university offers bachelor's, master's, and doctoral degrees and has approximately 4,300 students.</p>
<h2>Academics</h2>
<p><a>thumb|right|250px|Good Hall, the university's administration building</a></p>
<p>The University of Indianapolis offers nine associate's, 70 bachelor's, 22 master's, and 5 doctoral programs.</p>
<p>UIndy is organized into the following schools and colleges:</p>
<ul>
  <li>School of Education</li>
  <li>College of Arts and Sciences</li>
  <li>Krannert School of Physical Therapy</li>
  <li>School for Adult Learning</li>
  <li>School of Business</li>
  <li>School of Nursing</li>
  <li>School of Psychological Sciences</li>
  <li>School of Occupational Therapy</li>
</ul>
<p>UIndy also has an international branch campus, named University of Indianapolis&#x2014;Athens, in <a>Greece</a>.</p>
<h2>History</h2>
<p></p>
<p>The university was chartered in 1902, but instruction did not start until 1905. When established, it was formally called <strong><a>Indiana</a> Central University</strong> (ICU), but colloquially known as <strong>Indiana Central College</strong> (ICC) from 1921 to 1975.  &quot;ICU&quot; can still be seen in brick on the front side of <a>Krannert</a> Memorial Library on campus. In 1986, the name became <strong>University of Indianapolis</strong>.</p>

<h2>Sports</h2>
<h3>Athletics</h3>
<p>The University of Indianapolis' athletic teams are known as the <em><a>Greyhounds</a></em> and participate in <a>Division II</a> of the <a>NCAA</a>. Most of the teams are members of the <a>Great Lakes Valley Conference</a>. The football and swimming &amp; diving teams are members of the <a>Great Lakes Intercollegiate Athletic Conference</a>. The wrestling team is not affiliated with a conference. The school's highest finish in the NACDA Director's Cup was 5th in 1996-97 with 390.5 points. Also finished 8th in the Director's Cup in 2000-01 and in the top 20 from 2002-2005.</p>
<h3>Colors</h3>
<p>The colors of the University of Indianapolis are crimson and grey.</p>

<h3>Mascot</h3>
<p>The sports teams that represent the University of Indianapolis are called the Greyhounds, or just Hounds for short. The mascot is a <a>greyhound</a> named &quot;Ace&quot;.</p>

<h3>School song</h3>
<p>The university's song, simply called &quot;U of I Fight Song,&quot; was written in 1975 by James M. Stanton, at the time an Indiana Central senior. The words were rewritten when the university changed its name in 1986. In 2006, the cheer leaders changed the &quot;U of I&quot; portions of the song to &quot;UIndy&quot; to reflect the preferred shortened name of the school.</p>
