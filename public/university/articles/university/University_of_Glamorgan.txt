<p> </p>
<p>The <strong>University of Glamorgan</strong> () is a <a>university</a> based in <a>Pontypridd</a>, <a>Rhondda Cynon Taf</a>, <a>Wales</a> with campuses in <a>Treforest</a>, <a>Glyntaff</a>, <a>Merthyr Tydfil</a>, <a>Tyn y Wern</a> and <a>Cardiff</a>. The university has seven departments and faculties, and is the only university in Wales which has no current or former link with the <a>University of Wales</a>. The school was owned and funded by the major Welsh coal owners, through a levy of one tenth of a penny on each ton of coal produced by the companies involved. The school was taken over by <a>Glamorgan County Council</a> during the Depression, In 2007,  &quot;<a>ATRiuM</a>&quot;, a new facility for teaching and research in media, design and the arts was opened in <a>Cardiff city centre</a>.</p>
<p>The University currently serves around 21,500 students, with 10,227 registered as full-time <a>undergraduate</a>s.</p>
<h2>Academic rankings</h2>
<p></p>
<h2>Reputation</h2>
<p>The bulk of full-time students enter through the <a>UCAS</a> system with <a>A-levels</a> or equivalent qualifications and many of the University's degree courses are selective in that they require specific <a>A-levels</a> or above average grades for entry. According to the Times University rankings of 2011, Glamorgan is ranked 94th of the 114 British universities, maintaining its 2010 position. </p>
<ul>
  <li>A Next Generation Networks, IMS Experience Lab</li>
  <li>One of only 20 UK business schools to get an 'excellent ratings' from the government.</li>
  <li>Research in <a>biohydrogen</a> production and <a>wastewater treatment</a>.</li>
  <li>The only university in Wales to be awarded the <a>Environmental ISO 14001</a>.</li>
  <li>The University of Glamorgan, with <a>Cardiff University</a> and <a>South Wales Police</a>, forms the Universities Police Science Institute (UPSI) - a unique organisation in the UK dedicated to research and training in police related matters. Specialists in police and forensic related matters are organised within the Glamorgan Centre for Police Sciences.The University has its own Crime Scenes Investigation House. </li>
  <li>A <a>&#x00a3;</a>35 million (<a>&#x20ac;</a>46.7 million or <a>$</a>68.6 million) investment in the <a>city of Cardiff</a>, completed in 2007, houses the <strong><a>Atrium</a></strong>.</li>
  <li>The University, along with Cardiff, Swansea, Aberystwyth and Bangor Universities is part of the St David's Group, which is dedicated to acting together drive forward the knowledge economy in Wales.</li>
  <li>Glamorgan, in partnership with the University of Wales Newport, is leading the development of the University of the Heads of the Valleys Initiative (UHOVI)</li>
</ul>
<h2>Campuses</h2>
<p>The University has several campuses:</p>
<ul>
  <li><strong>Trefforest</strong> - the main campus plays host to the bulk of academic departments and facilities, including the indoor sports centre and students union. It is accessed via <a>Treforest railway station</a> from either <a>Cardiff</a>, <a>Merthyr</a> or the <a>Rhondda</a>; regular bus services or the <a>A470</a>. </li>
  <li><strong>Glyntaff</strong> - originally established purely for law, it now hosts exclusively the Nursing Courses under the Faculty of Health, Sport and Science. All Science and Sport, including Police Sciences.</li>
  <li><strong>Partner colleges</strong> - The University has specialised &quot;partner colleges&quot; throughout <a>South Wales</a>; for example, <a>Barry College</a> for <a>aeronautical engineering</a>.</li>
  <li><strong>Merthyr Tydfil</strong> - <a>Merthyr Tydfil College</a> has become part of the University of Glamorgan Group (but not part of the University), although its focus remains on delivering Further Education rather than Higher Education.</li>
  <li><strong>Tyn y Wern</strong> - Is home to the university's sports facilities and playing fields.</li>
  <li><strong><a>Atrium</a></strong> - The Cardiff School of Creative and Cultural Industries is based at a new Cardiff campus located close to <a>Cardiff Queen Street railway station</a>.</li>
</ul>

<h2>Facilities</h2>
<p>There is one halls of residence, within the Treforest campus:</p>
<ul>
  <li><em>Glamorgan Court</em> - Built in 1996, with all the rooms in the hall buildings arranged into clusters of six en-suite bedrooms with a shared kitchen. </li>
</ul>
<p>There is currently building work on site for new accommodation blocks, which should be ready September 2011.</p>
<p>The <a>Students Union</a> is also on the Treforest campus. The Student Union is the home to a number of sports teams, sports clubs and societies as well as TAG, the student newspaper.</p>
<p>The Union has also welfare, education and equalitiy support and there is also a new democratic structure change to Student Council. </p>
<p>Additional educational facilities include a 24-hour PC lab, wireless internet access in specified areas, world class equipment for nursing courses (including life sized dummies that simulate human beings, from breathing to giving birth), a TV studio, an on-campus radio studio, and two theatres </p>
<ul>
  <li><em>Oriel Y Bont</em> galleries, an art gallery that hosts a collection of <a>Ernest Zobole</a> paintings. In 2002 the gallery achieved Museum Status.</li>
  <li><em>Learning Resource Centre</em>: The library of the University has books and all other media facilities as well as daily newspapers from around the country and journal articles. Online research journals such as <a>Mintel</a> and <a>Keynotes</a> are available for students to use for free.</li>
</ul>
<h2>Awards</h2>
<ul>
  <li>In 2007 University of Glamorgan has been rated the &quot;most environmentally friendly university&quot; in Wales, and 4th &quot;greenest&quot; facility in the UK, in the 2007 Green League Table being published in the <a>Times Higher Education Supplement</a> (<a>THES</a>), produced by <a>People &amp; Planet</a></li>
  <li>Glamorgan is the first university in Wales, and only the 8th in the whole of the UK, to have been awarded the nationally recognised <a>Investor in People</a> status, for staff training and development.</li>
  <li>The Business School received <a>Chartered Institute of Purchasing and Supply</a> Centre of Excellence accreditation, being one of only 13 accredited centres in the UK.</li>
  <li>Glamorgan has received the first prize in the national competition for best course designs and teaching ability three times.</li>
  <li>Electronic Product Engineering within the School of Electronics, and Environmental Technology and Management, have been designated &quot;Centres of Expertise&quot; for Wales.</li>
</ul>
