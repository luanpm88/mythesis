<p><a>thumb|300px|Tirana University</a> <strong>University of Tirana</strong> (<em>Universiteti i Tiran&#x00eb;s</em>) is located in <a>Tirana</a>, <a>Albania</a>. It was the first university in Albania when it was founded in October 1957 as the <strong>State University of Tirana</strong> (<em>Universiteti Shtet&#x00eb;ror i Tiran&#x00eb;s</em>) by joining together five existing institutes of higher education, the most important being the Institute of Sciences, founded in 1947. Between 1985 and 1992 it was called <strong>Enver Hoxha University of Tirana</strong> (<em>Universiteti i Tiran&#x00eb;s &quot;Enver Hoxha&quot;</em>); after the <a>First Secretary</a> of the <a>Communist</a> <a>Party</a>, <a>Enver Hoxha</a>. Initially it comprised ten faculties, but in 1991 it was broken up into the <a>Polytechnic University of Tirana</a>, which was formed of the <a>engineering</a> faculties, and the main University of Tirana which comprised the seven remaining faculties covering <a>humanities</a>, <a>economics</a>, <a>natural science</a> and <a>medicine</a>.</p>
<p>The number of students attending this university is over 14,000 and the number of teaching staff is over 600. The Rector is <a>Dhori Kule</a> (from 2008).</p>
