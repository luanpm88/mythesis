<p><strong>M&#x00e4;lardalen University College</strong> (Swedish: <strong>M&#x00e4;lardalens <a>H&#x00f6;gskola</a></strong>), or <strong>MdH</strong>, is a <a>university college</a> located in <a>V&#x00e4;ster&#x00e5;s</a> and <a>Eskilstuna</a>, Sweden. It has 13,000 students and over 1,000 employees, of which around 40 are <a>professor</a>s. M&#x00e4;lardalen University College is the world's first environmentally certified school according to the international standard <a>ISO 14001</a>.</p>
<h2>Organization</h2>
<p>Since 1 January 2008, M&#x00e4;lardalen College has been officially organized into four schools:</p>
<ul>
  <li>School of Health, Care and Social Welfare</li>
  <li>School of Education, Culture and Communication</li>
  <li>School of Sustainable Development of Society and Technology</li>
  <li>School of Innovation, Design and Engineering</li>
</ul>
<p>Prior to 2008, the university college had 9 departments:</p>
<ul>
  <li>Biology and Chemical Engineering (IBK)</li>
  <li>Computer Science and Electronics (IDE)</li>
  <li>Public Technology (ISt)</li>
  <li>School of Business (EkI)</li>
  <li>Humanities (IHu)</li>
  <li>Innovation, Design and Product Development (IDP)</li>
  <li>Mathematics and Physics (IMa)</li>
  <li>Social Sciences (ISB)</li>
  <li>Caring and Public Health Sciences (IVF)</li>
</ul>
<h2>President</h2>
<p>Professor <a>Ingegerd Palm&#x00e9;r</a> was appointed President of M&#x00e4;lardalen University College by the Swedish Government and took office on 17 October 2005. Palm&#x00e9;r has a PhD in Mathematics and has filled various positions at the <a>Royal Institute of Technology</a>, <a>Stockholm University</a> and <a>Lule&#x00e5; University of Technology</a>. She served two full terms as President of Lule&#x00e5; University of Technology between 1994&#x2013;2005, leaving office due to term limits. She is a fellow of the <a>Royal Swedish Academy of Engineering Sciences</a>.</p>
