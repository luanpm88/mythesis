<p> The <strong>Blekinge Institute of Technology</strong> () (BTH) is a <a>Swedish</a> <a>Institute of technology</a> in <a>Blekinge</a> with more than 8,000 students (part-time, full-time) and offers 350 courses in 40 educational programmes in five schools at two campuses located in <a>Karlskrona</a> and <a>Karlshamn</a>. </p>
<p>The school focuses on applied <a>information technology</a> and <a>sustainable development</a>; additionally also offers programs in management, economy, health, planning, social science and the humanities.  </p>
<p>Blekinge Institute of Technology is located in the Telecom City area and works closely together with many telecommunications and software companies including <a>Vodafone</a> (now <a>Telenor</a>), <a>Ericsson AB</a> and Wireless Independent Provider (WIP). </p>
<h2>Outline</h2>
<p>The following locations host departments of the University in <a>Blekinge</a>:</p>
<ul>
  <li>Campus Gr&#x00e4;svik in Karlskrona, from 1989</li>
  <li>Campus Karlshamn, from autumn 2000</li>

<p>The Soft Center Campus in Ronneby existed from 1989 and was integrated in 2010 in the Campus in Karlskrona. </p>
<p>The University had been between 2006-2008 included in a strategic alliance with <a>V&#x00e4;xj&#x00f6; University</a> and <a>University of Kalmar</a> under the common name of the Academy of the Southeast. The Board, however, decided February 15, 2008 not to join in the fusion the other two parties, which resulted in the founding of <a>Linnaeus University</a>.</p>
<h2>Applied Information Technology</h2>
<p>The University offers a wide range of Bachelor, Master and PhD programmes oriented towards applied <a>Informatics</a>. Admission to graduate programmes is among the most competitive in Sweden due to having the highest number of international applicants. The faculty of the School of Computing consists of 11 professors and 50 PhD students, among those are high profile researchers such as Clas Wohlin. A <a>European Master on Software Engineering</a> programme is given in collaboration with <a>Technical University of Madrid</a> (Spain), <a>University of Kaiserslautern</a> (Germany) and <a>Free University of Bozen-Bolzano</a> (Italy).</p>
<h2>Sustainable Development</h2>
<p>Blekinge Institute of Technology contributed significantly to the development of <a>Strategic Sustainable Development</a> (SSD), an strategic approach to <a>sustainable development</a>. Leading figures of the Scandinavian sustainability movement, such as <a>Karl-Henrik Rob&#x00e8;rt</a> teach and resarch at the University. In cooperation with <a>Lund University</a> the <a>Centre for Innovation, Research and Competence in the Learning Economy</a> (CIRCLE) was established in 2004. Today CIRCLE is the largest national research Centre of Excellence by the <a>Swedish Governmental Agency for Innovation Systems</a> .</p>
