<p> <strong>California State University, San Bernardino</strong>, also known as <strong>Cal State San Bernardino</strong> or <strong>CSUSB</strong> is a <a>public university</a> and one of the twenty three general campuses of the <a>California State University</a> system. The main campus sits on  in the suburban <a>University District</a> of , <a>United States</a>, with a branch campus of  in <a>Palm Desert, California</a>, opened in 1986. In 2010, Cal State San Bernardino was named a 2011 Best College in the Western Region by <a>The Princeton Review</a> for the seventh straight year in a row, ranking CSUSB among the top 25 percent of universities across the nation. Founded in 1965, Cal State San Bernardino's enrollment annually tops 17,500.</p>
<p>CSUSB's sports teams are known as the Coyotes and play in the <a>California Collegiate Athletic Association</a> in the <a>Division II</a> of the <a>National Collegiate Athletic Association</a>.  The nickname was inspired by the coyotes that inhabit the area around the campus, which lies on the foothills of <a>San Bernardino Mountains</a>. The CSUSB women&#x2019;s volleyball team has won six <a>CCAA</a> and three West Region titles. The men&#x2019;s soccer team went to the NCAA Division II national semifinals, capturing the university&#x2019;s first California Collegiate Athletic Association title.</p>
<h2>History</h2>
<p>California State University, San Bernardino was created by the state legislature on April 29, 1960, as the San Bernardino-Riverside State College. Later, the California State College system's board of trustees chose a  site in the city of San Bernardino in 1963 and the official name was changed to California State College at San Bernardino. It opened in 1965 with 293 students and 30 faculty members. CSUSB earned its university status in 1984, officially becoming California State University, San Bernardino. Today, the university has more than 17,000 students and 65,000 alumni.</p>
<h2>Campus</h2>
<p> Built atop  of bedrock on the city&#x2019;s north side, CSUSB is framed to the north by the <a>San Bernardino Mountains</a>. More than  of new facilities have been built to meet students' academic and social needs. Campus residential housing provides more than 1,500 beds.</p>
<p><a>thumb|right|Entrance along University Parkway.</a> A new College of Education building opened in 2008.  The Santos Manuel Student Union has doubled in size in recent years, and a new  Student Recreation and Fitness Center was completed in 2007. Other recently constructed facilities include the Social and Behavioral Sciences and Chemical Sciences buildings.</p>
<p>The John M. Pfau Library, named after the university&#x2019;s first president, sits at the very center of the campus. Other distinctive university landmarks include: the clock tower above the Santos Manuel Student Union, the Robert V. Fullerton Art Museum, and the rounded, steel-rimmed James &amp; Aerianthi <a>Coussoulis Arena</a>, a modern, 4,000-plus seat sports and events venue&#x008d;&#x008d;&#x008d;&#x2014;one of the largest indoor arenas in the <a>Inland Empire</a>.</p>
<p>In 2009, the university received a major donation from the Pauline Murillo family to construct a $2 million teaching <a>observatory</a> on the campus. The <a>W.M. Keck Foundation</a> and the California Portland Cement Co. also made substantial contributions.</p>
<h3>Robert V. Fullerton Art Museum</h3>
<p>The Robert V. Fullerton Art Museum is among the 4 percent of museums in the United States accredited by the <a>American Association of Museums</a>. The Fullerton Art Museum's permanent collections consist of three distinct kinds of art: <a>ancient</a>, <a>ceramic</a>, and <a>contemporary</a>. A world-class collection of about 200 Egyptian artifacts and a smaller selection of Italian pottery are part of the museum's permanent holdings. Rotating shows feature artists from throughout the region and country. One gallery of the museum is dedicated to exhibiting the work of the school's own art students. The museum celebrated its 10th anniversary in 2006 and received accreditation by the <a>American Association of Museums</a> in 2008.</p>
<h3>Palm Desert campus</h3>
<p> <a>thumb|Palm Desert Campus</a></p>
<p>Opened in 1986, the <a>California State University, San Bernardino Palm Desert Campus</a> in <a>Palm Desert, California</a> () is home to upper-division and graduate students throughout the <a>Coachella Valley</a> and <a>Joshua Tree</a> areas. Since its inception, the Palm Desert Campus has maintained a close relationship with the nearby <a>College of the Desert</a>. The majority of Palm Desert Campus undergraduate students have transferred from <a>College of the Desert</a> through a dual admissions program.</p>
<p>A health sciences building for the four-year nursing program opened on the Palm Desert Campus in October 2008.</p>
<p>The Palm Desert Campus is unique for having been built entirely with private funds. This unique public-private partnership at CSUSB&#x2019;s Palm Desert Campus has drawn national attention and was featured in a front page story in the Sunday, Aug. 5, 2003, edition of the New York Times.</p>
<h2>Organization, Administration, and Funding</h2>
<p><a>thumb|left|The John M. Pfau Library sits at the center of the San Bernardino campus.</a> San Bernardino-Riverside State College became a part of the California College System system (now called the California State University system) in 1965 and eventually became California State University, San Bernardino. It, along with 22 other campuses, now forms the <a>California State University</a> system, which is the largest senior system of higher education in the <a>United States</a>. CSUSB university was founded by John M. Pfau, who served for 20 years as president and was succeeded by Anthony Evans, who served from 1982-1997. CSUSB&#x2019;s third president, Albert K. Karnig, has served since 1997. The 2007-2008 academic year  was the most successful fundraising year in the history of the university with $49 million in cash, gifts-in-kind and deferred gifts contributed by individuals, corporations and foundations. This total was the third highest in the California State University system, where giving rose 35 percent for the year. </pre>

<p><a>thumb|College of Natural Sciences</a></p>
<p>Cal State San Bernardino has taken a leading role in furthering the study and understanding <a>Middle Eastern Cultures</a>, and is the only CSU campus offering <a>Arabic language</a> and <a>Islamic history</a> courses. CSUSB offers degrees in <a>accounting</a> and <a>finance</a>, <a>computer science and engineering</a>, <a>geographic information</a> and <a>decision science</a>s, <a>nursing</a>, <a>health</a>, <a>psychology</a> and <a>public administration</a>, among many others.</p>
<p>CSUSB&#x2019;s University Center for Developmental <a>Disabilities</a> is a <a>clinic</a>al training program that provides evaluation, assessment, training, and support for <a>autistic</a> children, their parents, and siblings. two-year program that offers a comprehensive curriculum for students interested in pursuing careers in national service. It is one of three such programs in the country and the only one in the <a>California State University</a> system. The university also has collaborative educational programs with  nearby <a>Fort Irwin</a>. In addition, CSUSB's advanced accounting students provide free tax preparation services to local low-income, elderly, disabled, non-English-speaking residents.</p>
<h3>Education</h3>
<p>The university is one of the region's largest teacher-training institutions. In 2007, the university welcomed its first-ever class of <a>doctoral degree</a> candidates. The <a>Ed.D.</a> in educational leadership is a rigorous, research-based program that prepares pre-K through 12 and community college leaders to contribute to the study, development and implementation of educational reforms.</p>
<h3>Rankings</h3>
<p>Cal State San Bernardino was named a 2011 Best College in the Western Region by <a>The Princeton Review</a> for the seventh time in a row, ranking CSUSB among the top 25 percent of universities across the nation.</p>
<p>CSUSB ranks No. 1 among the 23 <a>California State University</a> campuses in first-to-second-year retention of African American students and ranked No. 3 in the retention of Latino students. Designated as a <a>Hispanic-serving institution</a>, the university ranks No. 1 in the nation in awarding bachelor's degrees in math to Hispanic students. CSUSB is ranked 12th in the nation in awarding bachelor's degrees to Hispanics and 20th in awarding master's, according to the May 4 issue of <a>The Princeton Review</a>. CSUSB is also listed as seventh overall in awarding bachelor's degrees in protective services to Hispanic students.</p>
<h3>Admissions, enrollment and retention</h3>
<p><a>thumb|right|250px|Student Recreation and Fitness Center</a> Admission to CSUSB is based on a combination of the applicant's high school cumulative <a>grade point average</a> (GPA) and standardized test scores. These factors are used to determine the applicant's California State University (CSU) eligibility index. More specifically, the eligibility index is a weighted combination of high school grade point average during the final three years of high school and a score on either the <a>SAT</a> or <a>ACT</a>. The average grade point average for incoming freshmen is 3.14. The average composite ACT score was 20 and the average SAT score was 900. Overall, 62% of applicants are accepted to CSUSB. <a>Enrollment</a> has increased by more than a third in recent years, and freshman enrollment has doubled. Due to the large number of applicants in the Fall 2010 quarter, CSUSB has declared &quot;campus impaction&quot; for the first time in its history. More than 70 percent of CSUSB students are the first generation of their families to attend college. <a>Latino</a> and <a>African American</a> student enrollments are the third highest of any university in California. CSUSB students are awarded on average 13 percent of CSU system scholarships, despite representing only 4 percent of the CSU's overall enrollment. Fifty-seven percent of full-time undergraduate students at CSUSB receive sufficient scholarships and grants to pay all fees and another 10 percent pay less than the full fees. Almost 75 percent of CSUSB students receive financial aid.  More than three quarters of the incoming 2009 freshmen class required remediation in either English or Math or both.</p>

<h4>Academic Excellence Scholarship program</h4>
<p>Initiated in 2002 by university president, Albert K. Karnig, the President&#x2019;s Academic Excellence Scholarship program invites the top 1 percent of graduating high school seniors in San Bernardino County to attend Cal State San Bernardino. The program provides a full scholarship, including tuition, books and a small stipend, to eligible students and is renewable for up to four academic years. The program is designed to attract the best and brightest students to CSUSB who might otherwise be lured to colleges outside the area. As of fall 2008, there were 140 enrolled presidential scholars. The first major donor to this program was Evelyn Magnuson, who extended her legacy in 2008 through a planned gift making CSUSB a beneficiary of her $2.4 million estate.</p>
<h2>Student life</h2>
<p><a>thumb|right|Coussoulis Arena</a> Like many other universities, much of the student life on campus revolves around extensive local outreach and retention programs. In addition to having the third highest <a>Hispanic</a> and <a>African American</a> student population of any university in California, CSUSB also has a very diverse campus of different ethnicities and nationalities. CSUSB is home to over more than 100 student clubs and organizations, including academic, cultural, religious, service and political organizations.</p>
<h3>Housing</h3>
<p>Cal State San Bernardino's <a>residence hall</a>s, which are referred to as &quot;The Villages,&quot; consist of three structures&mdash;Serrano Village, Arrowhead Village, and the University Village&mdash;which houses more than 1,500&nbsp;students in single and double rooms.</p>
<h3>Student organizations, and activities</h3>
<p>Student media organizations include the Coyote Chronicle, the student newspaper that is a part of the college media network, and Coyote Radio, a popular station for music, local news, talk and campus information. It is also one of only 50 college stations around the world listed as an official iTunes college station. Coyote Radio recently became the home and partner to Isla Earth, the award-winning radio science program. The Coussoulis Arena is a popular site for concerts, entertainment activities, commencement ceremonies and also serves kinesiology students.</p>
<h3>Athletics</h3>
<p><a>thumb|left|CSUSB Coyotes logo</a>&lt;!--Non free file removed by DASHBot--&gt; In 2009, CSUSB intercollegiate athletics celebrated its 25th anniversary. Established in 1984, the program offers men's and women's basketball and soccer, men's golf and baseball and women's softball, tennis, cross country, water polo and volleyball. The school's athletic mascot is the Coyote and the school colors are blue (Pantone 300) and black.</p>
<p>The Coyotes play in the <a>California Collegiate Athletic Association</a> (CCAA) in the <a>NCAA's</a> <a>Division II</a>. The men's and women's basketball and women's volleyball teams play in the James &amp; Aerianthi <a>Coussoulis Arena</a>, and the baseball team plays at <a>Arrowhead Credit Union Park</a> in <a>Downtown San Bernardino</a>.</p>
<p>Since 1984, the Coyotes have taken many local and regional championships and regularly finish high up in national tournaments. The men&#x2019;s soccer team went to the <a>NCAA Division III</a> national semifinals in 1987 and captured the university&#x2019;s first <a>NCAA Division II</a> <a>California Collegiate Athletic Association</a> title in 1991. In 1997, Scott Householder grabbed the university&#x2019;s sole national championship to date with a 273 for 72 holes, a record that still stands. Men&#x2019;s golf has finished third in the national tournament three times in its history.</p>
<p>The men&#x2019;s baseball team took West Region titles in 1990 and 1991. The men&#x2019;s basketball team has won three West Region titles, eight <a>CCAA</a> championships, and has made one appearance in the <a>NCAA Division II</a> national semifinals. The CSUSB women&#x2019;s volleyball team has won six <a>CCAA</a> and three West Region titles, has gone to the <a>NCAA Division II</a> semifinals three times, in 2003, in 2008, when it advanced to the finals, and in 2009.</p>
