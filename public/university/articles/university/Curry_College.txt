<p><strong>Curry College</strong> is a private <a>liberal arts</a>-based institution in <a>Milton</a>, <a>Massachusetts</a> that started as the School of Elocution in 1879.</p>
<h2>History</h2>
<p> The school was founded as the School of Elocution in 1879 on Boston's <a>Commonwealth Avenue</a> by <a>Anna Baright</a>, who married the school's namesake, <a>Samuel Silas Curry</a>, in 1882. Telephone inventor <a>Alexander Graham Bell</a> was the school's first chancellor. In 1943, the School of Elocution became Curry College.</p>
<h2>Academics</h2>
<p>Curry College offers certificates <a>bachelor's degree</a>s, and <a>master's degree</a>s in 20 majors. The Army and Air Force Reserve Officers Training Corps (ROTC) programs are offered through &quot;a cross-enrolled program with <a>Boston University</a>&quot;. Curry is accredited by the <a>New England Association of Schools and Colleges</a> (NEASC).</p>
<h2>Campus</h2>
<p>Its main campus in Milton is  near the <a>Blue Hills Reservation</a> and seven miles (11&nbsp;km) from downtown <a>Boston</a>. It includes the former <a>Joan of Arc Academy</a>. Curry has satellite campuses in <a>Plymouth</a>  and classroom locations at <a>Spaulding Rehabilitation Hospital</a> in <a>Boston</a> and at the <a>Medford Police Academy</a> in <a>Medford</a> which offer classes through the Division of Continuing and Graduate Studies.</p>
<h2>Student life</h2>
<p>Curry has an enrollment of approximately 3,880 students. 2,000 or so are traditional students from roughly 40 states and 23 countries, 1,500 are continuing education students, and about 380 are graduate students. Approximately 1,400 students reside on the Curry campus.</p>
<h3>Extra curriculars</h3>
<p>The <em>Curry Arts Journal</em> publishes &quot;the work of student writers, artists and photographers&quot;. The student newspaper is the <em>Currier Times</em>, which is &quot;published biweekly.&quot; The yearbook is entitled the <em>Amethyst</em>. Curry College's philosophy club, which was initiated in the spring of 2008, is called &quot;The New Academy&quot;. There is also a <a>commuter</a> club. and an <a>ultimate</a> club.</p>
