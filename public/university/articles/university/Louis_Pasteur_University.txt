<p><strong>Louis Pasteur University</strong> (<em>Universit&#x00e9; Louis-Pasteur</em>), also known as <strong>Strasbourg I</strong> or <strong>ULP</strong> was a large university in <a>Strasbourg</a>, <a>Alsace</a>, <a>France</a>. As of January 15, 2007, there were 18,847 students enrolled at the university, including around 3,000 foreign students. Research and teaching at ULP concentrates on the <a>natural sciences</a>, <a>technology</a> and <a>medicine</a>. On 1 January 2009, Louis Pasteur University became part of the refounded <a>University of Strasbourg</a> and lost its status as an independent university. </p>
<p>According to the <a>Academic Ranking of World Universities</a>, when it was an independent university, the ULP belonged to the first 100 rank (96th in 2006).</p>
<p>The university was a member of the <a>LERU</a> (League of European Research Universities). It was named after the famous 19th-century French scientist <a>Louis Pasteur</a>.</p>
<h2>Notable staff</h2>
<ul>
  <li><a>Louis Pasteur</a> (1822-1895), professor of chemistry</li>
  <li><a>Karl Ferdinand Braun</a> (1850-1918), professor of physics, <a>Nobel Prize</a> in 1909</li>
  <li><a>Albrecht Kossel</a> (1853-1927), physician, Nobel Prize in 1910</li>
  <li><a>Albert Schweitzer</a> (1875-1965),  <a>theologian</a>, <a>musician</a>, <a>philosopher</a>, and <a>physician</a>, Nobel Prize in 1952</li>
  <li><a>Jean-Marie Lehn</a> (1939-), French chemist, Nobel Prize in 1987</li>
  <li><a>Ren&#x00e9; Thom</a> (1923-2002),  French mathematician,  founder of catastrophe theory, laur&#x00e9;ate of the <a>Fields Medal</a> in 1958</li>
</ul>
