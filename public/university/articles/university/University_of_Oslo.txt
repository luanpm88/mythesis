<p></p>
<p>The <strong>University of Oslo</strong> (), is the <a>oldest</a> and largest <a>university in Norway</a>, situated in the Norwegian capital of <a>Oslo</a>. The university was founded in 1811 as <strong>The Royal Frederick University</strong> (in Norwegian <em>Det Kongelige Frederiks Universitet</em> and in Latin <em>Universitas Regia Fredericiana</em>) and was modelled after the recently established <a>University of Berlin</a>. It was originally named after <a>King Frederick</a> of Denmark and Norway and received its current name in 1939.</p>
<p>The university has faculties of (<a>Lutheran</a>) <a>Theology</a>, <a>Law</a>, <a>Medicine</a>, <a>Humanities</a>, <a>Mathematics</a> and <a>Natural Sciences</a>, <a>Dentistry</a>, <a>Social Sciences</a>, and <a>Education</a>. The Faculty of <a>Law</a> is still located at the old campus on <a>Karl Johans gate</a>, near the <a>National Theatre</a>, the <a>Royal Palace</a>, and the <a>Parliament</a>, while most of the other faculties are located at a modern campus area called <a>Blindern</a>, erected from the 1930s. The Faculty of Medicine is split between several university hospitals in the Oslo area.</p>
<p>Currently the university has about 27,000 students and employs about 4,600 people. It is considered one of the leading universities of Scandinavia, and has consistently been ranked among the world's top 100 universities by the <a>Academic Ranking of World Universities</a>; in 2010 it was ranked as the best in Norway, 4th best in the <a>Nordic countries</a>, 24th best in Europe and 75th best in the world.  The <a>QS World University Rankings</a> ranked the university 100th in the world. Until the founding of the University in 1811, the <a>University of Copenhagen</a> was the only university of <a>Denmark-Norway</a>. After the dissolution of the Dano-Norwegian union in 1814, close academic ties between the countries have been maintained. The University of Oslo was the only university in Norway until 1946, and hence informally often known as simply &quot;The University&quot;. It was also informally referred to as &quot;The Royal Frederick's&quot; (<em>Det Kgl. Frederiks</em>) for short. </p>

<h2>Seal</h2>
<p><a>thumb|The university seal as of 1842</a> The <a>seal</a> of the University of Oslo features <a>Apollo</a> with the <a>Lyre</a>, and dates from 1835. The seal has been redesigned several times, most recently in 2009. The former design dates from the 1980s.</p>
<h2>Student life</h2>
<p>Like all public institutions of higher education in Norway, the university does not charge tuition fees. However, a small fee of  (roughly ) per term goes to the student welfare organisation <a>Foundation for Student Life in Oslo</a>, to subsidise kindergartens, health services, housing and cultural initiatives, the weekly newspaper <em><a>Universitas</a></em> and the radio station <a>Radio Nova</a>.</p>
<p>In addition a the students are charged a copy and paper fee of  (roughly ) for full-time students and  (roughly ) for part-time students. Lastly a voluntary sum of  (roughly ) is donated to SAIH (Studentenes og Akademikernes Internasjonale Hjelpefond).</p>
<h2>Rankings</h2>
<p>The <a>Times Higher Education World University Rankings</a> ranked UiO 186th worldwide in 2010 (2nd in Norway behind the <a>University of Bergen</a>), while the <a>Shanghai Jiao Tong University</a>'s <a>Academic Ranking of World Universities</a> ranked UiO 75nd worldwide (and best in Norway).</p>
<p>Also in 2010, the <a>QS World University Rankings</a> ranked UiO 100th worldwide, while the <a>Webometrics Ranking of World Universities</a> ranked UiO 53rd worldwide.</p>
