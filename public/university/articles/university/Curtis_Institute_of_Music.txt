<p><strong>The Curtis Institute of Music</strong> is a <a>conservatory</a> in <a>Philadelphia</a>, <a>Pennsylvania</a>, that offers courses of study leading to a performance Diploma, Bachelor of Music, Master of Music in Opera, and Professional Studies Certificate in Opera. According to statistics compiled by <em><a>U.S. News &amp; World Report</a></em>, it has the lowest acceptance rate of any institution of higher education in the United States.</p>
<h2>History</h2>
<p>It was established in 1924 by <a>Mary Louise Curtis Bok</a>, and was named in honor of her father, <a>Cyrus Curtis</a>. After consulting with musician friends including <a>Josef Hofmann</a> and <a>Leopold Stokowski</a> on how best to help musically gifted young people, Bok purchased three mansions on Philadelphia's <a>Rittenhouse Square</a> and had them joined and renovated. She established a <a>faculty</a> of prominent performing artists and eventually left the institute with an endowment of <a>US$</a>12 million. [[File:Curtisinstofmusic.JPG|thumb|300px|Looking southeast from [[Rittenhouse Square]] toward The Curtis Institute's Main Building at the corner of Locust Street (on the left) and South 18th Street (on the right) (2006).]]</p>
<h2>Admission</h2>
<p>The institute has served as a training ground for <a>orchestra</a>l players to fill the ranks of the <a>Philadelphia Orchestra</a>, much like the <a>New England Conservatory</a> serves as a training ground for the <a>Boston Symphony Orchestra</a>,  although <a>composer</a>s, <a>organist</a>s, <a>pianist</a>s and <a>singer</a>s are offered courses of study as well.  </p>
<p>All pupils attend on full <a>scholarship</a>, but admission is extremely competitive. Besides composers, <a>conductors</a>, organists, pianists and singers, only enough students are admitted to fill a single orchestra. Accordingly, enrollment is in the range of 150 to 170 students. The acceptance rate is about one third that of <a>Ivy League</a> institutions such as <a>Columbia University</a>.</p>

<h3>Current administration</h3>
<p>, <a>Roberto Diaz</a> is President and director of the Institute.  Diaz is also a Curtis alumnus and faculty   member.  He was principal <a>violist</a> of the Philadelphia Orchestra from 1996 to 2006 and is a member of the Diaz Trio.</p>
<p>, <a>Otto-Werner Mueller</a> is the conductor of the <a>Curtis Symphony Orchestra</a>.</p>
