<p><strong>Juan Ram&#x00f3;n de la Fuente Ram&#x00ed;rez</strong> (born 5 September 1951 in <a>Mexico City</a>) is a <a>Mexican</a> <a>psychiatrist</a>, academician and politician who served as <a>Secretary of Health</a> in the cabinet of President <a>Ernesto Zedillo</a> (1994&#x2013;1999) and as <a>rector</a> of the <a>National Autonomous University of Mexico</a> (UNAM) from 1999 to 2007.</p>
<p>De la Fuente graduated with a bachelor's degree in <a>Medicine</a> from the National Autonomous University in 1976 and specialized in <a>Psychiatry</a> at the <a>Mayo Clinic</a> in <a>Rochester, Minnesota</a>, <a>United States</a>. When he returned to Mexico he founded the Clinical Research Unit of the Mexican Institute of Psychiatry and worked as a lecturer at UNAM's School of Medicine, for which he was elected Director in 1991. In 1995 he was elected President of the <a>Mexican Academy of Sciences</a> and four years latter was appointed Secretary of Health by President Zedillo.</p>
<p>In 1999, after a <a>10-months long student strike</a>, he resigned from the federal cabinet and was appointed <a>Rector</a> of the <a>National Autonomous University of Mexico</a>. After some negotiations the conflict ended through a major <a>plebiscite</a> although the federal police forces were called upon to remove and arrest the remaining strikers. In 2003 he was reappointed for a second term.</p>
<p> he is a member of the Council of the <a>United Nations University</a> and sits in the board of directors of the <a>Cervantes Institute</a> and <em><a>El Universal</a></em>, an influential newspaper in <a>Mexico City</a>. Also, in 2008 he was elected as President of the <a>International Association of Universities</a>.</p>
