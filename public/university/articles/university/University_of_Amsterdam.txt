<p> <a>thumb|260px|right|Buildings of the University of Amsterdam. The front building houses the Academic Club of the University</a></p>
<p>The <strong>University of Amsterdam</strong> () is a comprehensive research <a>university</a> located in the heart of the city of <a>Amsterdam</a>, <a>Netherlands</a>. With a budget of almost <a>&euro;</a>600 million, over 30,000 students and around 5,000 staff, the UvA is one of the major universities in <a>Europe</a>. There are seven faculties: <a>Humanities</a>, <a>Social</a> and <a>Behavioral sciences</a>, <a>Economy</a>, <a>Law</a>, <a>Science</a>, <a>Medicine</a> and <a>Dentistry</a>. It has a strong internationalization programme and offers over 85 English Master programmes taught in English, as well as a number of Dutch and English language courses. The University of Amsterdam (UvA) should not be confused with Amsterdam's other university, the <a>VU University</a>. In 2006, the UvA joined the <a>League of European Research Universities</a> (LERU). These world-class universities work together to invest in European 'centres of excellence', with the aim of maintaining Europe's strong position in the global knowledge economy.</p>
<h2>History</h2>
<h3>Athenaeum</h3>
<p><a>thumb|upright|The words &quot;Athenaeum Illustre&quot; on the gate of the Agnietenkapel</a> The commonly-held predecessor of the University of Amsterdam, the <em>Athenaeum Illustre</em> (Latin - the illustrious Athenaeum) was founded in the 14th-century Agnietenkapel in Amsterdam in 1632, to educate students in History and Philosophy. Professors lectured publicly and tutored privately. In January 1632 two internationally acclaimed scholars, <a>Caspar Barlaeus</a> and <a>Gerardus Vossius</a>, held their inaugural speeches here. In the course of the seventeenth century, Law, Medicine and Theology were introduced in the curriculum. Alumni (whilst it was still the Athenaeum) include <a>Cornelis Petrus Tiele</a>.</p>
<h3>Municipal university</h3>
<p>The Athenaeum remained a small institution until the nineteenth century, with no more than 250 students and eight teachers. This situation changed only slowly. In 1815 the Athenaeum Illustre was officially recognised as an institute of higher education. In 1877, Athenuem Illustre is reorganised to become the university of the municipality of Amsterdam. It became the <em>Gemeentelijke Universiteit van Amsterdam</em> (GU or 'Municipal University') and it received the right to confer doctoral degrees. The <a>professor</a>s were appointed by the <a>city council</a> and the <a>mayor</a> presided over the university administration. Because the <a>Amsterdam city council</a> was noted for its progressive politics this scheme guaranteed a large degree of intellectual freedom for the university.</p>
<h3>Universiteit van Amsterdam</h3>
<p>Very little then changed until 1961, when the national <a>government</a> took control of financial responsibility. The university ceased to be the <em>Gemeentelijke Universiteit</em> and finally became the <em>Universiteit van Amsterdam</em>.</p>
<p>In 1969 the university became nationwide news when the university's administrative center at the <em>Maagdenhuis</em> was occupied by students who wanted more democratic influence. During the seventies and eighties the university was often the target of nationwide student actions.</p>
<h2>Faculties</h2>
<p>The UvA has seven faculties. Each faculty is headed by a dean. Teaching and research are carried out in various departments and institutes within the individual faculties.</p>
<h3>Faculty of Dentistry</h3>
<p>The Academic Centre for Dentistry in Amsterdam () (ACTA) was founded in <a>1984</a> through a merger of the two dentistry faculties of the Universiteit of Amsterdam and the <a>Vrije Universiteit</a>, Amsterdam. ACTA conducts scientific research, teaches and provides patient care in the field of dentistry. ACTA is one of the largest dentistry education and training programmes in the world, with 540 staff members, an annual new-student enrolment of 128 and a total student body of 859.</p>
<h3>Faculty of Economics and Business</h3>
<p>The Faculty of Economics and Business () (FEB) was established in <a>1922</a>. The FEB, which includes the Amsterdam School of Economics (ASE) and the Amsterdam Business School (ABS), currently has around 4,000 students and nearly 600 staff.</p>
<h3>Faculty of Humanities</h3>
<p>The Faculty of Humanities () (FGw) is divided into six departments:</p>
<ul>
  <li><a>Dutch studies</a></li>
  <li><a>History</a>, <a>Archaeology</a> and <a>Area studies</a></li>
  <li><a>Language and Literature</a></li>
  <li><a>Media studies</a></li>
  <li><a>Philosophy</a></li>
  <li><a>Art</a>, <a>Religion</a> and <a>Cultural studies</a>.</li>
</ul>
<p>Around 6500 students follow the courses on offer, and there are 800 staff members.</p>
<h3>Faculty of Law </h3>
<p><a>thumb|right|The faculty of law at the Oudemanhuispoort</a> The Faculty of Law () (FdR) is housed in the Oudemanhuispoort, a historic building dating from 1602 situated in the centre of Amsterdam. With 4000 students and 350 staff members, the Faculty of Law at the Universiteit van Amsterdam is one of the largest faculties in the Netherlands. The Faculty offers eight <a>LLM</a> programmes, of which two are taught in English. In addition the Faculty offers three advanced LLM programmes, which are all taught in English. Research at the Faculty is undertaken by five research institutes which specialise in the following areas: <a>International law</a>, <a>Private law</a>, <a>Environmental law</a>, <a>Labour law</a> and <a>Information law</a>.</p>
<h3>Faculty of Medicine</h3>
<p>[[File:AMC.jpg|thumb|[[Academic Medical Center]]]] The Faculty of Medicine () (FdG), each year, approximately 350 first-year students begin their study of medicine at the <a>Academic Medical Center</a>. The first, four-year phase consists mainly of thematic teaching. The second, two-year phase consists of training - 'internships' - in- and outside of the AMC.</p>
<h3>Faculty of Science</h3>
<p>The Faculty of Science () (FNWI) is a leading centre of academic research and education. The Faculty of Science consists of four departments with 1200 researchers and lecturers, including 150 full- and part-time professors operating in ten research institutes. The main faculty buildings are located on the <a>Science Park Amsterdam</a>.</p>
<h3>Faculty of Social and Behavioural Sciences</h3>
<p>The Faculty of Social and Behavioural Sciences () (FMG) is the largest educational and research institution in the <a>social</a> and <a>behavioural</a> sciences in the Netherlands. Approximately 1,100 staff members contribute to the research and education programmes.</p>
<p>The Faculty is home to six departments: <a>Political Science</a>, <a>Sociology</a> and <a>Anthropology</a>, <a>Communication Science</a>, <a>Psychology</a>, <a>Social Geography</a>, <a>Planning and International Development Studies</a>, and <a>Educational sciences</a>.</p>
