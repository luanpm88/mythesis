<p> The <strong>University of Texas System</strong> comprises fifteen <a>education</a>al institutions in <a>Texas</a>, of which nine are academic  <a>universities</a> and six are <a>health</a> institutions.
The UT System also offers online courses and degrees from UT institutions via the <a>UT TeleCampus</a>.
The system is headquartered in <a>Downtown Austin</a>.
The system has a total enrollment of over 190,000 students.
Its <a>flagship</a> institution is the <a>University of Texas at Austin</a>.</p>
<p>The university system had an operating budget of $10 billion in 2007, with research expenditures totalling over $1.8 billion.</p>

<h2>Headquarters</h2>
<p>The University of Texas System is headquartered in <a>Downtown Austin</a>.
The system headquarters complex includes O'Henry Hall, Claudia Taylor Johnson Hall, Ashbel Smith Hall, the Colorado Building, the Lavaca Building, and the Norwood Tower.
Parking garages serving the complex include Parking Garage I, Parking Garage II, Parking Garage III, 300 West 6th Street Parking Garage, and the garage between the Colorado and Lavaca buildings.</p>

<h3>National rankings</h3>
<ul>
  <li><a>The University of Texas at Austin</a> was ranked by The Times as the world's 15th top ranking university overall.
Seven <a>doctoral</a> programs at the University rank in the top 10 in the nation and 22 degree programs rank in the top 25, according to a comprehensive study of the quality of graduate schools conducted by the <a>United States National Research Council</a>.</li>
<li><a>University of Texas Southwestern Medical School</a> was ranked the 17th highest ranking medical school in 2005 in the United States, with four of Texas's eleven <a>Nobel laureates</a>, more than any other medical school in the <a>United States</a>.</li>
  <li><a>The University of Texas M. D. Anderson Cancer Center</a> is one of the world's top ranking cancer centers in research and education.</li>
<li><a>The University of Texas Health Science Center at San Antonio's</a> Dental School ranked one of world's highest overall, and fourth worldwide in dental science publications.</li>
<li>The University of Texas System ranks No. 1 for biotechnology patents among 424 universities in the world, according the Milken Institute and its September 2006 report on universities' biotechnology research and commercialization.</li>
</ul>
