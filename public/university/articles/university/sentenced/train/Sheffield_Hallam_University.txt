<strong>Sheffield Hallam <a>University</a></strong> (<strong>SHU</strong>) is a <a>Higher Education</a> institution in <a>South Yorkshire</a>, <a>England</a>, based on two sites in <a>Sheffield</a>.
<em>City Campus</em> is located in the city centre, close to <a>Sheffield railway station</a>, and <em><a>Collegiate Crescent</a> Campus</em> is about two miles away, adjacent to <a>Ecclesall Road</a> in south-west Sheffield.</p>
<p>The university is the fourth largest in the UK in terms of enrolment,</p>
<h2>Organisation</h2>
<h3>Faculties</h3>
<p><a>thumb|SHU's Owen Building (left) and Stoddart Building (right), part of the City Campus on Arundel Gate.</a><a>thumb|SHU's
Student Union: Hallam Union.</a></p>
<p>SHU is divided into four faculties:</p>
<p><strong>Faculty of Arts, Computing, Engineering and Sciences (ACES):</strong> Art &amp; Design; Business Systems &amp; IT; Communication &amp; Media; Computers &amp; Computing; Engineering &amp; Technology; Mathematics &amp; Statistics; and Multimedia &amp; the Internet.</p>
<p><strong>Faculty of Development and Society (D&amp;S):</strong> Built Environment; Education; English; Geography and Environment; Architecture, Planning &amp; Urban Regeneration; Humanities; Law &amp; Community Justice; Social Sciences, Performing Arts.</p>
<p><strong>Faculty of Health and Wellbeing (HWB):</strong> Biosciences; Diagnostic Radiography; Nursing &amp; Midwifery; Occupational Therapy; Operating Department Practitioners; Paramedic Studies; Physiotherapy; Radiotherapy &amp; Oncology; Social Work; and Sport.</p>
<p><strong>Sheffield Business School (SBS) - formerly known as the Faculty of Organisation and Management (O&amp;M):</strong> Business &amp; Management; Facilities Management; Finance; Food &amp; Nutrition; Language &amp; Culture; and Tourism, Hospitality &amp; Events Management.</p>

<ul>
  <li>Art &amp; Design Research Centre</li>
  <li>Biomedical Research Centre</li>
  <li>Centre for Education and Inclusion Research</li>
  <li>Centre for Health and Social Care Research</li>
  <li>Centre for Professional and Organisational Development</li>
  <li>Centre for Regional Economic &amp; Social Research</li>
  <li>Centre for Science Education</li>
  <li>Centre for Sport and Exercise Science</li>
  <li>Centre for Sustainable Consumption</li>
  <li>Centre for Tourism, Leisure and Environmental Change</li>
  <li>Culture, Communication and Computing Research Institute</li>
  <li>Facilities Management Graduate Centre</li>
  <li>Materials and Engineering Research Institute</li>
  <li>Sport Industry Research Centre</li>
</ul>
<p>Through these centres of excellence, a number of spin-off companies have been formed, including:</p>
<ul>
  <li>Sheaf Solutions - automotive and aerospace organisation</li>
  <li>Hallam Biotech - biotech analysis and synthesis</li>
  <li>Materials Analysis &amp; Research Services (MARS) - materials analysis and solutions</li>
  <li>Bodycote - materials coating</li>
  <li>Design Futures - product design, packaging design, research &amp; strategy</li>
</ul>
<h2>Lifelong Learning Network</h2>
<p>SHU is the lead partner for <a>Higher Futures</a>, the <a>Lifelong Learning Network (LLN)</a> for South Yorkshire, North Derbyshire and North Nottinghamshire.</p>
<h2>League Tables and the National Student Survey</h2>
<p>In the National Student Survey, several subject areas at SHU have performed very well in terms of overall student satisfaction with their courses:  architecture was ranked 1st, geography was placed =1st, and planning and housing studies was placed 2nd.</p>
<p>In the most recent university league tables, SHU was placed 67th out of 117 UK universities by <em><a>The Guardian</a></em>, ranked 69th by <em><a>The Times</a></em> and 64th by <em><a>The Sunday Times</a></em>.
In the Guardian University Guide 2011, SHU was ranked 47th out of 112 universities in business and management studies.</p>
