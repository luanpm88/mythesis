<p><strong>BI Norwegian School of Management</strong> () is the largest business school in <a>Norway</a> and the second largest in all of Europe.
BI has in total 6 campuses with the main one located in <a>Oslo</a>.</p>
<h2>History</h2>
<p>BI Norwegian School of Management was founded in 1943 by <a>Finn &#x00d8;ien</a> as 'Bedrift&#x00f8;konomisk Institut' (), hence the abbreviation'BI'.
The current president is professor Tom Colbj&#x00f8;rnsen.
Past presidents include <a>J&#x00f8;rgen Randers</a>, co-author of the <a>Club of Rome</a> Report <a>Limits to Growth</a>; <a>Peter Lorange</a>, former president of <a>IMD</a> in <a>Lausanne</a>, <a>Switzerland</a>; <a>Leif Frode Onarheim</a>, a former member of the <a>Norwegian Parliament</a> and current CEO of leading Norwegian fish farming company <a>Marine Harvest</a>.
</p>
<h2>Current activities</h2>
<p>As of 2010 the school has 19,931 students.
This includes 9,567 full-time students.
There are 752 employees, of whom 370 are academic and 382 are administrative.
There are also 674 part-time lecturers.</p>
<p>BI offers a full set of programs for bachelor's degrees, master's degrees, and doctoral degrees, as well as executive education and tailor-made programs for businesses.
The teaching languages are English (BBA and postgraduate programs) and Norwegian (majority of undergraduate programs and programs for local business life).</p>
<p>The main campus in <a>Nydalen</a> (Oslo) was designed by <a>Niels Torp</a>, who also designed <a>Gardermoen Airport</a>.</p>
<h3>Norsk Kundebarometer</h3>
<p>Norsk Kundebarometer (NKB) () is a research program arranged at BI, with a focus on relations between customers and businesses.
Based on an annual survey from Norwegian households, it seeks to collect data that may be used for comparison between businesses, comparisons between various fields, and comparisons over time.</p>

<h3>Activities abroad</h3>
<p>BI has roughly 1500 students in <a>China</a> through its close relationship with <a>Fudan University</a> in Shanghai, and is also the majority shareholder of the <a>ISM University of Management and Economics</a> (previously known as International School of Management) with around 2000 students located in <a>Vilnius</a> and <a>Kaunas</a> in <a>Lithuania</a>.</p>

<h3>Student organizations</h3>
<p>The school has two student organizations, one for the main campus in Oslo and one for the other campuses.
The Oslo student organization is called &quot;Studentforeningen ved Handelsh&#x00f8;yskolen BI i Oslo&quot; (SBIO) ().
This union was formed in 2005 after the relocation of the three locations in Oslo into one - Campus Nydalen.
The three previous unions was called Bedrift&#x00f8;konomisk Studentersamfund (BS), BISON and M&#x00d8;SS.
BS was the oldest union, formed in 1964.
The union for the other campuses is &quot;BI Studentsamfunn&quot; (BIS) ().
This union was founded on February 7, 1987 and is today the largest student union of a private school in Norway.</p>
<p>The student newspaper is named <em>INSIDE</em>, and its circulation is 11,000.</p>
<p>The all-male student choir is named UFDA The Choir Boys and was established in 1986.</p>
