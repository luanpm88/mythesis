<p><strong>East Texas Baptist University</strong> or <strong>ETBU</strong> is a <a>private</a>, <a>coeducation</a>al  <a>Christian</a> university in <a>Marshall, Texas</a> associated with the <a>Baptist General Convention of Texas</a>.</p>
<h2>History</h2>
<p>ETBU is located on the site of the former <a>Van Zandt</a> Plantation, which is at the highest altitude in <a>Harrison County</a>.
ETBU was founded as the College of Marshall in 1912, after a campaign to create a <a>Southern Baptist</a> college in <a>East Texas</a>.
The campus' first building, Marshall Hall, was completed in 1916.
It was designed to house a gymnasium, library, chapel/theatre, administrative offices and classrooms.
The College of Marshall opened the following year in 1917 as a two year <a>junior college</a> and academy.
It became East Texas Baptist College in 1944</em></p>
<h2>Faculty</h2>
<p>The student to faculty ratio is 16:1 and the average class size is 22.
The accreditation was last reaffirmed in 2009, with the next reaffirmation due in 2019.
ETBU also is accredited by The Board of Nurse Examiners for the State of Texas for <a>Bachelor of Science in Nursing</a> (BSN), the Commissions of Collegiate Nursing Education, and the <a>National Association of Schools of Music</a>.
</p>
<p>ETBU ranked twelfth among forty-eight schools in the category of Best Baccalaureate Colleges in the West Region in the <a>U.S. News &amp; World Report</a>'s publication &quot;America's Best Colleges 2008&quot;.</p>
