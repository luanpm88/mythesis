<p>  <strong>Fort Lewis College</strong> is a small public <a>liberal arts college</a> and is a member of the <a>Council of Public Liberal Arts Colleges</a>; it is located between the <a>San Juan Mountains</a> and canyon country in <a>Durango</a>, <a>Colorado</a>.
It was originally located at the nearby former site of Fort Lewis, a <a>United States Army</a> post in Colorado that was decommissioned after 1911.</p>
<h2>Students</h2>
<p>The college's creation was part of a treaty agreement in 1911 when the region's Indian tribes ceded land to the federal government.
In exchange for the land, the government promised that Fort Lewis College would offer qualified Native Americans tuition-free education in perpetuity.
This offer remains in effect but covers only tuition, not other expenses.</p>
<h2>Programs</h2>
<p>Fort Lewis College strives to offer academic, cultural, and service programs.
Students can choose from 96 academic specialties in creating an individualized educational experience.
</p>
<p>In addition to academics, Fort Lewis College offers cultural, athletic, and professional programs:</p>
<ul>
  <li>An AACSB International Accreditation signifies a business program as being among the &#x201c;Best Business Schools in the World.&#x201d;
The Fort Lewis College <a>School of Business Administration</a> was the first undergraduate public liberal arts-based business institution to receive AACSB accreditation, and today is one of only four public liberal arts business schools in the world to be accredited.</li>
<li>The Fort Lewis College <a>Center of Southwest Studies</a> serves as a museum and research facility.
The Center has been honored by the Colorado Commission of Higher Education as a program of excellence in state-funded higher education.</li>
<li>The <a>Community Concert Hall</a> at Fort Lewis College is the premier performing arts venue for Southwestern Colorado and the entire <a>Four Corners</a> area, bringing diverse performers from all over the world to Durango.</li>
  <li>Receiving the Colorado State Small Business Development Center Star Award in 2004, the Fort Lewis <a>College Small Business Development Center</a> provides business start-up and growth assistance, plus courses on how to start a business.</li>
<li>At the 2007 <a>USA Cycling</a> Collegiate Road National Championships, Fort Lewis College won both the men's and women's Division I team time trial events.</li>
</ul>
<h2>College and community</h2>
<p>Durango serves the <a>Four Corners</a> as the economic and cultural hub of the region; the college offers a number of programs that reach out to this community:</p>
<ul>
  <li>The Fort Lewis College <a>Office of Community Services</a> assists local communities, students, and faculty to improve academic, social, economic, and the ecological well-being of the Four Corners region.</li>
<li>The Community Concert Hall at Fort Lewis College host many entertainment events for the Durango community.</li>
<li>Many faculty in the Music Department are active in Durango performing arts ensembles, including the Durango Choral Society, the San Juan Symphony, and several chamber ensembles and pop bands.</li>
<li>With Community Business Partnerships, Fort Lewis College offers the community an opportunity to enrich the academic lives of the students.
Among the partners are <a>First National Bank</a> of Durango, sponsors of the New Business Venturing Program, and the <em><a>Durango Herald</a></em>, sponsors of the Family Business Forums.</li>
<li><a>KDUR-FM</a> Community Radio provides a bridge between the college and <a>La Plata County</a> with music, public affairs, and alternative news programming.
The station provides educational and training opportunities for students and community members alike.</li>
<li>Fort Lewis College fields 11 varsity athletic teams that compete in the NCAA Division II and Rocky Mountain Athletic Conference.
Women compete in basketball, cross country, lacrosse, soccer, softball and volleyball.
Men compete in basketball, cross country, football, golf and soccer.</li>
<li>In 2005 and 2009, the Fort Lewis College men's soccer team won the NCAA Division II championship.
FLC finished second in NCAA Division II in 1999 and 2006.</li>
<li>The official mascot for Fort Lewis College is the Skyhawk.</li>
</ul>
<h2>History</h2>
<p>Fort Lewis College originated when the <a>U.S. Army</a> post of the same name was abandoned, and the government established a school for Native Americans on the site.
In 1911 courses in agriculture were added, with some college courses in 1925.
In 1933 the curriculum was limited to college-level courses, and in 1948 Fort Lewis became a junior college.
</p>
<p>The college moved to new quarters in Durango in 1956 and first granted <a>baccalaureate degree</a>s in 1964.
Fort Lewis was part of the Colorado State University System under the governance of the State Board of Agriculture.
Today an independent board of trustees governs Fort Lewis College.</p>
