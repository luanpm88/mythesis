<p><strong>INSEAD</strong> is an international graduate business school and research institution.
It has campuses in Europe (<a>France</a>), Asia (<a>Singapore</a>), and the Middle East (<a>Abu Dhabi</a>), as well as a research center in <a>Israel</a>.
The school offers a full-time <a>MBA</a> program, a <a>PhD in Management</a> program, and several <a>executive education</a> programs (including an <a>Executive MBA</a>).</p>
<p>INSEAD's flagship MBA program is currently ranked #1 globally by several leading publications including <a>Business Week</a> and <a>QS Global 200 Business Schools Report</a>.
The <a>Financial Times</a> ranked it number 5 in the world (tied with <a>Stanford Graduate School of Business</a>), and it has been the highest ranked one-year MBA program on the list for several years in a row.</p>
<p>INSEAD MBA students have the chance to study in three continents (through an alliance with the <a>Wharton School of the University of Pennsylvania</a> and the multi-campus structure).
Starting in September 2010, a campus exchange program with <a>Kellogg School of Management</a> will be offered.
INSEAD also has a reciprocal agreement with <a>Harvard Business School</a>, <a>Stanford Graduate School of Business</a> and <a>Kellogg School of Management</a> to share career services.
Alumni of the four schools have exclusive access to job opportunities database of each other.</p>
<h2>Overview</h2>
<h3>Campuses</h3>
<p>INSEAD has three campuses.
The original campus (Europe Campus) is located in <a>Fontainebleau</a>, near Paris, France.
INSEAD's second campus (Asia Campus) is in the <a>Buona Vista</a> district of the city-state of <a>Singapore</a>.
The third and newest campus (Middle East Campus) is located in <a>Abu Dhabi</a>, and currently serves only as a campus for open enrollment executive education programmes.</p>
<p>The MBA programme is taught on both European and Asian campuses, with most participants spending part of their curriculum on both campuses.
INSEAD has a North Americas office in New York City, and a Research Centre in <a>Israel</a>.</p>

<h2>Executive Education</h2>
<p>INSEAD holds both company/firm specific and open enrollment executive education programs at its campuses in Europe and Asia and at its Executive Education centre in Abu Dhabi.
INSEAD also works in partnership with corporate universities.
Participants usually come from senior or top management, with many years of experience within their company or industry and younger 'high-potentials' identified as being key in succession strategies within their companies.
Approximately 9,500 executives from over 120 countries undertake courses or programmes at INSEAD each year.</p>
<h2>MBA</h2>
<p>The INSEAD <a>MBA</a> curriculum comprises a range of required core courses and electives.
The core courses cover traditional <a>management</a> disciplines including <a>finance</a>, <a>economics</a>, <a>organizational behaviour</a>, <a>accounting</a>, <a>ethics</a>, <a>marketing</a>, <a>statistics</a>, <a>operations management</a>, international political analysis, <a>supply chain management</a>, <a>leadership</a> and corporate <a>strategy</a>.</p>
<p>There are approximately 80 electives on offer in the Accounting and Control, Decision Sciences, Economics and Political Science, Entrepreneurship and Family Enterprise, Finance, Marketing, Organisational, Strategy and Technology and Operations Management areas.</p>
<h3>Admissions</h3>
<p>Admission to the INSEAD MBA program is very competitive.
Applicants typically have over 5 years of work experience, have had multicultural interactions through work or personal experiences, and speak multiple languages.
The Admissions Committee looks for excellent academic performance, career progress, interpersonal skills and leadership potential.</p>
<p>All applicants must hold a <a>bachelor's degree</a> or equivalent, demonstrate fluency in English and are required to submit a lengthy application with detailed essays supporting their case, a profile, two letters of recommendation, official academic transcripts, their <a>Graduate Management Admission Test</a> (GMAT) score, a statement of integrity and their <a>PTE Academic</a>, TOEIC, TOEFL, IELTS or Certificate of Proficiency in English (CPE) score (for non-native English speakers) or entry language certification (for native English speakers).
MBA participants' average GMAT score has consistently been above 700 (90th percentile) over the last 5 years.</p>
<p>Applicants who pass the initial stage of the screening process by the Admissions Committee are invited to two interviews conducted by two INSEAD MBA alumni in their country of residence.</p>
<h3>Post MBA Careers</h3>
<p>The INSEAD Career Services team  assists companies wishing to recruit INSEAD MBAs and participants.
Approximately 120 companies/firms visit the Asia and Europe campuses during each recruitment season.
Career Services organises company presentations and bi-annual career fairs on both campuses, sources jobs on its internal platform CareerLink and publishes a CV book for each graduating class.</p>
<p>Main employers of 2009 class MBA participants and the number of graduates employed were <a>McKinsey &amp; Company</a> (78), <a>Boston Consulting Group</a> (44), <a>Bain &amp; Company</a> (43), <a>Booz &amp; Company</a> (23), <a>Standard Chartered Bank</a> (15), <a>A.T. Kearney</a> (11), <a>Roland Berger Strategy Consultants</a> (8), <a>Google</a> (7).</p>
<p>INSEAD has a reciprocal agreement with <a>Harvard Business School</a>, <a>Stanford Graduate School of Business</a> and <a>Kellogg School of Management</a> to share career services.
Alumni of the four schools have access to job opportunities database of each other.</p>
<h2>PhD</h2>
<p>Admission to INSEAD's PhD program is competitive with less than 5% receiving an offer.
Applicants must hold a University-level degree and have a working knowledge of English.
Applicants whose native language is not English are required to take the <a>TOEFL</a> test unless their university degree is from an institution where the curriculum was exclusively taught in English.</p>

<h3>Innovation in education</h3>
<p>A number of researches and initiatives are conducted at INSEAD to incorporate innovative learning approaches.</p>
<p>In particular centers conduct research in learning technologies and approaches such as:</p>
<ul>
  <li>INSEAD CALT (the Centre of Advanced Learning Technologies).
INSEAD CALT is and has been involved in many research projects, and in particular projects funded by the research programmes from the <a>European Commission</a> on approaches such as <a>business simulation</a>s, or <a>learning communities</a>.</li>
<li>INSEAD Learning Innovation Centre.
INSEAD Learning Innovation Centre was funded to managing innovation in INSEAD programme design and delivery.
For instance, INSEAD Learning Innovation Centre has introduced the use of <a>Second Life</a> virtual world as an education tool.</p>
<ul>
  <li>INSEAD <a>eLab</a>.
INSEAD elab  is an umbrella structure covering the related research and teaching activities at INSEAD.
INSEAD <a>eLab</a> has a particular focus on achieving a greater understanding of value creation and competitive advantage in the digital economy space.
Example of recent work: The impact of new media and <a>Web 2.0</a> platforms like <a>wikis</a> and <a>social network service</a>s such as <a>Blogs</a>, <a>Facebook</a>, <a>Twitter</a> and <a>YouTube</a> on business and government .
Other projects include GITR (Global Information Technology Report), GII (Global Innovation Index)  and INNOVAlatino.
Some of the recently developed models are '3Q Model of CIO Leadership' and 'Innovation Readiness Model'.
Annual Report 2008-09  is released.</li>
</ul>
<h2>Alumni</h2>

<p>The Alumni Association was founded by a group of alumni in 1961, and works in close partnership with INSEAD to provide services to the global INSEAD alumni community in 160 countries.
Many are members of the INSEAD International Alumni Association, which has 43 national alumni associations.</p>
<p>In addition to the national associations, INSEAD alumni have formed clubs and groups dedicated to specific industries or activities.
These include the <a>Energy Club</a>, the <a>INSEAD Healthcare Alumni Network</a> and the <a>Salamander Golf Society</a>.</p>
