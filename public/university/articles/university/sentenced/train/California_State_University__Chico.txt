<p><strong>California State University, Chico</strong> is the second-oldest campus in the twenty three campus <a>California State University</a> system.
It is located in <a>Chico</a>, <a>California</a>, about ninety miles north of <a>Sacramento</a>.
California State University, Chico is commonly known as <strong>Chico State</strong> or <strong>Cal State, Chico</strong>.</p>
<h2>History</h2>
<p>On March 12, 1887, a legislative act was enacted to create the Northern Branch State Normal School of California.
Less than a month later, Chico was chosen as the location.
On June 24, 1887, General <a>John Bidwell</a> donated 8 acres (32,000 m&#x00b2;) of land from his cherry <a>orchard</a>.
Then on July 4, 1888, the first cornerstone was laid.
On September 3, 1889, doors opened for the 90 enrolled students.
The library opened on January 11, 1890 with 350 books.
On June 20, 1891 the first graduation took place, a class of 15.(Chico Was Also Know For its Beauty)</p>
<p>In 1910, Annie Bidwell donated an additional two acres (8,000 m&#x00b2;) of land to be used for work with elementary agriculture.
The next year Mrs. Bidwell donated an orange orchard lot 55 x  as the children's playground, which is connected to the Training School.
Twenty years later in 1921, legislation was enacted to change the school's name to Chico State Teacher's College.
In 1922, Chico State Teacher's College added a junior college curriculum and awarded a certificate after two years.
Also in 1922 <a>Bidwell Mansion</a> was turned into a women's dormitory, Bidwell Hall.
In 1923 the first college paper, <em>The Collegian</em> was published.
In 1924, the state board of education allowed the school to grant <a>baccalaureate</a> degrees.
Also in 1924, the <a>wildcat</a> was chosen as the <a>mascot</a>.
In 1925 the <a>alumni</a> organization was founded.
In 1927 a fire destroyed the Normal Building.
That same year a gym was built on the grounds of Bidwell Mansion.
In 1929, the corner stone for the new administration building was laid on top of Normal Building's original corner stone.
In 1929 the student bookstore was established.</p>
<p><a>200px|left|thumb|Chico State campus in the spring</a> In 1935, Bidwell Hall was turned into a recreation and student center - the first student union.
Also in 1935 a legislative act changed the college name from Chico State Teachers College to Chico State College.
In 1937 evening classes started on campus and athletic fields were purchased from the Chico Board of Education.
In 1939, chimes were installed in library tower.
Sororities held a fund drive to raise $600 for them.
In 1940 the college offered civilian pilot classes.</p>
<p>In 1948, dorms for 200 male students were set up on west side of Warner Street.
The buildings were built during <a>World War II</a> and were used as bachelor quarters for a Marine Hospital in <a>Klamath Falls, Oregon</a>.
They were brought to Chico State in sections and reconstructed in the spring of 1948.
The two story barrack-like structures had 36 rooms, each occupied by 4 students.
North Hall later became a girls <a>dormitory</a>.
The speech and debate team was founded by Herbert Rae, Speech &amp; Drama Department Chair.</p>
<p>In 1950, California's governor allowed state colleges to grant Master of Arts degrees.
In 1951 the college reorganized from 18 departments into seven divisions with chairmen.
Then in 1956 a new flagpost and sign for in front of Kendall Hall was donated by the class of 1956.
In the following year, 1957, a new <a>cafeteria</a> was built the rose gardens were planted.
In 1958 the first &quot;telecourse&quot; was taught, Psychology 51.</p>
<p>KCSC, a student run radio station launched, broadcasting old-time radio dramas on the campus public address system was launched in 1951.</p>
<p>In 1972, Chico State College became California State University, Chico as a result of legislation passed in 1971.</p>
<p>In 1975, broadcasts of classes through <a>closed circuit TV</a> were used for the first time by residents in <a>Oroville</a>, <a>Marysville</a> and <a>Colusa</a>.
Also in 1975, <a>The Orion</a>, the campus student newspaper published its first issue.
In 1977, the other campus paper, <em>The Wildcat</em>, changed its name to <em><a>Chico News and Review</a></em> and moved off campus to become an independent publication.
In 1978 bike riding was restricted on campus.</p>
<p>Chico State's library was renamed for <a>Ted Meriam</a> in 1981.
Notably, the 75th Pioneer Days was held in 1985, but the event was canceled in 1987, ostensibly because of a <a>riot</a>.</p>
<p>In 1987, Chico State was ranked as the number one party school by <em><a>Playboy</a></em>  Chico State was not included on Playboy's most recent list of party schools.</p>
<p>In 1997 <a>Wild Oak Music Group</a>, an independent record company, was founded and is run by the Music Industry students within the College of Humanities and Fine Arts.</p>
<p>In 1989 <em>The Orion</em> wins the <a>National Pacemaker Award</a>, the first of nine the paper has won the top prize in college journalism.
CSU Chico opened its first sub-campus in <a>Redding</a>, affiliated with Shasta College, in 2007.</p>
<p>In 2005, student <a>Matt Carrington</a> was hazed to death at the <a>Chi Tau</a> house, which had previously been expelled from the university in 2001 due to violations.
Carrington died as a result of <a>water intoxication</a> during a hazing session involving the victim being forced to exercise and drink large quantities of water.</p>
<p>In 2010, the student body president, Joseph Igbineweka, a Nigerian (African) was stabbed multiple times.
It was labelled as a hate crime.</p>
<h2>Academics</h2>
<p>Chico State is known for academic excellence in engineering, science, computing, business, technology, environmental studies, Theatre Arts  and communication.
Chico State has more than 50 Departments and offers more than 150 undergraduate degrees.
It is organized into eight colleges:</p>
<ul>
  <li>College of Agriculture</li>
  <li>College of Behavioral &amp; Social Sciences</li>
  <li>College of Business</li>
  <li>College of Communication &amp; Education</li>
  <li>College of Engineering, Computer Science, &amp; Construction Management</li>
  <li>College of Humanities and Fine Arts</li>
  <li>College of Natural Sciences</li>
  <li>College of Graduate Studies</li>
</ul>
<p>The school has several libraries, including Meriam Library, which has several special collections of <a>Native American</a> and Californian history.</p>
<p>Chico State was ranked as the 6th Best Public Master's Level University in the West (2010) from <em><a>U.S. News &amp; World Report</a></em>.</p>
<p>Chico State's student-run theatre organization is &quot;Ink Blot Arts.&quot;
Their mission statement is to bring theatre, of all kinds, to people of all kinds.</p>

<h2>Campus</h2>
<p>The California State University, Chico campus consists of a 119-acre main campus and a 1,040-acre university farm.
<a>225px|right|thumb|Chico State's Kendall (Administration) Hall</a></p>
<p>Colusa Hall, completed in 1921 is the oldest building on campus.
It was used for purposes related to the industrial arts, but now is serves as an art gallery and public events facility.</p>
<h3>Residence Halls</h3>
<p>Currently, there are seven on-campus residential halls.
Sutter, Whitney, Shasta and Lassen halls are on the main campus, while Esken, Mechoopda and Konkow are near the athletic fields about a block and a half away from the main campus.
Whitney, Shasta and Lassen are the names of major mountains in Northern California, and the others are named after Indian tribes which used to inhabit the area.
Most buildings that make up the campus are named after counties in California.
University Village or &quot;UV&quot; is a university-owned dorm about a mile off campus.
The university opened its newest dorm, Sutter Hall, for the Fall 2010 semester.
It is located between Whitney and Shasta and Lassen halls.</p>
<h3>Recent Construction</h3>
<p>The Wildcat Recreation Center (WREC), approved by a 2005 student ballot initiative, opened in August 2009.
It has received a LEED rating of Gold for its sustainable and environmentally conscious features.</p>
<h2>Associated Students, Chico</h2>
<p></p>
<p>Associated Students, Chico is the <a>student government</a> at California State University, Chico.
Associated Students, Chico owns and operates several student services on-campus including all <a>vending machine</a>s, and <a>foodservice</a>s, as well as the campus <a>bookstore</a>.
The students of CSU Chico also own their own <a>student union</a> building named the <a>Bell Memorial Union</a> which houses the <em>Marketplace Cafe</em>, the <em>AS Bookstore</em>, and the student government offices.
Student officers are elected annually from among and by the students.
Students are assessed a mandatory Activity Fee at registration which funds the student government and other programs.</p>
<p>The AS. is generally divided into three areas, each the responsibility of one of three Associated Students <a>standing committee</a>s.
The AS' role as a government is manifested in the Government Affairs Committee.
The student union is administered under the original authority of the Bell Memorial Union Committee.
The administration of the businesses is under the original authority of the Business Committee.
All of these areas are under the ultimate authority of the AS Board of Directors.</p>
<h2>Athletics</h2>
<h3>Chico State Wildcats</h3>
<p>The school sponsors <a>soccer</a>, <a>basketball</a>, <a>cross country</a>, <a>golf</a>, and <a>track and field</a> for both men and women.
The school sponsors <a>softball</a> and <a>volleyball</a> for women, and <a>baseball</a> for men.</p>
<p>The school's <a>athletic director</a> is Anita Barker.
The school competes in <a>Division II</a> of the <a>National Collegiate Athletic Association</a> in the <a>California Collegiate Athletic Association</a>.
The Chico State baseball team plays at <a>Nettleton Stadium</a> and has won the <a>NCAA Division II World Series</a> in 1997 and 1999 and appeared in the title game in 2002, 2006.</p>
<p>The school finished third in the 2004-2005 <a>NACDA Director's Cup</a>.
In 1997 Chico State ended its football program citing rising insurance costs.</pre>
<p>CSU Chico won the NCAA Division II national championships in men's swimming and diving in 1973, 1974 and 1976.
The program was eliminated several years after the 1976 national championship season.
The men's soccer program lost in the title game in 2003.</p>
<p>In 2001 the women's rugby team won a Division I national championship.</p>
<h2>Service and recognition</h2>
<p><a>thumb|right|250px|Kendall Hall</a></p>
<p>California State University, Chico has once again been designated as a <a>member</a> of the President&#x2019;s Higher Education Community Service Honor Roll with Distinction.
This honor, the second in a row for CSU, Chico, highlights the University&#x2019;s civic engagement activities during 2007.</p>
<p>In 2006, the year the community service honor roll program was launched, CSU, Chico was one of 10 schools to be a finalist for President <a>George W. Bush</a>&#x2019;s Higher Education Community Service Award for Excellence in General Community Service.
In 2006-07 CSU, Chico was first among 215 colleges and universities in the U.S. by raising a record $187,000 for St. Jude Children's Research Hospital, the largest childhood cancer research center in the world.
CSU, Chico also set <a>records</a> for the highest number of participants writing the highest number of letters on behalf of <a>St.
Jude</a>.</p>
<p>CSU, Chico has set <a>records</a> for the number of volunteers taking part to raise money (2,021) and the number of letters sent out requesting support (in excess of 37,000).</p>
<p>Among the civic engagement <a>programs</a> at CSU, Chico are Community Action Volunteers in Education (CAVE) and Community Legal Information Center (CLIC).[<a>http://www.aschico.com/?Page=8</a>][<a>http://www.csuchico.edu/pols/clic/</a>]
CAVE student volunteers help more than 30,000 clients annually, and CLIC student volunteers provide free referrals and legal information to more than 13,000 clients.</p>
<p><em><a>The Orion</a></em> is Chico State's nationally acclaimed school newspaper, and has been named (on several occasions) the number one weekly collegiate publication.
The newspaper was inducted into the Associated College Press Hall of Fame in 2005.</p>
<h2>Sustainability</h2>
<p><a>225px|right|thumb|The Chico State Motto, &quot;Today decides tomorrow&quot;</a></p>
<p>CSU, Chico has received a number of honors for its leadership role in <a>sustainability</a> and environmental awareness and education.
CSU, Chico&#x2019;s <a>Green Campus Program</a> won the <a>Best Practices</a> award for Student Energy Efficiency in the CSU in April 2008.
In 2007, the University was awarded the <a>Grand Prize</a> by the National Wildlife Federation for efforts to reduce global warming.</p>
<p>The University was <a>ranked</a> on a list of 15 colleges and universities around the world cited for their leadership in sustainability and environmental programs.
<a>Grist Magazine</a>, a well-known Web-based environmental news and commentary publication, has placed CSU, Chico on its list of &#x201c;15 Green Colleges and Universities.&#x201d;
<a>The DailyGreen</a>, a popular Web site that bills itself as &#x201c;The consumer&#x2019;s guide to the green revolution,&#x201d; has featured CSU, Chico in a list of 10 top colleges and universities that includes Harvard University, Duke University, Middlebury College and Oberlin College.</p>
<p>Chico Professor <a>Jeff Price</a>, shared in the 2007 <a>Nobel Peace Prize</a> as a lead author of the <a>Intergovernmental Panel on Climate Change</a>, and is also Senior Fellow for Climate Change and Biodiversity at the United Nations Environment Program &#x2013; World Conservation Monitoring Center (UNEP-WCMC).</p>
<p>CSU, Chico University Printing Services has been awarded <a>Forest Stewardship Council</a> chain-of-custody <a>certification</a> by Scientific Certification Systems (certificate number SCS-COC-001517), supporting CSU, Chico&#x2019;s campuswide commitment to sustainability.
CSU, Chico is one of the first universities in the country to receive this certification.</p>
<p>CSU, Chico's executive director of the Institute for Sustainable Development is Scott McNall.[<a>http://www.csuchico.edu/sustainablefuture/letterDirector.shtml</a>]
Complete information on the University's involvement in sustainable issues is listed on the <a>Sustainable News</a> Web site.</p>
