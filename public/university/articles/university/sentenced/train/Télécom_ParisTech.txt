<p> </p>
<p>  <strong>T&#x00e9;l&#x00e9;com ParisTech</strong> (also known as <strong>ENST</strong> or <strong>T&#x00e9;l&#x00e9;com</strong> or <strong>&#x00c9;cole Nationale Sup&#x00e9;rieure des T&#x00e9;l&#x00e9;communications</strong>) is one of the most prestigious and selective <a>grandes &#x00e9;coles</a> in <a>France</a> and one of the finest institutions in the field of <a>Telecommunications</a>. Located in <a>Paris</a>, it is also a member of prestigious <a>ParisTech</a> and <a>Institut Telecom</a>.</p>
<p>It has established a school named <a>Institut Eur&#x00e9;com</a> in collaboration with <a>EPFL</a> at <a>Sophia-Antipolis</a>.</p>
<h2>History</h2>
<ul>
  <li>In 1845, Alphonse Foy, director of telegraphic lines, proposed a school specializing in telegraphy for Polytechnicians.
His proposition was rejected.</li>
<li>The school was founded on 12 July 1878 as the &#x00c9;cole Professionnelle Sup&#x00e9;rieure des Postes et T&#x00e9;l&#x00e9;graphes (EPSPT).</li>
<li>In 1912 the school's name was changed to &#x00c9;cole Sup&#x00e9;rieure des Postes et T&#x00e9;l&#x00e9;graphes (ESPT).</li>
<li>In 1934 the ESPT moved to rue Barrault, in the <a>13th arrondissement of Paris</a>.
</li>
  <li>In 1938 the school was renamed &#x00c9;cole Nationale Sup&#x00e9;rieure des Postes, T&#x00e9;l&#x00e9;graphes et T&#x00e9;l&#x00e9;phones (ENSPTT), and in the same year, the <a>President of France</a>, <a>Albert Lebrun</a> honored the school <a>Legion of Honor</a>.</li>
<li>During the <a>Second World War</a>, in 1942, the ENSPTT was divided into two schools: the ENSPTT and ENST.
The ENSPTT was closed on 31 December 2002.
</li>
  <li>In 1971 the <a>ENST</a> passed under the direct guardianship of the <em>Direction g&#x00e9;n&#x00e9;rale des t&#x00e9;l&#x00e9;communications</em>, and the development of the telecommunications during this period drove the state to create two associate schools: the <a>ENST Bretagne</a> in 1977 at <a>Brest</a>, and the <a>INT</a> in 1979 at <a>&#x00c9;vry</a>.
</li>
</ul>
<p><a>thumb|right|275px|Locations of Institut Telecom schools</a></p>
<ul>
  <li>In 1992 the <a>ENST</a>, together with the <a>EPFL</a>, founded the <a>Institut Eur&#x00e9;com</a> at <a>Sophia-Antipolis</a>.</li>
  <li>On 26 December 1996 the <a>Groupe des &#x00c9;coles des T&#x00e9;l&#x00e9;communications</a> (GET) was established.
It consists of a group of telecommunications schools including the ENST, the <a>Telecom Bretagne</a>, the <a>Telecom SudParis</a>, and <a>Institut Eur&#x00e9;com</a>.</li>
<li>On 21 September 2009, the school's name was changed to T&#x00e9;l&#x00e9;com ParisTech.</li>
</ul>
<h2>Present</h2>
<p>Future engineers are recruited from college (&quot;Classes Pr&#x00e9;paratoires&quot;) or after a bachelors degree in scientific areas (physics, mechanics, chemistry, computer science etc.).
Some other admission possibilities are after completing the <a>&#x00c9;cole Polytechnique</a> or from the French military and police.
ENST also provides education for the <a>Corps of Telecommunications</a>.</p>
<p>The Engineering diploma delivered by ENST is roughly equivalent to a Master of Engineering in the USA.
Around 250 engineers graduate each year from ENST.
20% to 25% of the graduates are non-French.
Courses cover all aspects of computer and communication engineering: electronics, signal processing, software engineering, networking, economics etc.</p>
<h2>Research at T&#x00e9;l&#x00e9;com ParisTech</h2>
<p>Research at T&#x00e9;l&#x00e9;com ParisTech consists of:</p>
<ul>
  <li>The optimization and the transmission of information </li>
  <li>The improvement in data processing </li>
  <li>Microelectronics, such as FPGA and DSP systems</li>
  <li>Image and signal processing, wavelets</li>
  <li>Artificial intelligence, data mining, distributed and real-time systems</li>
</ul>
<p>T&#x00e9;l&#x00e9;com ParisTech has four research laboratories:  </p>
<ul>
  <li>Department of Electronics and Communications : This laboratory consists of about one hundred researchers and teaching researchers (37 tenured) within 7 research groups</li>
  <li>Department of Computer Science and Networking</li>
  <li>Department of Signal and Image Processing</li>
  <li>Department of Economic and Social Sciences</li>
</ul>
<p>These departments are associated with the <a>CNRS</a> through the &quot;Laboratory of the Communication and Processing of Information&quot;.</p>
<h2>Formations for Engineer degree</h2>
<ul>
  <li><em>The common trunk (1&lt;sup&gt;st&lt;/sup&gt;year)</em> </li>
</ul>
<p>For the students admitted in 1st year, the <em>tronc commun</em> is devoted to the <em>bases des connaissances indispensables</em> (BCI).
It unfolds them to Paris and lasts a year for the students coming from the common Competition and those admitted on dismissed titles.
It takes place in Paris whether they've chosen the <em>Paris courses</em> or the <em>Sophia-antipolis courses</em>.
</p>
<ul>
  <li><em>The optional unified Cycle (2&lt;sup&gt;nd&lt;/sup&gt; and 3&lt;sup&gt;rd&lt;/sup&gt; years) - Paris courses</em></li>
</ul>
<p>The optional cycle is composed of 4 semesters dedicated to the training of the engineers.
The teachings of the optional cycle are organized in more than 120 <em>unit&#x00e9;s d&#x2019;enseignement</em>(E.U).</p>
<ul>
  <li>The optional unified Cycle (2&lt;sup&gt;nd&lt;/sup&gt; and 3&lt;sup&gt;rd&lt;/sup&gt; years) - Sophia-antipolis courses (at <a>Institut Eur&#x00e9;com</a>)<em></em></li>
</ul>
<p>The optional cycle is composed of 4 semesters, one of them being dedicated to end of study training.
The optional unified cycle works on the model of the unities of capitalizable values called &quot;modules longs&quot; (42 hours - 4 credits) or &quot;modules courts&quot; (21 hours - 2 credits).</p>

<h3>Master of Science courses</h3>
<p>Master of Science is offered through <a>Institut T&#x00e9;l&#x00e9;com</a> and <a>ParisTech</a>.
This program is intended for bachelor degree holders or of an equivalent diploma to 4 years of university studies in the domain of <a>Information Technology</a> and <a>Telecommunications</a>. The duration of courses will be 12 to 24 months and the course fee is 10,000&#x20ac;.
The courses offered at <a>Paris</a> campus will be initially  in <a>English</a> and gradually in <a>French</a>.
The courses offered at <a>Institut Eur&#x00e9;com</a>, <a>Sophia-Antipolis</a> campus will entirely be in English.
The degrees which are obtained are accredited by <em>Conference des Grandes Ecoles</em> and are called <strong><em>Master of Science</em></strong> (English) / <strong>Dipl&#x00f4;me National Master</strong> (French)<em>.</em></p>
