<p> </p>
<p><strong>Champlain Regional College</strong> was founded in <a>1971</a> and named in honour of <a>Samuel de Champlain</a>, the first governor of <a>New France</a>.
The College offers post-secondary pre-university (<a>Collegiate</a>), technical and training programs to communities in three distinct regions of Quebec.</p>
<h2>History</h2>
<p><a>right|thumb|200px|Samuel de Champlain</a> The college was named after <a>Samuel de Champlain</a>.
The college traces its origins to the merger of several institutions which became public ones in <a>1967</a>, with the creation of Quebec's <a>CEGEP</a>s and the collegiate system.</p>
<h2>Campus</h2>
<p>Located in <a>Quebec</a>, <a>Canada</a>, the College is a public English-language educational institution which is composed of administrative offices in <a>Sherbrooke</a> and three campuses: <a>Champlain College Lennoxville</a> in the <a>Lennoxville</a> borough of Sherbrooke, <a>Champlain College Saint-Lambert</a> in <a>Saint-Lambert</a> on <a>Montreal</a>'s South Shore, and <a>Champlain College St. Lawrence</a> in the <a>Quebec City</a> area.</p>
