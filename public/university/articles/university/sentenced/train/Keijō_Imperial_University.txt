<p> <img>  was a <a>historical</a> Japanese <a>university</a> that existed in <a>Keij&#x014d;</a> (now <a>Seoul</a>) between 1924 and the end of World War II, and the first predecessor to <a>Seoul National University</a>.</p>
<h2>History</h2>
<p>Keij&#x014d; Imperial University was founded in 1924 as the sixth <a>Imperial University</a> of <a>Japan</a> during the <a>period of Japanese rule</a>, followed by <a>Nagoya University</a> and <a>Osaka University</a> in 1931 and 1939 respectively.
While the other Imperial Universities located in Japan were run by Ministry of Education, Science and Culture, Keij&#x014d; Imperial University was run by <a>Governor-General of Korea</a>.</p>
<p>After the end of <a>World War II</a> Keij&#x014d; Imperial University was renamed to &quot;Ky&#x014f;ngs&#x014f;ng University&quot; () and became the first university of post-war Korea.</p>
<p>Ky&#x014f;ngs&#x014f;ng University was closed on August 22, 1946, by US Military Ordinance No. 102.
It was later merged with 9 other colleges into <a>Seoul National University</a>.</p>
