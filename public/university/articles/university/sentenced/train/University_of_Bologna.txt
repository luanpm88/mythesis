<p>The <strong>University of Bologna</strong> (, <strong>UNIBO</strong>) is the <a>oldest continually operating university in the world</a>, the word '<a>universitas</a>' being first used by this institution at its foundation.
The true date of its founding is uncertain, but believed by most accounts to have been 1088.
Since 2000, the University's motto has been <em><a>Alma mater</a> studiorum</em> (<a>Latin</a> for &quot;fostering mother of studies&quot;).
The university received a <a>charter</a> from <a>Frederick I Barbarossa</a> in 1158, but in the 19th century, a committee of historians led by <a>Giosu&#x00e8; Carducci</a> traced the founding of the University back to 1088, making it the oldest university in the strict sense in the world.
The university is <a>historically</a> notable for its <a>teaching</a> of <a>canon</a> and <a>civil law</a>.
Until modern times, the only degree granted at that university was the <a>doctorate</a>.</p>
<p>The University counts about 100,000 students in its 23 <a>faculties</a>.
It has branch centers in <a>Reggio Emilia</a>, <a>Imola</a>, <a>Ravenna</a>, <a>Forl&#x00ec;</a>, <a>Cesena</a> and <a>Rimini</a> and a branch center abroad in <a>Buenos Aires</a>.
Moreover, it has a school of excellence named <a>Collegio Superiore di Bologna</a>.</p>
<h2>Organization</h2>
<p>Higher education processes are being harmonised across the European Community.
Nowadays the University offers 128 different &quot;<em>Laurea</em>&quot; or &quot;<em>Laurea breve</em>&quot; first-level degrees (three years of courses), followed by a similar number of &quot;<em>Laurea specialistica</em>&quot; specialised degrees (two years).
However, some courses have maintained preceding rules of &quot;<em>Laurea specialistica europea</em>&quot; or &quot;<em>Laurea magistrale</em>&quot;, with only one cycle of study of five years, except for medicine which requires six years of courses.
After the &quot;<em>Laurea</em>&quot; one may attain 1st level Master.
After &quot;<em>Laurea specialistica</em>&quot; and &quot;<em>Laurea specialistica europea</em>&quot; are attained, one may proceed to 2nd level Master, specialisation schools, or doctorates of research.</p>

<h2>Affiliates and other institutions</h2>
<p>In early 1950s some students of the University of Bologna were among the founders of the review &quot;il Mulino&quot;.
On April 25, 1951, was published in <a>Bologna</a> the first issue of the review.
In a short time, &quot;il Mulino&quot; became one of the most interesting reference points in Italy for the political and cultural debate, and established important editorial relationships in Italy and abroad.
Editorial activities evolved along with the review: in 1954 was founded the il Mulino publishing house (Societ&#x00e0; editrice il Mulino) that today represents one of the most relevant Italian publisher.
Besides were initiated research projects (focusing mostly on the educational institutions and the political system in <a>Italy</a>), that eventually led, in 1964, to the establishment of the Istituto Carlo Cattaneo.</p>
