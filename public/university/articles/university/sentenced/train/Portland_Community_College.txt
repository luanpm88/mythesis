<p><strong>Portland Community College</strong> (or PCC) is <a>Oregon</a>'s largest <a>community college</a>, located in <a>Portland</a>, United States.
It serves over one million residents in the five county area of <a>Multnomah</a>, <a>Washington</a>, <a>Yamhill</a>, <a>Clackamas</a>, and <a>Columbia</a>.
PCC enrolls over 83,000 (55% female, 45% male) students annually in this  area in northwest Oregon.</p>
<h2>History</h2>
<p>The college was founded in 1961 as an <a>adult education</a> program for the local public school system, operating out of the former Failing&lt;!--
Failing is a noun, not an adjective --&gt; Elementary School.</p>
<p>In 1971 the Cascade Campus opened in North Portland, and the Rock Creek Campus in Washington County opened in 1976.
The district passed a $374 million bond measure in 2008.
PCC's $25 million Willow Creek Center opened in 2009 and earned a platinum LEED certification the next year.</p>
<h2>Facilities</h2>
<p>There are three main campuses, which are larger facilities offering two-year degrees and a range of typical student services:</p>
<ul>
  <li><a>Sylvania</a>, opened in the <a>Far Southwest</a> neighborhood of Portland in 1968 and serves over 26,000 students annually</li>
  <li><a>Rock Creek</a>, a  suburban campus, opened in 1976, near <a>Hillsboro</a>; also home to the <a>Washington County Museum</a></li>
  <li><a>Cascade</a>, an urban campus, opened in 1971, and located in the heart of North Portland</li>
  <li>Southeast Center, in the former home of the local <a>U.S. Corps of Engineers</a> headquarters</li>
</ul>
<p>There are several centers throughout the <a>metropolitan area</a>, which are smaller facilities offering more limited or specialized programs:</p>
<ul>
  <li>Central Portland Workforce Training Center, located near <a>OMSI</a></li>
  <li>Portland Metropolitan Workforce Training Center</li>
  <li>Willow Creek Center, opened in 2009 in Hillsboro</li>
</ul>
<p>PCC is a member of the <a>Northwest Athletic Association of Community Colleges</a>, with its men's and women's basketball teams competing against those from the community colleges of <a>Chemeketa</a>, <a>Clackamas</a>, <a>Mount Hood</a>, <a>Lane</a>, <a>Linn-Benton</a>, <a>Southwestern Oregon</a>, and <a>Umpqua</a>.

<h2>Community education</h2>
<p>PCC operates one of the largest non-credit life-long learning programs in the nation through its <a>community education</a> program.
Every year, PCC Community Education offers more than 4,000 Courses are offered in five general subject areas: Creative Arts, Home and Garden, Language and Culture, Recreation and Wellness, and Work and Life Balance.
</p>
<p>In addition to classes held at PCC campuses and centers, PCC Community Education partners with local business, schools, community centers, churches and parks to offer classes in neighborhoods throughout the PCC district.
Hundreds of classes are also held online.</p>
