<p> <strong>Joseph Willard</strong> (December 29, 1738 &ndash; September 25, 1804) was a <a>U.S.</a> <a>Congregational</a> clergyman and academic.
He was educated at the Dummer Academy (now known as <a>The Governor's Academy</a>) and <a>Harvard College</a> (<a>B.A.</a>, 1765; <a>M.A.</a>, 1768) and served as pastor in <a>Beverly, Massachusetts</a>.
In 1780 he was appointed first corresponding secretary of the <a>American Academy of Arts and Sciences</a>, and the following year he became <a>president</a> of <a>Harvard</a> in the midst of the <a>American Revolution</a>.
</p>
<p>Willard was the father of  <a>Cambridge</a> <a>Mayor</a> <a>Sidney Willard</a>.
</p>
<p>He served as <a>president</a> of <a>Harvard</a> until his death.</p>
