<p><strong>Confederation College</strong> is a provincially funded <a>community college</a> located in <a>Thunder Bay</a>, <a>Ontario</a>, <a>Canada</a>.
It was established in 1967, and has area campuses in <a>Dryden</a>, <a>Fort Frances</a>, <a>Geraldton</a>, <a>Kenora</a>, <a>Marathon</a> and <a>Sioux Lookout</a>.
The college, with its area campuses, serves an area of approximately 215,000 <a>square kilometre</a>s.
It is one of only two community colleges supporting <a>Northwestern Ontario</a>.
The press, Opus, is a member of <a>CUP</a>.</p>
<h2>History</h2>
<p>Confederation College was founded as a trade school in 1967, during the formation of Ontario&#x2019;s college system.
Air Vice Marshall (ret<code>d) <a>Douglas Bradshaw</a> was the first president of Confederation College from March 6, 1967 to 1974.
His vision of an aviation program in the north was implemented in the College&#x2019;s Aviation Centre of Excellence.
He died on 1/10/1996.
In his memory, the <a>Douglas Bradshaw</a> Athletic Achievement Award is presented to a graduating student, having the most improvement in and contribution to athletics and whose quality of spirit and competitive drive contributed to the overall success of the Confederation College Athletic program.
</code></p>
<h2>Programs</h2>
<p>The college offers a full range of programmes and educational services throughout the region, which includes full-time post-secondary programmes, part-time credit and non-credit courses, specialty programmes for business and industry, pre-employment and skills training programmes, apprenticeship programmes and cooperative/workplace training programmes.</p>
<p>Confederation College has 3,200 full-time <a>post-secondary</a> students, 410 <a>apprentice</a>s, 550 adult training students and 17,000 part time &amp; <a>continuing education</a> students.</p>
<h3>Thunder Bay campus</h3>
<p>The Thunder Bay campus is home to five campus buildings (Shuniah, Dorion, Neebing, Conmee &amp; McIntyre buildings), with the student residences located in the Sibley Hall Residence.
Also present on the Thunder Bay Campus is the <a>Thunder Bay Art Gallery</a>.
Located at the <a>Thunder Bay International Airport</a> is the college's <a>School of Aviation</a>.
The Thunder Bay campus is also home to the Confederation College Student Union offices.</p>
<h3>Dryden campus </h3>
<p>The Dryden Campus offers programs in Business, Community Service, Health and Skilled Trades.</p>
<h3>Fort Frances campus</h3>
<p>The Confederation College Rainy River District campus located in <a>Fort Frances</a>, <a>Ontario</a> offers a variety of courses to the area of Fort Frances.
The campus is located within the Fort Frances Multi-use facility, which also houses Fort Frances High School and the Townshend Theatre.</p>
<p>The Rainy River District campus offers a whole range of full-time and part-time courses ranging from post secondary diploma programs, certificate programs, continuing education courses, and general interest courses.</p>
<h3>Geraldton campus</h3>
<p>The Greenstone Campus in Geraldton offers programs in Business, Community Services, College Access, Health and Skilled Trades.</p>
<h3>Kenora campus</h3>
<p>The Confederation College Kenora Campus Located in <a>Kenora</a>, <a>Ontario</a>.
Offers a wide range of recognized college certificates and diplomas.</p>
<h3>Marathon campus</h3>
<p>The Northshore Campus in Marathon offers programs in Business, College Access, Community Services and Health, as well as specialized training in mining and diamond drilling.</p>
<h3>Sioux Lookout campus</h3>
<p>The Sioux Lookout Campus offers programs in Business, Health, Community Services and Skilled Trades.</p>
<h2>Scholarships</h2>
<p>Confederation College of Applied Arts and Technology joined Project Hero, a scholarship program cofounded by General (Ret'd) <a>Rick Hillier</a> for the families of fallen <a>Canadian Forces</a> members.
