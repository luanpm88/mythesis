<p> <strong>John Daniel Runkle</strong> (October 11, 1822 &ndash; July 8, 1902) was a <a>U.S.</a> educator and <a>mathematician</a>.
He served as acting president of <a>MIT</a> from 1868&#45;70 and president between 1870 and 1878.
</p>
<h2>Biography</h2>
<p>Professor Runkle was born at <a>Root</a>, <a>New York State</a>.
He worked on his father's farm until he was of age, and then studied and taught until he entered the <a>Lawrence Scientific School</a> of <a>Harvard University</a>, where he graduated in 1851.
His ability as a mathematician led in 1849 to his appointment as assistant in the preparation of the <em>American Ephemeris and Nautical Almanac</em>, in which he continued to engage until 1884.
He was <a>professor</a> of mathematics in the Massachusetts Institute of Technology from 1865 until his retirement in 1902.
Manual training was introduced into the institute <a>curriculum</a> largely at his instance.
He founded the <em><a>Mathematical Monthly</a></em> in 1859 and continued its publication until 1861, and he had charge of the astronomical department of the <em>Illustrated Pilgrim's Almanac</em>.
</p>
<p>In the town of <a>Brookline, Massachusetts</a>, Runkle was a chairman of the School Committee and an early advocate of mathematics and technical education.</p>

<h2>Memorials</h2>
<p><a>John D. Runkle School</a>, an elementary school located at 50 Druce Street in Brookline, was established in his name in 1897.</p>
<h2>Family</h2>
<p>His brother, Cornelius A. Runkle (9 December 1832 in <a>Montgomery County, New York</a>&ndash;19 March 1888 in <a>New York City</a>) graduated from <a>Harvard Law School</a> in 1855, and began practice in New York City.
He was subsequently made deputy collector and given charge of the law division of the New York Custom House.
This rendered him familiar with the legal questions involved in tariff and internal revenue litigation, and resulted in his devoting himself largely to that class of business.
For about twenty-five years, he acted as counsel for the <em><a>New York Tribune</a></em> association.
Cornelius's wife, Lucia Isabella Gilbert Runkle (born in <a>North Brookfield, Massachusetts</a> on August 20, 1844), was an editorial writer and contributor to the <em>Tribune</em>.
</p>
