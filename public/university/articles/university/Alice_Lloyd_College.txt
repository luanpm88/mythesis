<p><strong>Alice Lloyd College</strong> is a four-year <a>liberal arts</a> <a>work college</a> in <a>Pippa Passes, Kentucky</a>. It was co-founded by the journalist <a>Alice Spencer Geddes Lloyd</a>, a native of <a>Cambridge, Massachusetts</a> and <a>June Buchanan</a>, a native of <a>New York City</a>, in 1923, at first under the name of Caney Junior College, as an institution to educate leaders for <a>Appalachia</a> locally. It became a four-year, <a>bachelor's degree</a>-granting institution in the early 1980s. Alice Lloyd College is <a>accredited</a> by the <a>Southern Association of Colleges and Schools</a> (SACS).</p>
<h2>Academics</h2>
<p>As of 2010, Alice Lloyd College has implemented 13 major degree programs and eight pre-professional programs into its curricula.  The school has an acceptance rate of about one half of prospective students who apply. The student-to-faculty ratio of 12:1 is quite low.  </p>
<p>Ninety-five percent of Alice Lloyd College graduates are accepted into graduate and professional schools.</p>
<h2>Student finances and work-study</h2>
<p>While Alice Lloyd College does not rely on any direct financial support from the state or federal governments, they do accept federal and state student financial aid such as federal <a>Pell Grants</a>. Of the 16 percent of students who receive education loans, the average amount is approximately $800. According to the Project on Student Debt, each of Alice Lloyd's 2009 graduates carried an average debt of $6,500, which is well below the statewide average of $19,112 and the national average of $24,000. </p>
<p>Students are required to work in a <a>work-study</a> program regardless of financial situation. They are given jobs such as janitorial staff, office assistant, tutor, craft maker, <a>resident advisor</a>, maintenance, grounds or working in the cafeteria (Hunger Din). In addition to on-campus jobs, students can work at off-campus outreach projects. Students are required to work at least 160 hours per semester. The college is one of eight <a>work colleges</a> in the <a>United States</a> and one of two in Kentucky (<a>Berea College</a> being the other) that have mandatory work-study programs. </p>
<p>Students from 108 counties in the <a>Appalachian Mountains</a> region of Kentucky, <a>Ohio</a>, <a>Tennessee</a>, <a>Virginia</a>, and <a>West Virginia</a> pay no tuition through the Appalachian Leaders College Scholarship.</p>
<h2>Student life</h2>
<p>The dormitories house about 600 students, with rental prices averaging $1,900 annually. Alice Lloyd College requires students to live in gender-separated <a>dormitories</a> and only allows the opposite sex into a gender-specific dorm during &quot;open houses,&quot; after room checks have been made. Room checks consist of two <a>resident advisors</a> going into each room and making sure that it is clean and it does not contain any illegal substances. The college is located in <a>Knott County, Kentucky</a>, a <a>dry county</a>, thus alcoholic beverages are prohibited.</p>
<p>Tuesdays are professional dress days in which students are required by their instructors, except for <a>physical education</a> classes in the <a>Grady Nutt</a> Athletic Center, to dress in business attire to attend any class before 2:00 p.m.</p>
<p>While this college is not affiliated with any <a>religious denomination</a>, the college's mission statement emphasizes the role of <a>Christian</a> values. In addition, the College offers coursework in <a>religion</a> and has a chapter of <a>Baptist Collegiate Ministries</a>.</p>
<p>The college <a>choir</a> is called the &quot;Voices of Appalachia.&quot; The choir, formed in 1962, holds a tour annually in the spring, performing hymns and ballads. The choir has made several media appearances, including <a>NBC</a>'s <em><a>Today</a></em> and <em><a>CBS News Sunday Morning</a></em>. The Business &amp; Technology Center was completed in the fall of 2009 and was dedicated on October 10, 2009.</p>
<h2>Caney/Berger Scholarship</h2>
<p>The college owns the H.N. and Frances C. Berger Residence Hall, also known as Caney Cottage, an apartment complex near the campus of the <a>University of Kentucky</a> in <a>Lexington</a>. Students who graduate from Alice Lloyd and are accepted into UK's <a>graduate school</a> can apply to live in the Caney Cottage rent free. Those who attend other graduate schools can apply for cash scholarships that go toward tuition costs. After graduate school, scholarship recipients must commit to service in the Appalachian region.</p>
