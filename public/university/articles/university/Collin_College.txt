<p><strong>Collin College</strong>, formally known as the Collin County Community College District, is a <a>community college district</a> which serves <a>Collin</a> and <a>Rockwall</a> counties, located north and northeast of <a>Dallas</a>.  Founded in 1985, the district has grown as the county has grown: from around 5,000 students in 1986 to about 46,000 in 2009. With the domain change to <em>collin.edu</em> in January 2010, the college finished rebranding itself and is now known as &quot;Collin College.&quot; </p>
<p>As defined by the <a>Texas Legislature</a>, the official service area of Collin College is the following:</p>
<ul>
  <li>all of Collin and Rockwall counties, and</li>
  <li>those portions of Denton County within the cities of <a>Frisco</a> and <a>The Colony</a>, and those portions of the county included within the <a>Celina</a> and <a>Prosper</a> school districts.</li>
</ul>
<h2>Campuses</h2>
<p><a>thumb|left|Spring Creek Campus</a></p>
<p>The Central Park Campus in <a>McKinney</a> opened in 1985 with comprehensive renovations completed in the fall of 2009. New features include a multi-story parking garage, library, classrooms, offices, student development center and clock tower. The new library building totals . The Central Park Campus is a comprehensive campus.  </p>
<p>The Spring Creek campus in <a>Plano</a> opened in fall of 1988. By number of students, this campus is the largest, providing general classes and housing the college system's honors, fine arts, and athletics programs. The campus features a   art gallery, theatre center, gymnasium (Cougar Hall), and tennis facilities (Brinker Center Court).</p>
<p>The Preston Ridge Campus in <a>Frisco</a> opened in July 1995. The campus recently opened its new  &quot;D&quot; building including 26 general classrooms and four science labs. Collin College's business and high-tech programs are centered at Preston Ridge, and the culinary arts program moved there in 2009.</p>
<p>Collin College also has campuses of smaller capacity in <a>Allen</a>, located in <a>Allen High School</a>, in <a>Plano</a>, for continuing education, and one in <a>Rockwall</a>, (located in neighboring <a>Rockwall County</a>) within the <a>Rockwall Independent School District</a>&#x2019;s Administration/Education Center.</p>

<h2>Collin Higher Education Center</h2>
<p>In January 2010, Collin College opened the doors to its much-anticipated Higher Education Center, located at the intersection of Central Expressway and State Hwy. 121 in McKinney.</p>
<p>The Higher Education Center hosts &#x2013; among other things &#x2013; a number of bachelor&#x2019;s, master&#x2019;s and doctoral programs from five North Texas universities: Dallas Baptist University, Texas A&amp;M University-Commerce, Texas Woman&#x2019;s University, The University of Texas at Dallas and the University of North Texas. </p>
<p>Offerings vary per university. Programs range from communications, teaching, management, Spanish, psychology, child development, counseling, nursing, criminal justice, business administration and accounting.</p>
