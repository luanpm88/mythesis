<p>  <strong>Anderson University</strong> is an <a>accredited</a> private <a>Christian</a> <a>liberal arts university</a> in <a>Anderson, Indiana</a>. The college is affiliated with the <a>Church of God (Anderson, Indiana)</a>. Anderson University is a member of the <a>Council for Christian Colleges and Universities</a>, and the <a>Independent Colleges of Indiana</a> society. In 2010, <em><a>U.S. News &amp; World Report</a></em> ranked Anderson University among the <a>best colleges and universities</a> in the Midwest. The <a>Princeton Review</a> also has recognized Anderson University as one of the best colleges and universities in the Midwest for 2011. The university offers more than 60 undergraduate majors, as well as graduate programs in business, education, music, nursing, and theology. The young school moved rapidly to develop a wider general education program, changed its name to Anderson College and Theological Seminary, then Anderson College, and finally, Anderson University.  <img></p>
<h2>Organization</h2>
<p>Anderson University has grown to include an undergraduate liberal arts program, organized into two colleges; graduate programs in theology, music, nursing, education, and business; and a School of Adult Learning.</p>

<h4>Falls School of Business</h4>
<p>The Falls School of Business offers undergraduate majors in management, marketing, entrepreneurship, finance, and global business, as well as graduate MBA and DBA programs, including a residential MBA program designed especially for students who recently completed their undergraduate degree. The MBA program also includes dual-degree options in technology or nursing. MBA classes are offered in Indianapolis as well as Anderson. The school's programs are accredited by the Association of Collegiate Business Schools and Programs (ACBSP). The Falls School of Business was awarded the 2007 Pinnacle Award by the Hoosier chapter of the <a>Public Relations Society of America</a> (PRSA) for the business magazine, <em>The Falls Report</em>.</p>
<h4>School of Education</h4>
<p>The Anderson University School of Education offers licensing programs for elementary and secondary education in a variety of content areas. Dual licensing is also available in special education, teaching English as a New Language and TESOL certification, and middle school/junior high math. The school offers a graduate M.Ed. in Curriculum and Instruction, an M.Ed. in School Leadership and Administration (LEAD), and a Master of Music Education in conjunction with the School of Music. The school is accredited by the National Council for the Accreditation for Teacher Education (NCATE). The university's endowment was $20.8 million as of FY06. <h3>Athletics</h3>
<p> The <a>Anderson University Ravens</a> compete in athletics in the <a>NCAA</a> <a>Division III</a> and the <a>Heartland Collegiate Athletic Conference</a>. Men's sports offered at Anderson University include football, basketball, baseball, tennis, golf, soccer, cross country, and track &amp; field. Women's sports offered at Anderson University include basketball, softball, tennis, volleyball, soccer, golf, cross country, and track &amp; field. Formally the Tigers, AU's nickname was changed to the Ravens in 1937. The current mascot is <a>Rodney the Raven</a>.</p>
<h4>Hall of Fame</h4>
<p> The Anderson University Athletic Hall of Fame was started in 1997 with 10 inductees that year. Ten additional honorees were added in 1998. Four members have been inducted each year from 1999 to 2007. The names are nominees are submitted and given consideration by the Hall of Fame committee. The committee has 11 representatives and is headed by university's Athletic Director. The inductees are announced each Spring and formally inducted into the Hall of Fame at a banquet during the university's homecoming activities each Fall.</p>

<h2><em>&quot;Dreams. Discovery. Direction.&quot;</em> campaign</h2>
<p>At the beginning of the 2006-2007 school year, Anderson University announced the public phase of a $110 million capital campaign. The <em>&quot;Dreams. Discovery. Direction.&quot;</em> campaign is the university&#x2019;s largest fundraising effort to date. The campaign's goal is to raise support for endowment and scholarships, facilities, academic and student programs, and operational needs. An estimated $51 million is expected to be used for capital projects, $34 million for endowment and $25 million for operational support.  At the time of launch, the university announced that more than $61 million of the campaign goal had been received in gifts and commitments.  The campaign is set to conclude by 2011.</p>
