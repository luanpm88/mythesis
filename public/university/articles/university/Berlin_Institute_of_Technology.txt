<p>The <strong>Technische Universit&#x00e4;t Berlin</strong> (<strong>TUB</strong>; official transliteration <strong>Berlin Institute of Technology</strong>) is a research university located in <a>Berlin</a>, <a>Germany</a>. It was founded in 1879 and, with nearly 30,000 students, is one of the largest technical universities in Germany. It also has the highest proportion of foreign students out of universities in Germany, with 20.9% in the summer semester of 2007, roughly 5,598 students. The university alumni and professor list include <a>National Academies</a> elections, 2 <a>National Medal of Science</a> laurates and ten <a>Nobel Prize</a> winners.</p>
<p>The TU Berlin is a member of the <a>Top Industrial Managers for Europe</a> network, which allows for student exchanges between leading European engineering schools. It also belongs to the Conference of European Schools for Advanced Engineering Education and Research (<a>CESAER</a>). As of 2010, TU Berlin is ranked 48nd (2009: 62nd) in the world in the field of Engineering/Technology according to <a>Times Higher Education-QS World University Rankings</a>.. Top Study Links University Rankings 2010 ranks Technische Universit&#x00e4;t Berlin as the 7th best university in Germany. The university is ranked 137th in the World.</p>
<h2>History</h2>
<p><a>thumb|The old northern front of the main building, which was considerably damaged during the Second World War and replaced by a modern front in the 1960s</a></p>
<p>The institution was unified in 1879 under the name <em>Royal Technical College of Charlottenburg</em> (later Berlin) by merging the <em>Building Academy</em> (<a>Bauakademie</a>), established in 1799, and the <em>Vocational Academy</em>, established in 1829. Since 1916 it has been integrated with the former <em>Mining Academy</em>, which was the oldest institution, founded in 1770. The college was closed after <a>World War II</a> on April 20, 1945 and the university re-opened on April 9, 1946 under its current name.</p>
<h2>Campus</h2>
<p><a>thumb|South side of the main building (in winter)</a> <a>thumb|Main building (in summer)</a> <a>thumb|Telefunken-Hochhaus, the tallest building on campus, with a bird's-eye-view cafeteria on floor 20.</a> <a>thumb|Entrance of the main library of the Technical University of Berlin and of the Berlin University of the Arts</a></p>
<p>The TU Berlin covers ca. 600,000 m&#x00b2;, distributed over various locations in Berlin. The main campus is located in the <a>borough</a> of <a>Charlottenburg</a>. The seven schools of the university have some 28,200 students enrolled in more than 50 subjects (January, 2009).</p>
<h2>Organization</h2>
<p>Since April 4, 2005, the TU Berlin has consisted of the following schools:</p>
<ol>
  <li><a>Humanities</a></li>
  <li><a>Mathematics</a> and <a>Natural Sciences</a></li>
  <li><a>Process Sciences</a> and <a>Engineering</a></li>
  <li><a>Electrical Engineering</a> and <a>Computer Science</a></li>
  <li><a>Mechanical Engineering</a> and Transport Systems (including <a>Aerospace engineering</a>, <a>Automotive engineering</a>, naval and ocean engineering, and the planning and operation of transport systems)</li>
  <li>Planning - Building - Environment (merge of former schools of &quot;<a>Civil Engineering</a> and Applied <a>Geosciences</a>&quot; and &quot;<a>Architecture</a> - <a>Environment</a> - <a>Society</a>&quot;)</li>
  <li><a>Economics</a> and <a>Management</a></li>
</ol>
<h2>Faculty and staff</h2>
<p>7,601 people work at the university: 323 <a>professors</a>, 2,246 <a>postgraduate</a> <a>researchers</a>, and 2,078 personnel work in administration, the workshops, the library and the central facilities. In addition there are 2,301 student assistants and 142 <a>trainees</a> (March 2010).</p>
<p>International student mobility is applicable through <a>ERASMUS programme</a> or through <a>Top Industrial Managers for Europe</a> (TIME) network.</p>
<h2>Library</h2>
<p>The new common main library of the Technical University of Berlin and of the <a>Berlin University of the Arts</a> was opened in 2004 and holds about 2.9 million volumes (2007). The library building was sponsored partially (estimated 10% of the building costs) by <a>Volkswagen</a> and is named officially &quot;University Library of the TU Berlin and UdK (in the Volkswagen building)&quot;. Confusingly, the letters above the main entrance only state &quot;<a>Volkswagen Library</a>&quot; - without any mentioning of the universities. All former 17 libraries of the Technical University of Berlin and of the nearby University of the Arts were merged into the new library, but several departments still retain libraries of their own. In particular, the school of 'Economics and Management' maintains a library with 340,000 volumes in the university's main building (<em>Wirtschaftswissentschaftliche Dokumentation &#x2013; WiWiDok</em>).</p>
