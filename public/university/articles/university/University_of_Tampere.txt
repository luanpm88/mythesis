<p>  The <strong>University of Tampere</strong> () is a university in <a>Tampere</a>, <a>Finland</a>. It has 15,400 degree students and 2,100 employees. It was originally founded in 1925 in <a>Helsinki</a> as a Civic College (Kansalaiskorkeakoulu), and from 1930 onwards it was known as a School of Social Sciences (Yhteiskunnallinen korkeakoulu). In 1960, the institution relocated to Tampere, and in 1966 it was officially named University of Tampere.</p>
<p>The university is currently divided into nine schools:</p>
<ul>
  <li>Institute of Biomedical Technology</li>
  <li>School of Communication, Media and Theatre</li>
  <li>School of Education</li>
  <li>School of Health Sciences</li>
  <li>School of Humanities and Social Sciences</li>
  <li>School of Information sciences</li>
  <li>School of Languages, Translation Studies and Literary Studies</li>
  <li>School of Medicine</li>
  <li>School of Management</li>
</ul>
