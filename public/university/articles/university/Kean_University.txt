<p>  <strong>Kean University</strong> (pronounced &quot;cane&quot;), is a <a>public</a>, <a>coeducational</a> <a>university</a>, located in <a>Union Township</a>, <a>New Jersey</a>. It is a large, public <a>liberal arts</a> school, most known for its education program and graduating the most teachers in the state of New Jersey. It is also known for the physical therapy program which it holds in conjunction with <a>UMDNJ</a>, attracting students statewide.</p>
<p>Kean University was founded in <a>1855</a> in <a>Newark, New Jersey</a>, as the Newark Normal School. It was initially established for the exclusive purpose of being a teacher-education college.</p>
<p>In 1937, Kean became New Jersey State Teachers College at <a>Newark</a>. Then in 1958 the campus was physically moved from <a>Newark</a> to <a>Union Township</a>, site of the Kean family's ancestral home at <a>Liberty Hall</a>.</p>
<p>After taking on the name Kean College of New Jersey in 1973, the institution earning university status on September 26, 1997.</p>
<p>In 2009, the administration unveiled a major reorganization plan touted as the &quot;first step&quot; in privatizing the University. </p>
<h2>History</h2>
<p>The university was founded in 1855 in <a>Newark, New Jersey</a>, as the Newark Normal School. It was initially established for the exclusive purpose of being a teacher-education college.</p>
<p>In 1937, Kean underwent its first name change, becoming New Jersey State Teachers College at <a>Newark</a>. Then in 1958 the campus was physically moved from <a>Newark</a> to <a>Union Township</a>, site of the Kean family's ancestral home at <a>Liberty Hall</a>.</p>
<p>After taking on the name Kean College of New Jersey in 1973, the institution continued to broaden its scope over the next 24 years, earning university status on September 26, 1997.</li>

<h2>Campuses</h2>
<p>The main campus is located in <a>Union</a>. A smaller campus (East Campus) is located less than a mile away in <a>Hillside, New Jersey</a>, in the former <a>Pingry School</a>.</p>
<p>There are plans for a new campus in <a>Toms River, New Jersey</a>, called (Kean@Ocean) is currently in operation and is being housed at <a>Ocean County College</a> until the new campus is built.</p>
<p>Kean University had planned to open a campus in <a>Wenzhou</a>, in the <a>Zhejiang Province</a> of the <a>People's Republic of China</a> in 2007. <a>Kean University-Wenzhou</a> would have been China&#x2019;s first full-scale American-style university, but the plans became caught up in red tape in the Beijing government.</p>

<h2>Athletics, Clubs, and Arts</h2>
<p>Kean runs an <a>NCAA</a> <a>Division III</a> sports program, with national rankings in soccer, baseball and women's basketball. The Mascot is the Cougar, and the school colors are navy blue, baby blue, and white.  On May 29, 2007 Kean University won their first Division III College World Series, winning the national title in baseball, defeating Emory University by a score of 5-4 in 10 innings.</p>

<h3>Kean Alumni Stadium</h3>
<p>Located on the main campus,  Kean Alumni Stadium is a  multipurpose athletic facility that serves as home for the University's  football, field hockey, men&#x2019;s and women&#x2019;s soccer, men's and women's lacrosse, and men's and women's track and field teams.</p>
<p>The stadium, which seats 5,400 people, features a Sprinturf field as well as an eight-lane synthetic surface track, was constructed in 1998, on the site formerly known as Zweidinger Field.</p>
<h3>Harwood Arena</h3>
<p>The Harwood Arena opened in 2006. It features a basketball court, indoor track, Athletic Hall of Fame, concession stand, ticket sales, locker rooms, and faculty and staff offices. At gametime, bleacher seating is available to 3,200 Cougar fans.</p>
