<p><strong>Northwestern College</strong> is a private Christian college located in <a>Roseville, Minnesota</a>, and is <a>accredited</a> by the <a>North Central Association of Colleges and Schools</a>. Established in 1902 as <strong>Northwestern Bible and Missionary Training School</strong> by Dr. <a>William Bell Riley</a>, a <a>pastor</a> at the <a>First Baptist Church of Minneapolis</a>, Northwestern is the only non-denominational Christian <a>private college</a> in the state of <a>Minnesota</a>.</p>
<p><a>Billy Graham</a> served as the school's second president from 1948 to 1952.</p>
<h2>Mission statement</h2>
<p>&quot;Northwestern College exists to provide Christ-centered higher education equipping students to grow intellectually and spiritually, to serve effectively in their professions, and to give God-honoring leadership in the home, church, community and world.&quot;</p>
<h2>Undergraduate and Graduate Programs</h2>
<p>Northwestern offers 55 <a>bachelor's degrees</a>, 11 of which are through its  <a>FOCUS Degree Completion Program</a>. Northwestern offers 2 <a>master's degrees</a>. Areas of undergraduate study offered includes elementary and secondary education, <a>music</a>, <a>business</a>, <a>psychology</a>, <a>broadcasting</a>/<a>electronic media</a>, <a>art</a>, <a>English</a>, mathematics, life sciences, Biblical studies, and Christian ministries. Areas of graduate study includes organizational leadership and theological studies. In addition to a mandatory daily <a>chapel</a> program featuring local, national, and international speakers, students are required to earn a minor in <a>Bible</a> by taking 30 credits of Bible classes throughout their college career.</p>
<h2>Alternative Education Programs</h2>
<p>The <a>FOCUS Degree Completion Program</a> offers an <a>Associate of Arts</a> degree as well as degree-completion programs in <a>business</a>, <a>psychology</a>, <a>ministries</a>, and communication studies.</p>

<h2>Science and Mathematics</h2>
<p>Northwestern offers  accredited bachelor of science degrees in traditional and applied mathematics, mathematics education, biology, biochemistry, and engineering sciences.</p>
<p>Northwestern also has a cooperative dual-degree program with the University of Minnesota's Institute of Technology. In this program, students complete three years of study (on average) at Northwestern in either applied mathematics or engineering sciences. Students then transfer to the University of Minnesota to complete the requirements for a focused engineering degree.</p>
<h2>Media</h2>
<p>The campus houses the <a>KTIS</a> and <a>KTIS-FM</a> <a>radio stations</a>, broadcasting <a>contemporary Christian music</a> and programming to the <a>Twin Cities</a> area. In addition to KTIS, which began broadcasting in 1949, Northwestern College owns and operates media outlets in 6 other cities: <a>KFNW AM</a>, <a>KFNW-FM</a>, and <a>KFNL</a> in <a>Fargo, ND</a>; <a>KDNW</a> and <a>KDNI</a> in <a>Duluth, MN</a>; <a>WNWC-FM</a> and <a>AM</a> in <a>Madison, WI</a>; <a>KNWS-FM</a> and <a>AM</a> in <a>Waterloo, IA</a>; <a>KNWI</a> and <a>KNWM</a> in <a>Des Moines, IA</a>; and <a>KNWC</a> in <a>Sioux Falls, SD</a>.</p>
<p>Until July 2010, Northwestern College also owned <a>WSMR</a> in <a>Sarasota, Florida</a>; due to financial troubles, this station would close down and be sold to the <a>University of South Florida</a>, who reformatted the station with a classical music format.</p>
<p>The mission statement for Northwestern's media ministry is: &quot;The purpose of Northwestern Media is to lead people to Christ and to nurture them in their spiritual growth through Christ Centered media!&quot;</p>
<p>In addition to KTIS, Northwestern College operates a student radio station, <a>WVOE</a>, and a student television station, <a>NWC7</a>. WVOE is a station that broadcasts on FM exclusively to the surrounding campus as well as a live Internet stream. WVOE was named the 2009 National Religious Broadcasters iNRB College Radio Station of the Year award.</p>
<h2>Music</h2>
<p>Music at Northwestern has a long and proud heritage of serving the college and the greater Twin Cities area through its preparation of students to serve in their homes, churches, and communities through Christian leadership. Ensembles include nationally renowned College Choir and Symphony Orchestra, as well as Jazz Band, Symphonic Band, Men's Chorus, Women's Chorale, and numerous chamber ensembles. The Music Department is fully accredited by the National Association of Schools of Music. Majors include Music Education, Music Performance, Music Composition and General Music. A music minor is available to those who want a concentration of coursework in music but have other educational goals.</p>
<h2>Athletics</h2>
<p>Northwestern sponsors 18 intercollegiate athletic sports, including football, women's volleyball, men's and women's soccer, men's and women's cross country, men's and women's golf, men's and women's basketball, baseball, softball, men's and women's indoor and outdoor track and men's and women's tennis.</p>
<p>The Eagles are one of eight full time members of the <a>Upper Midwest Athletic Conference</a> (UMAC), and are members of the <a>National Collegiate Athletic Association</a> (NCAA) <a>Division III</a>. The school is also a member of the <a>National Christian College Athletic Association</a>.</p>
<p>On October 8, 2005, Northwestern's <a>football</a> team became the first college team in modern history to play two games in the same day. They first played <a>Trinity Bible College</a> at home in the early afternoon, winning 59-0. After a two-hour break, the team boarded a bus for the 6.5-mile (10.5&nbsp;km) drive to <a>Macalester College</a> for a 7:00 p.m. (0000 UTC, 9 October) game, which the Eagles won 47-14. [<a>http://sports.yahoo.com/ncaaf/news?slug=ap-onelongday&amp;prov=ap&amp;type=lgns</a>]</p>
<p>The Northwestern men's basketball team won the 2010 <a>NCCAA</a> Division I Men's Basketball National Tournament, defeating <a>King College</a> (Tenn.), for its first ever championship by a score of 58-54.[<a>http://www.nwceagles.com/news/2010/3/20/MBB_0321100010.aspx</a>]</p>
