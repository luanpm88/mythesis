<p><strong>Broome Community College</strong> is a <a>SUNY</a> two-year college in <a>Broome County, New York</a>. The college was founded in 1946 and went through several name changes. The school is located in <a>Binghamton, New York</a>. The college had a 2010 enrollment of over 6,000 students and has alumni of over 41,000.</p>
<p><strong>BCC</strong> serves students from a single campus on Upper Front Street in <a>Binghamton, New York</a>, though some classes are taught in <a>Owego, New York</a> at a small classroom center. The campus' 15 buildings comprise  of space, and feature recently upgraded athletic facilitites such as baseball fields, soccer and lacrosse field, publicly accessible tennis courts, the Dick Baldwin Gym, named after the third winningest <a>college basketball</a> coach across both two and four year colleges, and a new ice rink. There is also a theater which hosts campus performances of plays and other theatrical work, entitled <strong>The Little Theater</strong>.</p>
<p>It offers a variety of classes to all students that plan on finishing their 2 year degree at <strong>BCC</strong> or hope to transfer to a 4 year school. Most Broome Community College students transfer to Binghamton University after their first 2 years.</p>

<h2>Timeline</h2>
<ul>
  <li>1946. Established as <strong>New York State Institute of Applied Arts &amp; Sciences at Binghamton</strong> (with &quot;New York State&quot; sometimes abbreviated &quot;NYS&quot;).</li>
  <li>1953. Became <strong>Broome County Technical Institute</strong>.</li>
  <li>1956. Became <strong>Broome Technical Community College</strong>.</li>
  <li>1957. Moved to new campus on Upper Front Street (NY Route 11).</li>
  <li>1971. Name changed to <strong>Broome Community College</strong>.</li>
</ul>
<h2>Athletics</h2>
<p>The Broome Community College Hornets participate in the NJCAA as a member of Region III. Broome field's 15 Varsity Sports: Men's Baseball Men's Basketball Men's Cross Country Men's Golf Men's Ice Hockey Men's Lacrosse Men's Soccer Men's Tennis Women's Basketball Women's Cross Country Women's Lacrosse Women's Soccer Women's Softball Women's Tennis Women's Volleyball</p>
<p>The Women's Soccer Team won the National Championship in 2007 and 2008.</p>
