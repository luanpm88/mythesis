<p><strong>Northern College of Applied Arts and Technology</strong> is a <a>community college</a> in <a>Northern Ontario</a>. The College's catchment area extends across  or 160,000 square kilometres. More than 65 communities within Northeastern Ontario are served by four campuses located in <a>Timmins</a> (Porcupine), <a>Kirkland Lake</a>, <a>Moosonee</a>, and <a>Haileybury</a>. Annual enrolment is approximately 1,500 full-time students. Annual part-time and continuing education enrolment exceeds 11,000 students. Northern College is also home to the world-renowned <strong>Haileybury School of Mines</strong>.</p>
<h2>Mission and Vision</h2>
<p>Northern College Vision:  Success for our northern communities through learning and partnerships.</p>
<p>Northern College Mission:  To work with all of our communities to ensure quality, accessible education through innovative programs, services and partnerships.</p>
<p>Northern College Motto:  Your college.  Your community.  </p>
<h2>Programs</h2>
<p>Northern offers certificate and diploma programs in Business, Office Administration, Computer Studies, Engineering Technology and Trades, Health Sciences and Emergency Services, Human Services, Mining Engineering Technology, Instrumentation, Natural Resources, Police Sciences, Veterinary Sciences, and Welding Engineering Technology.</p>
<p>The College also offers pre-apprenticeship, apprenticeship, and co-op apprenticeship programs, as well as a Bachelor of Science- Nursing degree program in collaboration with Laurentian University. Aboriginal programs and services are also available. </p>
<ul>
  <li>There are 23 certificate, 29 diploma programs, 1 Collaborative Degree and 18 Apprenticeship programs. </li>
</ul>
<p>The full time programs are: Academic Upgrading, Aboriginal Programs, Business &amp; Office Administration, Computer Studies,  Engineering, Technology &amp; Trades, Health Sciences &amp; Emergency Services, Human Services, Mining Engineering Technology, Natural Resources, Police Services, Veterinary Sciences, and Welding Engineering Technology.</p>
<p>The Apprenticeship Programs are: Aboriginal Early Childhood Education, Construction &amp; Maintenance Electrician,  Construction Craft Worker, Educational Assistant, Native Residential Construction Worker, and Welder.</p>
<p>Northern College of Applied Arts and Technology, in cooperation with several other colleges, provides Correspondence Courses and Web-Based Learning.</p>

<h2>History</h2>
<p>Northern College was established during the formation of Ontario&#x2019;s college system in 1967.  Colleges of Applied Arts and Technology were established on May 21, 1965. It is an <a>Ontario College of Applied Arts and Technology</a>. The school was founded in 1967 as part of a provincial initiative to create <a>many such institutions</a> to provide career-oriented <a>diploma</a> and <a>certificate</a> courses, as well as <a>continuing education</a> programs to <a>Ontario</a> communities.</p>
<p>The first campus was built in <a>Kirkland Lake</a>, followed by <a>Porcupine</a>, <a>Haileybury</a>, and <a>Moosonee</a>. The Northern College <a>Tartan</a> associated with the Northern College of Applied Arts and Technology, was designed by Sylvia Martin. </p>
