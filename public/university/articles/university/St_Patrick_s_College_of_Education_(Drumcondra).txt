<p>  <strong>St Patrick's College of Education</strong> () is a linked college of <a>Dublin City University</a> since 1993, located in <a>Drumcondra</a>, <a>Dublin City</a>, <a>Ireland</a>. The college is in existence since 1875 and has a <a>Roman Catholic</a> ethos. It is the largest primary teacher training college in Ireland. Currently over 2,000 students are attending the college. St Patrick's College of Education was a recognised colleges of the <a>National University of Ireland</a> from 1975 to 1995. </p>
<p>The college offers a number of <a>undergraduate</a> courses, primarily in primary education and arts, and <a>postgraduate</a> courses, primarily in education and languages.</p>
<p>Notable buildings on the campus include Belvedere House whuch was built c.1640.</p>
<p>Residents of the house included:</p>
<ul>
  <li>Marmaduke Coghill (former British Chancellor of the Exchequer)</li>
  <li>Lord Singleton (MP for Drogheda)</li>
  <li>Lords Bowe and Lifford (Lords Chancellor)</li>
  <li>Lord Rokeby</li>
</ul>
<h2>Erins Hope</h2>
<p>St Pats are invited to enter the <a>Dublin Senior Football Championship</a> under the name Erins Hope. Erins Hope have been Dublin football champions on four occasions in 1887, 1932, 1956 and 1978. In 2007 Erins Hope failed to field a team for the Dublin championship and had to withdraw from the tournament.</p>
