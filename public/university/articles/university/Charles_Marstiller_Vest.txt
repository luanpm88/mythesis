<p> <strong>Charles &quot;Chuck&quot; Marstiller Vest</strong> (born September 9, 1941) is a <a>U.S.</a> educator and engineer. He served as President of <a>MIT</a> from <a>1990</a> until December, <a>2004</a>. He was succeeded as President by <a>Susan Hockfield</a>. On February 6, 2004, he was appointed to the <a>Iraq Intelligence Commission</a> by <a>President</a> <a>George W. Bush</a>.  He was later appointed president of the <a>National Academy of Engineering</a>.</p>
<p>Prior to his ascension to the MIT presidency, he served as the provost and professor of mechanical engineering at the <a>University of Michigan</a>. He is a graduate of <a>West Virginia University</a>.</p>
<p><a>Cambridge University</a> awarded him an <a>Honorary</a> <a>Doctorate in Law</a> in 2006. Harvard University has also given him an <a>Honorary</a> in 2005.</p>
<p>Vest is also a member of the <a>USA Science and Engineering Festival</a>'s Advisory Board.</p>
