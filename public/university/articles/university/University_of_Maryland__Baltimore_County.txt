<p>The <strong>University of Maryland, Baltimore County</strong>&lt;!-- the name has been discussed thoroughly, please check the talk page and the following citation before attempting to change it --&gt; (often referred to as <strong>UMBC</strong>) is an American honors public research university, located in <a>Baltimore County, Maryland</a>, mostly in the community of <a>Catonsville, Maryland</a>. Established as a part of the <a>University System of Maryland</a> in 1966, the university specializes in the natural sciences and engineering, while also offering programs in the liberal arts.</li>
  <li><strong>The College of Engineering and Information Technology</strong> offers different areas of study in five departments: the Departments of Chemical and Biochemical Engineering, Civil and Environmental Engineering, Computer Science and Electrical Engineering, Information Systems, and Mechanical Engineering.</li>
  <li><strong>The College of Arts, Humanities and Social Sciences</strong> houses the most departments in the university and awards more than half of all undergraduate and graduate degrees.  Among many others, it includes the departments of Political Science, History, Music, Visual Arts, and Sociology.  Many of the departments will be soon housed in the new Performing Arts and Humanities Building.  Groundbreaking is scheduled for September 2010.</li>
</ul>
<h3>Schools</h3>
<ul>
  <li><strong>The Erickson School of Aging</strong> offers undergraduate and graduate level programs that focuses on various aspects of aging studies, including policy and management issues.</li>
  <li><strong>The School of Social Work</strong> links with the University of Maryland, Baltimore's (UMB) School of Social work in offering undergraduate and graduate level programs.</li>
</ul>
<h3>Student Profile</h3>
<p>The average cumulative <a>GPA</a> of all undergraduate students in the Spring 2009 semester was 2.87.</p>
<h2>Campus</h2>
<h3>Location</h3>
<p>UMBC's campus is located on 500 acres.  It is 15 minutes from Baltimore's Inner Harbor and 45 minutes from Washington, D.C. Baltimore Washington International Airport is five minutes away, as are AMTRAK and light rail stations.</p>
<h3>Research and Technology Park</h3>
<p>bwtech@UMBC Research and Technology Park is a 71-acre development on the campus hosting technology, bioscience and research organizations, many of which are engaged in partnership with the University.   Research Park tenants include the <a>US Geological Survey</a>,  RWD Technologies, <a>US Forestry Service</a>, CardioMed Device Consultants, Audacious Inquiry, Med-IQ, Physician Practice, Inc., <a>Retirement Living TV</a>, Ascentium Corporation, Solvern Innovations, RMF Engineering, Inc., Convergent Technology, Clear Resolution Consulting, and Goddard Earth Science and Technology Center.</p>
<h3>Campus police</h3>
<p>UMBC police staff include sworn police officers, just like campus police forces of other universities.  Unlike the campus police of the <a>University of Maryland, College Park</a> and the <a>University of Maryland, Baltimore</a>, the campus police are not accredited by the <a>Commission on Accreditation for Law Enforcement Agencies</a>.  Accreditation is expected by the <a>University System of Maryland</a> mandated 2013 deadline. </p>
<h2>Student life</h2>
<p></p>
<p>The Resident Student Association and Student Events Board provide social programming during all academic semesters at UMBC. Over 100 student-run organizations exist on campus.</p>
<h3>Residential life</h3>
<p>There are ten housing areas housing approximately 4,500 students.</p>
<h2>Athletics</h2>
<p> <a>thumb|right|200px|UMBC Retrievers Logo</a> The school's sports teams are called the <a>Retrievers</a>, with colors black and gold. The mascot of the University is the <a>Chesapeake Bay Retriever</a>, the state dog of <a>Maryland</a>, named <a>True Grit</a>. There is a statue of True Grit that stands in front of the <a>Retriever Activities Center</a> (RAC). The Retrievers participate in <a>NCAA</a> Division 1 as a part of the <a>America East Conference</a>, fielding 17 varsity sports; eight men and nine women. The Retrievers <a>fight song</a> is the UMBC Riser, and was written by Dr. George LaNoue, a professor of policy sciences.</p>
<p>In 2009, the <a>men's lacrosse</a> team secured their fifth consecutive outright or shared America East regular season championship and their third America East tournament championship in four years. UMBC has secured a berth in the <a>NCAA tournament</a> each of the past four seasons. In 2007, the unseeded Retrievers upset seventh-seeded <a>Maryland</a>, 13&#x2013;9, in the <a>NCAA tournament</a> to advance to the Division I second round for the first, and so far only, time in school history.</p>
<p>The Retrievers won their first regular season American East Men's Basketball title in 2007-08, and also qualified for their first NCAA Division I Men's Basketball Tournament. They had previously competed in the Division II men's basketball tournament.</p>
<p>The Retrievers, Men's Swimming and Diving Program captured their 13th straight conference championship in the 2009-2010 Season.  Since Joining the America East Conference in 2004, the Retriever Men have not lost their crown.</p>
<p>In May 2010, the UMBC Athletic Department unveiled a new logo for the Retrievers.</p>
<h2>Financial aid and scholarships</h2>
<p>The <a>Meyerhoff Scholarship Program</a> at UMBC is a program focused on the cultivation of underrepresented minority scholarship and awareness in the math, science, and engineering disciplines. Other scholarship programs include the <a>CWIT Scholars Program</a>, the Humanities Scholars Program, the Linehan Artist Scholars Program, the Sondheim Public Affairs Scholars Program and the Sherman Teacher Education Scholars Program.</p>
<h2>Rankings</h2>
<p></p>
<p><a>The Carnegie Foundation</a> classifies UMBC as a research university with high research activity.  An overall ranking by <a>U.S. News</a> placed this university first in its 2011 list of <em>Up and Coming</em> schools.</p>
