<p><strong>Tel Aviv University</strong> (<strong>TAU</strong>) ( <em>Universitat Tel Aviv</em>) is a public <a>university</a> located in <a>Ramat Aviv</a>, <a>Israel</a>. In 2006, it had 29,000 students. </p>
<h2>History</h2>
<p>Located in Israel's cultural, financial and industrial center, Tel Aviv University is Israel's largest university. It is a major center of teaching and research, comprising nine faculties, 106 departments, and 90 research institutes. Its origins go back to 1956, when three research institutes - the Tel Aviv School of Law and Economics, the Institute of Natural Sciences, and the Institute of Jewish Studies - joined together to form the University of Tel Aviv. Initially operated by the Tel Aviv municipality, the university was granted autonomy in 1963. The Ramat Aviv campus covering an area of  was established that same year.</p>
<p>The university also maintains academic supervision over the Center for Technological Design in Holon, the New Academic College of Tel Aviv-Yaffo, and the <a>Afeka College of Engineering</a> in Tel Aviv. The <a>Wise Observatory</a> is located in <a>Mitzpe Ramon</a>.</p>
<h2>Rankings</h2>
<p>In 2010 <a>QS World University Rankings</a> ranked Tel Aviv University 138th in the world, making it the second highest ranked university in Israel. Its subject rankings were: 184th in Arts &amp; Humanities, 175th in Engineering &amp; IT, 226th in Life Sciences &amp; Biomedicine, 75th in Natural Sciences, and 121st in Social Sciences. </p>

<h2>Relations with other universities</h2>
<p><a>thumb|250px|Smolarz Auditorium</a> &lt;!-- Deleted image removed: <a>thumb|200px|Fastlicht Lecture Hall at Edificio De Mexico</a> --&gt; Tel Aviv University offers special programs of Jewish studies to teachers and students from the <a>United States</a>, <a>France</a>, <a>Brazil</a>, <a>Argentina</a> and <a>Mexico</a>. The programs are in English.</p>
<p>The Tel Aviv University Law Faculty currently has exchange programs from thirteen overseas universities. Namely: Michigan, Northwestern, Penn, Virginia, Cornell, Boston University, Temple, Cardozo, Toronto, Bucerius (Hamburg), Monash (Melbourne), Milan, and Seoul National University  </p>
<h2>School for Overseas Students</h2>
<p>The School for Overseas Students gives young people from different countries the opportunity to study at Tel Aviv University. The program is in <a>English</a> and also offers the opportunity to live and study in a <a>kibbutz</a>. The university offers about 20 courses a year in English, recruiting many top lecturers from overseas to teach.</p>
<p>Programs include Semester or Year Abroad, Degree Programs, and Specialized Programs.</p>
<p>Graduate programs include:</p>
<ul>
  <li>Middle Eastern History</li>
  <li>TESOL - Teaching English to Speakers of Other Languages</li>
  <li>Conflict Resolution and Mediation</li>
  <li>M.A. in Social Work with specialization in Crisis and Trauma Studies</li>
  <li>M.A. in Security and Diplomacy</li>
  <li>M.A in Jewish Studies</li>

  <li>M.A. in Environmental Studies</li>

<p>In May 2007, <a>New York University</a> and Tel Aviv University approved a plan to establish an NYU Study Abroad Campus in Israel based at Tel Aviv University.</p>
