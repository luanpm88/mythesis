<p> </p>
<p>The <strong>University of Geneva</strong> () is a public research university located in <a>Geneva</a>, <a>Switzerland</a>.</p>
<p>It was founded in 1559 by <a>John Calvin</a> as a <a>theological</a> <a>seminary</a> and <a>law school</a>. It remained focused on theology until the 17th century, when it became a center for <a>Enlightenment</a> scholarship. In 1873 it dropped its religious affiliations and became officially secular. Today, the university is the second-largest university in Switzerland. It has programs in various fields but is particularly acknowledged for its academic and research programs in <a>international relations</a> (with Geneva being hostess to a dense agglomeration of <a>international organization</a>s), <a>law</a>, <a>astrophysics</a>, <a>astronomy</a>, <a>genetics</a> (with a record of prominent contributions to the fields of <a>planetary science</a> and <a>genetics</a>) and <a>theology</a>.</p>
<p>Classes are taught mainly in <a>French</a>. The university holds and actively pursues teaching, <a>research</a>, and community service as its primary objectives. It was ranked number one generalist university in <a>continental Europe</a>, thirty-second world wide amongst the &quot;Top 100 Global Universities&quot; by <em><a>Newsweek</a></em> in 2006 and 81-83rd worldwide by the <em><a>Global University Ranking</a></em> in 2009. The university is a member of the <a>League of European Research Universities</a>.</p>
<p>In 2009, the University of Geneva celebrated the 450th anniversary of its founding through a wide range of <a>public events</a>.</p>
<h2>Degree system</h2>
<p>Before 2005, the University applied the <a>French education model</a> of granting <a>academic degree</a>s, with some minor differences: <em>demi-licence</em> (two years), <em><a>licence</a></em> (four years), <a><em>dipl&#x00f4;me d'&#x00e9;tudes approfondies</em> and <em>dipl&#x00f4;me d'&#x00e9;tudes superieures sp&#x00e9;cialis&#x00e9;es</em></a> (DEA/DESS) (1&#x2013;2 years), and <a>doctorate</a> (3&#x2013;5 years). The University now follows the requirements of the <a>Bologna process</a>: <a>bachelor's</a> (three years), <a>master's</a> (1&#x2013;2 years), Master of Advanced Studies (1&#x2013;2 years), doctorate (3&#x2013;5 years).</p>
<h2>Organization</h2>
<p><a>thumb|250px|right|Uni Mail</a> <a>thumb|250px|right|Uni Dufour</a></p>
<p>The University is composed of eight <a>faculties</a>:</p>
<ul>
  <li>Faculty of Sciences (<a>natural science</a>s)</li>
  <li>Faculty of Medicine (<a>medical school</a>)</li>
  <li>Faculty of Arts (<a>arts</a>)</li>
  <li>Faculty of Economics and Social Sciences with studies including <a>business school</a> (<a>Haute &#x00c9;cole Commerciale</a> - HEC), <a>economics</a>, <a>sociology</a> and <a>international relations</a>)</li>
  <li>Faculty of Law (Geneva Law School) (<a>law school</a>)</li>
  <li>Faculty of Protestant Theology (<a>Protestant</a> <a>theological school</a>)</li>
  <li>Faculty of Psychology and Educational Sciences (<a>psychology</a> and <a>education</a>)</li>
  <li>School of Translation and Interpretation (<a>translating</a> and <a>interpreting</a>)</li>
</ul>
<p>The university has also developed a <a>continuing education</a> programme. The university has a partnership with the nearby <a>Graduate Institute of International and Development Studies</a> and the <a>Bossey Ecumenical Institute</a>, and students at the university may take courses at these institutes.</p>
<p>Inter-faculty centers:</p>
<ul>
  <li>Institute for Reformation History (the <a>Reformation</a>)</li>
  <li>Computer Science Department (<a>computer science</a>)</li>
  <li>University Centre for Study of Energy Problems (<a>energy policy</a>)</li>
  <li>The European Institute of the University of Geneva (<a>European studies</a>, <a>European integration</a>)</li>
  <li>Interfaculty Center of Gerontology (<a>gerontology</a>)</li>
  <li><a>Swiss Center for Affective Sciences</a> (<a>affective science</a>)</li>
  <li><a>Center for Environmental Studies</a></li>
  <li><a>Geneva Finance Research Institute</a></li>
</ul>
