Serving over 90,000 students worldwide, UMUC is the largest 4-year public university in Maryland and one of the largest <a>distance learning</a> universities.
Like UMD, UMUC is a member of the <a>University System of Maryland</a>, which includes eleven separate public universities in Maryland.</p>
In 1970, UMUC became an independent institution, but in 1988, it became a part of the <a>University System of Maryland</a>.</p>
<p>Currently, UMUC offers courses and services at the four main Army Education Centers, located in Bagram Air Field, Kabul (Camp Phoenix), Salerno and Kandahar.
<p>Faculty members assigned to a downrange location teach servicemembers wherever they may be.
UMUC divisional headquarters are located in <a>Heidelberg, Germany</a> near <a>United States Army Garrison Heidelberg</a> and on <a>Yokota Air Base</a>, <a>Tokyo, Japan</a>, respectively.</p>
