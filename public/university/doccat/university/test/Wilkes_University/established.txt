The campus symbol is a letter &quot;W&quot; known as the &quot;flying W&quot; by students and alumni.</p>
<h2>History and Expansion</h2>
<p> Wilkes University was first established in 1933 by <a>Bucknell University</a> under the name &quot;Bucknell Junior College&quot; (BUJC) in <a>Wilkes-Barre</a>.
The Junior College also received support from leading members of the <a>Wilkes-Barre</a> community.</p>
<p>In 1947, Wilkes College was instituted as an independent, nondenominational four-year college, with programs in the arts, sciences, and a number of professional fields as well as numerous extracurricular activities.
Wilkes was given university status in 1990.</p>
Wilkes College opened the School of Pharmacy in 1996, and in 1999, through a donation from Mrs. <a>Geraldine Nesbitt Orr</a>, the <a>Nesbitt School of Pharmacy</a> at Wilkes University was established.</p>
<p>The Jay S. Sidhu School of Business &amp; Leadership was created in 2004.
<p>In 2005, the Henry Student Center was expanded and the University purchased an  building in downtown Wilkes-Barre.
This building serves as the Wilkes University communications center, housing the registrar, bursar, <a>indoor track</a> and field, and <a>ropes course</a>.</p>

<h3>University Towers</h3>
<p>The university purchased the University Towers apartment complex located at 10 East South Street from a private real estate company for $8.1 million.
