<p><strong>Norfolk State University</strong> (NSU) is a four-year, state-supported, coed, liberal arts, <a>historically black university</a> located in <a>Norfolk</a>, <a>Virginia</a>.
It is member school of the <a>Thurgood Marshall Scholarship Fund</a> and the <a>Virginia High-Tech Partnership</a>.</p>
<p>The Commission on Colleges of the <a>Southern Association of Colleges and Schools</a> has accredited Norfolk State to award associate, baccalaureate, master and doctoral degrees.

<h2>History</h2>
<p> The institution was founded in 1935 as the Norfolk Unit of <a>Virginia Union University</a> (VUU).
Eighty-five students attended the first classes held in 1935.
Mr. Samuel Fischer Scott, an alumnus of Virginia Union and <a>Portsmouth</a> native, served as the first director with the primary focus of maintaining the solvency of the school.
Dr. Lyman Beecher Brooks, a Virginia Union alumnus, succeeded Mr. Scott as director in 1938, and served as provost, 1963-1969, and the first president 1969-1975.
<p>In 1942, the school became independent of VUU and was named Norfolk Polytechnic College.
The Joseph G. Echols Memorial Hall is a large health, physical education, and ROTC complex with a seating capacity of 7,500.
<p>Work on a new library, currently scheduled to open in 2011, is underway.
Other recent construction on the campus includes the new police station (2006), the Marie V. McDemmond Center for Applied Research (2006), and the Spartan Suites Apartments (2005).</p>
They were featured performers in the <a>Honda Battle of the Bands</a> in 2007 and 2008.</p>
<p>All nine of the <a>National Pan-Hellenic Council</a> organizations currently have chapters at Norfolk State University.
The paper is available in a print (available twice a month) and <a>online</a> (updated daily).
The online edition was awarded Best Online Site and Best Online Video by the <a>Black College Communication Association</a> in 2009.</p>
<p> Norfolk State operates <a>WNSB(FM)</a> radio, which broadcasts in stereo 24 hours a day from the campus and covers all of the <a>Hampton Roads, Virginia</a> area, reaching the Eastern Shore of Virginia, northeast North Carolina and the <a>Richmond, Virginia</a> suburbs.
Norfolk State was formerly a member of the Eastern Intercollegiate Conference (1953&#x2013;1960) and the <a>Central Intercollegiate Athletic Association</a> (1962&#x2013;1996).
