<p> <strong>University of Detroit Mercy</strong> (UDM) is a private, <a>Roman Catholic</a> co-educational <a>university</a> in <a>Detroit</a>, <a>Michigan</a>, <a>United States</a>, affiliated with the <a>Society of Jesus</a> (the Jesuits) and the <a>Sisters of Mercy</a>.
Gerard Stockhausen, S.J., President from 2004&#x2013;2010 has been promoted to the Jesuit Conference in Washington, D.C.)  With origins dating from 1877, it is the largest <a>Roman Catholic</a> university in Michigan.
Located across three campuses in Detroit, the school offers more than a hundred academic degrees and programs of study, including <a>liberal arts</a>, <a>clinical psychology</a>, business, <a>dentistry</a>, law, <a>engineering</a>, <a>architecture</a>, nursing and allied health professions.</p>
Athletically, the University sponsors 19 <a>NCAA</a> <a>Division I</a> level varsity sports for men and women, and is a member of the <a>Horizon League</a>.
UDM was the host institution for the <a>2009 NCAA men's basketball Final Four and championship</a>.</p>
<h2>History</h2>
<p>The University of Detroit Mercy's origin dates back to 1877 with the founding of <strong>Detroit College</strong> by the <a>Society of Jesus</a>.
The college expanded into the <strong>University of Detroit</strong>, and in 1927 established a second campus.
In 1941, the <a>Sisters of Mercy</a> opened the <strong>Mercy College of Detroit</strong>.
In 1990, University of Detroit and Mercy College of Detroit consolidated to become &quot;University of Detroit Mercy&quot;.</p>
<p>Through its previous name as the &quot;University of Detroit&quot;, the <a>Roman Catholic</a> purpose of the university was not always recognized by the general public.</p>
<p>University of Detroit Mercy's mission and vision statements reflect the traditions of its religious sponsors.
founded on their collective <a>religious</a> traditions.</p>
<p>The University of Detroit Mercy, a Catholic university in the Jesuit and Mercy traditions, exists to provide excellent student-centered undergraduate and graduate education in an urban context.
A UDM education seeks to integrate the intellectual, spiritual, ethical and social development of our students.</p>
<p>The University of Detroit Mercy will be recognized as a premier private university in the Great Lakes (North America) region, distinguished by graduates who lead and serve in their communities.</p>
<p>Like all <a>universities</a>, UDM strives to offer quality higher education, but it also is committed to service of <a>faith</a> and promotion of <a>justice</a> and compassionate service to persons in need, as articulated in the Jesuit <a>worldview</a>.
Accordingly, the university, its students, staff and <a>alumni</a> aim to take an active role in strengthening <a>Detroit</a>, through community outreach and partnership with like-minded organizations.</p>

UDM comprises seven colleges and schools: the School of <a>Architecture</a>, College of <a>Business Administration</a>, School of Dentistry, College of Engineering and Science, College of Health Professions/McAuley School of Nursing, School of <a>Law</a>, and College of <a>Liberal Arts</a> and Education.</p>
The McNichols Campus is located on the southeast corner of McNichols Road and Livernois Avenue in northwest Detroit (near the Pilgrim Village and University District neighborhoods).
<p>The Riverfront Campus is home to UDM's School of <a>Law</a> in downtown Detroit at 651 East Jefferson across from the Renaissance Center.</p>
<p>The Corktown Campus, at 2700 Martin Luther King Jr. Boulevard, houses UDM's School of <a>Dentistry</a> and Dental Clinic.
The men's basketball team has consistently contended for the Horizon League title.
On April 12, 2008, UDM announced the hiring of Ray McCallum as Men's Basketball Coach.
McCallum is a veteran of more than 20 years in college basketball, most recently as assistant head coach at Indiana University.</p>
<p>McCallum's predecessor <a>Perry Watson</a> led a successful program at Detroit's <a>Southwestern High School</a> before coming to UDM after some years as an assistant coach at the <a>University of Michigan</a> and maintained strong recruiting ties within the city's public league.
<p><a>Dick Vitale</a>, ESPN's most well-known college basketball commentator, was the University of Detroit men's basketball Head Coach for four seasons (1973&#x2013;1977) before becoming the school's Athletics Director for 1977-78.
The following year he left to coach the <a>Detroit Pistons</a>.
In his final year as a college head coach in 1977, &quot;Dickie V.&quot; led the Detroit Titans to a school record 25 victories and the Round of 16 in the <a>1977 NCAA Tournament</a> before losing to Michigan, 86-81.
Vitale went on to coach the Detroit Pistons before beginning his broadcasting career with ESPN in 1979 and was the color commentator for the first college basketball game carried by the new network.
As its lead college basketball analyst, he helped make the network an integral part of the game's popularity.
An author of six books chronicling his love affair with basketball, Vitale received the Basketball Hall of Fame's <a>Curt Gowdy Media Award</a> (1998), won the NABC Cliff Wells Appreciation Award in 2000 and was admitted to the College Basketball Hall of Fame in 2000.</p>
<p>All home basketball games feature the <a>Titan Pep Band</a> which serenades audiences throughout the game.</p>
