<p> <strong>Delaware State University</strong> (also referred to as <strong>Del State</strong> or <strong>DSU</strong>), is an <a>American</a> <a>historically black</a>, <a>public university</a> located in <a>Dover</a>, <a>Delaware</a>, with two <a>satellite campus</a>es in <a>Wilmington</a>, Delaware, and <a>Georgetown</a>, Delaware.
<p>Five courses of study leading to a <a>bachelor's degree</a> were offered:  <a>agricultural</a>, <a>chemical</a>, <a>classical</a>, <a>engineering</a>, and <a>scientific</a>.
Undergraduate studies are organized into three undergraduate schools and a college of arts and sciences with a total of nineteen academic departments, and offer a wide variety of programs leading to <a>bachelors of arts</a>, <a>science</a> and social-work degrees.
<p>The undergraduate programs in <a>aviation</a>, <a>chemistry</a>, education, <a>nursing</a> and social work are approved by the <a>Federal Aviation Administration</a>, <a>American Chemical Society</a>, <a>National Association of State Directors of Teacher Education and Certification</a>, the <a>National League for Nursing Accrediting Commission</a>, the <a>National Council for Accreditation of Teacher Education</a>, and the <a>Council on Social Work Education</a>, respectively.&lt;!--5 programs/6 &quot;approvers&quot; - &quot;respectively&quot; doesn't quite work--&gt; The Division of Graduate Studies offers programs leading to the master's degree in <a>biology</a>, <a>business administration</a>, <a>chemistry</a>, education, <a>physics</a>, <a>social work</a>, and <a>historic preservation</a>, as well as five doctoral degrees (interdisciplinary applied mathematics and mathematical physics, <a>applied chemistry</a>, <a>neuroscience</a>, <a>optics</a>, and doctorate programs in education).</p>
