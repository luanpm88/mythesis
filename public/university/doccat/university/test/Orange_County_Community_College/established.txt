The College, with its main campus in <a>Middletown, New York</a> was founded in 1950, making it the first county-sponsored community college in the SUNY system.
It was also the first community college in the nation to offer a two-year nursing degree program.
The college also has a growing degree-granting branch campus in <a>Newburgh</a> and offers additional courses at <a>Warwick</a>, <a>Port Jervis</a>, and <a>Central Valley</a>.
The college has an engineering program with a good reputation and is one of the top two-year nursing colleges in the country.
The College employs an open enrollment policy in which any student who applies for admittance to the College is accepted.
