Founded as a teachers' college in 1923, the university was named after one of Texas' founding fathers, <a>Stephen F. Austin</a>.
Its campus resides on part of the homestead of another Texas founding father, <a>Thomas Jefferson Rusk</a>.</p>
<p>The <a>East Texas Historical Association</a> is based on the Stephen F. Austin campus.</p>
Stephen F. Austin offers classes through six colleges, and houses one of only two schools of forestry in the State of Texas (and the only forestry college in the timber-producing <a>East Texas</a> region).</p>
<p>Since 2007, Stephen F. Austin has served as the headquarters of the <a>Association for Business Communication</a>.</p>

All of SFA's athletic teams participate in the <a>Southland Conference</a> which hosts teams from the states of <a>Texas</a>, <a>Louisiana</a>, and <a>Arkansas</a>.</p>
<li><a>Basketball</a> &#x2014; The men's basketball team reached its first NCAA tournament in <a>2009</a> after winning the Southland Conference regular season and tournament.
They would go on to lose to the Syracuse University Orange by a score of 59-44.
The Large Coed team competes in the NCA Collegiate National Championship in Daytona Beach, Florida every April.</li>
In July 2007, SFA Small Coed won a partial paid bid to the NCA Collegiate National Championships.
The Small Coed team competed in the 2008 NCA Collegiate National Championship in Florida.</li>
SFA's football team earned a berth into the FCS playoffs in 2009, which was long awaited by students and fans.
Before then, SFA football had not seen a FCS Playoff game since 1995.For the first time in school history the Stephen F. Austin football team won the conference championship and automatic berth to the fcs playoffs in back to back years (2009 and 2010).
They defeated Northwestern State University in 2010 to win the Southland Conference Championship outright, something that had not been accomplished since 1989.

<li>The Stephen F. Austin State and Sam Houston State rivalry matchup is called the <a>Battle of the Piney Woods</a>.
<li>In 1960, a seven-foot, six-inch statue of an Indian named &quot;Chief <a>Caddo</a>&quot; was carved as a trophy between SFA and Northwestern State University (both schools are located in towns named for a branch of the Caddo tribe).
SFA is currently in possession of Chief Caddo, after defeating Northwestern State in November 2010.
<li>Every year at Homecoming a bonfire is lit by members of the organization that has the most points earned in a series of competitions leading up to the the Homecoming celebration.
Preceding the lighting of the bonfire, a &quot;Torchlight Parade&quot; is held, where students walk through campus with lights to the Homecoming Pep Rally.</li>
<li>The Sawyers were created in 1927 as the first men's social organization.
They were the first group to have a reserved section in the stands at football games, which was requested by Homer Bryce, for whom the stadium is named.
The Sawyers began the tradition of bringing axe handles to the football games and using them as noise makers/spirit sticks.
The Purple Haze Inc. revived the idea and tradition of the axe handle in 2002.</li>
The bleachers have been painted and stenciled for the group by the Athletic Department of SFA.</li>
But the now-popular hand signal utilized by SFA fans developed during Spring Break 1978 on South Padre Island, when a group of Alpha Chi Omega sorority girls noticed that other Texas colleges has hand signals specific to their schools (&quot;Hook 'Em Horns&quot; for the University of Texas, for instance.)
