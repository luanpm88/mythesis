<h2>History</h2>
<p>Founded in 1922 as Wichita Falls Junior College, it was renamed Hardin Junior College in 1937 when it moved to its present location.
In 1946, a senior division was added and it was renamed Hardin College.
In 1950 John Midwestern donated $2 million and the name was changed to Midwestern University.
In 1961 it became a state-supported university.
The school took its current name in 1975.</p>
<p>MSU is the only university in Texas with membership in the <a>Council of Public Liberal Arts Colleges</a> and was the first public university in Texas to establish a core curriculum.
The university is also a member of the <a>Association of American Colleges and Universities</a>.</p>
MSU is a member of the <a>Lone Star Conference</a> (LSC).
Students can participate in <a>intramural sports</a>.</p>
<p>The university's campus covers  with over 50 buildings uniformly built of red brick with tiled roofs and arched facades.</p>
<a>Air Force ROTC</a> is also offered with a cross-town agreement with the <a>University of North Texas</a>.</p>
