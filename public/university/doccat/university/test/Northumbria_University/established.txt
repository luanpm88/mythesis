<p>  <strong>Northumbria University</strong> is an academic institution located in <a>Newcastle upon Tyne</a> in <a>North East England</a>.
It is a member of the <a>University Alliance</a> - which claims to represent the majority voice in British Higher Education, with 22 'research engaged and business focussed' member institutions educating 26% of all UK students.
<h2>History</h2>
<p>Northumbria has its roots in ,established in the city of Newcastle upon Tyne by Dr John Hunter Rutherford in 1880 and opened formally by HRH The Duke of York in 1894.
In 1969 it became one of the new polytechnics later absorbing colleges delivering professional education for teachers.
Northumbria gained University status in 1992.
In 1995 Northumbria University was awarded responsibility for the education of healthcare professionals,which was transferred from the National Health Service.</p>
City Campus, located in the centre of Newcastle upon Tyne, is divided into City Campus East and City Campus West by the city's Central Motorway and linked by a &#x00a3;4million bridge which in 2006 was officially opened by the former Minister of State for Trade and Investment , <a>Lord Digby Jones</a>.</p>
City Campus East has become a Newcastle landmark since opening in September 2007, winning awards from <a>The Journal</a> newspaper and the Low Carbon New Build Project of the Year accolade.</p>
<p>City Campus West is undergoing refurbishment including the extension and landscaping of Ellison Quadrangle.
A &#x00a3;30m sports facility for students, staff and the community opened in 2010.</p>
<p>A second campus is located 2.6 miles (4&nbsp;km) outside of Newcastle, on <em>Coach Lane</em>, and is known as the <em>Coach Lane Campus</em> at <a>Cochrane Park</a> near the A188 (<em>Benton Road</em>).
It is in the <a>Dene</a> ward near <a>Longbenton</a> and round the corner from <a>Tyneview Park</a>; a large <a>Department for Work and Pensions</a> office, accessible via the <a>Four Lane Ends Interchange</a>.
A free shuttle bus runs between the two campuses.</p>
<p>The next stage of the redevelopment of City Campus West is a new vehicular access route to the north of the campus from Sandyford Road.
<p>The Sutherland Building, formerly the Medical School of Durham University (1887-38), which was a naval warehouse during World War II, and the Dental School of Durham University (1945&#x2013;78) is the home of Administrative Departments including Finance and Planning and Human Resources, using the space vacated when the School of Law moved to City Campus East.</p>
<p>The Student Union building, at City Campus West, underwent a multi-million pound makeover with new lobby and recreational facilities, and a refurbished bar and cafe space, in summer 2010.</p>
It specialises in business, arts and design, computing, environmental science, built environment, applied healthcare, sports science and psychology, and teacher education.</p>
<p>Northumbria also offers 'clinical' courses in law accredited by the <a>Law Society</a> and <a>Bar Council</a>.
The institution's Student Law Centre is a unique clinical legal education enterprise, where law students participate in a legal advice and representation scheme on behalf of real clients, as part of their academic and professional development.
The service is run as a full legal service, just like any other firm of solicitors.
<p>The School of Design in Newcastle upon Tyne also has a satellite campus in <a>Islington</a>, London.</p>
<li>Law</li>
<p>Northumbria University is international in its operations and reach, with programmes delivered in Newcastle-upon-Tyne, London and worldwide.
Northumbria University recruits in Asia, with numbers of students studying degrees at partner institutions in <a>Hong Kong</a>, <a>Malaysia</a>, <a>Bangladesh</a>, <a>Seoul</a> and elsewhere.</p>
<p><a>The Sunday Times</a> ranked Northumbria <strong>36th</strong> out of 119 <a>British</a> universities in the period 1998-2007 inclusive.
In 2007/8 Northumbria University was included in a list of the 10 biggest providers of taught Postgraduate degrees in the UK.</p>
The Engineering and Physical Sciences Research Council awarded &#x00a3;1.4 million to a Northumbria University research team working alongside the Universities of Birmingham, Central Lancashire, Swansea and London (Birkbeck) on energy consumption.
RTC North and a private company, Nonlinear Dynamics - a world leader in its field &#x2013; announced a research collaboration with Northumbria University which could lead to a major breakthrough in the production of bio-fuels.
A new company established by the University in 2010 will give manufacturers the chance to use computational chemistry to create &#x201c;designer molecules&#x201d; for the first time in an industrial setting.
<p>The Students' Union is run by students for students as a campaigning and representative organisation.
<p>The Students' Union offers a range of student activities such as Northumbria Student Community Action(NSCA), One Planet, Raise and Give (RAG), Give It A Go and Skills sessions.
It represents students in academic and non-academic matters through a nationally-recognised School Reps and Post Graduate Research Reps Systems, as well as newly introduced Community Reps and Caucus group chairs.</p>
<p>The university building contains several venues for students to socialise in a safe environment, chiefly at Habita (Formerly Bar One), Domain (formerly The Venue) and Reds.
It hosts a range of club nights, including &quot;Get Y' Skates On&quot;, which caters for the alternative types and the most popular night, &quot;Wiggle&quot; which often offers themed nights and a wide variety of mainstream music.
At the end of each term NSU also has a &quot;Final Fling&quot; - in 2009 the summer fling was replaced by &quot;Carnival&quot;.</p>
