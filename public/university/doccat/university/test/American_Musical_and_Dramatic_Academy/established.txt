<p><strong>The American Musical and Dramatic Academy</strong> (AMDA), is a college conservatory for the performing arts located in <a>New York City</a> and <a>Los Angeles, California</a>.
<p>The Conservatory offers both Bachelor of Fine Arts degrees and two-year Certificates in Professional Performance.
<h2>History</h2>
<p>AMDA was founded in 1964 by Phillip Burton along with other prominent people from the New York theatre community to provide practical training for the actor.
Burton was an acting teacher and is best known as having trained his adopted son, <a>Richard Burton</a>.
<p>The New York City campus is located on the <a>Upper West Side</a> of Manhattan.
The other half of the campus is located at the historical Ansonia building, where <a>Ziegfeld</a> once lived, on West 73rd Street.</p>
<p>The Los Angeles campus is located in the heart of the Hollywood Entertainment District, one block north of Hollywood Blvd.
and <a>Vine Street</a>, adjacent to the <a>Capitol Records Tower</a>, with its principal facility in the historic Vine Tower Building.</p>
