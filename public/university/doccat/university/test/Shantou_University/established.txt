<p> <strong>Shantou University</strong> (STU) (<a>Chinese</a>: &#x6c55;&#x5934;&#x5927;&#x5b66;) is a provincial level University in the Guangdong city of <a>Shantou</a>.
It was authorised and established in 1981 by the <a>State Council</a> and Guangdong Government under the &#x201c;<a>Project 211</a>&#x201d; program.
Mr. <a>Li Ka-Shing</a>, a global businessman and philanthropist, contributed HK $3.1 billion to the founding of the university.
The University's mission is to nurture aspiration, knowledge, perseverance, and achievement for the young.</pre>
<p>&lt;/div&gt; <strong>Shantou University</strong> (STU) was founded in 1981 and started student enrollment in 1983.
It is a key comprehensive university under the &#x201c;<a>Project 211</a>&#x201d; in Guangdong Province.
It is the only institution of higher education in the Chaoshan area that has provided tens of thousands of local residents with higher learning.
Its campus covers a total area of 2,106.11
<p>Since its inception, STU has benefitted from the great support and generous donations of Mr. Li Ka-shing, a distinguished international entrepreneur and philanthropist.
It is the only university in China that has received long-time funding from the <a>Li Ka Shing Foundation</a>, which has amounted to more than HK $3.6 billion.</p>
<p>STU is strongly supported by the Central, Provincial, and Municipal authorities.
President <a>Hu Jintao</a> visited the university in the early 1990s.
President <a>Jiang Zemin</a> paid two visits to the university and wrote the following inscription as praise and encouragement to the staff and students: &#x201c;The mission is demanding and the journey ahead is long.&#x201d;
Other leaders of the Central Government have also visited the university.
<p>STU is striving hard to become a modern institution of higher education with unique strength, international character, and global recognition.
Since 2002, the University has conducted an aggressive internationalization-orientated reform to cultivate talents with the motto, &#x201c;Aspiration, Knowledge, Perseverance, Achievement.&#x201d;
Through a series of pedagogical reforms, it aims to offer a platform for the exploration of higher educational reform in China.
Comrade <a>Li Lanqing</a>, member of the Standing Committee of Political Bureau of the CPC Central Committee and Vice Premier of the State Council, praised the University as &#x201c;the experimental zone for higher educational reform in China.&#x201d;
<h2>History</h2>
<h3>Background of Establishment</h3>
&lt;/div&gt; With the generous support and assistance of the Li Ka Shing Foundation and government officials at all levels, the university keeps pursuing comprehensive and profound reforms in academic management, human resources, and financial systems.
The University hopes to create an incubating environment for tertiary education in the 21st century; to provide students with integrity, creativity, and critical thinking skills; and to prepare them for the demands of China&#x2019;s rapid development.</p>
<p>Since its founding, STU has received support from various levels of government, ranging from the <a>Ministry of Education of the People's Republic of China</a>(MOE) to Guangdong Province and Shantou city, and has obtained generous financial aid from Mr. Li (more than HK $3.1 billion or US $397 million to date).
<p><a>190px|none|thumb|250px|On February 8, 1990, the STU inauguration ceremony was held after completion</a> </p>
<li>On August 26, 1983, the State Council granted permission to establish STU.</li>
<li>In 1983, STU began to enroll undergraduate students, and the Ministry of Education approved Shantou Medical Vocational School to be merged into STU and renamed Shantou University Medical College (SUMC).</li>
<li>On January 1, 1984, the STU foundation stone laying ceremony was held.</li>
<li>On June 20, 1986, <a>Deng Xiaoping</a> received Mr. Li Ka-shing in Beijing and suggested that STU should develop along freer lines and gradually attain the status of a national key university.</li>
<li>In December 1996, STU was short-listed as a candidate for the &quot;Project 211&quot;.</li>
<li>In 2001, the Preclinical Medicine Center for post-doctoral studies was established.</li>
<li>In 2002, Pathology and Pathophysiology were approved by the MOE as a National Key Discipline.</li>
<li>In 2002, the Credit System Reform and English Enhancement Program were launched.</li>
<li>In July 2003, STU passed the project assessment for &quot;Project 211&quot; in the &quot;<a>Ninth Five-Year Plan</a>&quot;.</li>
<li>In April 2007, STU was accredited &quot;outstanding&quot; by the Ministry of Education for its undergraduate programs after being audited by a team of high-caliber panelists elected by MOE.</li>
<p><a>none|thumb|250px||Computer-generated imagery of the exterior of the New Shantou University (STU) Library</a></p>
The serene campus is blanketed by rare trees and greenery.
This pristine setting combined with its functional infrastructure, such as the library, the stadium, and auditorium, offers students not only a good learning environment but also an excellent living community.
<li><strong>STU Campus Planning:</strong>  Herzog and Meuron, co-winners of the <a>Pritzker Architecture Prize</a> 2001, which is the &#x201c;Nobel Prize&#x201d; in Architecture, were commissioned as the chief designers of STU campus.
To develop an environment that is conducive to the nurture of creative thinking among students and faculty members, they have created, and continue to create, a unique campus space that unites humanity and nature by retaining the greenery and positioning a series of sculptures on the campus.
<li><strong>New Library:</strong>  The STU new library is designed by Ray Chen &#xff08;&#x9648;&#x745e;&#x5baa;&#xff09;, winner of the &#x201c;Design for Asia Award&#x201d; by Hong Kong Design Center.
It is a state-of-the-arte influence of traditional library resources and a cutting-edge digital library to serve STU development.
As a modern facility, it also marks the new development of the university library in the 21st century.</li>
<li><strong>Auditorium:</strong>  The Auditorium has a total area of over 5,600 sq.
Advanced lighting, audiovisuals, air conditioning, and seating make it comfortable and conducive to learning.</li>
<p>In June 2006, the bronze sculpture series, &#x201c;Living World&#x201d; from Ju Ming, a famous Taiwan sculptor, was positioned besides the picturesque reservoir on STU campus.
The lower part of the cylinder-shaped building that holds the sculpture is constructed as a half-open reservoir working room.
Such a building is connected to the reservoir dam with the abutment creating a warm and comfortable atmosphere on the campus.</p>
<p>As a world-class university conference center designed by well-known designer <a>William Chang Suk Ping</a> &#xff08;&#x5f20;&#x53d4;&#x5e73;&#xff09;, the ACC has over 50 rooms and the conference halls, all equipped with modern equipment including high speed internet.

<h3>Reform in Educational Approach</h3>
<p>STU continues to explore an initiative-oriented educational mode by incrementally enhancing inquiry-based learning and experience step by step.
It established the first Student Learning Center and Virtual Classroom in China to further improve the campus environment for students&#x2019; mutual learning, thus raising their awareness of life-long learning.</p>
It has established a number of centers and laboratories, which conduct basic scientific research and directly serve local economic development, including the Artificial Intelligence Manufacturing Technology Laboratory funded by the Ministry of Education, STU Division of National Key Discipline in Molecular Pathology, Research Institute of Tumor Pathology, Marine Biology Laboratory of Guangdong Province, Image-processing Technology Key Laboratory of Guangdong Province, CAD/CAM Center, Laboratory for Wind Tunnel Testing, Laboratory for Structural Engineering, FMS Laboratory and Institute for Artificial Intelligence and Pattern Recognition.
Among the projects, four were rated at the &#x201c;National Projects of Science and Technology.&#x201d;
The academic staff published 157 SCI-indexed papers, an increase from 2005 and 2006 in terms of the number of papers indexed by SCI, EI, and ISTP.
The University was approved as one of the first &#x201c;Province Intellectual Property Pilot Institutions&#x201d; by the Intellectual Property of Guangdong Province.
Distinguished scholars from first-class universities at home and abroad have been invited to present  seminars or lectures to the University&#x2019;s undergraduate students, so as to broaden their academic horizon.
<p>Under the auspice of the Li Ka Shing Foundation, STU has become one of the few universities in Mainland China that awards full scholarships to overseas exchange students.

Taking a cue from its exposure to international practices, the University has conducted a profound reform in financial management, human resources regulations and policies, distribution regulations, resource management, pedagogical methods, curricular system, and University administration.
As a result, Shantou University has been revitalized and has won acclaim from both China and abroad.
