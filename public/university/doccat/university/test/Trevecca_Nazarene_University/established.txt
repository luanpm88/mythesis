<h2>History</h2>
<p><a>thumb|left|A view of College Church from the campus entrance</a> TNU was founded in 1901 by <a>Cumberland Presbyterian</a> <a>minister</a> J. O. McClurkan as the &quot;Pentecostal Literary and Bible Training School&quot;.
Part of the Pentecostal Alliance, it started offering [[bachelor's degree]]s in 1910, and the school's name was changed to Trevecca College for Christian Workers in 1911, That same year, the school begrudgingly became an official college of the [[Church of the Nazarene]], in order to save itself financially.
Shortly after it had become a Nazarene institution, it absorbed the [[Southeastern Nazarene College]] of Georgia, but still found itself in [[bankruptcy]] and forced to sell its campus by 1932.
