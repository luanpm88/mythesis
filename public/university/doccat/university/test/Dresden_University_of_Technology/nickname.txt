<li>The <strong>Faculty of Economics</strong> comprises five departments, Business Education Studies (Wirtschaftsp&#x00e4;dagogik),  Business Management, Economics, Management Information Systems and Statistics.
<li>The <strong>Faculty of Languages, Literature and Culture</strong> is structured into five departments, American Studies, English Studies, German Studies, Philology, Romance Languages and Slavistics.
Also, through the <a>Erasmus programme</a> and partnerships with universities in the <a>USA</a>, there are many English, French and Spanish speaking students.
This office is responsible for handling international applications.</p>
Most notably the <em>Erasmus-Initiative TU Dresden</em> offers many group activities throughout the semester which are open to all students (not only to Erasmus participants).
There are eight big students' clubs and the summer campus party is considered to be the biggest in Germany.
There are cafeterias as at most universities and the largest <a>refectory</a> can compete with some restaurants even as far as menu size.</p>
