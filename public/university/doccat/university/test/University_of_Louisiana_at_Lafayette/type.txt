<p>The <strong>University of Louisiana at Lafayette</strong>, or <strong>UL Lafayette</strong>, is a coeducational, public research university located in <a>Lafayette</a>, <a>Louisiana</a>, <a>United States</a>, in the heart of <a>Acadiana</a>.
It also has a  <a>New Iberia Research Center</a> in <a>New Iberia, Louisiana</a>, which is among the world's largest private non-human, <a>primate</a> breeding colonies.
<p>Traveling shows featuring work from national and internationally known artists such as <a>Mark Rothko</a>, <a>James McNeil Whistler</a>, <a>Andrew Wyeth</a>, <a>Christopher Rauschenberg</a>, <a>Robert Rauschenberg</a>, <a>Deborah Butterfield</a> and <a>Pablo Picasso</a> have been featured regularly.
<p><a>KRVS</a> is a listener-supported public radio station facility currently licensed to UL Lafayette.
KRVS operates on campus, located in Burke-Hawthorne Hall and plays local music such as <a>Cajun</a>, <a>Zydeco</a>, <a>Swamp Rock</a>, <a>Swamp Pop</a>, <a>Blues</a>, <a>Jazz</a>, and a variety of other programs.
<p>The university offers 78 <a>undergraduate degree</a> programs, 27 <a>master's degree</a> programs, and 9 <a>Doctor of Philosophy</a> programs, which include <a>applied language and speech sciences</a>, <a>biology</a>, <a>cognitive science</a>, <a>computer engineering</a>, <a>computer science</a>, <a>educational leadership</a>, <a>English</a>, <a>francophone studies</a> and <a>mathematics</a>.
<li>&quot;Best Colleges 2011,&quot; Tier 2 designation in the national universities college category, <em><a>U.S. News &amp; World Report</a></em> <a>college rankings</a></li>
<p><strong>Young Professional Organization Chapters:</strong>&lt;br/&gt; UL offers a variety of young professional organizations associated with fields of study that routinely perform public services and interact with as well as promote local economic and environmental growth, public service projects or awareness.</p>
<p>The athletic program formally began in 1904 with a track and field program.
