<p><strong>Heythrop College</strong> is the specialist philosophy and theology constituent college of the <a>University of London</a> situated in Kensington Square, <a>Kensington</a>, <a>London</a>.
It offers <a>undergraduate</a> and <a>postgraduate</a> degrees in <a>philosophy</a>, <a>theology</a> and <a>psychology</a>, as well as research in related fields.</p>
<p>It was founded in 1614 by the <a>Jesuits</a> in <a>Leuven</a>, <a>Belgium</a>, then moved in 1624 to <a>Li&#x00e8;ge</a>.
During the wars surrounding the <a>French Revolution</a>, the college moved to Britain - <a>philosophy</a> was taught at <a>Stonyhurst College</a> in <a>Lancashire</a> and <a>theology</a> in St. Beuno's in North Wales.</p>
<p>In 1926, the colleges came together in <a>Heythrop Hall</a>, <a>Oxfordshire</a>, then moved to <a>London</a> in 1970.
It became a college of the University in 1971, and moved to its current <a>Kensington</a> location in 1993.
<p>The Union is managed by a team of eleven officers, elected annually.
