<p>In 2006, the <em><a>U.S. News &amp; World Report</a></em> <a>college and university rankings</a> put the UCCS College of Engineering and Applied Science as ranking the fourth-best among <a>public universities</a> and the 16th best overall among <a>bachelor</a> and <a>master's degree</a> <a>engineering</a> schools.
For public universities in the Master's Universities-West category it was ranked 6th.
It is the largest college at UCCS, offering undergraduate programs in <a>anthropology</a>, <a>art history</a>, <a>biology</a>, <a>chemistry</a>, <a>communication</a>, <a>economics</a>, <a>English</a>, <a>film studies</a>, gallery practice, <a>geography</a> and <a>environmental studies</a>, <a>history</a>, <a>mathematics</a>, <a>philosophy</a>, <a>physics</a>, <a>political science</a>, <a>psychology</a>, <a>sociology</a>, <a>Spanish</a>, and <a>visual</a> and <a>performing arts</a>.
<li>Undergraduate: <a>Bachelor of Science in Business Administration</a>; <a>Bachelor of Science</a> in <a>accounting</a>, <a>finance</a>, general business, <a>human resource management</a>, <a>information systems</a>, <a>international business</a>, <a>marketing</a>, marketing/professional golf management; organizational management, services management and sport management.</li>
It is accredited by the <a>National Council for the Accreditation of Teacher Education</a> (NCATE), the Colorado Department of Education (CDE), the <a>Colorado Commission on Higher Education</a> (CCHE) and the Council for Accreditation of Counseling and Related Educational Programs (CACREP).
<h3>School of Public Affairs</h3>
<p>The <strong>School of Public Affairs</strong> offers degrees in <a>criminal justice</a> and <a>public administration</a>.</p>
In the <a>U.S. News &amp; World Report</a> &quot;America&#x2019;s Best Colleges,&quot; the 2008 college rankings edition, &quot;the magazine&#x2019;s editors ranked the UCCS undergraduate engineering program ninth in the nation among public engineering schools offering bachelor&#x2019;s or master&#x2019;s degrees.&quot;</p>
<p>In 2006, the <a>American Society for Engineering Education</a> ranked the college seventh among U.S. public universities and 18th for all U.S. universities in the number of degrees awarded to woman.
