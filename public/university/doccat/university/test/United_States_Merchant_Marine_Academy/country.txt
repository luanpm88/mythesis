<p> <a>thumb|right|350px|Aerial view of United States Merchant Marine Academy</a>  The <strong>United States Merchant Marine Academy</strong> (also known as <strong>USMMA</strong> or <strong>Kings Point</strong>) is one of the five <a>United States service academies</a>.
From 1942-1945, the Academy graduated 6,895 officers.
Indoc is functionally run by upperclassmen but is overseen by officers of the United States Maritime Service who are part of the Commandant of Midshipman's staff.
&quot;Engine Majors&quot; sit for and upon successful completion of the examination are issued Third Assistant Engineer (3 A/E - Engineering Officer) Licenses Steam and Motor Vessels, Any Horsepower.
Marine Engineering Systems and Marine Engineering Systems &amp; Shipyard Management graduates are also qualified to sit for the Engineer In Training (EIT) examination administered by the National Council of Examiners for Engineering and Surveying (NCEES).
The Mariners won their first ICSA National (now &quot;North American&quot;) Championship in 1979.
Regardless, graduates are required to maintain their US Coast Guard issued merchant marine officer's license for a period of at least 6 years.</p>
