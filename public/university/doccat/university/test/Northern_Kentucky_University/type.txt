<p>  <strong>Northern Kentucky University</strong> (<strong>NKU</strong>) is a <a>public</a>, <a>co-educational</a> <a>university</a> located in <a>Highland Heights, Kentucky</a>, seven miles (11&nbsp;km) southeast of <a>Cincinnati, Ohio</a>.
NKU is primarily an <a>undergraduate</a>, <a>liberal arts</a> institution, but it also features <a>graduate</a> programs.
<p>NKU's <a>Grant County Center</a>, located in <a>Williamstown, Kentucky</a>, is a partnership between the Grant County Foundation for Higher Education and NKU.
NLS is composed of outstanding student leaders with a sophomore status or higher.

<h3>Public media</h3>
The university is also home to an independent, student-run <a>Internet radio</a> station, <em>Norse Code Radio</em>, online at <a>NorseCodeRadio.com</a>.</p>
<p>NKU is a national model of civic engagement and economic development initiatives.
Corporate and university partnerships include <a>The Scripps Howard Center for Civic Engagement</a>, <a>the Fifth/Third Entrepreneurial Center</a>, <a>the Metropolitan Education and Training Services Center</a>, <a>the Infrastructure Management Institute</a>, and <a>Fidelity Investments</a>.</p>
<p>Other centers on campus include <a>the Institute for Freedom Studies</a>, <a>the Center for Applied Ecology</a>, <a>the Small Business Development Center</a>, <a>the Institute for New Economy Technologies</a>, <a>the Center for Environmental Education</a>, <a>the Center for Integrative National Science and Mathematics</a> and <a>the Chase Local Government Law Center</a>.</p>
