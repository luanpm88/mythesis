Its teaching hospital, Universiti Kebangsaan Malaysia Medical Centre (UKMMC) <a>Pusat Perubatan Universiti Kebangsaan Malaysia</a> (PPUKM) is located in <a>Cheras</a> and also has a branch campus in Kuala Lumpur.
It was ranked by The <a>Times Higher Education Supplement</a> (THES) at number 185 in its annual ranking of the world's top 200 universities for 2006.
<h2>History</h2>
<p>The university was formally established on 18 May 1970.
It initially operated in a temporary campus located at <a>Lembah Pantai</a>, Kuala Lumpur with its first cohort of 192 students and 3 faculties.
In October 1977, it moved to its present location in Bangi, a campus of 1,096 hectares.
<li>Law</li>
<p>Postgraduate programmes are managed by the Centre for Graduate Management, which was established in 1983.
<p>The university adapted a collegiate system which was based on <a>Oxford</a> and <a>Cambridge</a>.It consists of 13 residential colleges, namely <a>Dato Onn</a>, Aminuddin Baki, Ungku Omar, <a>Burhanuddin Helmi</a>, <a>Ibrahim Yaakob</a>, Rahim Kajai, Ibu Zain, Keris Mas, Pendeta Za'ba, Tun <a>Hussein Onn</a>, Tun Syed Nasir, <a>Tun Dr. Ismail</a>, and Idris al-Marbawi.
<p>The university is accessible via direct the <a>UKM Komuter station</a>, a <a>commuter</a> <a>train halt</a> situated close to and named after the university.
The station strictly serves as a small two-platform train halt for <a>Rawang-to-Seremban</a> <a>KTM Komuter</a> train services.
