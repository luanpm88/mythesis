Founded as a school for <a>Quaker</a>s in 1885, the private school has more than 3,300 students combined between its main campus in Newberg and its centers in <a>Portland</a>, <a>Salem</a>, <a>Redmond</a> and <a>Boise</a>.
The  main campus is located near downtown Newberg close to the junction of <a>Oregon Route 99W</a> and <a>Oregon Route 219</a>.
<h2>History</h2>
<p>The university was founded in <a>Newberg, Oregon</a>, in 1885 by <a>Quaker</a> pioneers, originally serving as Friends Pacific Academy for several years before becoming a college in 1891 as <strong>Pacific College</strong>.
The Bruin mascot name comes from a real bear cub found in 1887, in the <a>Coast Range's</a> foothills near <a>Carlton</a>, about  west of Newberg.
After the hide deteriorated, a leather replica was created and called Bruin Jr. Students today still participate in student-government-sponsored class competitions called &quot;Bruin brawls&quot; for possession of the Bruin Jr.</p>
<p>In 1893, the school was incorporated as a joint-stock company and became a four-year school in 1925.
After four decades in the <a>National Association of Intercollegiate Athletics</a> (NAIA), George Fox University and the Northwest Conference switched affiliation to the <a>National Collegiate Athletic Association</a> in 1998.</p>
<p>The George Fox baseball team's 2004 NCAA Division III national championship, was named one of the top 50 moments in Northwest Sports History by Portland radio station KFXX AM 1080, &quot;The Fan.&quot; Dwight Kimberly, an associate professor of biology, received the Carnegie Foundation's Oregon Professor of the Year award in 2000.</p>
<p>The university student body has grown more than 500% since 1986, when enrollment was 549.
<p><a>thumb|The Stevens Center on the Newberg campus</a> George Fox grants <a>bachelor's</a>, <a>master's</a>, and <a>doctoral degree</a>s.
The university is a participant in the Richter Scholars program, which sponsors 15-25 students per year to perform original research.
George Fox offers a study-abroad through the &quot;Best Semester&quot; program offered by the <a>Council for Christian Colleges and Universities</a> (CCCU).</p>
<p>School of Education programs include a Master of Arts in Teaching (MAT), a Master of Arts in Counseling (MA), a Master of Education (MEd), a Doctor of Education (EdD), a Master of Arts in School Counseling (MA), a Master of Arts in Marriage and Family Therapy (MA) , Administrative License Programs and both a Library Media Endorsement and Reading Endorsement.
George Fox offers baseball, basketball, cross country, soccer, tennis, golf, and track and field for men.
In 2009, the school&#x2019;s women&#x2019;s basketball team went unbeaten (32-0) and capped the season with a 60-53 defeat of Washington University of St. Louis in the title game.
A weekday off from work and classes allows over 90% of eligible individuals the opportunity to volunteer at local churches, schools, nonprofits, etc. performing manual labor and maintenance work.</p>
<p>George Fox University is a center for Quaker thought, although only about 5% of the student body are Quakers, In 1984, the university founded its Center for Peace Learning, now known as the Center for Peace and Justice, as an outgrowth of its connection to the Friends peace testimony.</p>
<p>The Newberg campus includes two structures listed on the <a>National Register of Historic Places</a>.
One, <a>Minthorn Hall</a>, was built in 1886 and is still used for holding classes.
The other, <a>Jesse Edwards House</a>, was constructed in 1883 and serves as the residence for the university president.</p>
<p>George Fox University is the only Christian university in the <a>Pacific Northwest</a> classified by <a>U.S. News &amp; World Report</a> as a national university.
According to data compiled by U.S. News &amp; World Report magazine for its 2008 &#x201c;America&#x2019;s Best Colleges&#x201d; issue, George Fox ranks No. 20 out of approximately 1,400 accredited institutions in the nation in percentage of students (68%) studying outside the country before graduation.</p>
