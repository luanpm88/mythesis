The <a>college</a> opened on September 3, 2002 and is located on a  site in the southern foothills of Henderson, Nevada.
The site of the college raised environmental concerns as it was located next to a toxic waste dump.</p>
<p>In the past, some of Nevada State College's programs suffered from lower than expected enrollments.
<p>As of 2008, the university's six-year graduation rate is 16%.
<p>Since 2004, 861 students have graduated, 437 of them earning nursing degrees.
<p>Due to state budget cuts, there have been proposals to close down Nevada State College.
However, in March of 2010 members of the Board of Regents expressed their continued support of Nevada State College despite budget cuts.</p>
<p>In 2002, as part of the Clark County Conservation of Public Land and Natural Resources Act of 2002, the US Congress conveyed  to the Nevada System of Higher Education (NSHE) and the City of Henderson for the express purpose of creating a new four-year institution of higher education..</p>
<p>Under the leadership and guidance of its late President, Dr. <a>Fred Maryanski</a>, Nevada State College acquired accreditation, moved with its master plan for a  campus, saw rising enrollment, and the opening of its first permanent building, the Liberal Arts and Sciences building in August 2008.
The  building has faculty offices, labs and seven classrooms.
<p>Nevada State College offers regular and accelerated nursing degrees, education degrees, and liberal arts majors including psychology, biology, history, English, and criminal justice.</p>
Due to budget cuts, there have been proposals to close down Nevada State College along with other NHSE programs and schools.</p>
<p>On May 10, 2008, Nevada State College held its fifth graduation ceremony.
During the ceremony, officials referred to NSC as a small but growing college of roughly 2,000 students.
<p>In the Fall of 2008, Nevada State College launched <a>Nevada</a>&#x2019;s first bachelor of science degree in the education of the deaf and hard of hearing.
The program addresses the deaf culture and its integration of deaf students into specific subject areas.</p>
<p>Nevada State College also partners with <a>Touro University</a> to accommodate students in <a>Occupational Therapy</a>.
Through the partnership, students complete three years of their bachelor degree in Occupational Therapy Science at Nevada State College, then transfer to Touro University for the final two years.</p>
<p>As of the end of spring 2008, Nevada State College has graduated 16% of the full-time students who registered as freshmen in fall 2002, and 11% of 2003's incoming freshmen.
<p>Nevada State College was once troubled by low enrollments in majors like business administration.
In 2008, the management program at Nevada State College expected to have 40 students in its third year but had only two.</p>
<p>Among the different educational institutions of the NHSE, the largest 2009 budget cuts by the state legislature were for Nevada State College at 24.1%.
Neighboring <a>College of Southern Nevada</a> had its budget cut by only 4.9%.
<p>Nevada State College budget cuts could have resulted in larger class sizes, fewer available classes and construction project delays.
The university has laid off an undisclosed number of staff in 2008 as a cost saving measure.
NSC officials have also formed a committee to consider whether to hold back a part of each unit's operating budget for a mid-year review and impose a credit surcharge on student tuition.</p>
<p>In January 2010, students initiated the &quot;myNSCstory&quot; campaign as a response to proposed budget cuts and tuition increases.
The campaign involves handwritten notes from students, accompanied by photos, sent to state officials.
<p>Nevada State College offers financial aid in the form of grants, scholarships, and loans.</p>
