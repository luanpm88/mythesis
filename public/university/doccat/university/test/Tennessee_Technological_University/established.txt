It was formerly known as Tennessee Polytechnic Institute (1915), and before that as Dixie College, the name under which it was founded as a private institution in 1909.
It places special emphasis on <a>undergraduate education</a> in fields related to <a>engineering</a> and technology, although degrees in <a>education</a>, <a>liberal arts</a>, <a>agriculture</a>, <a>nursing</a>, and other fields of study can be pursued as well.
It is operated by the <a>Tennessee Board of Regents</a>, and its athletic teams compete in the <a>Ohio Valley Conference</a>.</p>
The <a>Princeton Review</a> also listed TTU as a &quot;Best College Value&quot; in 2006 and 2007.
TTU is  one of &quot;America's 100 Best College Buys&quot; as reported by Institutional Research &amp; Evaluation, Inc. in 2006.</p>

<li>&quot;Dammit the Dog&quot;: a former university president once said &quot;dammit&quot; to a dog in front of a crowd.
He covered by saying that was the dog's name.
<li>The golden eagle atop Derryberry Hall was stolen by students from a hotel in <a>Monteagle, Tennessee</a>.
After being retrieved by the owner of the hotel many different times, the hotel owner later donated the statue to the university.
The governor officially pardoned the students involved.</li>
<li>The &quot;Blizzard&quot; is a tradition which started in 1984 when students celebrated the first successful shot made by Tennessee Tech in a basketball game against <a>MTSU</a> by throwing showers of &quot;Tech Squares&quot; (toilet paper) into the air.
Since MTSU moved to the Sun Belt Conference, the Blizzard is now performed against <a>Austin Peay State University</a>.</li>
