<p>  <strong>Edith Cowan University (ECU)</strong> is located in <a>Perth</a>, Western Australia.
It was named after the first woman to be elected to an <a>Australian Parliament</a>, <a>Edith Cowan</a>, and is the only Australian <a>university</a> named after a woman.</p>
<p>ECU was granted university status in 1991 and was formed from an amalgamations of teachers' colleges with a history dating back to 1902 when the <a>Claremont Teachers College</a> was established; making ECU the modern descendant of the first institution of higher education in Western Australia .</p>
<p>The university offers more than 400 courses across two metropolitan campuses, in <a>Mount Lawley</a> and <a>Joondalup</a>, and a regional campus in the South West, <a>Bunbury</a>, 200&nbsp;km south of <a>Perth</a> ; with some courses also offered for study off-campus (<a>Distance Education</a>) .
Additionally, the university has partnerships with several education institutions to conduct courses and programs offshore</p>
<p>Divisions of note include the <a>Western Australian Academy of Performing Arts</a> (WAAPA), recognised as one of Australia's prestigious performing arts training academies; the School of Nursing, Midwifery and Postgraduate Medicine which offers the largest undergraduate nursing program in WA ; and the School of Education which offers the widest range of secondary teaching specialisations within WA .

<h4>Security, Law and Justice</h4>
<p>The University Council is the governing body of the organisation which controls and manages the operation, affairs, concerns and property of the university, in accordance with its Corporate Governance Statement.</p>
<p>The membership of the council is composed of people across various disciplines and groups as mandated under Part III, Sect.
9 of the Edith Cowan University Act 1984.
<p>ECU has three campuses, consisting of two metropolitan campuses at <a>Joondalup</a> and <a>Mount Lawley</a>, and one at <a>Bunbury</a>, in Western Australia's <a>South West Region</a>.
<p>The Joondalup Campus is the University's headquarters during the summer months and on-campus accommodation.
It is serviced by the Joondalup <a>CAT</a> and is close to the <a>Mitchell Freeway</a>.
Facilities on the campus include <a>Perth Graduate School of Business</a>, a range of performing arts facilities, a sport and fitness centre and on-campus accommodation.</p>
<p>The South West Campus is located in <a>Bunbury</a>, two hours drive south of Perth and the Claremont Campus being acquired by the <a>University of Western Australia</a>.</p>
<p>The university offers more than 400 courses across its three <a>Western Australia</a>n campuses, with some courses also offered for study off-campus (<a>Distance Education</a>) ; Arts Management; Aviation; the Home Economics specialisation in secondary teaching; Design and Technology secondary teaching;  an accreditted online Law degree; Social Science courses in Youth Work and Family Studies, and MBA programme was given a five star rating by <a>Graduate Management Association of Australia</a> in 2010.</p>
<p>Notable alumni of ECU include entertainer <a>Rolf Harris</a>; <a>Musa Aman</a>, the chief minister of the Malaysian state of <a>Sabah</a>; former soccer player <a>Alistair Edwards</a>; actors <a>Frances O'Connor</a>, <a>Hugh Jackman</a>, <a>Lisa McCune</a>, <a>Marcus Graham</a>, <a>William McInnes</a>, <a>Lucy Durack</a>, <a>Rachelle Durkin</a>, <a>Emma Matthews</a>, <a>Eddie Perfect</a>, <a>Tim Minchin</a> and <a>Simon Lyndon</a>; musician <a>Jamie Oehlers</a>; and Indigenous rights activist and former <a>AFL</a> player <a>Craig Turley</a>.</p>
