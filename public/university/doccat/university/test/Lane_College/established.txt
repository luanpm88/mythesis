<p> <strong>Lane College</strong> is a four-year, <a>historically black college</a> associated with the <a>Christian Methodist Episcopal Church</a>, located in <a>Jackson, Tennessee</a>, just northeast of the downtown area.
<h2>History</h2>
Planning for the school had begun in 1878, but the school's establishment was delayed by a <a>yellow fever</a> epidemic in the region in 1878.
Its primary purpose was the education of newly freed <a>slave</a>s, and the original curriculum focused on the preparation of &quot;teachers and preachers.&quot;
Church is considered to have been the school's founder, and his daughter, Jennie Lane, was its first teacher.</p>

<p><a>SS Lane Victory</a>, a <a>World War II</a> Victory Ship,  and one of the few such ships surviving, was named for Lane College.
It is now docked in <a>San Pedro, California</a> (which is part of the commercial harbor area of Los Angeles to the south of downtown).
It is now open as a museum.</p>
