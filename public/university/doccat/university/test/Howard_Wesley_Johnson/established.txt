He served as dean of the <a>MIT Sloan School of Management</a> between 1959 and 1966,  president of <a>MIT</a> between 1966 and 1971, and chairman of the MIT Corporation (the university's board of trustees) from 1971 to 1983.</p>
<p>Johnson graduated in 1943 with a bachelor's degree in business from <a>Central College</a> in Chicago.
He served in the Army in Europe during <a>World War II</a>, and returned to earn a masters degree in economics at the <a>University of Chicago</a>, where he taught from 1948 to 1955.
He joined the MIT faculty as an associate professor of management in 1955.&lt;ref
