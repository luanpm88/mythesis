<p> The <strong>University of South Alabama</strong> (<strong>USA</strong>) is a <a>public</a>, doctoral-level <a>university</a> in <a>Mobile</a>, <a>Alabama</a>, <a>USA</a>.
It was created by the <a>Alabama Legislature</a> in 1963, and replaced existing extension programs operated in Mobile by the <a>University of Alabama</a>.
Plans for this university were being made as early as 1956.</p>
<p>The University is divided into nine colleges and schools, and includes Alabama's second state-supported <a>medical school</a>.
To date, the University has awarded over 62,325 degrees.</p>
It has remained one of Alabama's fastest growing universities for the past several years.</p>
Doctoral level degrees are offered in several areas, including <a>Nursing</a>, <a>Medicine</a>, Basic Medical Sciences, Instructional Design, Communication Sciences and Disorders, <a>Audiology</a>, <a>Marine Science</a>, <a>Psychology</a>, and a Doctor of Pharmacy degree offered in collaboration with <a>Auburn University</a>.
The psychology doctoral program was initiated in 2009 and is one of a handful of such programs nationwide that offers a Combined degree, emphasizing both Clinical Psychology and Counseling Psychology.
Significant research is done by faculty members in the above fields.</p>
<p>The University of South Alabama has ten colleges:</p>
<li>Pat Covey College of Allied Health Professions</li>
<li>Mitchell College of Business</li>
<li>College of Engineering</li>
<li>College of Medicine</li>
<li>College of Nursing</li>
<p>The University is governed by a <a>Board of Trustees</a> appointed by and including the governor of Alabama.
The Board appoints a president of the University.
Since the founding of the University, there have been two presidents: <a>Frederick Palmer Whiddon</a> (served 1963-1998) and V. Gordon Moulton (since 1998).</p>
