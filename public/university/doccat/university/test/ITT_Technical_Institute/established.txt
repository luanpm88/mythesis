ITT Educational Services, Inc. (ESI) was spun off by <a>ITT Corporation</a> through an <a>initial public offering (IPO)</a> in 1994, with parent company ITT as an 83% shareholder.
<p>The company was founded in 1946 as Educational Services, Inc. and has been headquartered in <a>Carmel, Indiana</a>, since 1969.
From 1965 until its IPO in 1994, ITT Tech was a wholly owned subsidiary of <a>ITT Corporation</a> (as &quot;ITT/ESI&quot;).
<p>ITT Tech offers AAS, <a>bachelor's</a> BAS, and <a>master's</a> (business-only, online) degrees.
ITT Tech is <a>nationally accredited</a> by the <a>Accrediting Council for Independent Colleges and Schools</a>.</p>
<p>In August 1998, 15 former students alleged misrepresentation, fraud and concealment by ITT arising out of their recruitment and education at ITT campuses.
In September 1998, ITT settled all of the claims of the 15 claimants.
<p>On February 25, 2004, federal agents raided the company's headquarters and 10 of its campuses.
The investigation negatively affected the company's stock and triggered several class action lawsuits by investors.</p>
<p>In October 2005, ITT agreed to pay $730,000 to settle a lawsuit with California in which employees alleged that it inflated students&#x2019; grade point averages so they qualified for more financial aid from the State of California.</p>
