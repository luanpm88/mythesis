The ESSEC MBA (now Master of Science in Management) was ranked 7th in the International MBA Ranking by <a>The Wall Street Journal</a> in 2007.
The ESSEC BBA (formerly EPSCI), was ranked first in France in several rankings in 2007 and 2009.</p>
<p>ESSEC was created by <a>Jesuits</a> in 1907, and originally located in the heart of <a>Paris</a>, next to the <a>University of Paris II: Panth&#x00e9;on-Assas</a>, <a>rue d'Assas</a>.
Its campus was later relocated to <a>Cergy-Pontoise</a>, 30&nbsp;km west of Paris (<a>Val d'Oise</a>), in 1973.</p>
<p>The school is known for its very competitive entrance exams and has among its notable alumni many CEOs of French and international companies, including CEO of <a>Accenture</a> <a>Pierre Nanterme</a> and Europe CEO of <a>Coca-Cola</a> <a>Dominique Reiniche</a>.
The school, reputed for its international orientation, has partnerships with the best universities all over the world, such as <a>University of California-Berkeley</a>, <a>University of Chicago</a>, <a>Cornell University</a>, <a>Dartmouth College</a> or <a>Peking University</a> and set up a campus in Singapore in 2006.</p>
<p>- In 1997, ESSEC became the first <a>business school</a> outside of North America to be accredited by the <a>AACSB</a> (Association to Advance Collegiate Schools of Business);</p>
<p>- In 2003, ESSEC also received accreditation for all its programs from <a>EQUIS</a> (European Quality Improvement System);</p>
<li>ESSEC MSc in Management (&quot;Grande Ecole&quot;) : generally ranked 2nd in national rankings or 1st (by Le Nouvel Economiste in 2010 and 2009, by <a>L'Expansion</a> in 2008, 2007 and 2006).</li>

<p>ESSEC's flagship postgraduate programme is its Master of Science in Management, designed for students with no professional experience (instead of managers with 3&#x2013;5 years of experience like US or UK <a>MBA</a> programs).
The diploma is only delivered upon completion of a required amount of time spent in internships.</p>
<p>It takes place between May and June.
Most of the students who sit for the entrance examination come from the French &#x201c;<a>classes pr&#x00e9;paratoires</a>&#x201d;, also called &#x201c;classes pr&#x00e9;pa&#x201d;.
<p>ESSEC, along with many other business schools, tests the candidates on their knowledge in mathematics, economics, history, philosophy, general culture and languages.
The candidates must also pass a series of oral exams in order to enter the school.</p>

<p>The &quot;Institut de Management Hotelier International&quot;'s MBA in International <a>Hospitality Management</a>, originally a joint-degree between ESSEC and <a>Cornell</a> (although it is now only operated by ESSEC), prepares future managers:</p>
<p>- to reach the higher levels of management in independently owned hotels, resort developments, consulting activities (project development, market research, quality assurance, asset valuation), retirement communities, product sales (equipment, food, hardware and software), and family and entrepreneurial businesses.</p>
<p>Since its foundation in 1995, the MBA specialized in <a>Luxury</a> <a>Brand Management</a> has become a global reference in graduate education in the luxury industry, and is still today the only MBA specialized in luxury brand management in the world.
Thanks to partnerships with leading brands and institutions, the ESSEC MBA Luxe trains a new breed of managers at the heart of the ever-changing luxury sector.</p>
