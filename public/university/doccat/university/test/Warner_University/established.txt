<h2>History</h2>
<p>Warner Southern College was founded in 1968 by the Southeastern Association of the <a>Church of God</a> in <a>Anderson</a>, <a>Indiana</a>.
In 2008, the name was changed to Warner University.</p>
<p>Warner's  campus is located  miles south of <a>Lake Wales, Florida</a>, in the geographic center of the state between <a>Tampa</a> and <a>Orlando</a>.
<p>Warner is accredited by the Commission on Colleges of the <a>Southern Association of Colleges and Schools</a>, which is recognized by the <a>US Department of Education</a>.[<a>http://www.chea.org</a>]
The HEART Institute is a simulated Third World village which trains students in <a>appropriate technology</a> as well as giving them an opportunity to live in an environment similar to many underdeveloped parts of the world.</p>
The school is a member of <a>The Sun Conference</a> and the <a>National Association of Intercollegiate Athletics</a> (NAIA).
The Athletic Department qualified in the NAIA as a Champions of Character institution for three years in a row.
