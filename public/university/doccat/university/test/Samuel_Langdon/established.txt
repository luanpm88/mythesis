<p><strong>Samuel Langdon</strong> (January 12, 1723 &ndash; November 29, 1797) was a <a>U.S.</a> <a>Congregational</a> <a>clergyman</a> and educator.
After serving as pastor in <a>Portsmouth, New Hampshire</a>, he was appointed <a>president</a> of <a>Harvard University</a> in 1774.
<p>Born in <a>Boston, Massachusetts</a> in 1723,   Langdon attended <a>Boston Latin School</a> and graduated from Harvard in 1740.
While teaching in Portsmouth, New Hampshire, he studied <a>theology</a>, and was licensed to preach.
In 1745, he was appointed chaplain of a regiment, and was present at the capture of <a>Fortress Louisbourg</a>.
On his return, he was appointed assistant to Reverend James Fitch of the North Church of Portsmouth.
He was ordained as pastor in 1747, and continued in that charge till 1774, when he became president of Harvard.
<p>At Harvard, his ardent patriotism led him to adopt measures that were obnoxious to the <a>Tory</a> students, and although he endeavored to administer the government of the college with justice, his resignation was virtually compelled in 1780.
The following year, he became pastor of the <a>Congregational church</a> at Hampton Falls, New Hampshire.
<p>In 1788, he was a delegate to the New Hampshire convention that adopted the <a>Constitution of the United States</a>, often led its debates, and did much to remove prejudice against the Constitution.
<p>Langdon was distinguished as a scholar and theologian, and exerted a wide influence in his community.
The <a>University of Aberdeen</a> gave him the degree of <a>Doctor of Divinity</a> in 1762.
He was a member of the <a>American Academy of Arts and Sciences</a> from its foundation.
He published <em>Summary of Christian Faith and Practice</em> (1768); <em>Observations on the Revelations</em> (1791); <em>Remarks on the Leading Sentiments of Dr. Hopkins's System of Doctrines</em> (1794) and many sermons.
In 1761, in connection with Colonel <a>Joseph Blanchard</a>, he prepared and published a map of New Hampshire.
<p>Langdon died in <a>Hampton Falls, New Hampshire</a> on 29 November 1797.</p>
