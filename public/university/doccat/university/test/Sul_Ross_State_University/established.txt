It was founded in 1917 as <strong>Sul Ross Normal College</strong> and was made a university in 1969.
<p>Sul Ross State University offers certificate programs and associate, bachelor's and master's degrees.
The main campus is situated in the unique environment of the <a>Big Bend</a> region and is the primary institution of higher education serving a 19-county area in far <a>West Texas</a>.
SRSU has branch campuses in  <a>Uvalde</a>, <a>Del Rio</a>, and <a>Eagle Pass</a> (see below).
<p>The university is governed by the Board of Regents of the <a>Texas State University System</a>, which guides seven universities in the state.</p>

<h2>History</h2>
<p>The university is named in honor of <a>Lawrence Sullivan &quot;Sul&quot; Ross</a>, the son of a pioneer Texas family who went on to become a Texas Ranger, a Confederate States Army general during the American Civil War, the 19th Governor of Texas (USA), and a president of the Agricultural and Mechanical College of Texas, now called Texas A&amp;M University   .</p>
<p>The now <a>Lubbock</a>-based <a>West Texas Historical Association</a> has on occasion held its annual meeting at Sul Ross.</p>
Sul Ross State defeated UCLA and Long Beach State, respectively, in the championship matches.</p>
<p>In 2007, it was announced that 59-year-old <a>Mike Flynt</a> would be joining the Sul Ross football team.
He had played college football when he was originally enrolled in college and his <a>athletic eligibility</a> had not expired.
<h2>Rio Grande College of Uvalde, Del Rio, and Eagle Pass</h2>
<p>Sul Ross State University operates Rio Grande College on the campuses of <a>Southwest Texas Junior College</a> in <a>Uvalde</a>, <a>Del Rio</a> and <a>Eagle Pass</a>.
