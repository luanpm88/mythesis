<p><strong>The Hartt School</strong> is a school of performing arts located in <a>West Hartford, Connecticut</a>, <a>United States</a>.
The Hartt School is a professional school within the <a>University of Hartford</a>.
It was founded in 1920 by Julius Hartt and Moshe Paranov.
In 1957, it became one of the three original constituent schools of the University; this school of performing arts offers undergraduate and graduate degrees in music, dance, and theatre, and associated disciplines, and operates a community arts school.</p>
<h2>History</h2>
<p>Julius Hartt and Moshe Paranov founded the Hartt  School in 1920.
Both Moshe and Pauline Paranov were associated with the school for the rest of their lives.
Paranov was associate director of the Hartt School until 1932, when he became dean.
He became president of the school in 1957 after its affiliation with the University.
Pauline was the principal seamstress for many of the elaborate costumes used in Hartt productions.
Elizabeth Warner Paranov organized her late husband's papers and donated them to the University.
Moshe Paranov was well known throughout Greater Hartord and contributed enormously to the musical life of the community.</p>
<p>Before the College was built on its present campus &quot;Julius Hartt, Moshe Paranov and Associated Teachers,&quot; as they were known, occupied a small school in another part of Hartford.
Moshe was instrumental in the growth of the Hartt school and was known for calling on major business persons requesting funding for his school.

<p>The Hartt Music Education Division, founded in the 1940s by composer <a>Julia Smith</a>, is headed by Dr. John Feierabend, who is considered one of the leading authorities on music and movement development in early childhood.
Instrumental Music Ed students are required to teach at the &quot;String Project&quot; and &quot;Band Project&quot; held on campus at the University's Magnet School which is themed on Howard Gardener's Multiple Intelligences as well as direct rehearsals of the Wind Ensemble at Simsbury High School.
Students interact with internationally recognized jazz musicians in special workshops and master classes.
Students also interact with Hartford's community-based &quot;Artists Collective,&quot; an organization dedicated to the promotion of African and Caribbean art forms.</p>
Students train in classical (Vaganova) and contemporary ballet and modern (Graham, Lim&#x00f3;n, and Taylor) styles with additional coursework in music, dance composition, pedagogy, kinesiology, repertory, technology in dance, and dance history.
The Full Force Dance Company is in residence at the Hartt School.</p>
<p>The Theatre division has a &quot;partnership in training&quot; with <a>Hartford Stage</a> Company and the <a>Goodspeed Opera House</a>.
<li><strong>Foot in the Door Ensemble</strong></li>
<p>Foot in the Door is an ensemble that plays new and contemporary music.
<p>Capitol Winds is a symphonic band composed of students from the University of Hartford as well as members of the Greater Hartford community.</p>
<p>The Vocal Division has five choral ensembles open to Hartt students as well as students from outside of The Hartt School.</p>
<p>A treble choir of SSA with 25 to 30 voices that performs a variety of music for female voices, both a capella and accompanied repertoire.
<p>A mixed choir of SATB with 45 to 60 singers that performs a variety of both mixed-choir and men&#x2019;s or women&#x2019;s repertoire.</p>
<p>A mixed chorus for SATB with 35 to 45 singers that performs a wide variety of a cappella and accompanied repertoire from the Baroque period to the present day in four to six concerts each year.</p>
<p>For nearly four decades, the growth of The Hartt School was nurtured by the philanthropy of Alfred C. Fuller, founder of the famous Fuller Brush Company.
Support from the Fuller family enabled Hartt to build the Alfred C. Fuller Music Center, the four-story building that houses The Hartt School on the University of Hartford's main campus.
In addition, the Center houses faculty offices, a community room, a bank, and a cafe.
<p>The Hartt School's faculty perform, teach, and present all over the country and around the world.
