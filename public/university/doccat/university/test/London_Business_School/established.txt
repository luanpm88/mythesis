<p><strong>London Business School</strong> (LBS) is an international <a>business school</a> and a constituent college of the <a>University of London</a>, located in central London, beside <a>Regent's Park</a>.
LBS teaches <a>postgraduate</a> programmes in finance and management, in addition to its <a>MBA</a> flagship program, Sloan Fellowship Program for experienced business executives, Masters in Finance (also known as MiF, a finance specialist programme), Masters in Management for students with less than a year's work experience, PhD, as well as non-masters programmes for business executives.
It was established in 1964, after the Franks Report recommended the establishment of two business schools, as part of existing universities (London Business School and <a>Manchester Business School</a>), but with considerable autonomy.
It has close collaborations with the nearby <a>University College London</a> and the Modern Language Centre at <a>King's College London</a>.
In 2010, London Business School was ranked number one in the world for its MBA programme by the <em><a>Financial Times</a></em>.
In 2009 the school shared this ranking with <a>The Wharton School</a> of the <a>University of Pennsylvania</a>.
The school's admissions process at London Business School is highly selective, making it is one of the most competitive business schools in the world.
A further 3,000-plus executives attend the School executive education programmes each year.
<p>London Business School holds the <a>European Foundation for Management Development</a> <a>Equis</a> accreditation as well as that of the <a>AACSB</a>.

<h2>Masters in Management</h2>
<p>In September 2009 the school launched an eleven month <a>masters degree</a> in management aimed at students with less than one year of work experience.
The programme aims to provide a strong foundation in all areas of business knowledge and the practical skills demanded by top graduate recruiters.</p>
<p>The programme is completed as a series of compulsory modules with lectures, workshops, guest speakers, case studies and group and individual project work.
The content of the degree resembles the first year of the school's MBA programme.</p>
The School offers a portfolio of 31 <a>Executive Education</a>  programmes in general management, strategy, leadership, marketing, human resources and finance.
<p>London Business School offer Open Programmes for individuals in General Management, Strategy, Leadership, Marketing, Human Resources and Finance.</p>
<p>Additionally, The Centre for Management Development (CMD) at London Business School designs and delivers Custom Programmes for groups of executives in organisations around the world.
<p>The LBS campus is located in <a>Marylebone</a>, on the perimeter of <a>Regent's Park</a> and a short walk from <a>Baker Street</a> underground station.
