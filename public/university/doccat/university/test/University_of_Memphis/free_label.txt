<p><em>The Daily Helmsman</em>, the independent daily newspaper on the campus, in operation since 1925, remains a prominent student organization.
<p>Despite gradual expansion of the campus to the west and south, the campus is fairly compact and retains a park-like, tree-lined setting.
The center of the main campus comprises buildings that made up the original campus.
<p>Future plans include a regulation indoor soccer stadium and track facility, capable of hosting large-scale NCAA Division I track-and-field meets.</p>
<p>In April 2008, the student-run Environmental Action Club ran a Green Power Campaign to promote a student referendum to add a &#x201c;Green Fee&#x201d; to tuition payments to fund clean, renewable energy and other campus sustainability projects.
<h2>Athletics</h2>
<p>The editorially independent student newspaper of the University publishes 55,000 copies a day, four days a week, and employs a paid staff of more than 30 which includes an editorial team of six, more than 10 staff writers, photographers, copy editors, and other staff members during the Fall and Spring semesters.
<em>Helmsman</em> alumni have gone on to jobs at many prestigious news organizations, such as <a>The New York Times</a>, <a>Rolling Stone magazine</a>, and Southern Living magazine, among others.</p>
<p>Numerous religious centers are located on the campus, including the Wesley Foundation (a United Methodist student center), the Baptist Student Center, the University Catholic Center and Catholic Student Center, Barth House Episcopal Student Center, and Reformed University Fellowship and Memphis Hillel.
The MSS performs more than any other student ensemble on campus, and for approximately 350,000 fans each fall.
TOM II, the current Tiger mascot, attends all Tiger Football home games, and he can also be found at many other University events throughout the year as a powerful and majestic symbol of Tigers Athletics.
<p>When the University of Memphis first fielded a football team in the fall of 1912, no one had selected a nickname for the squad.
The Tiger nickname continued on with students and alumni, eventually being adopted as the official nickname for the University of Memphis in 1939.</p>
