<p> The <strong>State University of New York at Oswego</strong>, also known as <strong>Oswego State</strong> and <strong>SUNY Oswego</strong>, is a <a>public university</a> in the <a>City of Oswego</a> and <a>Town of Oswego</a>, <a>New York</a>, on the shore of <a>Lake Ontario</a>.
It is a member institution of the <a>State University of New York</a> <a>system</a>.</p>
<p>Founded in 1861 as <strong>Oswego Primary Teachers' Training School</strong> by <a>Edward Austin Sheldon</a>, in 1942 the <a>New York Legislature</a> elevated it from a <a>normal school</a> to a <a>degree</a>-granting <a>teachers' college</a>.
The <strong>New York State Teachers College at Oswego</strong> was a founding member of the State University of New York system in 1948.
In 1962 Oswego broadened its scope to become an arts and sciences institution.
SUNY Oswego now consists of four colleges an schools: the College of Liberal Arts and Sciences; the School of Business; the School of Education; and the new School of Communications, Media and the Arts, launched in 2008.
<p><a>thumbnail|right|300px|Campus as viewed from Glimmerglass Lagoon</a> Originally founded in the city of Oswego by Sheldon to train teachers to meet pressing educational needs, the college moved to its current location on the shore of Lake Ontario in 1913 after <a>Sheldon Hall</a> was constructed.
Development of the campus was planned by the architectural firm of <a>Skidmore, Owings and Merrill</a>, who designed the major buildings.</p>
Recent years have witnessed the launch of a $700 million campus-wide renovation and renewal program, with the new Campus Center acting as the social hub of campus.</p>
<p>The college's new social hub known as the Campus Center Complex, which opened in fall 2007, includes new construction and renovation of the existing Swetman/Poucher complex.
The $25.5 million  Campus Center portion, the new construction, includes a convocation hall and <a>ice arena</a>, food court, box office, fireplace lounge, breakfast nook and reservable spaces.
Academic departments in the Campus Center include English and creative writing, modern languages and literatures and philosophy, while the Office of Learning Services stands ready to assist students who need help outside the classroom.
In addition, the <a>College Honors Program</a> is located in the Campus Center.</p>

<p><a>Penfield Library</a> is an academic library that supports the instructional, research and service goals of <a>SUNY Oswego</a>, one of 64 campuses of the <a>State University of New York</a>.
The library is named after Lida S. Penfield (1873&#x2013;1956), once chair of the English department.
The current  facility opened in 1968, replacing a library of the same name in what is now Rich Hall.
The library is home to the <a>Millard Fillmore and Marshall Family Papers</a> and numerous <a>Digitized Collections</a> including the Emergency Refugee Shelter at Fort Ontario (Safe Haven) and the <a>Millard Fillmore</a> papers.</p>
<p>Penfield Librarians provide Research help for SUNY Oswego students through <a>&quot;Ask a Librarian&quot;</a> services in a variety of methods, including chat, e-mail, phone, in-person, and through extended appointments with subject librarians.</p>

<li><strong>Bridge Street Run</strong> - The <a>Bridge Street Run</a> is a <a>pub crawl</a> that now takes place during the spring semester the last Friday before finals week starts.
Students put on white t-shirts, start at the Front Door Tavern on East 10th and Utica Streets, and make their way down Bridge Street (<a>New York State Route 104</a>) in the City of <a>Oswego</a>.
The event has been a tradition in various forms at SUNY Oswego for over 30 years.
The college officially discourages the practice.</li>
