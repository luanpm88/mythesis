<p><strong>Agnes Scott College</strong> (commonly known as <strong>Agnes Scott</strong>) is a private undergraduate college in the United States.
<p>According to the 2010 <em><a>US News and World Report</a></em>, Agnes Scott is ranked the 59th best liberal arts college in the country.
<p><a>Princeton Review</a>'s 2011 <em>The Best 373 Colleges</em> ranks the college as follows:&lt;br&gt; No. 3 for &quot;Easiest Campus to Get Around&quot; (ASC&#x2019;s second consecutive year in Top 10) &lt;br&gt; No. 8 for &quot;Town Gown Relations Are Great&quot; (ASC&#x2019;s third consecutive year in Top 10) &lt;br&gt; No. 10 for &quot;Best Quality of Life&quot; &lt;br&gt; No. 19 for &quot;Don&#x2019;t Inhale&quot; &lt;br&gt; No. 20 for &quot;Stone Cold Sober&quot; (ASC traditionally places Top 20 in this category)</p>
<li>In 2009, Agnes Scott students were recognized for excellence in many fields, with four Fulbright Scholars; two Goldwater Scholars (the same as Harvard ), recognizing work in mathematics, engineering or science; one Truman Scholar for public service leadership potential; one Kemper Scholar providing career development in business administration and one Jessie Ball DuPont Fellowship providing two-year work and study in philanthropy.</li>
