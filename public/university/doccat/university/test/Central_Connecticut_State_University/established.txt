<p><strong>Central Connecticut State University</strong>, a state university in <a>New Britain</a>, is the oldest public university in Connecticut.
<p>The school was moved to its present campus in 1922.
<h2>History</h2>
<h2>History</h2>
<p>1849:  Founded as New Britain Normal School to train teachers (6th normal school in US)    1867&#x2013;69: Closed due to opposition in <a>Connecticut General Assembly</a>.
1930: State Board of Education extends normal school curriculum from two to three years   1933: General Assembly creates Teachers College of Connecticut.
1983: Renamed Central Connecticut State University</p>
<li><a>Association of American Colleges &amp; Universities</a>: one of 16 &quot;<a>Leadership Institutions</a>&quot; in the nation</li>
<li>Honors Program called &quot;Absolutely Outstanding&quot; in <a>Princeton Review</a>'s &quot;The Best Northeastern Colleges&quot;, 2006 edition</li>
<li>2001, first institution to offer an online Master of Science in data mining.</li>
<li>Recognized by the NAACP in 2008 as an official stop on the underground railroad.</li>
<p>CCSU is noteworthy for its politically active student body.
The CCSU College Republicans generally serve the conservative/right-leaning students, hosting events including forums and speeches.
<p>CCSU is one of the founding institutions of the <a>Hartford, Connecticut</a>, <a>Springfield, Massachusetts</a> regional economic and cultural partnership alliance known as New England's <a>Knowledge Corridor</a>.</p>
