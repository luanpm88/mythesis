<p>  The <strong>Joe Kubert School of Cartoon and Graphic Art</strong>, or <strong>Joe Kubert School</strong>, located in <a>Dover</a>, <a>New Jersey</a>, is a three-year <a>technical school</a> that teaches the principles of <a>sequential art</a> and the particular craft of the <a>comics</a> industry as well as commercial <a>illustration</a>.
The school's instructors are full-time professionals working in the industry, many of them graduates of the school themselves, and the instruction is hands-on and practical.
The school has a reputation for demanding and intensive coursework, and has a dropout rate of roughly 30%.</p>
<p>Founded in September <a>1976</a> by <a>cartoonist</a> <a>Joe Kubert</a> (and his wife Muriel), it was and still is the only accredited school devoted entirely to cartooning.
Its first graduating class of <a>1978</a> included <a>Stephen R. Bissette</a>, <a>Thomas Yeates</a>, and <a>Rick Veitch</a>, and its subsequent alumni include many other successful and well-known comics <a>penciler</a>s and <a>inker</a>s.</p>
Students cannot drop classes and if they fail one class, they fail the entire year.
Each class is two hours and forty-five minutes.
Classes start at 8:30 AM and end at 2:45 PM, with a forty-five minute lunch from 11:15 AM to 12:00 PM.</p>

<li><strong>&quot;Humor &amp; Caricature I&quot;</strong> teaches how to <a>caricature</a> humans, as well as production of humorous <a>comic strip</a>s.

<li><strong>&quot;Design I&quot;</strong> teaches fundamentals of <a>design</a>.

<li><strong>&quot;Lettering&quot;</strong> teaches comic book <a>lettering</a>, in both <a>dialogue</a> and <a>sound effect</a> fonts.

<li><strong>&quot;Layout I&quot;</strong> teaches the fundamentals of <a>layout</a> and <a>composition</a>.

Taught by <a>Adam Kubert</a> (&quot;<a>X-Men</a>,&quot; &quot;<a>The Hulk</a>&quot;, &quot;<a>Superman</a>&quot;) and Fernando Ruiz.</li>

<li><strong>&quot;Human Figure I&quot;</strong> is another life drawing class.

Taught by <a>Ian Dorian</a> and <a>Ravindra Rana</a>.</li>
<li><strong>&quot;Color Illustration I&quot;</strong> teaches <a>color theory</a> and how to produce <a>painting</a>s in such media as <a>acrylic paint</a> and <a>watercolor</a>.
Taught by <a>Toby Cypress</a> (&quot;<a>The Tourist</a>,&quot; &quot;<a>Rodd Racer</a>&quot;).</li>

<li><strong>&quot;Business of Cartoon Art I&quot;</strong> teaches how to market oneself as an artist, and how to look for and secure jobs.
<li><strong>&quot;Humor &amp; Caricature III&quot;</strong> continues caricature lessons and production of humorous comic strips.
<li><strong>&quot;Advanced Techniques and the Development of Style&quot;</strong> teaches advanced techniques in art, including composition, layout, design, and rendering.
<li><strong>&quot;Sequential Art&quot;</strong> is the third Narrative Art class, and actual sequential pages make up roughly 90% of the assignments.

<li><strong>&quot;Writing&quot;</strong> teaches how to write scripts for comics, and how to develop characters and plots.
<li><strong>&quot;Color Illustration II&quot;</strong> continues the lesson on color theory and painting.
Taught by Toby Cypress and Ravindra Rana.</li>
<li><strong>&quot;Methods and Materials III&quot;</strong> continues the lesson on painting.
Oil painting is also covered in this class.
The class is on basic lessons in cartooning, and is taught by <a>Fernando Ruiz</a> and <a>Fabio Redivo</a>.</p>
