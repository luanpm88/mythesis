<p><strong>Edinburgh Napier University</strong> is a <a>university</a> in <a>Edinburgh</a>, <a>Scotland</a>.
<h2>History</h2>
<p>Edinburgh Napier University was opened as <strong>Napier Technical College</strong> in 1964, taking its name from <a>John Napier</a>, the inventor of <a>logarithms</a>, who was born at <a>Merchiston Castle</a> &#x2014; the site of the University's <a>Merchiston</a> campus.
Since 1971, it has offered <a>degree</a>-level education.
Three years later, it merged with the <a>Sighthill</a>-based Edinburgh College of Commerce to form <strong>Napier College of Commerce and Technology</strong>, which became a <a>Central Institution</a> in 1985.</p>
<p>The college was renamed <strong>Napier Polytechnic</strong> in 1986 and in the same year acquired the former <a>Hydropathic hospital</a> buildings at <a>Craiglockhart</a>. It gained full <a>university</a> status in June 1992 as <strong>Napier University</strong>.
In 1994, Napier University acquired its <a>Craighouse campus</a>.
In 1996, the university gained a new Faculty of Health Studies through a merger between the Scottish Borders College of Nursing and Lothian College of Health Studies.</p>
<p>In February 2009 the University launched its new title, Edinburgh Napier University, to reflect its location in Scotland's capital city.<a>thumb|left|150px|Edinburgh
Napier University Tartan</a></p>
The award was made for 'Innovative housing construction for environmental benefit and quality of life', recognising the contribution made by the University's Building Performance Centre towards improving sound insulation between attached dwellings.</p>
<p>The motto of the University, <em>Nisi sapientia frustra</em> (meaning &quot;Everything is in vain without knowledge&quot;) echoes the motto of the <a>City of Edinburgh</a>, <em>Nisi Dominus frustra</em> (meaning &quot;Everything is in vain without the LORD&quot;).
Previously the university used the Clan Napier Tartan; the Chief of Clan Napier welcomed the new University tartan.</p>
It offers a range of creative courses, including film, graphic design, acting, publishing and product design.
<p>Edinburgh Napier's Business School has achieved Chartered Management Institute (CMI) certification and was rated best new business school in the United Kingdom by the Guardian in 2007.
The University was also named the best University in Scotland for graduate level employment according to the Sunday Times University Guide 2007.
The School of Computing at Napier University is the largest computing department in Scotland.</p>
<p>Edinburgh Skillset Screen and Media Academy (ESSAMA) is one of only three dual-status Skillset Screen &amp; Media Academies in the UK.
A collaboration between Edinburgh Napier University and Edinburgh College of Art (eca), the Academy is recognised by the industry as a UK centre of excellence in film practice education.
<p>The university is based around its <a>Merchiston</a>, Craighouse, <a>Craiglockhart</a> and <a>Sighthill</a> campuses.
<p><strong>Sighthill Campus</strong> The <a>new Sighthill Campus</a> opened to students in the Faculty of Health, Life &amp; Social Sciences in January 2011.
The campus has received the BREEAM excellence rating.
<p>The Sighthill campus is also home to a new sports facility which includes a biomechanics laboratory and an environmental chamber which can recreate high altitude conditions with controllable temperature and humidity levels to simulate varying climatic conditions.</p>
It incorporates the <a>Craiglockhart Hydropathic Hospital</a> buildings which were for a time known as Craiglockhart War Hospital, where <a>First World War</a> poets <a>Wilfred Owen</a> and <a>Siegfried Sassoon</a> were treated.
The Craiglockhart Campus was refurbished in 2004 and contains two lecture theatres, language labs and computing facilities.</p>
It is built around the refurbished shell of <a>Merchiston Castle</a>, the family home of John Napier, after whom the University is named.
Merchiston Castle is also the ancient seat of <a>Clan Napier</a>.
It also incorporates the 500-seat <a>Jack Kilby</a> Computing Centre, named after the inventor of integrated circuits and the handheld calculator.</p>
The union building also contains the office of the sabbatical officers and Independent Student Advice Service advisors.</p>
<p><strong>Craighouse Campus</strong> The Craighouse Campus is based around the former <a>Thomas Clouston</a> Clinic which was a psychiatric hospital until 1990.
A tender process is currently underway for the sale of the Craighouse campus.</p>
<p>Edinburgh Napier has nine dedicated Institutes of Research and Innovation.</p>
<p>Edinburgh Napier has been ranked the top university in Scotland for its environmental credentials by the People and Planet Green League 2010.
The university was also awarded the Carbon Trust Standard in 2010 for achieving a 19% reduction in carbon emissions between 1 AUgust 06 - 31 July 2009.</p>
<p>The Edinburgh, Lothians, Fife and Borders Regional Articulation Hub (ELRAH), led by Edinburgh Napier, comprises 15 university and college partners.
<p>Edinburgh Napier also has articulation agreements and partnerships to deliver courses with higher education institutions in China, Hong Kong, and Malaysia.
The university also has offices in Beijing and Hyderabad.</p>
<p>In 2010, the University announced a collaboration with global education provider Navitas to found <a>Edinburgh International College (EIC)</a>, an on-campus associate college.
EIC provides degree level and Pre-Masters pathways in a range of subject areas, including Accounting and Finance, Business, Tourism and Computing.</p>
<p>Edinburgh Napier University's <a>students' union</a> is called Napier Students' Association.</p>
It was founded as a tabloid newspaper in 1993 by <a>Neil McIntosh</a>.
<p>The Students' Association supports a variety of sporting and cultural societies.</p>
