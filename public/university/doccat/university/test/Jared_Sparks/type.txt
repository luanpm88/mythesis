Sparks's extensive conversations and subsequent correspondence informed Tocqueville's best-known work, <em><a>Democracy in America</a></em>.</p>
<p>He also edited the <em>Library of American Biography</em>, in two series (10 and 15 vols respectively, 1834-1838, 1844-1847), to which he contributed the lives of <a>Ethan Allen</a>, <a>Benedict Arnold</a>, <a>Marquette</a>, <a>La Salle</a>, <a>Count Pulaski</a>, <a>Jean Ribault</a>, <a>Charles Lee</a> and <a>John Ledyard</a>, the last a reprint of his earlier work.</p>
