Founded in October 1898 by Abbot Bernard Pennings, a <a>Norbertine</a> priest and educator, the school was named after Saint <a>Norbert of Xanten</a>.
<h2>History</h2>
Norbert College was established when Abbot Bernard Pennings, a Dutch immigrant priest from the <a>Premonstratensian</a> abbey of <a>Bern (Netherlands)</a>, founded the college to train young men for the priesthood.
Frances I. Van Dyke, a seminarian, was the first and, at the time, the only student.
St. Norbert is the first and only institution of higher learning in the world sponsored by the Premonstratensian order.
Abbot Pennings later started a commerce program at the college for lay students before retiring in 1955.</p>
With his strong fiscal management abilities, Dr. Webb established the first substantial permanent endowment for the school.</p>
Under his leadership, the enrollment topped 2000, and extensive renovations and construction activities expanded the college's physical facilities.</p>
<a>Thomas Kunkel</a>, former Dean of the <a>Philip Merrill College of Journalism</a> at the <a>University of Maryland, College Park</a>, became the seventh president of the college on July 1, 2008.</p>
Norbert College offers undergraduate programs in more than 40 areas of study, leading to a <a>Bachelor of Arts</a>, <a>Bachelor of Science</a>, <a>Bachelor of Music</a>, or <a>Bachelor of Business Administration</a> degree.
A <a>Bachelor of Science in Nursing</a> degree is also offered through a joint effort with the <a>Bellin College of Nursing</a>.
In addition to its undergraduate offerings, St. Norbert College offers three <a>masters-level</a> graduate programs in education, theological studies, and liberal studies.</p>
Norbert College has a student-to-faculty ratio of 14:1 and an average class size 22.
<p>Over the past 15 years, St. Norbert has been ranked as one of the top five comprehensive (bachelor's-level) colleges in the Midwest, by <em><a>U.S.News &amp; World Report</a></em>.
The college is also listed among the &quot;best in the Midwest&quot; by <a>Princeton Review</a>.</p>
<p>In 2009, St. Norbert was ranked 151st of out 600 by <a>Forbes</a> on their list of America's Best Colleges.</p>
<p> The campus consists of , much of which borders the Fox River.
Students typically walk to classes, even in the winter.
Directly behind the Campus Center is a pavilion and docks where St. Norbert hosts its fall Back to School Festival, a kick off to the new school year, with fireworks, a picnic, and live entertainment on the Fox River waterfront.
Gries Hall was recently added to SNC campus, as well as a new library.</p>
The Baer mall, in front of Main Hall, is another scenic location.</p>
<p>More than 74% of students live on campus in residence halls, apartments and townhouses.
St. Norbert requires all traditional undergraduate students not registered as commuters to live on-campus to strengthen the bond of community.
St. Norbert encourages its students to become involved in their community through community service, and also by participating in one of the 16 fraternities, sororities, and independent social groups.
A major activity for St. Norbert students participate in is the annual &quot;Into The Streets&quot; community service project that provides service to organizations in De Pere and neighboring cities.
<p>Greek life at St. Norbert includes four sororities and four fraternities, as well as two Greek governing groups.
Greek groups collectively completed a total of 1,385 hours of service during the 2006-2007 school year.</p>
<p><strong>&#x0391;&#x0394;&#x0393;</strong> <a>Alpha Delta Gamma</a>  recently reactivated in the '09-'10 school year, and will celebrate it's rechartering in the Fall 2010 semester.
<p><strong>&#x0394;&#x03a5;</strong> <a>Delta Upsilon</a> was started May 18, 1992 by 14 individuals.
This fraternity has sponsored food drives for Paul&#x2019;s Pantry, raised money for its nationally-affiliated <a>Boys and Girls Club</a>, participated in <a>Relay for Life</a>, and recorded 130 service hours in 2007 with 18 members.</p>
<p><strong>&#x0394;&#x03a6;&#x0395;</strong> <a>Delta Phi Epsilon</a> International Sorority, founded in 1917, is a sorority dedicated to diversity, known as one of the first sororities not to discriminate based on ethnicity, race, or creed.</p>
<p><strong>&#x039a;&#x0392;&#x0393;</strong> <a>Kappa Beta Gamma</a> helps with multiple causes, including the <a>National Multiple Sclerosis Society</a>, Relay for Life, and <a>Red Cross</a> blood drives.
This group is the oldest sorority on campus founded in 1954.
It is active in philanthropies, including serving at the House of Hope in <a>Green Bay</a>.</p>
<p>The St. Norbert College &quot;Green Knights&quot; participate in <a>NCAA</a> <a>Division III</a> athletics and have been members of the <a>Midwest Conference</a> since 1982.
Norbert's men's hockey team competes in the <a>Northern Collegiate Hockey Association</a>.
Since his arrival the Green Knights have enjoyed a 100-17 Midwest Conference record, while competing in the national playoffs seven of those years.</p>
<p>The <a>Green Bay Packers</a> have conducted training camp on the St. Norbert campus since 1958, making this the league's longest training camp relationship between a team and school.
St. Norbert hosted <a>Fordham University</a>, the alma mater of legendary Packers coach <a>Vince Lombardi</a>, in 1982.
