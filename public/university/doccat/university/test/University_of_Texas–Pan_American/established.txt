<p> <strong>The University of Texas&#x2013;Pan American</strong> (also referred to as <strong>UTPA</strong> or <strong>UT&#x2013;Pan Am</strong>) is part of <a>The University of Texas System</a>.
Located in <a>Edinburg, Texas</a> (Hidalgo County), it serves the <a>Rio Grande Valley</a> and southern Texas with baccalaureate, masters-level, and doctoral degrees.
Since the University&#x2019;s founding in 1927, it has grown from 200 students to over 17,000, making UTPA the <a>tenth-largest</a> university in the state of <a>Texas</a>.
UTPA also operates an Upper Level Studies Center in Rio Grande City, Starr County, Texas.</p>
<h2>History</h2>
Founded in 1927 as a two-year <a>junior college</a> administered by the <a>Edinburg School District</a>.</li>
Designated as a <a>junior college</a> in 1933 and admitted to the <a>Association of Colleges and Secondary Schools of Southern States</a>.</li>
<a>Hidalgo County</a> permitted to hold referendum for a four-year university in 1951; school became <strong>Pan American Regional College</strong> on December 20, 1951.
Became 22nd member institution of the Texas System of Colleges and Universities in 1965, as a state <a>senior college</a>.
Achieved full university status and changed name to PAU.
Established second campus at <a>Brownsville</a> in 1973 (now <a>University of Texas at Brownsville</a>).
In December 1988, board members reached merger agreement with the <a>University of Texas System</a> pending state legislative approval (one of a series of similar mergers among state universities during that time period), granted in September, 1989.</li>
Adopted present name subsequent to merger, preserving the nearly 40-year legacy of the Pan American name.</li>
The stadium was completed in 2001.</p>
The center opened in 2003 and was rededicated and moved to a new location in 2009.

As part of its <a>mission statement</a>, the university offers a wide variety of degrees spanning across 6 colleges.
<p>US News and World report rates UTPA as a tier 2 University.
Among public schools in Texas, UTPA ranked only behind the University of Texas and Texas A&amp;M.</p>
<p>Historically UTPA has had open enrollment such that any student to gradate from an approved public or private high school was granted admission to the University.
Any student who gradates from a recognized Texas high school as a member of the top 10% or his/her graduating class is guaranteed admission.</p>
<p>In 1997 UTPA started the University Scholars Program in an attempt to retain top local high school students.
The first tier is high school graduation standing.
The proportion of students who receive some sort of financial aid is 86%.</p>

Jackson was selected 4th overall during the 1964 NBA draft.
He played on the 1964-65 NBA all rookie team and played in the NBA All Star game that same year.
Jackson played his entire career with the <a>Philadelphia 76ers</a>.</p>
<p>The basketball program, during the mid 1970's was coached by <a>Abe Lemons</a>.
Coach Lemons was later hired away by the University of Texas at Austin.</p>
<p>Upon Lemons' departure to the <a>University of Texas</a>, <a>Bill White</a> was named the head basketball coach.
<p>The basketball program was later coached by <a>Lon Kruger</a>, who later went on to coach at <a>Kansas State University</a>, <a>University of Florida</a>, <a>University of Illinois</a> and <a>University of Nevada, Las Vegas</a>.
He also coached the <a>Atlanta Hawks</a> of the <a>NBA</a>.</p>
<p>UTPA baseball squad placed 4th in the 1971 College World Series under Coach <a>Al Ogletree</a>.</p>
<p>It has been announced that the Broncs would be charter members of the previously football-only <a>Great West Conference</a> when it begins all-sports play in 2008.
During the fall of 2008, the UTPA Cross Country team was the first to win the Great West Conference team title.
UTPA was previously a member of the <a>Sun Belt Conference</a> from 1991-1998.</p>
