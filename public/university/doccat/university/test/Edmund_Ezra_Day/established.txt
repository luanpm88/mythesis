<p>Day received his undergraduate and master's degrees from <a>Dartmouth College</a> and his <a>doctorate</a> in <a>economics</a> from <a>Harvard</a>.
He went on to serve as the fifth president of <a>Cornell University</a> from 1937 to 1949.
While in office, he helped establish the <a>School of Industrial and Labor Relations</a> at Cornell.</p>
<p>The administrative building at Cornell, Day Hall, is named after Edmund Ezra Day.
He was interred in <a>Sage Chapel</a> on Cornell's campus.
