<p><strong>Marmara University</strong> (<a>Turkish</a> <em>Marmara &#x00dc;niversitesi</em>) is a public university in <a>Turkey</a>.
Situated in <a>Istanbul</a>, Marmara University has succeeded in becoming the second largest university in the whole country.
The University's <a>mission</a> is to impart and promote knowledge and values consistent with universal standards, to meet the technological, social and cultural challenges of a global society, to contribute to the creation of an informed society, which learns about and shares this knowledge and values and to emphasize the <a>Europe</a>an dimension in the delivery of educational opportunities.

<h2>History</h2>
<p>Marmara University  is an institution with roots extending back into the 19th century when the <a>Hamidiye College</a> of Higher Commercial Education was established in the <a>Ca&#x011f;alo&#x011f;lu</a> district of <a>Istanbul</a> on January 16, 1883.
At the time, it was the only leading higher education institution for studies in commerce and economics.
13 students were the first graduates in 1887.
The School joined the Ministry of Education in 1889, but was abolished in 1893 to be renovated and re-opened later.
It was re-opened on October 15, 1897 and has carried out its educational programs ever since.
<p>From 1923 to 1959, the college was called the Higher Education School of Economics and Commerce and became the <a>Istanbul Academy of Economic and Commercial Sciences</a> in 1959.
Marmara University was formally established in 1982, when the <a>Istanbul Academy of Economic and Commercial Sciences</a> was consolidated with several schools of higher education from different fields offering undergraduate-level study in <a>medicine</a>, <a>dentistry</a>, <a>law</a>, and <a>fine arts</a>.
To these were added faculties of arts and sciences, education, and a school of foreign languages to provide a full complement of university courses.
Today, the university consists of 14 faculties, 9 vocational schools of higher education and 11 graduate institutes.
<p><a>thumbnail|left|267px|Marmara University Entrance</a> Marmara University with more than 3000 distinguished faculty members and a student body reaching 60.000, located in several different campuses scattered throughout the cosmopolitan city of <a>Istanbul</a>, following a great tradition it has been one of the leading institutions of higher education in <a>Turkey</a> for 124 years.
The university promoted itself rapidly to an international characteristic together with the Faculties of Engineering, Medicine and Dentistry where the medium of instruction being in <a>Turkish</a> and <a>English</a>, university also comprises the Faculty of Economics and Administrative Sciences which provides education in four different languages: <a>Turkish</a>, <a>English</a>, <a>French</a> and <a>German</a>, thus making Marmara University the only &#x201c;multi-lingual&#x201d; university in <a>Turkey</a>.
Having a remarkable success especially in the area of <a>Medicine</a> and <a>Law</a> both the faculty members and the students compete in their participation and dedication to academic work to enhance the educational quality and the effective implementation of scientific studies in Marmara University.</p>
<p>Marmara University not only to providing up-to-date contemporary education to fulfill the requirements of today&#x2019;s modern society with the guidance of the <a>norms of enlightenment</a> but also preparing the students for the future through participatory <a>democratic administration</a> that is in accordance with the principles set by the <a>constitution of Turkey</a>.

<p>Marmara University comprises 14 faculties, 9 vocational schools of higher education (generally providing 2-year associate bachelor degrees and 4-year Bachelors' in some cases), 11 graduate institutes and 32 research centers.
The University currently offers a total of 136 associate bachelor's and bachelor degree programmes.
Marmara University also has a Center of Continuing Education offering 20 different certificate programmes in a variety of areas, including foreign languages, computer training and initial teacher training courses.
Marmara University is unique among Turkish universities in that it is the only one that currently offers education at both undergraduate and post-graduate levels in four languages of instruction: Turkish, French, English and German.</p>

<p>[[Image:Prmarmara.JPG|thumbnail|500px|right|Presidency of Marmara University in [[Hippodrome of Constantinople|Sultanahmet]] Region]] Welcoming international students as well as providing education at international standards in almost all fields, Marmara University, with its long history, had rapidly became a modern and international university which hosts and participates in scientific meetings, cultural activities, art exhibitions and sports along with various other national and international events.
Marmara University is one of the few Turkish universities, which is a member of the <a>European University Association (EUA)</a>.
The Faculty of Economics and Administrative Sciences and the Institute of Social Sciences are in the process of preparation for the quality assessment carried out by <a>EQUIS</a>, an international system of accreditation in management and business administration in Europe.
The Institute of Social Sciences is now an institutional member of <a>EFMD</a>, European Foundation for Management Development.
The Faculty of Law consistently participates in several <a>moot court</a> competitions around the globe such as <a>Phillip C. Jessup Moot Court Competition</a> in <a>United States</a> and <a>Willem C. Vis Moot Court Competition</a> in <a>Austria</a>.
The Faculty of Law along with the Department of Political Science and International Relations represents the university in several different <a>Model United Nations</a> programs every year.
To realize its international presence, Most recently Marmara University acts to forge links with other European universities and also with the institutions outside the EU that will allow students and researchers to access a wide range of opportunities.
Many academic units within the university have been successful in developing student/lecturer exchanges within the framework of the <a>LLP</a>, <a>Erasmus/Socrates</a> programmes offered by the <a>European Commission</a>.
Within the Faculty of Economics and Administrative Sciences, the Department of Political Science and International Relations alone has developed Erasmus Agreements with the Centre for European Studies, <a>Jagiellonian University</a>, <a>Poland</a>; Department of Political Science, <a>Stockholm University</a>, <a>Sweden</a>; Faculty of Preservation of Cultural Heritage, <a>University of Bologna</a>, <a>Italy</a>; Faculty of Political and Social Science, <a>University of Antwerp</a>, <a>Belgium</a>; Institute for Political Science, <a>University of Regensburg</a>, <a>Germany</a> and the Institute for Political Science, <a>Johannes Gutenberg University of Mainz</a>, Germany; College of Social Pedagogy, Alice Salomon Vocational College Hannover, Germany.
While the Faculty of Law has its links with <a>University of Munster</a>, <a>Free University of Berlin</a>, <a>University of Bielefeld</a>, <a>University of Cologne</a> from <a>Germany</a>, <a>University of Athens</a> from <a>Greece</a>, <a>University of Linz</a> from <a>Austria</a>, <a>Paris Descartes University</a> from <a>France</a> and <a>University of Siena</a> from <a>Italy</a>.</p>
Every year the students of Marmara University organize an 'International Week' to promote the social standings of the international community of Marmara University along with offering a wide-ranged cultural program to the guest-students invited from all across the world to the event.</p>
<h3>Efficiency in Erasmus Programme</h3>
The students who admitted to program had chances to study 1-2 terms in one of the 230 partner universities of 22 European Countries.
%95 of the admitted students applied to the undergraduate and graduate programs while other %5 had the chance of admitting to the foreign internship programs.
Mentioned students financially supported in their time abroad by the university and <a>European Commission</a>.
Due to the statistics published by the <a>EU Education and Youth Programms Center</a>, Marmara University is one of the top 3 choices among the foreigner students who have applied to the exchange programs offered in Turkey.</p>
