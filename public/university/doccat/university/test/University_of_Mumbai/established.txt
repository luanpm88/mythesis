<p>The <strong>University of Mumbai</strong> (<a>Marathi</a>: &#x092e;&#x0941;&#x0902;&#x092c;&#x0908; &#x0935;&#x093f;&#x0926;&#x094d;&#x092f;&#x093e;&#x092a;&#x0940;&#x0920;), (formerly <strong>University of Bombay</strong>), is a <a>state university</a> situated in <a>Maharashtra</a> state of <a>India</a>.
It has been given a five-star ranking by <a>NAAC</a>.
The name of the University was changed from University of Bombay to University of Mumbai according to a Government Gazette dated 4 September 1996.
It is abbreviated as either (UoM) for University of Mumbai or as (MU) for Mumbai University.
Initially, the <a>Elphinstone College</a> building was used for Mumbai University.</p>
<p>Most of the <a>college</a>s in the city of Mumbai and the districts of <a>Thane</a>, <a>Raigad</a>, <a>Ratnagiri</a> and <a>Sindhudurg</a> are affiliated with the University of Mumbai.
The University of Mumbai offers Bachelors, Masters and Doctoral degrees to students.
It has affiliations with several high ranking research institutes in the world, including <a>Veermata Jijabai Technological Institute</a> (VJTI) (formerly Victoria Jubilee Technical Institute), <a>Institute of Chemical Technology</a> (formerly UDCT), <a>Tata Institute of Fundamental Research</a> (TIFR), a <a>deemed university</a> of the University of Mumbai, and the <a>Tata Memorial Hospital</a>.

<h2>Mumbai University libraries</h2>
<p>The main Library or Central Library is Jawaharlal Nehru Library (JNL) with almost a million books (850,000), documents and <a>Scientific Journals</a>, <a>Thesis</a>, <a>Encyclopedia</a>s, along with over 30,000 <a>microfilm</a>s and over 1200 rare manuscripts, <a>IMF</a> reports, <a>census</a> records and access to several hundreds of <a>E-Journal</a>s through online subscription.
Together the University of Mumbai collection of Books and Scientific journals includes over a million documents.</p>
These include the Mumbai University Institute of Chemical Technology, formerly (<a>UDCT</a>).
The prestigious <a>Veermata Jijabai Technological Institute</a> (VJTI) located in Mumbai is the Central Technical Institute of Maharashtra State.
Xavier's College</a> was the first degree-granting college affiliated to the University.</p>
<p>A minor campus running mostly extramural courses is located in the town of <a>Ratnagiri</a>.
<p>Another large campus is situated in <a>Kalina</a>, <a>Santacruz</a> in suburban Mumbai.
It also has <a>social sciences</a> and <a>behavioral sciences</a> departments offering masters and doctoral programs, which include the Department of Economics and the Department of Psychology.
The Kalina campus also includes certain science departments including the Department of <a>Biotechnology</a>, Department of <a>Physics</a>, Department of <a>Information Technology</a> and Department of <a>Mathematics</a>, and has most of the <a>humanities</a> and language departments of Mumbai University at the masters and doctoral level.
The National Center for Nanosciences and Nanotechnology is also located in this campus along with the Department of Biophysics, a one of its kind department in Western India.
<p><a>thumb|left| Completed Rajabai Tower and University Hall, ca 1905</a>The original campus is in <a>Fort</a>, Mumbai, near the southern end of the city of Mumbai.
It is built in the <a>Gothic</a> style of architecture and includes a library that holds many original <a>manuscript</a>s.
The University of Bombay was established at the Fort Campus in 1857.
In the same year universities were established in the two other <a>Presidency</a> towns of <a>Calcutta</a> and <a>Madras</a>.
Fort Campus was one of the first educational institutions established by the British in India following <a>Sir Charles Wood</a>'s <a>Dispatch on Education</a> in 1854.</p>
