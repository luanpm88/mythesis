<p>  <strong>National Institute of Technology Calicut</strong> () or <strong>NITC</strong> is a federally funded technical university established by an <a>act</a> of the <a>Indian Parliament</a>, located at <a>Kattangal</a> in <a>Chathamangalam</a> which is situated about 22&nbsp;km north-east of <a>Kozhikode</a> (<a>Calicut</a>) City in <a>Kerala</a>, <a>India</a>.
NITC is one of the twenty <a>National Institutes of Technology</a> - one in each major state of India - established by the <a>Government of India</a> for fostering national integration by imparting technical education to students from all over the country.
NITC is among the very few institutions in the country to boast a <a>Supercomputer</a> of its own.</p>
<h2>History</h2>
<p><strong>NITC</strong> was set up in <a>1961</a> as <a>Regional Engineering College</a>, <a>Calicut</a> (CREC) as the ninth of its kind and the first one to be established during the <a>Third Five Year Plan</a> period.
Till the formation of the <a>Calicut University</a> in 1963 the institute was affiliated with the <a>Kerala University</a>.
It was largely due to the efforts of <a>Pattom Thanu Pillai</a>, then Chief Minister of Kerala that the institute came into being.
Prof.S.Rajaraman, former principal of <a>Government Engineering College, Thrissur</a> was appointed as the special officer in 1961 to organise the activities of the college till Dr. M. V. Keasva Rao took charge as the first principal of the college.
The classes were initially held at the Government Polytechnic at West Hill, Calicut before it moved to its present campus in 1963.
The college started with an annual intake of 125 students for the under-graduate courses, in a campus of .</p>
<p>The intake for the undergraduate courses was increased to 250 in 1966, 150 for the first year and 100 for the preparatory course.
The annual intake was reduced from 250 to 200 from the year 1968-69 on account of industrial recession.</p>
<p>After Prof S. Unnikrishnan Pillai took charge as Principal in 1983, the Training and Placement Department was started with a view to organise campus recruitments for students.
The college moved into the area of <a>Information Technology</a> as early as 1984 with the commissioning of multi user PSI Omni system and HCL workhorse PCs.
In 1987 the college celebrated 25 years of its existence and new postgraduate were started and the <a>CEDTI</a> was established in the campus the following year.
In 1990 <a>Shankar Dayal Sharma</a> inaugurated the <a>Architecture</a> Department Block and construction of a computer centre was completed.
In 1996, the institute website (the first in Kerala) was launched.
The <a>Indian Institute of Management Kozhikode</a> functioned from the NIT Calicut campus in its first few years of existence before moving to its new campus in <a>Kunnamangalam</a> in 2003.</p>
<p><a>left|thumb|300px|Aerial view of the Electronics Lecture Hall Complex (ELHC) at NITC.</a> <a>The Ministry of Human Resource Development</a>, Government of India, accorded NIT status to REC Calicut in June 2002 granting it academic and administrative autonomy.
It was a lead institute under the <a>World Bank</a> funded Technical Education Quality Improvement Program (TEQIP) which began in 2002.
In 2003, students were first admitted to the flagship undergraduate <a>B.Tech</a> through the <a>All India Engineering Entrance Exam</a>.
The National Institutes of Technology Act is the second legislation for technical education institutions after the Indian Institutes of Technology Act of 1961.

<p>NIT Calicut has set up the School of Management Studies (SOMS), which will initially offer the two-year Master of Business (MBA) program.
The admission to the MBA will be based on CAT scores.</p>
<p>The Union Ministry of Human Resource Development, has started IT finishing schools at NIT Calicut and seven other national institutions: the Indian Institute of Technology (IIT), Roorkee, and the National Institutes of Technology (NITs) at Durgapur, Kurushetra, Jaipur, Surathkal, Trichy and Warangal.
<p>The Institute has set up an International Liaison Office mainly to follow up the MoUs (Memorandum of Understanding) signed between NITC and other Institutions in the world and also to guide the students from abroad currently studying and those interested in joining the Institute in future.</p>
A, B, and C Hostels have triple rooms but currently accommodate four or five students per room due to increase in intake of students and shortage of hostel facilities.
Postgraduate students and research scholars reside in PG I and PG II hostels.
The total number of inmates of NITC Hostels is nearly 2,245.
A mini canteen is available in the hostel premises.
<p>An extension counter for NITC Multipurpose Co-operative Society is functioning in A and D hostels.
It also subscribes to more than 200 print journals.The institute has a <a>digital library</a>, <strong>NALANDA</strong> (Network of Automated Library and Archives), which houses a good collection of online resources.
It is also part of the <a>INDEST</a> consortium, which aims at networking the libraries at the important technical institutions in India.</p>
<p>NITC has a Supercomputer <strong>PURNA</strong> (Parallel Universal Remote Numerical Analyser) which is accessible from anywhere in the campus and is provided for the use of all students and faculty members.
<p>NIT Calicut holds two major annual events, the techno-management festival <a>Tathva</a>, held in the monsoon semester and the cultural festival <a>Ragam</a>, which is held in the winter semester.</p>
It is usually held during the month of September and lasts for four days after which the normal academic session resumes.Tathva over the years has come to be regarded as one among the biggest technical festivals of South India.It has been held every year since its inception in 2001.
<p> Ragam is the cultural festival of NITC.
Colleges and universities all over Kerala and outside the state compete with each other in events as wide ranging as trivia quizzes and dance competitions to be crowned champions of the festival.
Some of the notable performers who have lighted up the shows in previous years include Shaan, Shankar Mahadevan, Kartik, Benny Dayal, and Parikrama.
'Breathe Floyd' a Pink Floyd tribute band from UK performed during Ragam '09.Ragam'10 featured KK,Naresh Iyer and &quot;Higher On Maiden&quot; a tribute band to Iron Maiden.</p>

<p>Under the constitution of the National Institutes of Technology Act 2007, the <a>President of India</a> is the Visitor to the Institute.
The Board is headed by the <a>Chairman</a>, who is appointed by the Visitor.
The Director, who is the secretary of the Board, looks after the day-to-day running of the institute.
The Board of Governors also has nominees of the Central Government, the State Government, the NIT Council &amp; the Institute Senate.</p>
