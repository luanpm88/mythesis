<p><strong>Tompkins Cortland Community College</strong> is a public two-year college supported by  <a>Cortland</a> and <a>Tompkins</a>  Counties.
Tompkins Cortland Community College is one of 64-member institutions of the <a>State University of New York</a> system.</p>
<h2>History</h2>
<p>The College was founded in 1967 and opened in 1968 in <a>Groton</a>, New York.
A multi-million dollar construction project complete in 2007 recently added a new athletics facility, a student center, and expanded and enhanced the college's library.</p>
<p>The College has seen a steady increase in enrollment over the past decade, with now more than 3,800 students.
<p>Tompkins Cortland Community College offers more than 50 degree and certificate programs, including biotechnology, business administration, communication and media arts, computer sciences, construction technology, creative writing, criminal justice, engineering science, hotel and restaurant management, liberal arts and sciences, nursing, paralegal, photography, sport management, and wine marketing.
About half of the Tompkins Cortland students transfer to a four-year college, with <a>Binghamton University</a>, <a>Cornell University</a>, <a>Cortland State</a>, <a>Ithaca College</a>, <a>Niagara University</a>, and the <a>Rochester Institute of Technology</a> being some of more popular transfer options.</p>
The Panthers compete as a Division III member of the <a>National Junior College Athletic Association</a> (NJCAA) and as part of the <a>Mid-State Athletic Conference</a>.
TC3 offers men's soccer, basketball, baseball, lacrosse, and golf and women's soccer, volleyball, basketball, softball, and golf.
The College offers a lighted turf soccer/lacrosse field, a 1,500-seat gymnasium, an  field house, and on-campus baseball and softball parks.
Former Panthers have continued their playing careers at places like <a>Drake University</a>, <a>Wofford College</a>, <a>Post University</a>, <a>SUNY New Paltz</a>, <a>Cortland State</a>, and <a>Ithaca College</a>.
<p>TC3.biz is the College's business development and training center.
In addition to customized training, a complete schedule of dozens of non-credit professional development programs is offered to individuals.
<p>Tompkins Cortland was one of the first community colleges in New York to offer on-campus housing, beginning its residential life program in 1999 with two buildings.
The program has grown to now include seven buildings and more than 800 bedrooms.
TC3 works with school districts in Central New York allowing high school students to earn college credit while taking classes in high school.</p>
<p>The College partners with several institutions in foreign countries to provide educational opportunities on our campus to their students through the Global Connections program.
The agreements allow international students the opportunity to study in their home institution during the regular academic year and in the <a>United States</a> at TC3 for two semesters, either in the summer or during the traditional academic year.
<p>Tompkins Cortland students have the opportunity to learn in a different culture through a handful of study abroad options.
Students and community members have the chance to take faculty-led trips to <a>Costa Rica</a>, <a>Guatemala</a>, <a>Ireland</a>, <a>Nicaragua</a>, <a>Russia</a>, and <a>Ukraine</a>.</p>
