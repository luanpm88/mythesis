<p>In a given year, 800,000 people are born in France, 480,000 pass the <a>baccalaureat</a> (high school diploma), and 37,000 of them are admitted in preparatory classes according to their grades in high school.
Their intensive coursework includes Mathematics, either Economic History or Economics, Philosophy and Literature (<em>culture g&#x00e9;n&#x00e9;rale</em>), English and another foreign language.
They are public and attended by hundreds of students expecting to apply the following year as well as other candidates and professors.</p>
<p>French students who attended a <em>classe pr&#x00e9;paratoire</em> are taught fundamental managerial sciences during their first year at HEC.  Courses include <a>economics</a>, <a>accounting</a>, <a>finance</a>, <a>law</a>, <a>psychology</a>, <a>sociology</a> and <a>mathematics</a> (models, <a>operations research</a>, and <a>statistics</a>).
Second year students are also given the opportunity to spend one semester in a foreign university.
<p>There are around 130 clubs or &quot;associations&quot; on campus.
