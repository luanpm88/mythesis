<p><strong>HEC Paris</strong> or <strong>&#x00c9;cole des Hautes &#x00c9;tudes Commerciales de Paris</strong> is a French <em><a>Grande Ecole</a></em>.
Because of this selection, HEC is regarded as one of the most prestigious schools in France together with the <a>&#x00c9;cole Polytechnique</a> (engineering) and the <a>&#x00c9;cole Normale Sup&#x00e9;rieure</a> (sciences and literature) which have similar recruitment processes.</p>
<p>Together with leading parisian schools of engineering such as the <a>Ecole Polytechnique</a>, HEC is a constituent of the <a>Paris Institute of Technology</a> (ParisTech), an association of top Grandes Ecoles willing to create a collegiate university by the year 2020.</p>
The <a>CEMS MiM</a>, a European programme offered to the students of the Grande Ecole is also ranked in the top 3.</li>
<li><strong>MBA</strong>: the Master in Business Administration is generally ranked among the top 20 programmes worldwide, the last rankings of the <em><a>Wall Street Journal</a></em> and <em><a>The Economist</a></em> even ranked it among the top 10 MBA worldwide.</li>
HEC requires 26 hours of written exams, all of them being shared with other schools such as <a>ESSEC</a> and <a>ESCP Europe</a>.
<p>French is not a requirement and the average GMAT of admitted students is 720.</p>
<p>Direct admits, including international students, enter directly into the second year where they begin a business intensive program.
<li><a>Sciences Po Paris</a>: Master in Public and Corporate Management</li>
<p>Several &quot;Majeures&quot; are jointly organized with prominent French engineering schools such as the <a>&#x00c9;cole Polytechnique</a> or the <a>&#x00c9;cole des Mines de Paris</a> with grande &#x00e9;cole students of these institutions joining their curriculum.</p>
<p>HEC offers other Masters of Science for holders of non-French bachelors.
