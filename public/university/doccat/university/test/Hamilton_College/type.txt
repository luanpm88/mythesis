<p><strong>Hamilton College</strong> is a private, independent, <a>liberal arts college</a> located in <a>Clinton</a>, <a>New York</a>, <a>United States</a>.
Rivalries with many of these schools, Middlebury in particular, predate the establishment of the conference.</p>
<p>In 2011, <a>U.S. News &amp; World Report</a> ranked Hamilton the 18th best national liberal arts college in the United States.
This process caused a great deal of controversy &#x2014; mostly surrounding the decision to preclude fraternities from exercising any use of their houses.
The sororities are <a>Alpha Chi Lambda</a>, <a>Alpha Theta Chi</a>, <a>Gamma Xi</a>, <a>Kappa Delta Omega</a>, <a>Kappa Sigma Alpha</a>, <a>Sigma Lambda Upsilon</a>, and <a>Phi Beta Chi</a>.</p>
Hamilton sponsors 28 sports, including: <a>Baseball</a> (M), <a>Basketball</a> (M&amp;W), <a>Crew</a> (M&amp;W), <a>Cross Country</a> (M&amp;W), <a>Field Hockey</a> (W), <a>Football</a> (M), <a>Golf</a> (M), <a>ice hockey</a> (M&amp;W), <a>Lacrosse</a> (M&amp;W), <a>Outdoor and Indoor Track &amp; Field</a> (M&amp;W), <a>Soccer</a> (M&amp;W), <a>Softball</a> (W), <a>Squash</a> (M&amp;W), <a>Swimming</a> &amp; <a>Diving</a> (M&amp;W), <a>Tennis</a> (M&amp;W), <a>Volleyball</a> (W).</p>
Notable recent speakers have included <a>Salman Rushdie</a>, <a>Jared Diamond</a>, <a>Stanley Lombardo</a>, <a>Peter Meineck</a>, <a>Kenneth Miller</a>, <a>David Corn</a>, <a>John Zogby</a>, <a>Roger Kimball</a>, <a>Paul Finkelman</a>, <a>Jay Sekulow</a>, <a>Colleen Sheehan</a>, <a>Steven F. Hayward</a>, <a>Andrew C. McCarthy</a>, <a>Mark Bauerlein</a>, <a>Jonah Goldberg</a>, <a>Elizabeth Fox-Genovese</a>, <a>Jeffrey Sutton</a>, <a>Richard L. Hanna</a>, <a>Dinesh D'Souza</a>, and the speakers of the Great Names series (see below).</p>
<p>Notable faculty have included intellectuals and philosophers such as <a>Leo Strauss</a>; writers such as <a>Natalie Babbitt</a>, <a>Alex Haley</a>, and poet <a>Howard Nemerov</a>; composers such as <a>Jay Reise</a>; and diplomats and politicians such as <a>Edward S. Walker</a>, <a>Jack F. Matlock, Jr.</a>, and <a>Bernie Sanders</a>.</p>
