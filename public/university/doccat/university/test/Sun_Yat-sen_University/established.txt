<p><strong>Sun Yat-sen University</strong> or <strong>Zhongshan University</strong> () is a prominent university in the southern part of <a>China</a>, located mainly in <a>Guangzhou</a>.
The university is named after Dr. <a>Sun Yat-sen</a>, a <a>physician</a> and later founder of the <a>Republic of China</a>.</p>
<p>The university consists of 4 campuses (South Campus, North Campus, East Campus and Zhuhai Campus) with a total area of 6.17 square kilometers.
<p>The university motto, &quot;Study extensively; Enquire accurately; Reflect carefully; Discriminate clearly; Practise earnestly&quot; (&#x535a;&#x5b66;&#x3001;&#x5ba1;&#x95ee;&#x3001;&#x614e;&#x601d;&#x3001;&#x660e;&#x8fa8;&#x3001;&#x7b03;&#x884c;), is from one of the Chinese classics, <em><a>Doctrine of the Mean</a></em>.</p>
<h2>History</h2>
<p>Sun Yat-sen University, originally known as National Kwangtung University (&#x56fd;&#x7acb;&#x5e7f;&#x4e1c;&#x5927;&#x5b66;), was founded in <a>1924</a> by Dr. <a>Sun Yat-sen</a>.
<p>The campus was originally located at Shipai (&#x77f3;&#x724c;), Eastern <a>Guangzhou</a>.
In the early 1950s, Sun Yat-sen University was merged with <a>Lingnan University</a> and relocated to Kangle Yuan (&#x5eb7;&#x4e50;&#x56ed;), Southern <a>Guangzhou</a>, which is now called the South Campus.
In 2000, the Zhuhai Campus was established in Tangjiawan (&#x5510;&#x5bb6;&#x6e7e;), city of <a>Zhuhai</a>.
In 2001, the former <a>Sun Yat-sen University of Medical Sciences</a> merged into Sun Yat-sen University, constituting the current North Campus.
The East Campus was established in 2004 in <a>Guangzhou Higher Education Mega Center</a> (&#x5e7f;&#x5dde;&#x5927;&#x5b66;&#x57ce;), Panyu, <a>Guangzhou</a>.</p>
<p><a>thumb|right|Medical college of Sun Yat-sen University</a></p>
<p>Sun Yat-sen University is a comprehensive university including the humanities, social sciences, natural sciences, technical science, medical science, pharmacology and management science.
<p>According to <a>2005 Chinese University Ranking</a> published by The <a>21st Century Business Herald</a>, Sun Yat-sen University was ranked in 9th place in 2005.
<p>According to <a>The Times Higher Education World University Rankings 2010-2011</a> published by The <a>Times</a>, Sun Yat-sen University was ranked in 171th place in the world, in 22nd place in Asia and in the 5th place in China.</p>
