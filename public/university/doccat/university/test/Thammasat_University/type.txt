It also cooperates with some of the top universities around the globe.
Today the faculty offers undergraduate programsme in newspaper and print media, radio and television broadcasting, cinematography, advertising, public relations, and communications management.
Many of the graduates continue their Master&#x2019;s or Ph.D. in prestigious UK universities, such as Imperial, LSE, UCL, Warwick.</p>
All of them are international programmes, with English language as a medium of instructions.
The institute enjoys strong links with <a>Thailand National Science and Technology Development Agency</a>, <a>Thailand Science Park</a>, national graduate schools (such as <a>JGSEE</a>, <a>TGIST</a>, and <a>TAIST</a>) and many international universities, notably its neighbour <a>Asian Institute of Technology</a>, and a group of Japanese and European universities.</p>
As a one-year certificate program, foreign students study within five clusters focusing on the social sciences, art and cultures, public health, development and migration.</p>
<a>Abhisit Vejjajiva</a>, current Prime Minister of Thailand and leader of the <a>Democrat Party</a>, <a>Ammar Siamwalla</a>, former president of the <a>Thailand Development Research Institute</a>, and <a>Jermsak Pinthong</a>, former <a>senator</a>, are former lecturers at the Faculty of Economics.</p>
