<p><strong>Lee University</strong> is a private, coeducational <a>university</a> located in <a>Cleveland</a>, <a>Tennessee</a>.
Lee became a university in 1997 and continues to maintain a strong <a>Christian</a> identity.</p>
Lee University is now the 5th largest undergraduate enrollment among the 103 Christian colleges who are member institutions of the <a>Council for Christian Colleges and Universities</a>.</p>
<p>Although Lee is notable for its religious education; pre-medicine, business, elementary education, and <a>music</a> are also considered among its strongest specialties, and it is consistently ranked by <em><a>US News and World Report</a></em> among the top <a>Southern</a> <a>liberal arts</a> colleges.</p>
Many activities such as <a>chapel</a> attendance (offered 4 times per week, students are required to attend 2 services), <a>service</a> requirements (10 hours per semester-80 total hours to graduate), and the <a>study abroad</a> program (Typically taken during a summer break), are required of all graduates before they can receive their respective degree.
The available men's sports are: <a>Baseball</a>, <a>Basketball</a>, <a>Cross Country</a>, <a>Golf</a>, <a>JV Basketball</a>, <a>Soccer</a>, and <a>Tennis</a>.
Lee's Lady Flames soccer team won the NAIA National Championship in 2008 and in 2009.</p>
