<p> <strong>John George Kemeny</strong> () (May 31, 1926 &ndash; December 26, 1992) was a <a>Hungarian American</a> <a>mathematician</a>, <a>computer scientist</a>, and educator best known for co-developing the <a>BASIC programming language</a> in 1964 with <a>Thomas E. Kurtz</a>.
Kemeny served as the 13th President of <a>Dartmouth College</a> from 1970 to 1981 and pioneered the use of computers in college education.
Kemeny chaired the presidential commission that investigated the  <a>Three Mile Island accident</a> in 1979.
In 1940, he took the whole Kemeny family to the United States Kemeny's family settled in New York City where he attended <a>George Washington High School</a>.
He graduated with the best results in his class three years later.
He had lived in <a>Etna</a>, near the Dartmouth campus.</p>
