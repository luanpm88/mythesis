<p>The <strong>State University New York College at Oneonta</strong> (also known as the <strong>SUNY College at Oneonta</strong>, <strong>SUNY Oneonta</strong>, <strong>Oneonta State</strong>, and <strong>O-State</strong>) is a four-year liberal arts college in <a>Oneonta</a>, New York, United States, with approximately 5,800 students.
In each of the past three years, SUNY Oneonta was ranked by US News in the top tier of &#x201c;America&#x2019;s Best Colleges,&#x201d; selected by Kiplinger&#x2019;s magazine as one of the &#x201c;Kiplinger 100 Best Values in Public Colleges,&#x201d; and named to the President&#x2019;s Higher Education Community Service Honor Roll.</p>
<h2>History</h2>
<p>Established in 1889, the SUNY College at Oneonta was a founding member of the State University of New York system in 1948.
With the addition of programs in the arts and sciences in 1964, SUNY Oneonta began its growth into today&#x2019;s college of the liberal arts and sciences.</p>
<p>It is often viewed as the &quot;gem&quot; of the SUNY system, because of its stellar education.
Admission to the College is highly competitive, and it has consistently been ranked among the <strong>&quot;SUNY Five Elite&quot;</strong> -- five of the best SUNY's, which include Binghamton University, University at Albany, Stony Brook University, and SUNY Geneseo.</p>
<p>The mission of the college is &#x201c;to foster the individual student&#x2019;s intellectual, personal, and civic development.&#x201d;
A highly selective college, SUNY Oneonta is dedicated to providing an exceptional educational experience at an affordable cost.
The teaching and learning process is the college&#x2019;s highest priority, and faculty members actively work with students from the first year to the graduate level.
As a friendly and inclusive campus, SUNY Oneonta welcomes and engages with a wide variety of people, cultures and ideas, valuing close relationships with the local community and service to community partners.</p>
<p>*The <a>SUNY Oneonta College Observatory</a> is home to the largest telescope in New York, a 1-meter (40&quot;) Newtonian reflector.</p>
