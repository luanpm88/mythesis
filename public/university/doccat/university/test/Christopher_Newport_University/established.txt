It was established in 1961 as a two-year school of the <a>College of William and Mary</a>.
It became a four-year college in 1971, and a <a>university</a> in 1992.
Today, CNU is the youngest comprehensive university in the Commonwealth.
In recent years, applications have gone up by more than 700 percent, and the students' SAT average has increased by more than 240 points.
<p>The institution is named after <a>Christopher Newport</a>, the leader of the expedition that brought the first <a>English</a> settlers to <a>Jamestown</a>.</p>
<h2>History</h2>
<p>&lt;!-- Deleted image removed: <a>thumb|250px|right|Making &quot;History&quot; as the new McMurran Hall is opened on the night of Monday, January 4th, 2010</a> --&gt;</p>
<p>In 1960 the city of Newport News joined together with the <a>Commonwealth of Virginia</a> to create Christopher Newport College(CNC), which opened its doors in 1961 and at the time was located in the old <a>John W. Daniel</a> School building.
The college was founded as an extension of the <a>College of William &amp; Mary</a> and offered extension courses that had already been available in the area for some time.
In 1964 the college was relocated to its current location, a  tract of land purchased and donated by the city.
In this same year, the college's first permanent building was dedicated as <a>Lewis Archer McMurran, Jr.</a> Hall, named after the assemblyman whose sponsored legislation led to the schools establishment.
In 1971, CNC became a 4 year college  Finally in 1992, the college received university status under the leadership of then president Anthony R. Santoro who oversaw the building of the first residence hall and the original Captain John Smith Library.
Eventually former senator <a>Paul S. Trible</a> replaced Santoro as president of the university, after which the former senator announced his plans for the university's expansion.</p>
Christopher Newport University was ranked #7 nationally as an &quot;Up-and-coming'&quot; liberal arts college in the annual 2009 <a>U.S. News and World Report</a> college rankings.
In 1999, CNU was named #2 among regional public liberal arts colleges in the South by U.S.News &amp; World Report guide to &quot;America'sBest Colleges.&quot;</p>
<p>Christopher Newport University offers a variety of four year bachelors of sciences and bachelors of arts degrees.
<h3>Joseph W. Luter III College of Business and Leadership</h3>
<p>The College of Business is located in the Business and Technology Center, locally abbreviated as The BTC Building.
In 2005 the school of business was renamed to the <a>Joseph W. Luter III</a> School of Business.
The school was renamed after a 5 million dollar donation from the <a>Smithfield Packing Company</a>.
<a>Joseph W. Luter, III</a>, the president and CEO of the company at the time said, &quot;I have always felt strongly about the importance of contributing to the community and to the people with whom we come into contact every day.
..I have always encouraged <a>Smithfield Foods</a>, Inc. and its subsidiaries to focus on helping their local communities in a direct way.&quot;[<a>http://www.smithfieldfoods.com/Enviro/Press/press_view.asp?ID=294</a>] in regards to the companies donation.
The College of Business is accredited by the <a>AACSB</a> and offers bachelors degrees in <a>Accounting</a>, <a>Economics</a>, <a>Finance</a>, <a>Management</a> and <a>Marketing</a>.
Each academic department is primarily associated with a building on campus.
The department of Biology, Chemistry &amp; Environmental Science, generally located in the Science Building, offers bachelors degrees in <a>Biology</a>, <a>Chemistry</a>, <a>Environmental Science</a> and <a>Ornamental Horticulture</a> however the Ornamental Horticulture program was dropped in 2008.
The Physics, Computer Science &amp; Computer Engineering, or PCSE, department offers several bachelors degree as well as a master in 5 programs.
Undergraduate programs include degrees in <a>Computer Engineering</a>, <a>Applied Physics</a>, <a>Computer Science</a> and <a>Information Science</a>.
The Information Science program offers several concentrations which include <a>Networking</a> &amp; <a>Communications</a>, <a>Information Systems</a>, and <a>Business Administration</a>.
Sharing Gosnold Hall with the PCSE Department is the Mathematics Department which offers a degree in <a>Mathematics</a>.</p>
<p>As of January 2010 the Departments of Communication Studies, English, History, Government, Modern and Classical Languages and Literature as well as the Department of Philosophy and Religious Studies are located in Lewis Archer McMurran, Jr. Hall.
McMurran is the newest building on campus.
The <a>History</a> department offers degrees in History.
The <a>English</a> Department offers a lone degree in English.
Concentrations include <a>Creative Writing</a>, <a>Journalism</a>, Language Arts, <a>Literature</a>, <a>Technical Writing</a> and <a>Writing</a>.
For languages other than English, the Modern &amp; Classical Languages &amp; Literatures department offers degrees in German, French, Spanish, and Classical Studies.
The department of Philosophy and Religious Studies offers a Bachelors of Arts, and includes Religious Studies and Pre-Seminary options for a concentration.</p>
<p>The Psychology and Sociology &amp; Anthropology and Socialwork Departments, offer degrees in <a>Psychology</a>, <a>Social Work</a> and <a>Sociology</a>.
The sociology program also offers options for a concentration with include <a>Criminology</a>, Culture/Socialization/Society and General sociology studies.
<p>With the addition of the <a>Ferguson Center for the Performing Arts</a>, the schools Fine and Performing arts programs has been greatly enhanced.</p>
