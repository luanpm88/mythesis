<p>The <strong>Sri Sathya Sai University</strong> (formerly <em>Sri Sathya Sai Institute of Higher Learning</em>) is the university founded by <a>Sri Sathya Sai Baba</a>, under Section 3 of the <a>University Grants Commission</a> Act of 1956.
<a>Sathya Sai Baba</a> is also the chancellor of the institute.
<p>The university is based on the <a>gurukula</a> system of ancient India wherein education is provided totally free from Kindergarten to Post Graduation.
All students, from first grade to postgraduate studies, stay in the hostel.
The Sri Sathya Sai University in <a>Prashanthi Nilayam</a> is the only one of the two colleges in India to have received an &quot;A++&quot; rating by the <a>National Assessment and Accreditation Council</a> (an autonomous body established by the <a>University Grants Commission</a>).
<h2>History</h2>
<p>The Sri Sathya Sai University was founded on November 22, 1981, by <a>Sri Sathya Sai Baba</a>.
An autonomous body; it has been recognized by the Ministry of Education, Government of India, and the University Grants Commission (UGC), as a Deemed University, vide their notification no.F9-11/81-U.3
<p>The Institute has grown out of the colleges founded earlier by Sri <a>Sathya Sai Baba</a> in <a>Anantapur</a> (<a>Andhra Pradesh</a>), <a>Whitefield</a> (<a>Karnataka</a>) and <a>Prashanthi Nilayam</a> (<a>Andhra Pradesh</a>).
The latter being considered as the primary objective of education.
<li>9 June 1969 - Inauguration of Sri Sathya Sai Arts and Science College for men at Brindavan.</li>
<li>28 November 1978 - Sathya Sai Baba laid the foundation-stone of the College of Arts, Science and Commerce at Prasanthi Nilayam</li>
<li>22 November 1981 - Inauguration of Sri Sathya Sai University as <a>Deemed University</a></li>
<li>20 November 2000 - Inauguration of Sri Sathya Sai Mirpuri College of Music </li>

<h2>Sathya Sai Baba University Muddenahalli</h2>
<p>A new multi-crore campus is being established at <a>Muddenahalli</a>-<a>Kanivenarayanapura</a>, the birthplace of Sir M. Visvesvaraya, on the outskirts of Chickballapur.
The campus will be adjacent to the prestigious <a>Indian Institute of Technology Muddenahalli</a> and <a>Visvesvaraya Institute of Advanced Technology</a>.
The graduates will attain scholarship in the scriptures of the Ramayana, the Bhagavatha and the Bhagavad Gita.
A hillock at Muddenahalli, said to be the highest spot in the area, will house the administrative office of the university.
Once the building is constructed it could be viewed from the Bangalore-Puttaparthi-Hyderabad highway.
A large mantapam has been erected atop the hillock, according to the Sathya Sai Seva organisation </p>
