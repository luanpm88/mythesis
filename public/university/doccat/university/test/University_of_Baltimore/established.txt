<p>The <strong>University of Baltimore</strong> (UB), located in <a>downtown</a> <a>Baltimore, Maryland</a> in the <a>Mt.
Charles Street</a>, is part of the <a>University System of Maryland</a>.
<p>The <em>Lower Division Initiative</em> is a program that began in 2005 to extend the University of Baltimore's current position to once again offer the first two years of the baccalaureate degree.
In April 2005 the <a>University System of Maryland</a>'s Board of Regents approved plans that would allow UB to start accepting freshmen and sophomores.
Under the original plan, freshmen and sophomore would be admitted starting in the fall of 2006.</p>
<p>In a unanimous vote that took place on February 15, 2006, the Maryland Higher Education Commission approved a revised mission statement submitted by the University of Baltimore, thus enabling the University to return to four-year undergraduate status.
This is the same initiative that already received approval from the Board of Regents in April of the prior year; however, the plan was revised slightly and now calls for freshmen to be admitted in the fall of 2007.</p>
The University says that it will be offering Freshman &quot;free&quot; tuition for their first year.
The university offers <a>Bachelor of Science</a> degrees programs in the following subjects: Applied Information Technology, Business Administration, Corporate Communication, Criminal Justice, Forensic Studies, Health Systems Management, Human Services Administration, Management Information Systems, Real Estate and Economic Development, Simulation and Digital Entertainment.
Through a joint program, UB and <a>Towson University</a> offer a <a>Master of Business Administration</a> degree program.
The university awards <a>Master of Science</a> degrees in Accounting, Applied Psychology, Criminal Justice, Human Services Administration, Interaction Design and Information Architecture, and Negotiations and Conflict Management.
The university awards <a>Master of Arts</a> in Legal and Ethical Studies as well as Publications Design.
In addition, it offers <a>Master of Fine Arts</a> in Creative Writing &amp; Publishing Arts and Integrated Design.
It also awards several different Graduate Certificates, including Accounting, Business, Technology Commercialization, Information Design, and Professional Counseling.</p>

Campus Recreation and Wellness at University of Baltimore is continually growing for the betterment of the university and surrounding community.
The Recreation Center, located on the third and fourth floor of the Academic Center, offers fitness classes free of charge on a first come/first serve basis to all members and hosts the Sport Club and Intramural Sports teams.
At one time, UB owned and operated a golf driving range in the <a>Mount Washington</a> neighborhood of <a>Baltimore</a>.
Campus Recreation Services seeks to serve the recreation, fitness, health and leisure needs of the university community through instructional and competitive sport activities, including aerobics classes, golf lessons, intramural sports, informal recreation and sport clubs.
The Recreation Center facilities include basketball, racquetball, badminton and volleyball courts, weight and cardio rooms, aerobic and spinning studios, indoor golf cage, foosball, darts, Wii gaming system, as well as locker rooms and a sauna.
