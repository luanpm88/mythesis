<p>The <strong>University of Louisiana at Monroe</strong>, or <strong>ULM</strong>, is a <a>coeducational</a> <a>public</a> <a>university</a> in <a>Monroe</a>, <a>Louisiana</a> and part of the <a>University of Louisiana System</a>.</p>
<p>ULM is the only university in Louisiana to offer a master's in <a>gerontology</a> and the only <a>public university</a> offering the <a>Doctor of Pharmacy</a> (Pharm.D.) degree.
<p><a>James Erwin Cofer Sr.</a> left the presidency after eight years in 2010 to head <a>Missouri State University</a> in <a>Springfield</a>, <a>Missouri</a>.
He was succeeded by <a>Nick Bruno</a> as the eighth <a>president</a> of ULM.</p>
<h2>History</h2>
<li>1934: Change to Northeast Center of Louisiana State University</li>
<li>2002: &quot;Reclaim Our Campus&quot; effort to help recover from financial and auditing difficulties.</li>
<p>Established in 1956, the College of Pharmacy is accredited by the American Council on Pharmacy Education, including one of seven Toxicology programs in the U.S.  The College of Pharmacy moved from the main campus to the off-campus (Bienville) building in 2007.
Doctoral program in marriage and family therapy began in 1996.</p>
<p>The Division of Theatre and Drama, established in 1953, was headed by <a>George C. Brian</a> (1919&#x2013;2007), <a>Ph.D.</a>, a <a>Baton Rouge</a> native who was an <a>actor</a>, <a>director</a>, <a>filmmaker</a>, <a>singer</a>, <a>dancer</a>, and patron of the <a>arts</a>.
He revitalized the program and brought <a>Broadway</a> stars to perform with his students.</p>
<p>The English department publishes <em>Turnrow</em>, a bi-annual journal of short fiction, poetry, visual art, and interviews.</p>
<p>ULM has the only Atmospheric Science Bachelor's program in Louisiana and Arkansas.
ULM joined the <a>Sun Belt Conference</a> for all sports on July 1, 2006 after playing in the <a>Southland Conference</a> in all sports except football (swimming and diving team was in Sun Belt Conference, but was dropped in 2005).
ULM shared the 2005 Sun Belt Conference football championship with <a>Arkansas State University</a> and the <a>University of Louisiana-Lafayette</a> (ASU received the conference's <a>New Orleans Bowl</a> bid due to tiebreaking procedures).
Fant was the first ULM coach to win three hundred games.</p>
<p>The university is home to several award-winning groups including the <a>Sound of Today</a> and the competition cheerleading squad.
The ULM water ski team is the most successful in the history of collegiate <a>water skiing</a>, having won 22 NCWSA national championships since 1979.
Bayou DeSiard was the host site of the first national championships in that year.</p>
<p>The football and <a>baseball</a> stadiums and ULM Activities Center were designed by <a>architect</a> <a>Hugh G. Parker</a> of <a>Bastrop</a>.
<p>In just three seasons in the Sun Belt, the Warhawks have won conference titles in men's basketball (2006-07 West Co-Champion) and baseball (2008 regular season).
<p>The ULM football team posted its first six-win season since moving to Division I-A in 2007 going 6-6, which included a 21-14 victory at Alabama.</p>
