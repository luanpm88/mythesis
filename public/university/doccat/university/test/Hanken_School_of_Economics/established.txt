<p>The <strong>Hanken School of Economics</strong> (Swedish: <em>Svenska handelsh&#x00f6;gskolan, &quot;Hanken&quot;</em>) is a Finnish business school.</p>
<p>Hanken is a <a>Swedish-language</a> business school of university standing located in <a>Helsingfors</a> and <a>Vasa</a>, <a>Finland</a>.
Hanken is one of the oldest business schools in the Nordic countries.
Hanken is a leading, internationally accredited university in the field of economics and business administration.
The school was founded in 1909 under the name <em>H&#x00f6;gre Svenska Handelsl&#x00e4;roverket</em> and was given its current name in 1927.
Hanken has two campuses, one in <a>Helsingfors</a> and one in <a>Vasa</a>.</p>
<p>In 2000 Hanken was awarded the <a>EQUIS</a>-accreditation by <a>EFMD</a> (European Foundation for Management Development).
The accreditation was renewed in 2005 and 2008.
Hanken was awarded the <a>AMBA</a> accreditation in 2008.
A person studying at Hanken is known in Swedish as <em>hankeit</em>, which is a registered trademark since 2006.</p>
<p>Hanken has five departments: Accounting and Commercial Law, Economics, Finance and Statistics, Management and Organisation, Marketing.
<p>Hanken offers PhD education within all of its 10 majors.
The programme was awarded the AMBA accreditation in 2008.
Hanken &amp; IFL Corporate Development was founded in 2005 by Hanken and IFL at <a>Stockholm School of Economics</a>.</p>
