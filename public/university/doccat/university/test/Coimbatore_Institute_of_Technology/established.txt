<h2>History</h2>
<p>Coimbatore Institute of Technology was founded in the year 1956 by Prof P. R. Ramakrishnan, son of a wealthy Industrialist, after completing a brilliant academic career at the <a>Massachusetts Institute of Technology</a> (MIT) and <a>Sloan School of Management</a> in the USA.
He was also one of the earliest Indian to graduate from MIT.
The trust was formed under the name V. Rangaswamy Naidu Educational Trust with the noble aim of disseminating knowledge in the fields of science, engineering and technology to the student community.
The philanthropic founders envisioned a unique professional learning order with special emphasis on  industrial training.</p>
The proximity of the college to the roadways, railways and airways is one of the added advantage of the students.</p>
<p>The Mission of CIT is to impart high quality education and training to its students to make them World-class Engineers with a foresight to the changes and problems, and pioneers to offer innovative solutions to benefit the nation and the world at large.</p>
<p>The Institute strives to inculcate a sound knowledge in Engineering along with realised social responsibilities to enable its students to combat the current and impending challenges faced by our country and to extend their expertise to the global arena.</p>
<p>CIT was affiliated to Madras University from 1956 to 1980.
In the year 1980, the Institute got affiliated  to Bharathiar University and subsequently to Anna University in 2001.
The Institute   was granted autonomous status in the year 1987 and is accredited by the National Board of Accreditation.
As part of the invaluable service rendered to the society by V. Rangaswamy Educational Trust, the CIT Sandwich Polytechnic College was started in 1961.
The  National Board of Accreditation has accredited the Mechanical Engineering (Sandwich) and Mechanical Engineering diploma programmes.
<p>The Institute, under <a>Bharathiar University</a>, was granted autonomous status by  UGC  and Government of <a>Tamil Nadu</a> with effect from the Academic year 1987-88.
The purpose of autonomy is to provide freedom for the institution to design its own curriculum and evaluation system to produce high quality engineers suitable to meet the current  challenges of the industry and society.
The revision is based on the feedback from the industry, employers, faculty, students and the current status of technology.
The Red Buildings in the campus houses   Laboratories for various departments.
CIT  has 40,000sq.m. of built up area for  Academic and Administration purposes and all facilities for most of the sports and games.
There is a separate  Library block, state of the art  Auditorium, a Guest House, a Canteen, Hostel blocks and a Bank.</p>
