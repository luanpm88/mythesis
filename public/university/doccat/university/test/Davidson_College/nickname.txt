Majors are offered in over twenty fields, as are several minors and self-designed interdisciplinary options.</p>
<p>According to <a>The Princeton Review</a>, Davidson is ranked among the top twenty colleges nationally for the following categories: &quot;Best Overall Academic Experience For Undergraduates,&quot; &quot;Professors Get High Marks (#1),&quot; &quot;Professors Make Themselves Accessible (#16),&quot; &quot;Students Study the Most(#10),&quot; &quot;School Runs Like Butter (#4),&quot; &quot;Town-Gown Relations are Great (#3),&quot; &quot;Easiest Campus to Get Around (#3),&quot; and &quot;Best Quality of Life (#16).&quot;
Davidson prides itself on a student body chosen not only for their academic promise, but also for their character.</p>
In addition to the one major required for graduation, students may pursue a second major, a minor, or a concentration.
Interdisciplinary concentrations are offered in Applied Mathematics, Asian Studies, Computer Science, Education, Ethnic Studies, Film and Media, Gender Studies, Genomics, International Studies, Medical Humanities, Neuroscience, and Southern Studies.</p>
In 2007 and 2008, the Cunningham Fine Arts building, home to several smaller performance spaces, faculty offices, classrooms and set construction facilities, will be completely renovated.</p>
Approximately 24% of the Davidson on-campus student body participates in varsity sports.
Davidson has the second smallest enrollment of any school in Division I football.</p>
<p>Davidson's sports teams are known as the Wildcats.
Their colors are red and black, although since 2008, many sports including football, men's basketball, and men's soccer have moved towards a brighter hue of red and white.
Sports that compete in other conferences include <a>football</a> in Division I Football Championship Subdivision <a>Pioneer Football League</a>, men's and women's swimming and diving in the <a>Colonial Athletic Association</a>, Field Hockey in the <a>Northern Pacific Field Hockey Conference</a>, and women's lacrosse in the <a>National Lacrosse Conference</a>.</p>
<p>The Wildcat men have competed in 10 NCAA tournaments (1966, 1968&ndash;70, 1986, 1998, 2002, 2006&ndash;08).
Curry led the nation in scoring in 2008-09, his last season at Davidson before declaring for the 2009 NBA Draft.
In <a>2006&ndash;07</a>, the team completed its regular-season conference schedule with only one loss and entered the <a>Southern Conference Tournament</a> as a No. 1 seed, where the team would win the Southern Conference Tournament for the second consecutive season.</p>
In 2004&ndash;05, the Wildcats were undefeated in conference play at 16&ndash;0 and advanced to the third round of the <a>NIT</a>.
In 2001&ndash;02, the Wildcats won the Southern Conference Tournament and lost a close game to Ohio State in the first round of the <a>NCAA Men's Division I Basketball Championship</a>.</p>
<p>In addition to Driesell and McKillop, a number of notable NCAA men's basketball head coaches have coached at Davidson.
<p>The men's soccer team at Davidson was declared a varsity sport in 1956 and had their first All-American player, Claude Finney, just four years later in 1960.</p>
Davidson lost 3&ndash;2 in overtime against San Diego in the semifinal game, but the team received plenty of accolades.
Ukrop led the nation with 31 goals and 72 points and was awarded the Adi Dassler Award, given to the nation's best player.
In 2007, Davidson's Library completed a project to digitally archive all past issues of the Davidsonian.</p>
The Student Activities Office encourages and is available for students wishing to develop an organization not yet established at Davidson.</p>
