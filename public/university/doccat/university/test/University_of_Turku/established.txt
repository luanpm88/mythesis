<p>  The <strong>University of Turku</strong> (<a>Finnish</a> <em>Turun yliopisto</em>, <a>Swedish</a> <em>&#x00c5;bo universitet</em>), located in <a>Turku</a> in <a>southwestern</a> <a>Finland</a>, is the second largest <a>university</a> in the country as measured by student enrollment, after <a>University of Helsinki</a>.
It was established in 1920 and also has faculties at <a>Rauma</a>, <a>Pori</a> and <a>Salo</a>.
The university is a member of the <a>Coimbra Group</a>.</p>
<h2>History</h2>
<p> The first university to be established in Turku was <a>The Royal Academy of Turku</a>, in 1640, which was transferred to the new Capital, <a>Helsinki</a>, after the <a>Great Fire of Turku</a> in 1827.</p>
<p>Three famous Finns began their studies in Turku in 1822.
These were <a>Johan Vilhelm Snellman</a>, <a>Elias L&#x00f6;nnrot</a>, and <a>Johan Ludvig Runeberg</a> who have a statue on University Hill.
Another reminder of the Royal Academy is the <a>Old Academy Building</a> near the University's campus.</p>
<h3>The University of Turku</h3>
<p><a>thumb|250px|The grand opening of the University of Turku in 1922.
On the left is Artturi H. Virkkunen, the first rector of the university</a> The modern University of Turku was founded in 1920.
The Finnish intelligentsia wanted a purely <a>Finnish</a> university, the first of its kind in Finland.
To honour the memory of these donors, the University has named its specially created <a>liqueur</a> &quot;22 040&quot;.
Developed by the University's own food chemists, the liqueur does homage to some of the distinctive fruits of the Finnish landscape: the <a>cloudberry</a>, the <a>rowan</a> and the <a>sea buckthorn</a>.</p>
<p>The first premises of the University of Turku were in the centre of the city, by the market square.
In the 1950s a new campus was built on Ryss&#x00e4;nm&#x00e4;ki (<em>Russian Hill</em> &ndash; now known as University Hill).
In the 1960s the University started to expand rapidly, a process that still continues.</p>
<p>The University was made a public institution in 1974.</p>
<p>Since 1995 the University of Turku has been a member of <a>Coimbra Group</a>.</p>
<p>In January 2010, <a>Turku School of Economics</a> merged with the University of Turku, forming a seventh faculty of the university.</p>
<p><a>thumb|right|250px|Natural Science Building I (left) and Main Building (right)</a> <a>thumb|right|250px|ICT Building</a> <a>thumb|right|250px|Department of History</a> <a>thumb|right|250px|Turku School of Economics</a> The university is governed by three bodies, the <a>Board of Directors</a>, The <a>Rector</a> and the <a>Chancellor</a>.</p>
<p><em>The Board</em> is the highest decision-making organ of the University and is made up of 19 individuals both from academia, politics and studentship.
It develops the University's operating processes and approves plans concerning its financing and activities; it decide on the guidelines of granting appropriations; it give its opinion in any matters of significance that concern the University in principle and the Board approves the service regulations and other similar rules.</p>
<p><em>The Rector</em> directs the activities and processes of the University and solves any issues concerning its general management.
The Rector represents the University and uses the University's right to speak in courts of law and in dealings with the authorities.
<p><em>The Chancellor</em>, appointed by the <a>President of Finland</a> upon proposal of the <a>Finnish Government</a>, shall promote science and scholarship, look after the general interests of the University and supervise its activities.
The Chancellor confirms the standing orders and other corresponding general regulations of the University.
The most visible task of the Chancellor is the appointment of the Professors and the Docents of the University.
<p>The university is divided into seven faculties.
<li>School of History, Cultural Research and Art Studies</li>
<li>School of Languages and Translation Studies</li>
<li>Department of Biochemistry and Food Chemistry</li>
<li><a>Faculty of Law</a></li>
<li><a>Faculty of Social Sciences</a>
<li>Department of Political Science and Contemporary History</li>
<li>Teacher training school in Turku</li>
<li>Teacher training school in Rauma</li>
<li><a>Turku School of Economics</a></li>
<p>The University of Turku shares a campus with <a>&#x00c5;bo Akademi University</a> and elements of <a>Turku Science Park</a>.
The area also encompasses <a>Turku University Hospital (TYKS)</a> and the <a>Student Village</a>.
The Administration Building and the surrounding complex was built in the fifties on what became to be known as the University Hill.
The university has also acquired some former <a>military barracks</a> built in the 19th century.
The latest additions include Educarium, which houses the Faculty of Education, Publicum, which houses the Faculty of Social Sciences and the newly completed <a>ICT</a> building which houses the Computer Science Faculties.</p>
<p>University of Turku has been involved in many research projects in the <a>Peruvian Amazon</a>.
With the Peruvian Amazon Research Institute (<em>Instituto de Investigaciones de la Amazon&#x00ed;a Peruana - IIAP</em>), based in <a>Iquitos</a>, has been studiyng the Amazon in the <a>BIODAMAZ</a> Project.</p>
