<p>Tuck grants only one degree, the <a>Master of Business Administration</a>, alongside shorter programs for executives and recent college graduates, although there are opportunities for dual degrees with other institutions.
<p>In 1942, the school's name changed to the Amos Tuck School of Business Administration, and in 1953, the degree program changed to the modern <a>Master of Business Administration</a> (MBA).
<p><a>thumb|left|The Feldberg Business and Engineering Library</a></p>
In the 2010 <a>QS Global 200 Business Schools Report</a> Tuck School of Business was indexed as the #14 business school in North America.</p>
<p>Applicants to the Tuck School are evaluated based on undergraduate academic performance, <a>Graduate Management Admission Test</a> (GMAT) standardized test scores, essays, recommendations, written applications, and interviews, if applicable.
<p>Like many other business schools, Tuck encourages its students to have post-undergraduate work experience before applying to the MBA program.
The average incoming student has five years of full-time work experience, and the average student age is 28, ranging from 25 to 32 years.
