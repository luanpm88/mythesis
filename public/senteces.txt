<p><strong>Blythe Katherine Danner</strong> (born February 3, 1943) is an American <a>actress</a>. She is the mother of actress <a>Gwyneth Paltrow</a> and director <a>Jake Paltrow</a>.</p>
<h2>Early life</h2>
<p>Danner was born in <a>Philadelphia</a>, <a>Pennsylvania</a>, the daughter of Katharine and Harry Earl Danner, a bank executive.&lt;ref&gt;<a>Blythe Danner Biography (1944?-)&lt;!-- Bot generated title --&gt;</a>&lt;/ref&gt; She has a brother, opera singer/actor Harry Danner, a sister, former performer turned director Dorothy (Dottie) Danner, and a half brother, violin maker William Moennig. Danner is of <a>Pennsylvania Dutch</a> ancestry.&lt;ref name=laetgi&gt;{{Cite news|last=Hughes|first=Mike|coauthors=|title='Grownups' star finds role is a welcom distraction|pages=|publisher=Lansing State Journal|date=2004-11-20|url=<a>http://pqasb.pqarchiver.com/lansingstatejournal/access/1800643341.html?FMT=ABS&amp;FMTS=ABS:FT&amp;type=current&amp;date=Nov+20%2C+2004&amp;author=Hughes+Mike&amp;pub=Lansing+State+Journal&amp;desc='Grownups'+star+finds+role+is+a+welcom+distraction&amp;pqatl=google</a>|accessdate=2010-12-17}}&lt;/ref&gt; She attended <a>George School</a>, a private Quaker secondary school in <a>Newtown, Bucks County, Pennsylvania</a>, and <a>Bard College</a>, where she graduated in 1965.{{Citation needed|date=July 2009}}</p>
<h2>Career</h2>
<p>Danner first appeared on stage with the Theater Company of Boston and the Trinity Square Repertory Company (now <a>Trinity Repertory Company</a>) in <a>Providence, Rhode Island</a>. She first gained national attention at age 25 by winning the <a>Theatre World Award</a> for her performance in the Lincoln Center Rep's production of <em><a>The Miser</a></em>. In 1970, she appeared in her first film role, in a television production of <em>Dr. Cook's Garden</em>.  She was the toast of Broadway when she created the adorable ingenue in <em><a>Butterflies are Free</a></em> for which she won a <a>Tony Award</a> in 1970 (a role Goldie Hawn played in the film). </p>
<p>In 1972, Danner portrayed Martha Jefferson in the movie version of <em><a>1776</a></em>. Also that same year, she played a jilted wife opposite <a>Peter Falk</a> and <a>John Cassavetes</a> in the <em><a>Columbo</a></em> episode &quot;Etude in Black&quot;.</p>
<p>Her earliest starring film role was opposite <a>Alan Alda</a> in <em>To Kill a Clown</em> (<a>1972</a>). Not by coincidence, Danner appeared in the episode of <em><a>M*A*S*H</a></em> entitled <em><a>The More I See You</a></em>, playing the love interest of Alda's character <a>Hawkeye</a>. She played the role of Zelda Fitzgerald in <em>The Last of the Belles</em> (1974). She was the eponymous heroine in the film <em><a>Lovin' Molly</a></em> (<a>1974</a>) (directed by <a>Sidney Lumet</a>). She appeared in <a>Futureworld</a> playing Tracy Ballard with co-star <a>Peter Fonda</a> (<a>1976</a>). In the film version of Neil Simon's semi-autobiographical play <em><a>Brighton Beach Memoirs</a></em> (1986), she portrayed a middle-aged <a>Jew</a>ish mother, and in the 1982 TV movie <em><a>Inside the Third Reich</a></em>, she played the wife of <a>Albert Speer</a>. She has appeared in two films based on the novels of <a>Pat Conroy</a>, <em><a>The Great Santini</a></em> (<a>1979</a>) and <em><a>The Prince of Tides</a></em> (<a>1991</a>), as well as two television movies adapted from books by <a>Anne Tyler</a>, <em><a>Saint Maybe</a></em> and <em><a>Back When We Were Grownups</a></em>, both for the <a>Hallmark Hall of Fame</a>. </p>
<p>Danner is more recently known for her role opposite <a>Robert De Niro</a> in the <a>2000</a> comedy hit <em><a>Meet the Parents</a></em> and its sequels, <em><a>Meet the Fockers</a></em> and <em><a>Little Fockers</a></em>.</p>
<p>From 2001 to 2006, she regularly appeared on <em><a>Will &amp; Grace</a></em> as <a>Will Truman</a>'s mother <a>Marilyn</a>. From 2004 to 2006, she starred in the TV series <em><a>Huff</a></em>. In 2005, she was nominated for three Emmy Awards: for her work on <em><a>Will &amp; Grace</a></em>, <em><a>Huff</a></em> and <em><a>Back When We Were Grownups</a></em>. Emmy host <a>Ellen DeGeneres</a> poked fun at Blythe Danner during the award ceremony, saying that Danner should not be nervous because she was almost certain to win at least one Emmy, which she did, for <em>Huff.</em> In July 2006, she won a second consecutive <a>Emmy</a> award for <em><a>Huff</a></em>. For 25 years, she has been a regular performer at the Williamstown Summer Theater Festival, where she also serves on the Board of Directors.</p>
<p>In 2006, Danner was awarded an inaugural <a>Katharine Hepburn</a> Medal, alongside fellow honoree <a>Lauren Bacall</a>, which recognizes &quot;women whose lives, work and contributions embody the intelligence, drive and independence of the four-time-Oscar-winning actress,&quot; by <a>Bryn Mawr College</a>'s Katharine Houghton Hepburn Center.&lt;ref&gt;<a>Bryn Mawr College - Katharine Houghton Hepburn Center&lt;!-- Bot generated title --&gt;</a>&lt;/ref&gt;</p>
<h2>Environmental activism</h2>
<p>In addition to her acting work, Blythe Danner has been involved in environmental issues such as <a>recycling</a> and <a>conservation</a> for over 30 years. She has been active with <a>INFORM, Inc.</a>, is on the Board of Environmental Advocates of New York and the Board of Directors of the <a>Environmental Media Association</a>, and won the 2002 <a>EMA Board of Directors Ongoing Commitment Award</a>. She was instrumental in implementing curbside recycling in <a>Santa Monica</a> and in retaining the <a>New York City</a> recycling program despite threatened budget cuts in 1991{{Citation needed|date=April 2010}}.  In 2002 Danner, her husband <a>Bruce Paltrow</a>, and her daughter <a>Gwyneth Paltrow</a> worked together on a series of <a>public service announcement</a>s encouraging use of alternative energy sources and alternative fuel vehicles.</p>
<h2>Health care activism</h2>
<p>After the passing of her husband Bruce Paltrow to oral cancer, she became involved with the Oral Cancer Foundation,&lt;ref&gt;<a>Oral Cancer Foundation 2007&lt;!-- Bot generated title --&gt;</a>&lt;/ref&gt; a national 501(c)3 non profit charity. In 2005 she filmed a public service announcement that played on TV stations around the country about the risks associated with oral cancer, and through that shared the personal pain associated with the loss of her husband publicly to further awareness of the disease and the need for early detection. She continues to donate her time to the foundation, and has appeared on morning talk shows, and has done interviews in high profile magazines such as People to further public awareness of the disease and its risk factors. Through The Bruce Paltrow Oral Cancer Fund, administered by the Oral Cancer Foundation, she continues to raise awareness and funding for oral cancer issues, particularly those involving communities in which disparities in health care exist.</p>
<h2>Personal life</h2>
<p>Danner is the widow of producer <a>Bruce Paltrow</a>, who died from complications of pneumonia while battling oral <a>cancer</a> in 2002, and the mother of actress <a>Gwyneth Paltrow</a> and director <a>Jake Paltrow</a>. Danner first co-starred with her daughter in <a>1992</a> in the TV movie <em><a>Cruel Doubt</a></em> and then again in the <a>2003 film</a> <em><a>Sylvia</a></em>, playing mother to Gwyneth Paltrow's titular character.</p>
<p>She is also the aunt of actresses Hillary Danner and <a>Katherine Moennig</a> and is sister-in-law (through brother Harry) of <a>opera</a> director Dorothy Danner.</p>
<p>Additionally, she is mother-in-law to <a>Coldplay</a> front man <a>Chris Martin</a> who married Gwyneth in 2003 and is grandmother to their two children: Apple Blythe Alison Martin and Moses Bruce Anthony Martin.</p>
<p>Although she has worked frequently on TV and on stage, Danner put her film career on hold for a number of years to raise her children. Danner often said the proudest night of her life was when Gwyneth won an <a>Academy Award</a> for Best Actress (for <em><a>Shakespeare in Love</a></em>) and Danner was the first person her daughter thanked, tearfully, followed by her father and grandfather.</p>
