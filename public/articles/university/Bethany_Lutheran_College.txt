<p> <a>thumb|right|250px|Old Main, built in 1911.</a> <a>thumb|right|250px|Trinity Chapel</a>  <strong>Bethany Lutheran College</strong> <strong>(BLC)</strong>, is a private residential <a>liberal arts</a> college founded in 1927. BLC is a <a>Christian</a> <a>coeducational</a> college operated by the <a>Evangelical Lutheran Synod</a>. The campus of BLC overlooks the <a>Minnesota River</a> valley in <a>Mankato, Minnesota</a>, a community of about 50,000.</p>
<h2>History</h2>
<p>Bethany Ladies College opened in 1911 with 44 students and a faculty of four. In 1927, the Norwegian Synod of the American Evangelical Lutheran Church (now known as the <a>Evangelical Lutheran Synod</a>) purchased the campus for dual use as both a <a>high school</a> (Bethany Lutheran High School; closed in 1969) and <a>junior college</a> (Bethany Lutheran College). In 1946 <a>Bethany Lutheran Theological Seminary</a> (BLTS) began as a department of the college; in 1975 it became a separate institution.</p>
<p>In 2001 Bethany awarded its first <a>baccalaureate</a> degrees, completing a five year transition from its 74-year history as a junior college.</p>

<h2>Athletics</h2>
<p>The Vikings are members of the <a>Upper Midwest Athletic Conference</a> in the <a>NCAA</a> <a>Division III</a>. The school offers the following sports: baseball, men's and women's basketball, men and women's cross country, golf, men's and women's soccer, softball, men's and women's tennis and volleyball.</p>
