<p>The <strong><a>Hochschule</a> f&#x00fc;r Musik &quot;Hanns Eisler&quot;</strong> (College or Academy of Music &quot;Hanns Eisler&quot;) is a music <a>conservatoire</a> in <a>Berlin</a>, <a>Germany</a>. It was established in <a>East Berlin</a> in 1950 as the Deutsche Hochschule f&#x00fc;r Musik (German College of Music) because the older Hochschule f&#x00fc;r Musik Berlin (now the <a>Berlin University of the Arts</a>) was in <a>West Berlin</a>. After the death of one of its first professors, composer <a>Hanns Eisler</a>, the school was renamed in his honor in 1964. Located in Berlin's famed <a>Gendarmenmarkt</a>, the college has an international faculty and programs in performance, composition, voice, conducting, jazz and popular music, arts management, musical theater, stage direction and <a>music theory</a>. Also notable are its institutes for new music and musicians' health.</p>
