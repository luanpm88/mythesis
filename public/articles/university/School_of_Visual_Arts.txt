<p>The <strong>School of Visual Arts</strong> (<strong>SVA</strong>), is a <a>proprietary</a> <a>art school</a> located in Manhattan, <a>New York City</a>, and is widely considered to be one of the leading art schools in the <a>United States</a>. It was established in 1947 by co-founders <a>Silas H. Rhodes</a> and <a>Burne Hogarth</a> as the <strong>Cartoonists and Illustrators School</strong>&lt;!--&quot;Cartoonists&quot; and &quot;Illustrators&quot; both plural per Silas Rhodes bio at <a>http://www.aiga.org/content.cfm?contentalias=SilashRhodes</a>, official site of AIGA, the professional association for design--&gt; and was renamed in 1956. It offered its first degrees in 1972.  SVA is a member of the <a>Association of Independent Colleges of Art and Design</a> (AICAD), a consortium of 36 leading art schools in the United States.</p>
<h2>Curriculum</h2>
<p>SVA is a fully accredited for-profit college that requires the completion of a four-year, 120 <a>credit</a> course for a <a>Bachelor of Fine Arts</a> degree. This includes 72 accumulated credits' worth of studio classes (where the <a>curriculum</a> requires the creation of some type of art), 30 accumulated credits of <a>Humanities and Sciences</a> courses, 12 accumulated credits of <a>art history</a> courses, and six discretionary credits.  The <a>Master of Fine Arts</a>  degree requires completion of 60 credits and a thesis project, while the <a>Master of Professional Studies</a> degree requires 30-36 credits and a thesis project, depending on the program. The <a>Master of Arts in Teaching</a> degree requires the completion of 36 credits and a thesis project.</p>
<pre>&#x0009;</pre>
<p>As of 2000, the <a>undergraduate</a> <a>degree</a>s offered at SVA are <a>Advertising</a>, <a>Animation</a>, <a>Cartooning</a>, <a>Computer Art</a>, <a>Film</a> &amp; <a>Video</a>, <a>Fine Arts</a>, <a>Graphic Design</a>, <a>Illustration</a>, <a>Interior Design</a>, <a>Photography</a>, and Visual &amp; Critical Studies.</p>
<p>In 1983, the school introduced its first <a>graduate</a> offering, a <a>Master</a> of <a>Fine Arts</a> program in <a>painting</a>, <a>drawing</a> and <a>sculpture</a>. Currently, SVA offers graduate programs in sixteen fields: <a>Art Education</a>; <a>Art Criticism</a> &amp; <a>Writing</a>; <a>Art Practice</a>; <a>Art Therapy</a>; <a>Branding</a>; <a>Computer Art</a>; <a>Design</a>; <a>Design Criticism</a>; <a>Digital Photography</a>; <a>Fashion Photography</a>; <a>Illustration as Visual Essay</a>; <a>Live Action Short Film</a>; <a>Photography</a>, <a>Video</a> and Related Media; Social Documentary Film; and Interaction Design.</p>
<p>There are also non-degree departments offering courses in <a>Art History</a> and <a>Humanities</a> &amp; <a>Sciences</a>, and a Continuing Education Division that offers non-credit courses from most SVA departments.</p>
<h2>Location and campus</h2>
<p>The school has two <a>Manhattan</a> locations: in the <a>Gramercy Park</a> neighborhood, on the East Side; and in the <a>Chelsea</a> neighborhood, on the West Side, with a number of buildings catering to classes in different departments.</p>
<p>;Main building The Main Building is located at 209 East <a>23rd Street</a>, between <a>Second Avenue</a> and <a>Third Avenue</a>, and features classrooms, administrative offices, a cafeteria (Moe's Cafe) and an amphitheater on the third floor. The upper floors are mostly designated for the film, video, graphic design, advertisement, illustration and cartooning classes. The building&#x2019;s lobby and an adjoining room also serve as a museum space for exhibits and public events.</p>
<p>;Second Avenue building The school does not own this entire building, which is located at 380 Second Avenue, but only three of its floors, including the second, where the school&#x2019;s library and some classrooms are located, the fifth floor, where undergraduate animation studios and the graduate design department are located, the seventh floor, where the illustration classrooms and studios are located, and the eighth floor, where administrative offices, and classrooms designated for Humanities and Sciences classes are located.</p>
<p>;Photography building Located at 214 East 21st Street, this building is where classrooms and studios used for undergraduate and graduate photography classes are located, as well as the school&#x2019;s <a>radio station</a>, WSVA, and some administrative offices.</p>
<p>;West Side building This building, located from 133-141 West 21st Street, between <a>Sixth Avenue</a> and <a>Seventh Avenue</a> in <a>Chelsea</a>, contains most of the studios serving drawing and painting classes, particularly for freshmen. It also features classrooms for courses in interior design, printmaking, BFA &amp; MFA computer art, and art history. The lower level also features an art gallery and a cafeteria.</p>
<p>SVA also owns the building across the street, at 132 West 21st Street, which has offices, classrooms and studios for undergraduate <a>Cartooning</a> &amp; <a>Illustration</a>, and graduate <a>Interaction Design</a>, <a>Illustration as Visual Essay</a>, <a>Computer Art</a>, <a>Art Education</a>, <a>Art Therapy</a>, <a>Art criticism</a> and <a>Writing</a>.</p>
<p>;BFA Fine Arts building Located at 335 West 16th Street, this building houses the BFA Fine Arts Department, Sculpture Studios and Digital Lab.</p>
<p>;SVA Theatre Located at 333 West 23rd Street, between 8th and 9th Avenues, in Chelsea. It was formerly the Clearview Chelsea West Cinema, and was purchased in 2008. Renovation of it began that September, and it opened in January 2009. Designer and SVA Acting Chairman Milton Glaser produced designs for the Theater's interior and exterior of the building, including the sculpture situated atop its marquee. The  facility houses two separate auditoriums, one with 265 seats and the other with 480, hosts class meetings, lectures, film screenings and other public events. Community partners that have used the theater include the Tribeca and GenArt film festivals, Mayor <a>Michael Bloomberg</a>&#x2019;s PlaNYC environmental initiative, and the Mayor&#x2019;s Office of Film, Theatre and Broadcasting.</p>
<p>;Galleries SVA has three <a>gallery</a> spaces: the Visual Arts Gallery, at 601 West 26th Street, 15th floor; the West side Gallery, at 141 West 21st Street; and the SVA Gallery/Visual Arts Museum, at 209 East 23rd Street. The galleries show a mix of student and professional art.</p>
<p>;Residence halls There are a number of residence halls for students at SVA:</p>

  <li>The New Residence is located at 215 E. 23rd St. The building houses only freshmen and is an apartment styled dormitory.</li>

  <li>A single dormitory was located at the corner of Lincoln and State Street.
