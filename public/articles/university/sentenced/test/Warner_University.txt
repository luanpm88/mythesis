<p>  <strong>Warner University</strong> is a co-educational, <a>accredited</a> private <a>Christian</a> <a>liberal arts college</a> in <a>Lake Wales, Florida</a> affiliated with the <a>Church of God (Anderson)</a>.</p>
<h2>History</h2>
<p>Warner Southern College was founded in 1968 by the Southeastern Association of the <a>Church of God</a> in <a>Anderson</a>, <a>Indiana</a>.
In 2008, the name was changed to Warner University.</p>
<h2>Campus</h2>
<p>Warner's  campus is located  miles south of <a>Lake Wales, Florida</a>, in the geographic center of the state between <a>Tampa</a> and <a>Orlando</a>.
</p>
<h2>Student enrollment</h2>
<p> Enrollment averages 1,000 students and it has a student-to-faculty ratio of 16:1.
Its student body represents 26 states and 18 countries.</p>
<h2>Awards and recognition</h2>
<p>Warner has received recognition for Campus Diversity by  <em>U.S. News &amp; World Report's</em> &quot;America's Best Colleges&quot;  for four consecutive years.
</p>
<h2>Academics</h2>
<p>Warner is accredited by the Commission on Colleges of the <a>Southern Association of Colleges and Schools</a>, which is recognized by the <a>US Department of Education</a>.[<a>http://www.chea.org</a>]
and offers 20 undergraduate degree programs for traditional students.
For working adults, Warner offers a Master of Business Administration degree, a Master of Arts in Education degree, a Bachelor of Arts in Church Ministry degree, and a Bachelor of Arts in Organizational Management degree.
The Church Ministries and Organizational Management degrees are available via distance learning programs.
</p>
<p>Warner also operates a unique training facility known as the HEART (Hunger Education and Resources Training) Institute.
The HEART Institute is a simulated Third World village which trains students in <a>appropriate technology</a> as well as giving them an opportunity to live in an environment similar to many underdeveloped parts of the world.</p>
<h2>Athletics</h2>

</p>
