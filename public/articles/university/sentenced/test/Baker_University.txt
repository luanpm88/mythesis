<p><strong>Baker University</strong> is a private, residential <a>university</a> located in <a>Baldwin City, Kansas</a>, <a>United States</a>.
Founded in 1858, it is the oldest university in <a>Kansas</a> and is affiliated with the <a>United Methodist Church</a>.
Baker University is made up of four schools.
The College of Arts and Sciences and the undergraduate courses in the School of Education (SOE) are located at the campus in <a>Baldwin City</a>, Kansas.
These schools provide education for students pursuing undergraduate degrees in any of more than 40 areas of study.
The School of Professional and Graduate Studies (SPGS) and the graduate branch of the SOE serve working adults at campuses in <a>Lee's Summit</a> plus <a>North Kansas City, Missouri</a> and <a>Overland Park</a>, <a>Topeka</a>, <a>Lawrence</a>, and <a>Wichita</a>, Kansas.
The School of Nursing, which is operated in partnership with Stormont-Vail HealthCare in Topeka, offers a Bachelor of Science in Nursing degree.
Enrollment in all four schools has grown to a total student population of more than 4,000.
There are more than 900 students on the Baldwin City campus, approximately 3,000 in SPGS and SOE programs, and 160 at the School of Nursing.
A recent survey by the Kansas City Business Journal listed Baker University's MBA program as the most popular in the Kansas City metro area.
With 641 MBA students enrolled in the fall 2009 semester, Baker ranked No. 1.</p>

<h2>Ranking</h2>
<p><a>U.S. News and World Report</a> has most recently ranked Baker University as the number 144 national liberal arts college and the top private university in Kansas.
The <a>Princeton Review</a> consistently ranks the University as a top school in the Midwest within its peer group.
In addition, Baker has been recognized for its value by <a>Money</a> magazine and <a>Barron's</a> Best Buys in College Education.
Four <a>Rhodes Scholars</a>, one winner of the <a>Pulitzer</a> in journalism, two Barry M. Goldwater Scholars, and one Jack Kent Cooke Scholar have graduated from Baker.
There are 16,000 living alumni.</p>
<h2>Athletics</h2>
<p>The school's athletic teams, the Wildcats, have only one official color: orange.
The 17 sports compete in the <a>NAIA</a>, Region V, and the <a>Heart of America Athletic Conference</a>.
Baker University history includes a 22-9 victory against the <a>University of Kansas</a> in the first intercollegiate football game to take place in Kansas.
Additionally, Baker has over 100 All-Americans and nearly 100 conference titles.
Women's sports began at Baker in 1978.
</p>
<p><a>Emil S. Liston</a>, former Baker basketball, football and baseball coach, helped create the NAIA and the first national basketball tournament.
Baker's stadium is now named in his honor.
<a>Charlie Richard</a>, the late former football coach, is honored in the <a>College Football Hall of Fame</a> for his success at Baker.
He has the highest winning percentage of an NAIA football coach and led the Wildcats to the playoffs 10 times during his 14-year tenure.</p>
<h3>Facilities</h3>
<p>Located inside the Charlie Richard Outdoor Sports Facility, Liston Stadium, built in the 1930s, is home to football, track, and select soccer matches.
The majority of soccer matches are played at North Park.
Also in the complex are A.B. Cavaness Field, and Sauder Field, home to the softball and baseball teams.
</p>
<p>Volleyball and basketball compete in the George F. Collins Jr. Center, which is also home to the Athletic Hall of Fame.
The Cross Country team makes its home at the Baldwin City Municipal Golf Course.
</p>
<p>The entire Charlie Richard Outdoor Sports Facility underwent dramatic improvements between 2002 and 2006.
Upgrades included new artificial turf, a new track, the addition of stadium seats and the Tom Hedrick Press Box, lights for baseball and softball, and other improvements.
</p>
<p>Training facilities and the weight room are located in Mabee Memorial Hall, as is the old gym.
Last renovated in 1995, they offer indoor practice space for the cheer and dance squad as well as the baseball and softball teams.</p>
