<p> <strong>Medaille College</strong> is a private <a>liberal arts</a> college located in the historic <a>Olmsted Crescent</a> of <a>Buffalo, New York</a>, that draws extensively from the <a>Western New York</a> and <a>Southern Ontario</a> regions.
It is a private, nonsectarian, co-educational institution.</p>
<h2>History</h2>
<p>Founded in 1875 as the <a>Sisters of Saint Joseph</a> to train teachers.
The school obtained a state charter and became Mount Saint Joseph College in 1937.
In 1968, the school enlarged its mission and gained its current name.
Enrollment is now at 2,759 students.</p>
<h2>College Profile</h2>
<p>Medaille is structured similar to <a>AMCC</a> universities such as <a>Penn State Erie</a> and <a>St.
John Fisher College</a>.
The curriculum is patterned around <a>Liberal Arts &amp; Science</a> foundations, which emphasize <a>interdisciplinary coursework</a> and <a>private university</a> standards.
Within the past year, numerous faculty members have been awarded research grants through such organizations as the <a>National Endowment for the Arts</a>, <a>National Science Foundation</a>, and <a>National Institutes of Health</a>.</p>
<p>A member of the <a>Allegheny Mountain Collegiate Conference</a> (AMCC), Medaille fields 11 <a>NCAA Division III</a> athletic teams for men and women.</p>

<h2>Locations</h2>
<p>Medaille's main campus is located in <a>Buffalo, New York</a>, on a tree-lined urban setting at the intersection of <a>New York State Route 198</a> and Parkside Avenue.
The campus is within the <a>Olmsted Crescent</a>, a historic area of parkways and landscape designed by <a>Frederick Law Olmsted</a>.</p>
<p>Adjacent to <a>Delaware Park</a> and the <a>Buffalo Zoological Gardens</a>, the main campus is easily accessible by car, bus or Metro Rail.
The College is served by the Humboldt-Hospital <a>NFTA</a> rapid transit station, and a circulator shuttle runs frequently between auxiliary parking at the zoo and campus.
Medaille is close to the <a>Albright-Knox Art Gallery</a> and the <a>Burchfield-Penney Art Center</a>, as well as the <a>Buffalo &amp; Erie County Public Library</a>, <a>Museum of Science</a>, and the <a>Erie County Historical Society</a>.</p>
<p>In addition to the Buffalo campus, Medaille also has branch campuses in <a>Rochester, NY</a> and <a>Amherst, NY</a>.
These campuses offer undergraduate degrees for adult students in <a>Business Administration</a>, and graduate degrees in <a>Business Administration</a> and <a>Organizational Leadership</a>.
In addition, the Amherst campus offers graduate degrees in <a>Education</a>, <a>Mental Health Counseling</a>, and <a>Psychology</a>.</p>
<h2>Housing</h2>
<p> Since the fall of 2001, Medaille has built two residence halls.
Both halls use <a>T-3</a> internet access at each bedside, cable-television and additional amenities.
The South Residence Hall offers double-occupancy rooms with private bathrooms, laundry facilities, and computer stations (one per floor).
Student lounges overlooking the campus quad and main building offer a place of study and conversation.
The North Residence Hall contains apartments for upper-class resident students and suites for entering freshmen and current resident students.
Both facilities offer a card-access security feature.</p>
<h2>Athletics</h2>
<p><a>150px|noframe|right</a> Medaille is a charter member of the <a>Allegheny Mountain Collegiate Conference</a>, as an <a>NCAA Division III</a> school.</p>
<p>The women's basketball team won the AMCC championships in 2004-05, 2005&#x2013;06,and 2009&#x2013;2010; the men's soccer team won the AMCC championships in 2005,2006,2007,2008, 2009, and 2010.
In 2010, The men's soccer team also erached the sweet 16, the furthest ant Medaille Sports team has ever advanced; and the baseball team won the AMCC championship in 2007.</p>
<p>The women's basketball team made appearances in the NCAA tournament in 2004-05 and 2005&#x2013;06, and the men's soccer made appearances in 2005 and 2006.</p>
<p>The 2008-2009 men's basketball team reached the AMCC finals for the first time in 2009, the team won its first AMCC championship against Pitt-Bradford, four years after going 0-25.
With the win the team earned an automatic bid to the NCAA Division III tournament.
That run ended early with a first round 90-79 loss at the hands of <a>John Carroll University</a>.</p>
