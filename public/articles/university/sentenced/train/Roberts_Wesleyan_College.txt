<p><strong>Roberts Wesleyan College</strong> (<strong>RWC</strong>) is a Christian <a>liberal arts college</a> located in <a>North Chili</a>, <a>New York</a>.
It is the first educational institution established for <a>Free Methodists</a> in <a>North America</a>.
Roberts is accredited by the <a>Middle States Association of Colleges and Schools</a>, is a member of the <a>Association of Colleges and Universities of the State of New York</a>, the <a>Rochester Area Colleges</a>, the <a>Association of Free Methodist Educational Institutions</a>, the <a>Council of Independent Colleges</a> (CIC), and the <a>Council for Christian Colleges and Universities</a> (CCCU).</p>
<p><strong>Northeastern Seminary</strong> (<strong>NES</strong>) is a <a>graduate school</a> of theology located on the campus of Roberts Wesleyan College.
NES has been fully <a>accredited</a> by the <a>Association of Theological Schools in the United States and Canada</a> since 2003.
It is also accredited by the <a>Middle States Association of Colleges and Schools</a>  and the New York State Board of Regents <a>University of the State of New York</a>.</p>
<h2>History</h2>
<p>Roberts Wesleyan College was originally established as <strong>Chili Seminary</strong> by <a>Benjamin Titus Roberts</a> in 1866.
Roberts was a social activist who opposed slavery and oppression of the poor, and was a supporter of women's right to vote.
He began the school to train young people to become servant leaders with high moral character.</p>
<p>In 1885, its name changed to <strong>Chesbrough Seminary</strong> in response to the $30,000 gift of benefactor A.M. Chesbrough.
In 1945, it was renamed <strong>Roberts Junior College</strong> in honor of B.T. Roberts, the founder both the college and of the <a>Free Methodist Church</a>.
Four years later, it was renamed as Roberts Wesleyan College in order to pay homage to <a>John Wesley</a>, the founder of <a>Methodism</a> and the Wesleyan Church.</p>
<p>In 1998, Roberts Wesleyan College established <strong>Northeastern Seminary</strong> as its <a>graduate school</a> of <a>theology</a>.</p>
<h2>Academics</h2>
<p>Roberts offers undergraduate and graduate programs.
Through Northeastern Seminary, students can complete a Master of Arts in Theology, Master of Divinity, Doctor of Ministry degree, and several certificate programs in Buffalo and Rochester.</p>
<p>The school has an 11:1 student-to-faculty ratio.</p>
<h2>Student life</h2>
<p>The majority of the college's students live in college-owned housing as required by the college for students coming from out of town.
Men and women are in separate dorms.</p>
<p>Students are required to go to 22 of 40 <a>chapel</a> services every semester.
Roberts also requires that its students sign a statement saying that they will abstain from alcohol, tobacco, gambling, plagiarism and sexual immorality while enrolled at the College.
There is no <a>dress code</a>.</p>
<p>Numerous student groups are active on campus as well as off.
Some of these include: <a>Acting on AIDS</a>, At the Foot of the Cross, Beacon Newspaper, <a>Campus Crusade for Christ</a>, Chesbronian Yearbook, Dance Ministry, Drama Ministry, Gospel Choir, <a>Habitat for Humanity</a>, In Jesus' Name (Street Ministry), And He Made Me (Multicultural Student Union - leadership team) Intramural Athletics, mission trips, Student Association, <a>SIFE</a>, and Worship Teams.</p>
<h3>Athletics</h3>
<p>Roberts belongs to two national athletics associations: the <a>National Association of Intercollegiate Athletics</a> (NAIA) and the <a>National Christian College Athletic Association</a> (NCCAA).
Roberts offers twelve varsity sports: <a>basketball</a> (men and women), <a>soccer</a> (men and women), <a>track and field</a> (men and women), <a>cross-country</a> (men and women), <a>tennis</a> (men and women), <a>golf</a> (men), and women&#x2019;s <a>volleyball</a>.</p>
