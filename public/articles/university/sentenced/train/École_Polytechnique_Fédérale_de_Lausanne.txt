<p>The <strong>&#x00c9;cole polytechnique f&#x00e9;d&#x00e9;rale de Lausanne</strong> (<strong>EPFL</strong>) is one of the two <strong>Swiss Federal Institutes of Technology</strong> and is located in <a>Lausanne</a>, <a>Switzerland</a>.
EPFL is ranked among the top universities in the world.
EPFL is evaluated as continental Europe's #1 and world's #20 university in the field of Engineering, Technology and Computer Sciences in the academic ranking of world universities (ARWU) by Shanghai Jiao Tong University in 2010 (Europe's #1 and world's #15 in 2009).
EPFL is in the heart of Europe and is one of Europe's leading institutions of science and technology and is a member of <a>Top Industrial Managers for Europe</a> network.</p>
<p>The school was founded by the Swiss Federal Government with the stated mission to:</p>
<ul>
  <li>Educate engineers and scientists</li>
  <li>Be a national center of excellence in science and technology</li>
  <li>Provide a hub for interaction between the scientific community and industry</li>
</ul>
<p>The sister institution in the German-speaking part of Switzerland is the <em>Eidgen&#x00f6;ssische Technische Hochschule Z&#x00fc;rich</em> (<a>ETH Z&#x00fc;rich</a> or ETHZ).
Associated with several specialised research institutes, the two sister institutes form the <a>ETH Domain</a>, which is directly dependent on the <a>Federal Department of Home Affairs</a>.</p>
<h2>History</h2>
<p>Founded in 1853 as a private school under the name <em>&#x00c9;cole Sp&#x00e9;ciale de Lausanne</em>, it became the technical department of the public <em>Acad&#x00e9;mie de Lausanne</em> in 1869.
When the latter was reorganized and acquired the status of a university in 1890, the technical faculty changed its name to <em>&#x00c9;cole d'Ing&#x00e9;nieurs de l'Universit&#x00e9; de Lausanne</em>.
In 1946, it was renamed the <em>&#x00c9;cole Polytechnique de l'Universit&#x00e9; de Lausanne (EPUL)</em>.</p>
<p>In 1969, the <em>EPUL</em> was separated from the rest of the University of Lausanne and became a federal institute under its current name.
EPFL, like ETHZ, is thus directly controlled by the Swiss federal government.
In contrast, all other <a>universities in Switzerland</a> are controlled by their respective <a>cantonal</a> governments.</p>
<p>EPFL operates a nuclear reactor, <a>CROCUS</a>, a <a>Tokamak</a> <a>fusion</a> reactor, and <a>P3</a> bio-hazard facilities.
Following the nomination of <a>Patrick Aebischer</a> as president in 2000, EPFL has started to develop into the field of life sciences.
It absorbed the <a>ISREC</a> (Swiss Institute for Experimental Cancer Research) in 2008.</p>
<h2>Campus</h2>
<p><a>thumb|Aerial view of the EPFL campus</a> Originally, EPFL was in the center of Lausanne.
In 1978, EPFL moved to its new campus in <a>Ecublens</a>, a suburb south-west of Lausanne on the shores of <a>Lake Geneva</a>.
In 2002, the department of architecture also moved to the campus in Ecublens.
This united all departments of EPFL on the same site.</p>
<h3>Buildings</h3>
<p>The campus consists of about 65 buildings on 136 acres.
Built according to the growth of the school, the campus includes different types of architectures:</p>
<ul>
  <li>Late 70s-80s: modularized building, used today by the Schools of Basic Sciences and Architecture, Civil and Environmental Engineering, Mechanical and Electrical Engineering</li>
  <li>90s: buildings with institutes from the Schools of Engineering Sciences and Techniques, Computer and Communication Sciences, and the Scientific Park (PSE)</li>
  <li>Modern: new buildings (2002-2004) with Microengineering, Communications and Architecture institutes, the School of Life Sciences and the College of Management.</li>
</ul>
<p>EPFL and the nearby <a>University of Lausanne</a> share an active sports center five minutes away from EPFL campus on the shores of Lake Geneva.</p>

<h2>Students and traditions</h2>
<p>Several music festivals are held yearly at EPFL.
The most important one is the <a>Balelec Festival</a>, organized in May.
It proposes about 30 concerts and welcomes 15,000 visitors.</p>
<p>Other smaller festivals include <em>Sysmic</em> organized in April by the students of the Department of Microengineering, hosting two stages for local and national bands, and <em>Artiphys</em>, organized by the students of the Physics Department.</p>
<p>EPFL maintains several long-standing <a>student exchange program</a>s, such as the junior year engineering and science program with <a>Carnegie Mellon University</a> in the United States, as well as a graduate Aeronautics and Aerospace program with the <a>ISAE</a> in France.</p>
<h2>Statistics</h2>
<p>[[Image:Crocus-p1020484.jpg|thumb|[[CROCUS]], the only <a>nuclear reactor</a> of the French-speaking part of Switzerland]] In 1946, there were 360 students at EPFL.
In 1969, EPFL numbered 1,400 students and 55 professors.
The university continued to grow rapidly, and in 2002, there were 5,872 students enrolled.</p>
<p>In 2004 there were more than 9000 people at EPFL.
About 6000 of these were students, with the remainder consisting of professors, assistants and even entrepreneurs located in the <em>Parc Scientifique</em> of EPFL.
There were over 100 nationalities at EPFL, with over 50% of the teaching staff coming from outside Switzerland.</p>
<p>In the year 2009-2010, there were over 7000 students on the campus.</p>
<p>The EPFL advised on the <a>Alinghi</a> project, leading to success in the <a>America's Cup</a> in New Zealand in 2003 and in <a>Valencia</a> in 2007.
EPFL is also developing a sun-powered plane, <em><a>Solar Impulse</a></em>, designed to be completely autonomous (capable of <a>circumnavigation</a>).
<a>Bertrand Piccard</a> is one of the two pilots flying the plane.
There is also a <a>sensor network</a> installed in the I&amp;C building called SensorScope which reports live temperature and light measurements.</p>
<p>For teaching purposes the EPFL hosts the only <a>nuclear reactor</a> in the French-speaking part of Switzerland, <a>CROCUS</a>.</p>
<h2>Rankings</h2>
<p>According to a study conducted by Times Higher Education (THE) based on the publication and citation data provided by Thomson Reuters, EPFL is ranked Nr.1 in Europe in the field of Engineering (November, 2010)  .
Leiden Rankings also rank EPFL Nr.1 in Europe (2010-2011). .
The 2010 <a>QS World University Rankings</a> placed EPFL at 32nd overall in the world, moving up ten places from its position in the 2009 <a>THE-QS World University Rankings</a> (in 2010 <a>Times Higher Education World University Rankings</a> and <a>QS World University Rankings</a> parted ways to produce separate ranking).</p>
