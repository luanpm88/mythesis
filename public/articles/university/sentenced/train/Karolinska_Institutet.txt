Caroline Institute buildings at street <em>Hantverkargatan</em> in [[Kungsholmen]], Stockholm]] <strong>Karolinska Institutet</strong> (often translated from <a>Swedish</a> into <a>English</a> as the <strong>Karolinska Institute</strong>, and in older texts often as the <strong>Royal Caroline Institute</strong>) is one of Europe's largest medical <a>universities</a>.
According to the 2010 <a>Academic Ranking of World Universities</a> by <a>Shanghai Jiao Tong University</a>, the Karolinska Institute is the 42nd ranked university in the world and Sweden's highest ranked university.
It is also Europe's highest ranked university in Clinical Medicine and Pharmacy and the only non-U.S.
medical school ranked within the world's top 10.
In the <a>Times Higher Education World University Rankings</a> the Karolinska Institute ranked 29th on the list of Top 50 universities for Life Sciences and Biomedicine.</p>
<p>The <a>Karolinska University Hospital</a>, located in Solna and Huddinge, is associated with the university as a research and teaching hospital.
Together they form an <a>academic health science centre</a>.
It is one of Sweden's largest centres for training and research, accounting for 30 percent of the medical training and 40 percent of the medical academic research conducted nationwide.
While most of the medical programs are taught in Swedish, the bulk of the <a>Ph.D.</a> projects are conducted in English.</p>
<p>It was founded in 1810 on <a>Kungsholmen</a> island on the west side of <a>Stockholm</a>.
Its main campus was moved decades later and located in <a>Solna</a>, just outside <a>Stockholm</a>, and a second campus more recently in Flemingsberg/Huddinge south of Stockholm.</p>
<p>A committee of the institute appoints the laureates for the <a>Nobel Prize</a> in <a>Physiology or Medicine</a>.
The institute is a member of the <a>League of European Research Universities</a>.</p>
<h2>History</h2>
<p>Karolinska Institutet was founded in the period between 1810 and 1811, following the <a>Finnish War</a>, as a training center for army surgeons.
The original name was at first 'Medico-Chirurgiska Institutet'.</p>
<p>In 1817 the prefix 'Karolinska' was added as a reference to the then Swedish king <a>Karl XIII</a>.
The full name thus became 'Kongliga Carolinska Medico Chirurgiska Institutet'.</p>
<p>In 1968 this name was changed to 'Karolinska Institutet'.</p>

<ul>

<li><a>Carl Gustaf Mosander</a> (1792&#x2013;1858; student of Berzelius, his successor 1836), chemist, discoverer of the elements <a>lanthanum</a>, <a>erbium</a> and <a>terbium</a>.</li>

  <li><a>Ivar Wickman</a> (1872&#x2013;1914), pediatrician, pupil of Medin, polio expert</li>
  <li><a>Hugo Theorell</a> (1903&#x2013;1982), <a>Nobel Laureate in Physiology or Medicine</a> in 1955</li>
  <li><a>Torsten Wiesel</a> (1924-), <a>Nobel Laureate in Physiology or Medicine</a> in 1981</li>

<h2>Research at Karolinska</h2>
<p>Researchers at Karolinska largely focus on medical and biomedical research in all disease areas.
Particular strengths are in neuroscience, cancer biology, immunology, and metabolic diseases.</p>
