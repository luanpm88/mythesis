<p>&lt;onlyinclude&gt;<strong>Strayer University</strong>, formerly <strong>Strayer College</strong> of <a>Baltimore, Maryland</a>, is a private, <a>for-profit</a> <a>educational institution</a>.
The Strayer University campuses are owned by <strong>Strayer Education, Inc.</strong>, headquartered in <a>Arlington, Virginia</a>.</p>
<p>Strayer University specializes in higher education for working adults seeking career advancement.
More than 54,000 students are enrolled at over 80 <a>campus</a>es (in 17 states and the District of Columbia) or in <a>online</a> <a>distance education</a> programs.&lt;/onlyinclude&gt;</p>
<h2>History</h2>
<p><a>S.
Irving Strayer</a> founded a small <a>Maryland</a> college in <a>1892</a>, <strong>Strayer's Business College</strong>.
In <a>1969</a>, the school was renamed Strayer College when it received licensure to grant the <a>Bachelor of Science</a> degree.
Authorization to grant the <a>Master of Science</a> degree followed in <a>1987</a>.
In <a>1996</a>, Strayer Education, Inc., was established to take Strayer College public and raise capital for expansion.
This expansion included the ability to offer online classes to students around the world.
In <a>1998</a>, Strayer College was granted university status by the District of Columbia Education Licensure Commission and became Strayer University.</p>
<p>Currently, Strayer University has more than 75 campuses, to include locations in Alabama, Arkansas, Delaware, Florida, Georgia, Kentucky, Maryland, New Jersey, North Carolina, Ohio, Pennsylvania, South Carolina, Tennessee, Utah, Virginia, Washington, D.C., and West Virginia.</p>
<p>According to MSNBC, Strayer and <a>DeVry Institute</a> produced the most students with degrees in computer science of any college in America in 2001.</p>
<p>On May 29, 2008, Strayer University installed Sondra Stallard as the university&#x2019;s 13th president.
Stallard joined Strayer University after serving as dean of the School of Continuing and Professional Studies at the <a>University of Virginia</a>.</p>
<h2>Campus locations</h2>
<p>Strayer University currently has more than 80 campuses.</p>
<h2>Academic programs</h2>
<p>Strayer University's academic disciplines include degree and certificate programs in accounting, business, criminal justice, economics, information systems, management, public administration, health services administration, and education.</p>
<h2>Strayer Education, Inc.</h2>
<p>Strayer Education, Inc., is a publicly traded corporation (), a <a>holding company</a> for the college and other assets.
The corporation was established in <a>1996</a> to take what was then Strayer College public and raise capital for expansion.
It is located at 1100 Wilson Boulevard, Arlington, Virginia 22209.</p>
<h2>Strayer University Online</h2>
<p>The Online Division of Strayer University is located in an industrial park at 8540 Cinder Bed Road, Lorton, Virginia 22079.</p>
