<p>The <strong>University of Bergen</strong> ()  is located in <a>Bergen</a>, <a>Norway</a>.
Although founded as late as 1946, academic activity had taken place at <a>Bergen Museum</a> as far back as 1825.
The <a>university</a> today serves more than 14,500 students.
It is one of seven universities in Norway, the other six being the <a>University of Oslo</a>, the <a>Norwegian University of Science and Technology</a> in <a>Trondheim</a>, the <a>University of Troms&#x00f8;</a>, the <a>University of Stavanger</a>, the <a>University of Agder</a> and the <a>Norwegian University of Life Sciences</a> in <a>&#x00c5;s</a>.</p>
<h2>Tuition</h2>
<p>Home students (European citizens) do not pay fees to the university.
Students are however required to be members of the student welfare organisation.
As of Fall 2009, this fee (<em>semesteravgift</em>) is NOK 470 (approx.
<a>US$</a> 80) per semester, and provides access to several services, including cultural activities, childcare, refunds for many medical expenses and subsidized accommodation.</p>
<h2>Rankings</h2>
<p>In 2010 the university was ranked as number 135 worldwide by the <a>Times Higher Education World University Rankings</a>&lt;ref&gt;<a>http://www.timeshighereducation.co.uk/world-university-rankings/2010-2011/top-200.html</a>&lt;/ref&gt;, and as number 133 worldwide by the <a>QS World University Rankings</a>&lt;ref&gt;<a>http://www.topuniversities.com/university-rankings/world-university-rankings/2010/results/101-200</a>&lt;/ref&gt;.
UiB was also ranked number 148 worldwide in the July 2010 <a>Webometrics Ranking of World Universities</a>&lt;ref&gt;<a>http://www.webometrics.info/top12000.asp?offset=100</a>&lt;/ref&gt;.</p>

<h3>Faculty of Law</h3>
<p>[[Image:Faculty of Law in Bergen.JPG|thumb|The Faculty of Law at the University of Bergen, seen from [[Magnus VI of Norway|Magnus Lagab&#x00f8;te]]s plass.]]</p>
<p>The Faculty of Law was established as a separate faculty in 1980, with legal studies and research having been conducted at the university since 1969.
The faculty is one of three Norwegian institutions which offer legal studies, the other two being the law faculties at the <a>University of Oslo</a> and the <a>University of Troms&#x00f8;</a>.
The faculty offers a five-year programme leading to a <a>Master's degree</a> in law and a three-year <a>PhD</a> programme, and currently has approximately 1900 students.</p>

<h3>Faculty of Psychology</h3>
<p>The University of Bergen is the only institution in the Nordic countries where the study of <a>psychology</a> has been assigned to its own faculty.
Established in 1980, it educates psychologists and is responsible for the University's <a>pedagogic</a> education.</p>
