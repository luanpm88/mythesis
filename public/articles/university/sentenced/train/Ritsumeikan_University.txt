<p> </pre>
<p> is a <a>private university</a> in <a>Kyoto</a>, <a>Japan</a>.
With the Kinugasa Campus, the university also has a satellite called Biwako-Kusatsu Campus (BKC) in <a>Kusatsu</a>, <a>Shiga Prefecture</a>, and an internationalized <a>Ritsumeikan Asia Pacific University</a> (APU) in <a>Beppu</a>, <a>&#x014c;ita Prefecture</a>.
</p>
<p>Ritsumeikan was founded by Kojuro Nakagawa in 1900, and was upgraded to full university status in 1922.
Historically, the school was seen as a liberal alternative to the state-run <a>Kyoto University</a>.
Today, Ritsumeikan is one of the most internationalized mainstream universities in Japan and it has established exchange programs with hundreds of schools worldwide, including a pioneering exchange with the <a>University of British Columbia</a> and a dual degree program with <a>American University</a>.</p>
<h2>Origins of the name Ritsumeikan</h2>
<p><a>thumb|130px|left</a></p>
<p>Its name was adapted from a <a>Mencius</a> quotation:</p>
<blockquote>
  <p>Some die young, as some live long lives.
This is decided by fate.
Therefore, one's duty consists of cultivating one's mind during this mortal span and thereby establishing one's destiny.</p>
</blockquote>
<p>It literally means &quot;place to establish one's destiny.&quot;

<h2>Facilities</h2>
<p><a>thumb|200px|left|Art Research Center</a></p>
<h3>Research Center for Disaster Mitigation Systems</h3>
<p>Established in April 2005 on the Biwako-Kusatsu Campus, work at this center focuses on disaster mitigation using sensor systems and computer networks.</p>
<h3>Art Research Center</h3>
<p>Located at Kinugasa Campus, this center houses the Digital Humanities Center for Japanese Arts and Cultures, which focuses on research on Japanese art and culture using digital archives, databases and geographical information systems.</p>
<h3>Museums</h3>
<p>The <a>Kyoto Museum for World Peace</a> seeks to critically examine Japan&#x2019;s militaristic past, and includes numerous exhibits ranging from the Sino-Japanese War of 1894-5 to the Iraq War.</p>
<h3>SR center</h3>
<p>Located at BKC campus, This center is established for research using <a>Synchrotron Radiation</a>.</p>
<h2>Controversy</h2>
<p>As of 2006, Ritsumeikan University and the General Union were in dispute over a contract system that limits employment for some educators to three years.</p>
