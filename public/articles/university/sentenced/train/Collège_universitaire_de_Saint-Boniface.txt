<p> The <strong>Coll&#x00e8;ge universitaire de Saint-Boniface</strong>, or CUSB, is a <a>university college</a> affiliated with the <a>University of Manitoba</a> and located in <a>Saint Boniface, Manitoba</a>, <a>Canada</a>.
It is oriented primarily towards the French-speaking community of Saint Boniface and others who want to learn, or be educated through, the French language and <a>Franco-Manitoban culture</a>.
</p>
<h2>History</h2>
<p><a>thumb|left|200px|Louis Riel Sculpture @ Coll&#x00e8;ge universitaire de Saint-Boniface</a></p>
<p>St-Boniface College has a strong <a>Roman Catholic</a> religious affiliation.
It originates from a school for boys founded in 1818 by <a>Monseigneur</a> <a>Norbert Provencher</a>.
It was incorporated into its current form in 1871.</p>
<p>Consolidation was a way to strengthen this small and financially insecure institution.
The University Of Manitoba was established in 1877 by combining three existing church colleges: St. Boniface Roman Catholic, <a>St John's</a> <a>Anglican</a>, and <a>Manitoba College</a> <a>Presbyterian</a>.</p>
<h2>Aboriginal</h2>
<p>The Coll&#x00e8;ge universitaire de Saint-Boniface&#x2019;s Canada Research Chair on M&#x00e9;tis  Identity provides research into Aboriginal culture.</p>
