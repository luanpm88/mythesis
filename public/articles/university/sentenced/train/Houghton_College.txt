<p><strong>Houghton College</strong> is a Christian <a>liberal arts</a> college affiliated with the <a>Wesleyan Church</a>.
The college is a member of both the <a>Christian College Consortium</a> and the <a>Council for Christian Colleges and Universities</a>.
The main campus is in <a>Houghton</a>, in the <a>Genesee Valley</a> of southwestern <a>New York State</a>.</p>
<h2>History</h2>
<p>Houghton College began in 1883 as Houghton Seminary, a <a>coeducation</a>al <a>high school</a> founded by Willard J. Houghton, a <a>Wesleyan</a> <a>Methodist</a> minister.
In 1899, the first few college classes were offered.
James Luckey was appointed president in 1908, and Houghton College received its provisional charter from New York in 1923.
A permanent charter was granted in 1927, and accreditation by the <a>Middle States Association of Colleges and Schools</a> came in 1935.
Stephen Paine was appointed president in 1937.
When the former Buffalo Bible Institute merged with Houghton College in 1969, the West Seneca campus was created.
Wilber Dayton was appointed president in 1972, and <a>Daniel R. Chamberlain</a> was appointed president in 1976.
The college initiated its first <a>master&#x2019;s degree</a> program in 2004, and Shirley Mullen was appointed president in 2006 and currently holds that position.</p>
<h2>Campus</h2>
<p>Houghton College&#x2019;s main <a>campus</a> is in the <a>hamlet</a> of Houghton, in <a>Allegany County, New York</a>, about  southeast of <a>Buffalo, New York</a> and  southwest of <a>Rochester, New York</a>.
The -<a>acre</a> campus sits on the <a>Allegheny Plateau</a> at roughly , on the site of the former Caneadea Indian Reservation.
The West Seneca Campus is the primary setting for Houghton&#x2019;s adult degree completion program in management, called P.A.C.E.</p>
<h2>Academics</h2>
<p>Houghton College grants 2-year and 4-year degrees in 46 majors.
The college also offers two graduate degrees, the <a>master of arts</a> in music and <a>master of music</a>, through the Greatbatch School of Music.
Over 90 percent of all applicants are accepted for admission each year.</p>
<h3>First Year Honors</h3>
<p>A distinctive First Year Honors Program is for qualified first-year students.
There are three options: London Honors, East Meets West, and Science Honors.
The London Honors program began in 1996, and involves the second semester of the first-year spent in <a>London</a>, with several trips elsewhere in the <a>United Kingdom</a>.
The program still continues to explore the roots of Western culture and ideas and uphold academic standards.
</p>
<h2>Student life</h2>
<p>There are two dormitories for female students: Gillette Hall (formerly East Hall) and Lambein Hall.
Men live in Rothenbuhler Hall (formerly South Hall) and Shenawana Hall.
Male and female juniors and seniors can live in two of four townhouses or the coed flats apartments.
Students can also apply to live in approved off-campus housing, called Community Living Opportunities (CLOs).
Seniors with over 120 credits automatically qualify for CLO housing.</p>
<h3>Athletics</h3>
<p>The Houghton Highlanders field 10 varsity teams.
Women participate in <a>soccer</a>, <a>basketball</a>, <a>field hockey</a>, <a>volleyball</a>, <a>track</a>, and <a>cross country</a>.
Men participate in <a>soccer</a>, <a>basketball</a>, <a>track</a>, and <a>cross country</a>.
Houghton is a member of the <a>NAIA</a> <a>American Mideast Conference</a>.
Houghton also has junior varsity teams in men's and women's soccer and basketball.
Club and intramural sports for men are soccer, volleyball, football, basketball, and indoor soccer.
Club and intramural sports for women, soccer, volleyball, basketball, and indoor soccer.
Coed club and intramural sports are handball, water polo, volleyball, and softball.
Coed frisbee is also a popular sport, but is not supported since the team plays games on Sundays.</p>
