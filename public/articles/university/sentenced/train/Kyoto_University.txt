<p>, or   is a major <a>national university</a> in <a>Kyoto</a>, <a>Japan</a>.
It is the second oldest university in Japan, and formerly one of Japan's <a>Imperial Universities</a>.
The university has about 22,000 students enrolled in its undergraduate and graduate programs.</p>
<h2>History</h2>
<p>The forerunner of the Kyoto University was the  founded in <a>Osaka</a> in 1869, which, despite its name, taught physics as well.
( is a <a>transcription</a> of a <a>Dutch</a> word <a>chemie</a>.)
Later, the  was established in the place of <em>Seimi-kyoku</em> in 1886, it then transferred to the university's present main campus in the same year.</p>
<p> as a part of the <a>Imperial University</a> system was established on June 18, 1897, The university claims six Nobel Laureates and two Fields Medalists among its faculty and alumni.
The university is also known as the starting point for the <a>Kyoto School</a> philosophical movement.</p>

<h3>Rankings</h3>
<p>Kyoto University is acknowledged as one of the top two universities in Japan, fueling a rivalry with <a>Tokyo University</a>.
Kyoto University was ranked 24&lt;sup&gt;th&lt;/sup&gt; in the world in the 2010 <em><a>Academic Ranking of World Universities</a></em> and 11&lt;sup&gt;th&lt;/sup&gt; worldwide in the Global University Ranking.&lt;sup&gt;[<a>http://ed.sjtu.edu.cn/rank/2006/ARWU2006_Top100.htm</a>]&lt;/sup&gt;.
A human competitiveness index &amp; analysis by the Human Resources &amp; Labor Review, and published in Chasecareer Network, ranked the university 24th internationally and 2nd in Asia for 2010 .</p>
<p><a>QS World University Rankings</a> in 2010 placed Kyoto University 25th overall in the world, one behind <a>Tokyo University</a>, and maintaining its 2009 position in the <a>THE-QS World University Rankings</a>.
The 2010 global rankings make Kyoto University the third highest ranked university in Asia, the highest being the <a>University of Hong Kong</a>.</p>
<h2>Campuses</h2>
<p><a>thumb|240px|right|The Clocktower</a> The university has three campuses in <a>Yoshida</a>, <a>Kyoto</a>; in <a>Gokash&#x014d;</a>, <a>Uji</a>; and in <a>Katsura, Kyoto</a>.</p>
<p>Yoshida Campus is the main campus, with some laboratories located in Uji.
The Graduate School of Engineering is currently under process of moving to the newly-built Katsura Campus.</p>
<h2>Athletics</h2>
<p> Kyoto University competes in 48 sports.
The university is a member of the <a>Kansai Big Six Baseball League</a>.</p>
