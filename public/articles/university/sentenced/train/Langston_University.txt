<p> <strong>Langston University</strong> is an institution of higher learning located in <a>Langston</a>, <a>Oklahoma</a>, <a>USA</a>.
It is the only <a>historically black college</a> in the state, and the westernmost historically black college in the United States.
Though located in a rural setting just 10 miles (16 km) east of <a>Guthrie</a>, Langston also serves an urban mission with University Centers in both <a>Tulsa</a> and <a>Oklahoma City</a>.</p>
<h2>History</h2>
<p>The school was founded in 1897 and was known as the Oklahoma Colored Agricultural and Normal University.
Langston University was created as a result of the second <a>Morrill Act</a> in 1890.
The law required states with land-grant colleges (such as <a>Oklahoma State University</a>, then Oklahoma A&amp;M) to either admit African-Americans, or provide an alternative school for them to attend as a condition of receiving federal funds.
Langston University is named for <a>John Mercer Langston</a> (1829-1897), <a>civil rights</a> pioneer, first <a>African American</a> member of <a>Congress</a> from <a>Virginia</a>, founder of the <a>Howard University</a> Law School, and American consul-general to <a>Haiti</a>.
</p>
<p>Poet <a>Melvin B. Tolson</a> taught at Langston from 1947 until 1964.
Tolson was portrayed by <a>Denzel Washington</a> in the film <a>The Great Debaters</a>.</p>
<h2>Academics</h2>
<p>The University offers <a>associate's</a>, <a>bachelor's</a>, <a>master's</a>, and <a>doctoral</a> <a>degrees</a> in a variety of <a>discipline</a>s.
It is known as a leader in the field of agricultural research, and hosts the internationally recognized E (Kika) de la Garza Institute for <a>Goat</a> Research.
The School currently has an open doors admissions policy.
LU has nearly 3,000 students from several states representing a diverse student body at an Oklahoma college.</p>
<p>The university was accredited with a <a>Doctorate</a> of <a>Physical Therapy</a> program in 2005.</p>
<h2>Student activities</h2>
<p>The student life at Langston University garnered the attention of <a>BET</a> and the second season of the reality television show <a>College Hill</a> was filmed there.</p>
<p>The university boasts one of the nation's top choral programs and its nearly 150 piece show band is sought after for bowl games, parades, government ceremonies and marching competitions across the nation and abroad.
</p>
<p>The Langston University Quiz Bowl team won games against Spelman and an all-star team from the City Colleges of Chicago on the 2009 nationally televised &quot;Black College Quiz&quot; show airing nationally throughout Black History Month in 2009 to make the championship game.</p>
<h2>In the Media</h2>
<p>In 2010, Langston University garnered the attention of local news media outlets reporting more assaults on the Langston University Campus between the years of 2007 and 2009 than the two largest universities in the State of Oklahoma combined.</p>
