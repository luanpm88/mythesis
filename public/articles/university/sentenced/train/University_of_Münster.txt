<p>The <strong>University of M&#x00fc;nster</strong> (, WWU) is a public <a>university</a> located in the city of <a>M&#x00fc;nster</a>, <a>North Rhine-Westphalia</a> in <a>Germany</a>.
The <em>WWU</em> is part of the <a>Deutsche Forschungsgemeinschaft</a>, a society of Germany's leading research universities.
The WWU has also been successful in the German &quot;Excellence Initiative&quot;.</p>
<p>The WWU is the leading university in Germany in terms of CEO alumni in Top 500 companies.</p>
<p>With more than 40,000 students and over <a>130 fields of study</a> it is Germany's third largest university and one of the foremost centers of German intellectual life.
The university offers a wide range of units (e.g.
Business, Geography, IT, Mathematics and many more) and several courses in English (e.g.
<a>PhD/Doctorate</a>, Masters in Geoinformatics, Geospational Technologies or Information Systems).</p>
<p>The university is associated with nine <a>Gottfried Wilhelm Leibniz Prize</a> (most prestigious as well as most-funding prize in Europe), two <a>Nobel prize</a> (one former student and one professor) and one <a>Fields Medal</a> winners as professors and many more from former students.</p>
<h2>History</h2>
<p>The University has its roots in the Jesuiten-Kolleg M&#x00fc;nster, which was founded in 1588.
But today's University of M&#x00fc;nster was founded in 1780 with four faculties: <a>Law</a>, <a>Health Science</a> (<a>Medicine</a>), <a>Philosophy</a> and <a>Theology</a>.
The ceremony of constitution was performed by <a>Franz Freiherr von F&#x00fc;rstenberg</a>.
In 1805 the university was extended to the <a>Prussia</a>n University of Westphalia.
It got its current name from Emperor <a>Wilhelm II</a> in 1907: Westf&#x00e4;lische Wilhelms-Universit&#x00e4;t (WWU).</p>

<h2>Student life</h2>
<p>The <strong>University of M&#x00fc;nster</strong> offers a very active student life.
Student organisations such as <a>AIESEC</a>, <a>AEGEE</a>, <em>MOVE</em>, <em>MTP</em> and many more are well represented and usually have a high number of members.
A wide range of language courses is also offered (<a>UNIcert</a>).
The city of <a>M&#x00fc;nster</a> itself has a very active night life with over 1.000 bars, discotheques and clubs and the city of the <a>Peace of Westphalia</a> also has - of course - a very rich cultural life.</p>
