<p>The <strong>University of Sudbury</strong> is a <a>Roman Catholic</a> bilingual <a>university</a> in <a>Sudbury</a>, <a>Ontario</a>, <a>Canada</a> which offers courses in <a>French</a> and <a>English</a>.
Programs include <a>religious</a> studies, <a>philosophy</a>, native studies and <a>folklore</a> (entirely in French).
It is a <a>federated school</a> of <a>Laurentian University</a>.</p>
<pre> </pre>
<p>The university is a member of <a>L'Association des universit&#x00e9;s de la francophonie canadienne</a>, a network of academic institutions of the Canadian Francophonie.&lt;ref&gt;<a>AUFC&lt;!-- Bot generated title --&gt;</a>&lt;/ref&gt;</p>
<h2>History</h2>
<p>The university was founded as the <strong>Coll&#x00e8;ge du Sacr&#x00e9;-Coeur</strong> in 1913 and was the first, and for a long time, the only college in <a>Northern Ontario</a>.
The college changed its name in 1957 and became a founding member of the <a>Laurentian federation</a> in 1960.</p>
<p>A plaque was erected by Ontario Heritage Foundation, Ministry of Culture, Tourism and Recreation at Notre Dame &amp; Kathleen Streets, Sudbury </p>
