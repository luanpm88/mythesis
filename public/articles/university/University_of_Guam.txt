<p> <strong>University of Guam</strong> (or <em>Unibetsed&#x00e5;t Gu&#x00e5;han</em> in <a>Chamorro</a> and commonly abbreviated as <em>U.O.G.</em>) is a four-year <a>land-grant institution</a>, located in the village of <a>Mangilao</a> on the island of <a>Guam</a> in the <a>Western Pacific Ocean</a>. It is accredited by the <a>Western Association of Schools and Colleges</a> and offers 34 degree programs at the undergraduate level and 11 master&#x2019;s level programs.</p>
<p>Of the University&#x2019;s 3,387 students, 91% are of <a>Asian</a>-<a>Pacific Islander</a> ethnicity, and nearly 69% are full-time (fall 2008 figures). A full-time faculty of about 180 supports the University&#x2019;s mission of &quot;Ina, Diskubre, Setbe&quot;&mdash;which translates to &quot;To Enlighten, to Discover, to Serve.&quot; </p>
<h2>History</h2>
<p>University Of Guam was founded in 1952 as a two year teacher-training school known as the Territorial College of <a>Guam</a>, established by Governor <a>Carlton Skinner</a>.  In 1960, the college moved to the present campus in the central district of <a>Mangilao</a>. In 1965, the college was accredited as a four-year, degree granting institution. By 1968, enrollment had reached 1,800 students while staff and faculty totaled more than 130. It was designated as a <a>land grant institution</a> by the <a>United States Congress</a> in 1972.</p>
<p><a>Rosa Roberto Carter</a> served as the President of the University of Guam from 1977 to 1983.</p>
