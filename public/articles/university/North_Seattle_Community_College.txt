<p> <strong>North Seattle Community College</strong> (<strong>NSCC</strong>) is a two-year <a>community college</a> in <a>Seattle</a>, <a>Washington</a>. It is one of the three colleges comprising the <a>Seattle Community College District</a> (the others are <a>Seattle Central Community College</a> and <a>South Seattle Community College</a>), and one of the 32 member colleges of the <a>Washington Community and Technical Colleges</a> system. </p>
<p>Founded in 1970, NSCC is <a>accredited</a> by the <a>Northwest Commission on Colleges and Universities</a>, and offers more than 40 career training, <a>continuing education</a>, and <a>college preparation</a> programs.</p>
<h2>Location</h2>
<p>The main <a>campus</a> occupies 62 <a>acre</a>s, including  of environmentally sensitive <a>wetland</a>s, in the <a>Licton Springs/North College Park</a> neighborhood of the <a>Northgate</a> district of Seattle.&lt;!-- Consolidated 3 references into 1 --&gt; The campus is five miles (8&nbsp;km) north of the Seattle city center. The college is easily accessed from <a>Interstate 5</a>, and several <a>King County Metro</a> <a>bus</a> routes.</p>
<h2>Programs</h2>
<p>NSCC programs include college prep and transfer, <a>cross-disciplinary</a>, continuing and senior adult education; business, <a>early childhood education</a>, electronics and engineering, information technology, health and medical, <a>nanotechnology</a>, and <a>real estate</a> professions.  NSCC is also home to the Watch Technology Institute, the only two-year program in the art of Swiss <a>watchmaking</a> and repair in the <a>Western United States</a>.</p>
<h2>Miscellaneous</h2>
<p>North Seattle has a local chapter of the national <a>Phi Theta Kappa</a> honors society.</p>
