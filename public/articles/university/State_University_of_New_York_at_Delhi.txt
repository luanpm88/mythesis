<p>The <strong>State University of New York at Delhi</strong> (also called <strong>SUNY Delhi</strong>) is one of the technology colleges of the <a>State University of New York</a> (SUNY) system. It is located in the <a>Town of Delhi</a>, <a>New York</a>, <a>United States</a> which is in the <a>Catskill Mountains</a>. The university, entirely in Delhi, is partially within the <a>Village of Delhi</a>. Over 2,200 students attend the institution. Delhi&#x2019;s intercollegiate athletic teams are called the Broncos.</p>
<p>Delhi offers 13 <a>bachelor's degree</a>s, 40 <a>associate's degree</a>s and one-year certificates. There are four academic divisions at Delhi: Applied Sciences and Recreation; Business and Hospitality; Liberal Arts and Sciences; and Technology. </p>
<p>Delhi has completed a multi-million dollar renovation of Farrell Hall.</p>

<h2>Veterinary science</h2>
<p>Notable is Delhi&#x2019;s veterinary science technology program -- it was the first of its type in the <a>United States</a>, and it continues to be a model for similar programs around the country.</p>
<h2>Culinary program</h2>
<p>SUNY Delhi&#x2019;s culinary team won the <a>American Culinary Federation</a>&#x2019;s New York State Junior Hot Food Championship in 2000, 01, 02, 03, 04, 05, 06, 07, 08, 2010, the eighth year for Delhi to earn this title in the last 10 years.</p>
