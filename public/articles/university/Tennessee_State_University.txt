<p>   <strong>Tennessee State University (TSU)</strong> is a <a>land-grant university</a> located in <a>Nashville, Tennessee</a>. TSU is the only state-funded <a>historically black university</a> in Tennessee.</p>
<h2>History</h2>
<p> TSU was originally organized as the Agricultural and Industrial State Normal School in 1909 and began serving students on June 19, 1912. Its status was raised to a four-year teachers' college in 1922, and two years later it was renamed the Agricultural and Industrial State Normal College.  After another five years, &quot;Normal&quot; was dropped from its name.</p>
<p>The present-day Tennessee State University exists as a result of the court-ordered merger on July 1, 1979, of Tennessee State University and the former traditionally White institution, the <a>University of Tennessee at Nashville</a>, which had begun as an extension of the Knoxville-based University of Tennessee.</p>
<p>By 2009, approximately 100 of its football players had been drafted by the <a>National Football League</a>.</p>
