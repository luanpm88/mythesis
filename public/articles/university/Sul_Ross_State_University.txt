<p><strong>Sul Ross State University</strong> (SRSU), a <a>public university</a> in <a>Alpine, Texas</a>, is named for former Texas governor, <a>Civil War</a> general <a>Lawrence Sullivan Ross</a>.  It was founded in 1917 as <strong>Sul Ross Normal College</strong> and was made a university in 1969. </p>
<p>Sul Ross State University offers certificate programs and associate, bachelor's and master's degrees. The main campus is situated in the unique environment of the <a>Big Bend</a> region and is the primary institution of higher education serving a 19-county area in far <a>West Texas</a>. SRSU has branch campuses in  <a>Uvalde</a>, <a>Del Rio</a>, and <a>Eagle Pass</a> (see below). </p>
<p>The university is governed by the Board of Regents of the <a>Texas State University System</a>, which guides seven universities in the state.</p>

<h2>History</h2>
<p>The university is named in honor of <a>Lawrence Sullivan &quot;Sul&quot; Ross</a>, the son of a pioneer Texas family who went on to become a Texas Ranger, a Confederate States Army general during the American Civil War, the 19th Governor of Texas (USA), and a president of the Agricultural and Mechanical College of Texas, now called Texas A&amp;M University   .</p>
<p>The now <a>Lubbock</a>-based <a>West Texas Historical Association</a> has on occasion held its annual meeting at Sul Ross.</p>
<h2>Athletics</h2>
<p>The school athletic teams are called the Lobos. They compete in the <a>NCAA</a>'s <a>Division III</a>.</p>
<p>Sul Ross State University's Department of Athletics sponsors Men's Intercollegiate Baseball, Basketball, Cross Country, Football, Tennis and Track along with Women's Intercollegiate Softball, Cross Country, Tennis, Track and Volleyball.</p>
<p>In 1970 and 1971, the women's volleyball team won the first two intercollegiate women's volleyball national championships ever held, conducted by the forerunner of the <a>Association for Intercollegiate Athletics for Women</a>.  Sul Ross State defeated UCLA and Long Beach State, respectively, in the championship matches.</p>
<p>In 2007, it was announced that 59-year-old <a>Mike Flynt</a> would be joining the Sul Ross football team.  He had played college football when he was originally enrolled in college and his <a>athletic eligibility</a> had not expired.  [<a>http://sports.espn.go.com/ncf/news/story?id=2986313</a>].</p>
<h2>Rio Grande College of Uvalde, Del Rio, and Eagle Pass</h2>
<p>Sul Ross State University operates Rio Grande College on the campuses of <a>Southwest Texas Junior College</a> in <a>Uvalde</a>, <a>Del Rio</a> and <a>Eagle Pass</a>. Rio Grande College offers a limited number of both undergraduate and graduate programs.</p>
