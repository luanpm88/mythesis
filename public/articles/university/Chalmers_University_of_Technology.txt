<p> <strong>Chalmers University of Technology</strong> (, often shortened to <strong>Chalmers</strong>), is a <a>university</a> in <a>Gothenburg</a>, <a>Sweden</a>, that focuses on research and education in technology, natural science and architecture. Material science and Architecture programs are one of the top in the world.</p>
<h2>History</h2>
<p>The University was founded in 1829 following a donation by <a>William Chalmers</a> (1748&#x2013;1811), a director of the <a>Swedish East India Company</a>, whose ships sailed across the world to supply Europe with goods from the East. He donated some of his fortune for the establishment of an &quot;industrial school&quot;. Chalmers was run as a private institution until 1937, when the institute became a state-owned university. In 1994 the school once again became a private institution, owned by a foundation. Chalmers is one of only three universities in Sweden which are named after a person, the other two being <a>Karolinska Institutet</a> and <a>Linnaeus University</a>.</p>

<h2>Students</h2>
<p>Approximately 40 per cent of Sweden&#x2019;s graduate engineers and architects are educated at Chalmers. Each year around 250 post graduate degrees are awarded as well as 850 graduate degrees. About 1,000 post-graduate students attend programmes at the university and many students are taking Master of Science engineering programmes and the Master of Architecture programme. From the year 2007 all Master's programmes are taught in English for both national and international students. This was a result of the adaption to the <a>Bologna process</a> that started in 2004 at Chalmers (as the first technical university in Sweden). Currently about 10% of all students at Chalmers come from countries outside Sweden to enroll in a Master's or Ph.D. programme.</p>
<p><a>List of International Masters Programmes at Chalmers</a>&lt;br&gt; <a>Previous List of International Masters Programmes at Chalmers</a> (login now required)</p>
<p>Around 2,700 students also attend Bachelor of Science engineering programmes, merchant marine and other undergraduate courses at <a>Campus Lindholmen</a>. Chalmers also shares some students with Gothenburg University in the joint IT University project. The <a>IT University</a> focuses exclusively on information technology and offers Bachelor and Master programmes with degrees issued from either Chalmers or Gothenburg University, depending on the programme.</p>
<p>Chalmers confers honorary doctoral degrees to people outside the university who have shown great merit in their research or in society.</p>
<p>In July 2009, Chalmers has announced that it will ban students from Iran and North Korea from admissions into their nuclear programmes due to both countries nuclear threats.</p>
<h2>Organization</h2>
<p>Chalmers is an <a>aktiebolag</a> with 100 shares &#x00e0; 1000 <a>SEK</a>&lt;ref&gt;<a>Statues of Chalmers University of Technology Foundation, paragraph 3</a> (this is a translation of the <a>swedish text</a>.)&lt;/ref&gt;, all of which is owned by a private foundation (<a>Chalmers University of Technology Foundation</a>) which appoints the university board and the president. The foundation has its members appointed by the Swedish government (4 to 8 seats), the departments appoints one member, the <a>student union</a> appoints one member and the president automatically gains one chair.&lt;ref&gt;<a>Statues of Chalmers University of Technology Foundation, paragraph 5</a>&lt;/ref&gt; Each department is led by a department head, usually a member of the faculty of that department. The faculty senate represents members of the faculty when decisions are taken.</p>

<h2>Campuses</h2>
<p><a>right|250px|the gate of Chalmers(Gibraltar Campus)</a> In 1937, the school moved from the city center to the new Gibraltar Campus, named after the mansion which owned the grounds, where it is now located. The <a>Lindholmen College Campus</a> was created in the early 1990s and is located on the island of <a>Hisingen</a>. Campus Johanneberg and Campus Lindholmen, as they are now called, are connected by bus line number 16, but there have been numerous complaints that the campuses are too isolated from each other.</p>

<h2>Ties and partnerships</h2>
<p>Chalmers has partnerships with major industries mostly in the Gothenburg region such as Ericsson, Volvo and SKF. The University has general exchange agreements with many European and US universities and maintains a special exchange program agreement with <a>National Chiao Tung University</a> (<a>NCTU</a>) in Taiwan where the exchange students from the two universities maintains offices for, among other things, helping local students with applying and preparing for an exchange year as well as acting as representatives (<a>NCTU Europe - NCTU students at Chalmers</a>, <a>Chalmers Asia - Chalmers students at NCTU</a>). It contributes also to the <a>Top Industrial Managers for Europe</a> (TIME) network.</p>
<p>A close collaboration between the Department of Computer Science and Engineering at Chalmers and ICVR at ETH Zurich is being established.</p>
