<p> The <strong>University of Troms&#x00f8;</strong> () is the world's northernmost <a>university</a>. Located in the city of <a>Troms&#x00f8;</a>, <a>Norway</a>, it was established in 1968, and opened in 1972. It is one of seven universities in Norway. The University of Troms&#x00f8; is the largest research and educational institution in northern Norway. The University's location makes it a natural venue for the development of studies of the region's natural environment, culture, and society.</p>
<p>The main focus of the University's activities is on the <a>Auroral light research</a>, <a>Space science</a>, <a>Fishery science</a>, <a>Biotechnology</a>, <a>Linguistics</a>, <a>Multicultural societies</a>, <a>Saami culture</a>, <a>Telemedicine</a>, social medicine and a wide spectrum of Arctic research projects. The close vicinity of the <a>Norwegian Polar Institute</a>, the <a>Norwegian Institute of Marine Research</a> and the Polar Environmental Centre gives Troms&#x00f8; added weight and importance as an international centre for Arctic research. Research activities, however, are not limited to Arctic studies. The University researchers work within a broad range of subjects and are recognised both nationally and internationally. </p>
<p>On January 1, 2009 the University of Troms&#x00f8; was merged with <a>Troms&#x00f8; University College</a>. </p>

<h2>Logo</h2>
<p>The <a>raven</a>s in the university's logo are <a>Huginn and Muninn</a>. In <a>Norse mythology</a>, Hugin and Munin travel the world for <a>Odin</a>, bringing him news and information. Hugin represents <a>thought</a> and Munin <a>memory</a>. Ravens are an early Norse symbol, used f.ex. on the <a>raven banner</a>.</p>
