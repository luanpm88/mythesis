<p> <a>thumb|right|250px|Barcelona Campus Nord</a> The <strong>Technical University of Catalonia</strong> () or <strong>UPC</strong>, sometimes called UPC-Barcelona Tech (though it exists beyond Barcelona) is the largest engineering university in <a>Catalonia</a>, <a>Spain</a>. The objectives of the UPC are based on internationalization, as it is Spain's technical university with the highest number of international PhD students and Spain's university with the highest number of international master's degree students. In contrast with <a>polytechnics</a> in other parts of the world, the UPC-BARCELONA TECH is a university aiming at achieving the highest degree of engineering excellence and has bilateral agreements with several top-ranked European universities.</p>
<p>The university founded in March, 1971 and was originally known as the <em>Universitat Polit&#x00e8;cnica de Barcelona</em>.  it has 24 schools in <a>Catalonia</a> located in the cities of <a>Barcelona</a>, <a>Castelldefels</a>, <a>Manresa</a>, <a>Sant Cugat del Vall&#x00e8;s</a>, <a>Terrassa</a> and <a>Vilanova i la Geltr&#x00fa;</a>. The UPC has about 30,000 students and 2,500 professors and researchers.</p>
<p>, it hosts Europe's most powerful supercomputer, the <a>MareNostrum</a>.</p>
