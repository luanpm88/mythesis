<p><strong>The Art Institute of Seattle</strong> (AIS) in <a>Seattle</a>, <a>Washington</a> is one of <a>The Art Institutes</a>, a system of more than 40 educational institutions located throughout <a>North America</a>, providing education in design, media arts, fashion and culinary arts.  The Art Institutes system is a subsidiary of <a>Education Management Corporation</a> (EDMC), which is headquartered in <a>Pittsburgh, Pennsylvania</a>.</p>
<p>The Art Institute of Seattle was founded in 1946 as the Burnley School for Professional Art. It became part of The Art Institutes and changed its name to The Art Institute of Seattle in 1982. Accredited by the <a>Northwest Commission on Colleges and Universities</a> (NWCCU) [<a>http://www.nwccu.org/Directory%20of%20Inst/Alpha%20Cluster/a_d.html</a>], The Art Institute of Seattle is located in several buildings near the <a>Elliott Bay</a> waterfront in the <a>Belltown</a> district of <a>Downtown Seattle</a>, near many of the city's design studios, restaurants and corporate offices.</p>
<h2>Fashion Design Program</h2>
<p>The Art Institute of Seattle's fashion design students and faculty have been featured in local newspaper articles including <a>The Seattle Times</a>' April 10, 2008 article about AIS's annual fashion design student showcase &quot;Underground Couture&quot; [<a>http://seattletimes.nwsource.com/html/living/2004337400_nwwcouture10.html</a>], as well as <a>The Seattle Post-Intelligencer</a>'s April 25, 2008 article about fashion trends [<a>http://seattlepi.nwsource.com/business/360526_shortskirts25.html?source=mypi</a>].</p>
<p>In addition, fashion designer Blayne Walsh, a contestant on Season 5 of <a>Bravo (television network)</a>'s <em><a>Project Runway</a></em>, is a 2005 graduate of The Art Institute of Seattle.[<a>http://www.bravotv.com/Project_Runway/season/5/bios/bios.php?designer=blayne</a>]</p>
