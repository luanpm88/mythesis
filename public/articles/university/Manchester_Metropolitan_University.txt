<p> <strong>Manchester Metropolitan University</strong> (<strong>MMU</strong>) is a university in <a>North West England</a>. Its headquarters and central campus is in the city of <a>Manchester</a>, but there are outlying facilities in the county of <a>Cheshire</a>. It is the third largest university in the <a>United Kingdom</a> in <a>terms of student numbers</a> behind the <a>Open University</a> and it's neighbour <a>Manchester University</a>. It is a member of University Alliance and is classed as a new university.</p>
<h2>History</h2>
<p>On 1 January 1970 Manchester <a>Polytechnic</a> was formed by the amalgamation of Manchester College of Art and Design, Manchester College of Commerce (founded 1889) and <a>John Dalton</a> College of Technology. On 1 January 1977, the polytechnic merged with the Didsbury College of Education and Hollings College, and on 1 January 1983 with City of Manchester College of Higher Education. In 1987 the institution became a founding member of the <a>Northern Consortium</a>. Having previously been a local authority institution, the polytechnic became a corporate body on the 1st April 1989, as allowed by the terms of the <a>Education Reform Act 1988</a>.</p>
<p>It was granted university status as &quot;Manchester Metropolitan University&quot; by the <a>Privy Council</a> on 15 September 1992 under the provisions of the <a>Further and Higher Education Act, 1992</a>. The university absorbed Crewe and Alsager College of Higher Education on 1 October 1992 and the Manchester School of Physiotherapy in 2003.</p>
<h2>Campuses</h2>
<p> <a>thumb|Buildings on the MMU Cheshire Alsager campus.</a> <a>thumb|The All Saints Building on the Manchester Campus</a> Its headquarters and central campus stands to the south of Manchester city centre adjacent to the <a>University of Manchester</a> and the <a>Royal Northern College of Music</a>. There are a further four campus locations situated in other parts of the city. Further afield there are campuses at <a>Crewe</a> and <a>Alsager</a> in Cheshire and one in <a>Didsbury</a>.</p>
<p>As of 2010 a &#x00a3;300 million investment programme is under way and will produce the largest physical change to the University's estate since its foundation. The university administration hopes the consolidation from seven to two sites will bring benefits in terms of academic and research collaboration and opportunities for interdisciplinary work and courses.</p>
<h2>Organisation</h2>
<p>The University is led by the Board of Governors which is responsible for the character, educational direction and policy of the university. The University's Vice Chancellor is Professor John Brooks, who, with the directorate and executive are responsible for the implementation of the vision across the university and the operation and management of university. The Directorate comprises the Pro-Vice Chancellors and Deans of faculties.</p>
<p>It is organised into eight faculties:</p>
<ul>
  <li>Art and Design</li>
  <li>Health, Psychology and Social Care</li>
  <li>Humanities, Law and Social Science</li>
  <li>Science and Engineering</li>
  <li>MMU Business School</li>
  <li>MMU Cheshire</li>
  <li>MMU Institute of Education</li>
  <li>Hollings Faculty</li>
</ul>
<p><a>The Manchester Metropolitan School of Theatre</a> is a <a>drama school</a>, which trains students to become professional actors. The course offered is a BA (Hons) in Acting. The school is one of the 22 members of the <a>conference of drama schools</a> and the <a>National Council for Drama Training</a>, which means the course is a nationally acclaimed programme. Students who complete the course successfully are eligible for full <a>equity</a> status. The school has links with many local theatres and television companies, such as <a>Granada</a> and the <a>BBC</a>. Graduates from the school of theatre include <a>Amanda Burton</a>, <a>Steve Coogan</a>, <a>Richard Griffiths</a>, <a>Graham Fellows</a>, <a>Victoria Wood</a>, <a>Julie Walters</a> and <a>Burn Gorman</a>.</p>
<p>In the last academic session, the University had over 34,000 students. The University employs 4,400 staff, comprising almost 1,500 full-time teaching staff, 700 part-time teaching staff and 2,200 support staff.</p>
<h2>Research</h2>
<p>MMU has ten research institutes with many more research groups throughout the university. The institutes are:</p>
<ul>
  <li>The Research Institute for Business and Management</li>
  <li>The Dalton Research Institute</li>
  <li>The Education and Social Research Institute</li>
  <li>The English Research Institute</li>
  <li>The Information Research Institute</li>
  <li>The Manchester European Research Institute</li>
  <li>The Manchester Institute for Research and Innovation in Art and Design</li>
  <li>The Manchester Institute of Social and Spatial Transformations</li>
  <li>The Research Institute for Health and Social Change</li>
</ul>
<h2>Students' Union</h2>
<p> The <a>Students' union</a> has buildings on the All Saints, Crewe and Alsager campuses. The Students' Union MMUnion exists to represent all members at the Manchester Metropolitan University and students on accredited external courses. MMUnion is controlled by the Union Officers Group formed of seven students and graduates of the university, elected by the students to control the Union on their behalf.</p>
