<p><strong>National Cheng Kung University</strong> (), also <strong>Koxinga University</strong>, is a <a>national university</a> in <a>Tainan City</a>, <a>Taiwan</a>. Its abbreviation is NCKU. In Chinese, it is shortened to &#x6210;&#x5927; (Ch&#x00e9;ng D&#x00e0;). Cheng Kung is named after <a>Koxinga</a> (Cheng Ch'eng-kung), a historical personage who defeated the <a>Dutch</a> and founded the <a>Kingdom of Tungning</a> in Taiwan. </p>
<p>NCKU is one of the most prestigious universities in Taiwan, with high reputations in <a>science</a>, <a>engineering</a>, <a>medicine</a>, <a>management</a>, <a>planning</a> &amp; <a>design</a>. According to <a>Times Higher Education</a>- QS World University Rankings and <a>Performance Ranking of Scientific Papers for World Universities</a>, NCKU was ranked second or third among all universities in Taiwan. Many surveys throughout the years also show that its students are most desired by Taiwanese companies. NCKU has 9 colleges, 40 departments, 82 graduate institutes, and 54 research centers, and is also a major top university, education center and area network center in Taiwan. </p>
<h2>Overview</h2>
<p>Currently, there are nearly 22,000 students in National Cheng Kung University through 9 colleges, 40 departments, 82 graduate institutes, and 55 research centers. Starting from 2006, the <a>Ministry of Education</a> will offer NCKU 1.7 billion <a>NT$</a> annually for 5 consecutive years for its excellent academic performance and potential.</p>
<h2>History</h2>
<p>National Cheng Kung University was established under <a>Japanese occupation</a> in January 1931 as . After the end of Japanese rule, the school name was changed to Taiwan Provincial Tainan Junior College of Technology (&#x53f0;&#x7063;&#x7701;&#x7acb;&#x53f0;&#x5357;&#x5de5;&#x696d;&#x5c08;&#x79d1;&#x5b78;&#x6821;), then Taiwan Provincial College of Engineering School (&#x53f0;&#x7063;&#x7701;&#x7acb;&#x5de5;&#x5b78;&#x9662;). </p>
<p>When central government of the <a>Republic of China</a> moved to Taiwan in 1949, it was one of the three existing colleges in Taiwan.  As the number of colleges expanded, it was upgraded to a <a>provincial</a> university in 1956, then national university in 1971. Former Minister for Education <a>Wu Jin</a> served as the first president of the new National Cheng Kung University.  </p>
<h2>Campuses</h2>
<p><a>thumb|250px|Chi Mei Building, Tzu-Chiang Campus</a> NCKU boasts 9 campuses: Cheng-Kung, Sheng-Li, Kuang-Fu, Chien-Kuo, Tzu-Chiang, Ching-Yeh, Li-Hsing, Kuei-Jen, and An-Nan Campuses.</p>
<p>On January 12, 2011, the Y. S. Sun Green Building Research Center was inaugurated. It is the world's first green educational center as well as Taiwan's first <a>zero carbon</a> building. The -facility cost NT$30 million (US$4.41 million) to construct, below the original NT$180 million budget.&lt;ref name=&quot;quirky&quot; /&gt; Natural ventilation, limited power usage, reduced window size, and energy efficient lighting all contribute to the building's carbon neutrality.&lt;ref name=&quot;quirky&quot; /&gt;</p>
