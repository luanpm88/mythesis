<p><strong>Appalachian State University</strong> is a comprehensive (<a>Master's L</a>), <a>public</a>, <a>coeducational</a> university located in <a>Boone, North Carolina</a>, United States. Appalachian () State, also referred to as <strong>App State</strong>, <strong>Appalachian</strong>, <strong>ASU</strong> or simply <strong>App</strong>, is the sixth largest institution in the <a>University of North Carolina</a> system. The university has been ranked among the top 15 Southern Master's Universities since the <a>U.S. News and World Report</a>'s <em>America's Best Colleges Guide</em> began publication in 1986. In 2001, Appalachian was recognized by <a>TIME</a> Magazine as a <em>College of the Year</em>.</p>
<h2>History</h2>
<p>Appalachian State University began in 1899 when a group of citizens in <a>Watauga County</a>, under the leadership of Blanford B. Dougherty and his brother Dauphin D. Dougherty, began a movement to educate teachers in <a>Northwest North Carolina</a>. Land was donated by Daniel B. Dougherty, father of the leaders in the enterprise, and by J. F. Hardin. On this site a wood frame building, costing $1,000, was erected by contributions from citizens of the town and county. In the fall of 1899, the Dougherty brothers, acting as co-principals, began the school which was named <strong>Watauga Academy</strong>. The first year saw 53 students enrolled in three grades. compared to a total ASU enrollment of 15,871 students. The campus encompasses , including a main campus of  with 21 residence halls, four dining facilities, 19 academic buildings, and 11 recreation/athletic facilities. It displays rotating exhibits indoors and outdoors, some exhibits being culturally specific to the Appalachians, and offers community outreach programs through art courses. Farthing Auditorium, a 1,734 seat performance venue, hosts artists from around the world.</p>
<h2>Administration</h2>
<p>The University of North Carolina's Board of Governors plans and develops the coordinated system of higher education with the state. They establish university policy but delegate daily operation of Appalachian State to a <a>chancellor</a>. The chancellor likewise delegates some duties to the <a>provost</a>, several vice-chancellors, and other administrative offices. These administrative offices are advised by several university committees on the needs of campus constituents, as represented by a Faculty Senate, Staff Council and <a>Student Government Association</a>.</p>

<h2>Student life</h2>
<p>Students at ASU enjoy a variety of outdoor activities. The mountains offer snowboarding, skiing, tubing, rock climbing, hiking, rafting, camping, and fishing on and around the Blue Ridge Parkway. ASU also supports many clubs and organizations such as Greek organizations, academic and diversity clubs, and sports clubs.</p>
<p>ASU also offers off campus courses. Off campus programs offer students the ability to maintain family and careers while working toward a degree. Full-time undergraduate programs are available in Elementary Education, Advertising, Criminal justice, Management, and Psychology.</p>
<h2>Greek life</h2>
<p>Greek life on Appalachian State University's campus is made up of 17 fraternities and 8 sororities and these members compose roughly 10 percent of the campus population.  There are several events that the Greeks hold each year.  Lots of these raise money for the surrounding community.  Such events include Lip Sync which takes place during the Annual Greek Week and raises money for a local nonprofit agency.  Greek community service events are held by each fraternity and sorority on campus.  Individual sororities and fraternities all have major philanthropies that hold certain events each year to raise money for the specific cause.  Sororities and fraternities mix with each other at social functions and also encourage a strong Panhellenic unity.  Not only do fraternities and sororities mix at social functions, but also mix to do service events like roadside clean-up.</p>
<p>Appalachian State has a Panhellenic Residence Hall which houses members of Alpha Delta Pi, Alpha Phi, Chi Omega, Delta Zeta, Kappa Delta, Phi Mu, Sigma Kappa, and Alpha Omicron Pi.</p>
<p>IFC Fraternities on campus include Alpha Sigma Phi, Delta Chi, Delta Tau Delta, Kappa Alpha Order, Kappa Sigma, Lambda Chi Alpha, Pi Kappa Alpha, Pi Kappa Phi,Sigma Alpha Epsilon, Sigma Phi Epsilon, Tau Kappa Epsilon and Theta Chi.<a>Phi Gamma Delta</a> (FIJI) will colonize in Fall 2009.</p>
<p>NPHC Organizations on campus include Alpha Phi Alpha, Alpha Kappa Alpha, Delta Sigma Theta, Kappa Alpha Psi, Omega Psi Phi, Zeta Phi Beta and Phi Beta Sigma.</p>
<p>Appalachian State also features three music organizations, including <a>Phi Mu Alpha Sinfonia</a>, <a>Kappa Kappa Psi</a>, and <a>Sigma Alpha Iota</a>.</p>
<h2>Athletics</h2>
<p> Appalachian's sports teams are nicknamed the <a>Mountaineers</a>. The Mountaineers compete in <a>Division I</a> of the <a>National Collegiate Athletic Association</a> (NCAA) and are members of the <a>Southern Conference</a>. Appalachian fields <a>varsity team</a>s in 20 sports, 10 for men and 10 for women. The <a>Mountaineer football</a> team competes in the <a>Football Championship Subdivision</a> (formerly I-AA).</p>
<p><a>Kidd Brewer Stadium</a> is the 25,000 seat home of Appalachian football. Affectionately nicknamed &quot;The Rock&quot;, the stadium is located at an elevation of .</p>
<p>The <a>George M. Holmes Convocation Center</a> is the home court for Appalachian's basketball teams. The  arena, with seating for 8,325, is also the home for volleyball and indoor track and field.</p>
<p>University Recreation (UREC) also offers 19 club sports that compete with other regional institutions on a non-varsity level. They are: lacrosse (men's and women's), rugby (men's and women's), soccer (men's and women's), ultimate frisbee (men's and women's), volleyball (men's and women's), climbing, cycling, equestrian, fencing, ice hockey, skiing, racquetball, snowboarding, swimming, and triathlon.</p>
<p>The university's cycling team has had success at the regional and national level, they compete within the Atlantic Collegiate Cycling Conference.  The team competes in every discipline of <a>bicycle racing</a> that is achnowledged by <a>National Collegiate Cycling Association</a> within <a>USA Cycling</a>.  This includes <a>road bicycle racing</a>, <a>Mountain bike racing</a> and <a>Cyclocross</a>.  The team won the Division 2, as established by <a>USA Cycling</a>, collegiate team mountain bike national championships in 2008.  They won the Division 2 collegiate team cyclocross national championships in 2008 and 2009.  The team is now recognized as a Division 1 team.</p>
<h4>Football</h4>
<p></p>
<p>Appalachian won three consecutive Division I FCS (I-AA) <a>national championships</a> in <a>2005</a>, <a>2006</a>, and <a>2007</a>, over the <a>University of Northern Iowa</a>, the <a>University of Massachusetts</a>, and the <a>University of Delaware</a>, respectively. The Mountaineers are the first FCS team to win three straight national championships since the playoffs began in 1978. They are also the first Division I program to win three consecutive national championships since <a>Army</a> accomplished the feat in 1944, 1945, and 1946.</p>
<p>In a milestone for ASU athletics, the Appalachian State football team played their season opener at the <a>fifth-ranked</a> <a>University of Michigan</a> in front of the largest crowd to ever witness an ASU football game on September 1, 2007. Appalachian State beat <a>Michigan</a> in the game that would become known as &quot;The Horror&quot; <a>34-32</a> and became the first <a>Division I FCS</a> (I-AA) football team to defeat a <a>Division I FBS</a> (I-A) team ranked in the <a>AP</a> poll.</p>
<h2>Events and news</h2>
<p>An Appalachian Summer Festival, hosted by the university, has been named one of the &quot;Top 20 Events in the Southeast&quot; by the Southeast Tourism Society for more than a decade.</p>
<p>In 2004, a committee for the Appalachian Family Caravan tour created a promotional video titled &quot;<a>Hot Hot Hot</a>,&quot; shown throughout the area by Chancellor Kenneth E. Peacock. The video became an inadvertent <a>internet phenomenon</a> and was featured on <a>VH1</a>&#x2019;s <em><a>Web Junk 20</a></em> program in early 2006. The video was never intended to promote Appalachian State to anyone but the Family Caravan, much less as a recruiting tool for prospective students. The video is no longer used by the university, due to student and alumni protests.</p>
<p>In 2002, <a>MTV</a>'s program <em><a>Road Rules</a></em> visited ASU to produce an episode called <em>Campus Crawl</em>, aired on-campus during an annual, winter student swimming event called the &quot;Polar Plunge&quot;. The show's participants also crossed a high-wire strung between Coltrane and Gardner Halls.</p>
<p>On July 2, 2008 The Appalachian State Football team received an ESPY nomination for the biggest upset in their 34-32 win over the Michigan Wolverines on September 1, 2007</p>
<p>On April 23, 2010 Former ASU Quarterback Armanti Edwards was drafted by the Carolina Panthers. Though a superb quarterback at ASU the Panthers plan on using Edwards as a wide receiver.</p>
