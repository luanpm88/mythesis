<p> </p>
<p>The <strong>University College of the North (UCN)</strong> is a post-secondary institution located in Northern <a>Manitoba</a>, <a>Canada</a>. UCN has a student body of approximately 3500 annually, and a staff of approximately 230.   The president and vice-chancellor of UCN is Dr. Denise K. Henning. The Chancellor for UCN is <a>Ovide Mercredi</a>, former National Chief of the <a>Assembly of First Nations</a>.</p>
<h2>Student life</h2>
<ul>
  <li>UCN is committed to providing students with education and training characterized by a culture of openness, inclusiveness, and respect of Aboriginal and Northern values.</li>
</ul>
<h2>Mission</h2>
<p>The mission of the University College of the North is to ensure northern communities and people will have opportunities, knowledge and skills to contribute to an economically, environmentally and culturally healthy society inclusive and respectful of diverse northern and Aboriginal values and beliefs.</p>
<h2>Vision</h2>
<p>Rooted in the sacred teachings of Aboriginal peoples, the University College of the North prepares lifelong learners and graduates as positive and effective leaders through the creation of knowledge and the development and delivery of innovative and ethical programming.</p>
<h2>History</h2>
<p> It was established on July 1, 2004, with the passage of the <em>University of the North Act</em> in the <a>Legislative Assembly of Manitoba</a>.  It is the successor to the former <strong>Keewatin Community College (KCC)</strong>.</p>
<h2>Campus</h2>
<p><a>thumb|300px|right|University College of the North's The Pas campus.</a></p>
<ul>
  <li>UCN has two main campuses, in <a>The Pas</a> and <a>Thompson</a>. There are 12 regional centres in <a>Churchill</a>, <a>Cross Lake</a>, <a>Easterville</a>, <a>Flin Flon</a>, Pukatawagan(Mathias Colomb), Grand Rapids (Mistapawistik), Nelson House (Nisichawayasihk ), Norway House (Kinasao Sipi), Oxford House (Bunibonibee), St. Theresa Point (Island Lake), <a>Split Lake</a>, and <a>Swan River</a>. Nine of UCN's 12 regional centres are in First Nations communities.</li>
</ul>
<h2>Programs</h2>
<ul>
  <li>UCN provides more than 65 degree, diploma, and certificate programs in the faculty of arts and science, faculty of trades and technology, faculty of health, faculty of education, faculty of business, and as part of community-based contract training.</li>
</ul>
<h2>Aboriginal</h2>
<p>The UCN Council of Elders provides guidance through the sharing of traditional knowledge, beliefs and values. There are Aboriginal Centres at UCN's two main campuses. The Mamawechetotan Centre in The Pas and ininiwi kikinwamakewin in Thompson offer programs that promote cross-cultural awareness.</p>
