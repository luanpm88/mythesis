<p><strong>Athlone Institute of Technology</strong> (<strong>AIT</strong>, <em>Institi&#x00fa;id Teicneola&#x00ed;ochta Bhaile &#x00c1;tha Luain</em> in <a>Irish</a>) formerly <strong>Regional Technical College, Athlone</strong> is one of the original network of <a>Regional Technical Colleges</a>; it was built in <a>Athlone</a>, <a>Ireland</a>. It was established in 1970 and was upgraded to an <em>Institute of Technology</em> in 1998.</p>
<p>The institution offers a wide variety of <a>undergraduate</a> and <a>postgraduate</a> courses: <a>National Certificate</a>s, <a>National Diploma</a>s and <a>academic degrees</a>. The institution offers the only <a>polymer</a> degree course in Ireland.</p>
<p>The college consists of two <a>campuses</a>, the main one on the East side of Athlone, housing most of the faculties and academic facilities such as the library, while the Northgate Street campus, in the town centre, is home to the Art and Design courses.</p>
