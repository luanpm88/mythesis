<p> <strong>Lane College</strong> is a four-year, <a>historically black college</a> associated with the <a>Christian Methodist Episcopal Church</a>, located in <a>Jackson, Tennessee</a>, just northeast of the downtown area. It is primarily a <a>liberal arts</a> institution.</p>
<h2>History</h2>
<p>Lane College was founded in 1882 by the Colored Methodist Episcopal (C.M.E.) Church in America as the &#x201c;C.M.E. High School.&#x201d; Planning for the school had begun in 1878, but the school's establishment was delayed by a <a>yellow fever</a> epidemic in the region in 1878. Its primary purpose was the education of newly freed <a>slave</a>s, and the original curriculum focused on the preparation of &quot;teachers and preachers.&quot; Bishop Isaac Lane of the C.M.E. Church is considered to have been the school's founder, and his daughter, Jennie Lane, was its first teacher.</p>

<h2>Athletics</h2>
<p>The Lane College Department of Athletics sponsors men's intercollegiate baseball, basketball, football, cross country, and tennis along with women's intercollegiate softball, basketball, cross country, volleyball and tennis.</p>
<p>The athletic teams <a>nickname</a>d the &quot;Dragons&quot; are sponsored and compete in <a>Division II</a> of the <a>NCAA</a>. The athletic teams compete as a part of the <a>Southern Intercollegiate Athletic Conference</a>. <img></p>

<h2>Namesake</h2>
<p><a>SS Lane Victory</a>, a <a>World War II</a> Victory Ship,  and one of the few such ships surviving, was named for Lane College.  It is now docked in <a>San Pedro, California</a> (which is part of the commercial harbor area of Los Angeles to the south of downtown). It is now open as a museum.</p>
