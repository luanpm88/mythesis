<p><strong>Nassau Community College</strong> (NCC) is a <a>two-year college</a>. It is located in <a>East Garden City, New York</a>, <a>USA</a>. The school is in <a>Nassau County</a> on <a>Long Island</a>. NCC maintains a nationwide reputation for academic excellence and ease of transferability to four-year institutions.</p>
<h2>History</h2>
<p><a>thumb|left|Buildings P and T</a> Created as part of the <a>State University of New York</a> (SUNY) in 1959. When the college opened, on February 1, 1960, it had 632 students, and classes were held in an old courthouse. When <a>Mitchel Air Force Base</a> was closed, the college obtained substantial property, including buildings to develop its new campus, on what is now known as <a>Mitchel Field</a>. (The government still retains some housing and other facilities in the vicinity of the Nassau campus.) </p>
<h2>Academics</h2>
<p>Nassau Community College annually awards the largest number of <a>Associate degree</a>s in the State of New York and the third largest number of Associate degrees for two-year public colleges in the United States. The strongest programs at Nassau Community College are <a>mathematics</a>, <a>physics</a>, <a>chemistry</a>, and <a>engineering</a>.</p>
<p>NCC offers the A.A. degree in Liberal Arts focused on humanities and social sciences, specialized A.A. and A.S. transfer degrees, and A.A.S. degrees and certificate programs designed to serve immediate employment goals of students.&lt;ref name=AcademicOverview/&gt;  Intern/work-study opportunities with local, national and international businesses and organizations are available for students in both career and liberal arts programs.&lt;ref name=AcademicOverview/&gt;  </p>
<h2>Nassau Community College Cultural Program</h2>
<p>This organization within the Nassau Community College arranges theater productions on campus in the NCC College Center Building and also presents &quot;Classics for Kids&quot; which is for both parents and children.</p>
