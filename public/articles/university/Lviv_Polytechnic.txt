<p><strong>Lviv Polytechnic National University</strong> () is the largest scientific university in <a>Lviv</a>. Since its foundation in 1844 it was one of the most important centres of science and technological development in <a>Central Europe</a>. In the <a>interbellum period</a>, the Polytechnic was one of the most important technical colleges in Poland, together with the <a>Warsaw Polytechnic</a>. Today the University is also the National Defense University (Military Institution).</p>
<h2>History</h2>
<p></p>
<h3>Austrian Empire</h3>
<ul>
  <li>1817 : The <a>Austrian Empire</a> opened a secondary technical school in <a>Lemberg</a>, divided into a technical school and a commercial school.</li>
  <li>November 4, 1844 : The school was upgraded to the Technical Academy Lemberg. Its first director was <a>Austria</a>n <a>Florian Schindler</a>, former director of the Technical College in <a>Br&#x00fc;nn</a> (Brno). The building was situated at the corner of Virmenska and Teatralna streets in the building of Darovsky. The school had two departments - technical and commercial. Education lasted three years. </li>
  <li>November 1, 1848 : During the <a>Revolutions of 1848</a>, the town's center was shelled by the Austrian artillery of General <a>Wilhelm Hammerstein</a>. The building of the Technical Academy was destroyed by fire. Lectures were held in Town Municipality building (3-rd floor) and continued there till 1850.</li>
  <li>December 4, 1850: Studies resume in the newly-restored building.</li>
  <li>1851 The number of students at the Technical Academy was 220, out of which 98 were Polish, 50 Jewish, 48 German, 19 Ukrainian/Ruthenian, 4 Czech and 2 Hungarian. In the same year, professor <a>Wawrzyniec Zmurko</a> (graduate of the Vienna Polytechnic) became director of the Department of Mathematics, as the first Pole in the history of the school. Zmurko is considered as founder of the <a>Lemberg School of Mathematics</a>.</li>
  <li>1852/1853: The beginning of the Academy reorganization, which was suggested by <a>Josef Weiser</a>. He wanted the Academy to be modelled after Paris Polytechnic, with two-level education.</li>
  <li>1857-1868 Rudolf G&#x00fc;nsberg was the assistant of Chemistry and the assistant Professor of Technological Chemistry. </li>
  <li>1870 : A Decree of Emperor <a>Franz Joseph I of Austria</a> established Polish as the official language of the school. Most professors not proficient in Polish left the Polytechnic.</li>
  <li>1872 : The Ministry of Affairs of Religions and Education gave permission to teach chemical technologies. Rudolf G&#x00fc;nsberg started as the full Professor of Applied Chemistry.</li>
  <li>March 12, 1872 : Professor of physics <a>Feliks Strzelecki</a> was elected as the first Rector.</li>
  <li>April 1, 1874 - October 1877 : Academy obtained permission to build new academic premises. <a>Julian Zachariewicz</a> was elected as the construction superintendent. He ordered that the facade of the building be modelled after the building of the Munich Polytechnic.</li>
  <li>October 7, 1877 : The first telephone conversation on the territory of the <a>Austro-Hungarian Empire</a> took place, followed by a lecture of Doctor <a>Roman Gostkowski</a>. The Telephone line connected the Assembly Hall of the Main Building with the premises of the Department of Technical Chemistry.</li>
  <li>November 15, 1877 : Inauguration of the new Rector - Professor of architecture Julian Zachariewicz. On the same day, consecration of newly-constructed school's building took place, carried out by three Lvov's Archbishops - Roman Catholic, Greek-Catholic and Armenian-Catholic and witnessed by Governor of Galicia, <a>Alfred Potocki</a>.</li>
  <li>1877 : Technical Academy was renamed to Polytechnical School (<em>Technische Hochschule</em>). However, the rector as well as other professors refrained from using a German-sounding name, and insisted on calling it in Polish <em>Szkola Politechniczna</em>. </li>
  <li>September 13, 1880 : Emperor Franz Joseph I visited the Polytechnical School. During that visit he ordered <a>Jan Matejko</a> to depict the technical progress of mankind in 11 pictures. Now these pictures decorate the Assembly Hall. The Emperor signed a guest book in Polish, the book is now kept in <a>Wroc&#x0142;aw</a>.</li>
</ul>

<ul>
  <li>1893 Due to efforts of <a>Stanislaw Madejski</a>, Minister of Education of Galicia, diplomas of the Polytechnic are regarded equal to diplomas of other renowned European schools of this kind. </li>
  <li>1894 The 50th jubilee of the Polytechnical School. To commemorate that date professor <a>W&#x0142;adys&#x0142;aw Zaj&#x0105;czkowski</a> published the book &quot;The Imperial Polytechnical School in Lviv. Historical essay on its foundation and development as well as its present state&quot;.</li>
  <li>February 13, 1894  Polytechnic School Statute was adopted.</li>
  <li>1905 Lviv Polytechnical School possessed the second place in the number of students after Vienna.</li>
  <li>1914 As there were no limits on foreign students, in that year students from Russian part of Poland made some 30% of all. In that year, the school owned 11 laboratories and an astronomical station, and its library had some 20 000 books.</li>
</ul>
<h3>Second Polish Republic</h3>
<ul>
  <li>November 1918: Students and professors of the Polytechnic take part in the <a>Polish-Ukrainian war</a> over Eastern Galicia. Among those fighting on Polish side, there are <a>Kazimierz Bartel</a>, <a>Stefan Bry&#x0142;a</a> and <a>Antoni Wereszczynski</a>, who later became the rector.  </li>
  <li>November 8, 1919: Polish Government Unifies the <a>Agricultural Academy in Dublany</a> and Higher School of Forestry (Lwow) with Politechnical School.</li>
  <li>June 28, 1920: Adoption of the New Statute and renaming the Polytechnical School into Lviv Polytechnic (Polish: <em>Politechnika Lwowska</em>).</li>
  <li>November 19, 1922: The Polytechnic is awarded by the Polish Government with Cross of Defenders of Lw&#x00f3;w. Earlier in that year, Marshall of <a>France</a> <a>Ferdinand Foch</a> comes to Lwow and is awarded the title of <em>doctor honoris causa</em> of the school.</li>
  <li>February 23, 1931: Council of the Faculty of Agriculture and Forestry of Polytechnic conferred academic rank of Honorary Doctor to professor <a>Nils Handson</a> (<a>Stockholm</a>, <a>Sweden</a>).</li>
  <li>1934: Construction of the building of the Library on Professor Street 1 was finished.</li>
  <li>November 11, 1936: President <a>Ignacy Mo&#x015b;cicki</a> awards the school with Order <a>Polonia Restituta</a> in appreciation of its achievements.</li>
  <li>October 1939: The Polytechnic was renamed to Lviv Polytechnical Institute.</li>
  <li>July 4, 1941 (at night): On Vuletsky Hills <a>Germans</a>, with help from Ukrainian nationalists, <a>shot professors</a> of the Polytechnic Institute - <a>Wlodzimierz Krukowski</a>, <a>Antoni &#x0141;omnicki</a>, <a>Stanislaw Pilat</a>, <a>W&#x0142;odzimierz Sto&#x017c;ek</a>, <a>Kasper Weigel</a>, <a>Roman Witkiewicz</a> and others.</li>
  <li>July 26, 1941: Professor <a>Kazimierz Bartel</a> was murdered in the basements of Gestapo headquarters.</li>
  <li>Spring 1942 - Spring 1944: Special three-month courses for <a>electrical engineers</a>, road and bridge <a>civil engineers</a>, agrarian engineers, etc. were working in the premises, of the present Mechanical Technology Department. After the war, these classes were continued in <a>Gliwice</a>. </li>
  <li>Autumn 1944: The 100th jubilee of Lviv Polytechnical Institute was celebrated very quietly in Lviv - the <a>Second World War</a> was still going on.</li>
</ul>
<h3>Soviet Union</h3>
<ul>
  <li>1945 the Geodetic Department was founded. Most professors of Polish ethnicity, leave Lviv for Poland. The Polish traditions developed at  the Polytechnic were continued at the <a>Silesian University of Technology</a> in <a>Gliwice</a> and <a>Wroc&#x0142;aw University of Technology</a>.</li>
  <li>In October 1946 the Lviv Polytechnical Institute began to publish the periodical newspaper &quot;Lviv Polytechnic&quot;.</li>
  <li>1952 the Radio-engineering Department was founded.</li>
  <li>1962 the Automation, Electromechanical and Mechanical Technology Departments was founded.</li>
  <li>1966 the Economical Engineering Department was founded.</li>
  <li>1967 the Department of Technology of Organic Substances was founded.</li>
  <li>1970 The second building of the Library was erected.</li>
  <li>1971 the Heating Engineering Department was founded.</li>
  <li>1989 Democratic changes began at Polytechnical Institute</li>
  <li>April 10, 1991 Inauguration of the first democratically elected Rector for the last 50 years - Yu. Rudavsky.</li>
</ul>
<h3>Ukraine</h3>
<ul>
  <li>1992 Computer Engineering Department and Information Technology Department was founded.</li>
  <li>1992 Institute of Humanities was founded on the basis of the following chairs: 
    <ul>
      <li>History of Ukraine, its Science and Technology</li>
      <li>Ukrainian Language</li>
      <li>Politology</li>
      <li>Philosophy</li>
      <li>Foreign Languages (English, German, French, Spanish, Russian, Japanese)</li>
    </ul>
  </li>
  <li>1993 the Department of Applied Mathematics was founded.</li>
  <li>June 1993 the  Lviv Polytechnical Institute got the status of university, becoming Lviv Polytechnic State University.</li>
  <li>1994 Lviv Polytechnic got the status of national university becoming Lviv Polytechnic National University.</li>
  <li>July 8, 2009 the Lviv Polytechnic received the status of self-governing (autonomous) national research university</li>
</ul>
