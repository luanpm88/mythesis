|logo =<a>245px</a> }} The <strong>University of California, Santa Cruz</strong>, also known as <strong>UC Santa Cruz</strong> or <strong>UCSC</strong>, is a <a>public</a>, <a>collegiate</a> <a>university</a>; one of ten campuses in the <a>University of California</a>.
<p>Founded in 1965,&lt;ref name=&quot;overview&quot;&gt;&lt;/ref&gt; UC Santa Cruz began as a showcase for progressive, cross-disciplinary undergraduate education, innovative teaching methods and contemporary architecture.
<p>In April 2010, UC Santa Cruz opened its new $35 million Digital Arts Research Center; a project in planning since 2004.
The heavily-forested area has allowed UC Santa Cruz to operate a <a>recreational vehicle park</a> as a form of student housing.
<p>UC Santa Cruz is currently ranked #71 in the list of Best National Universities in the United States by the <em><a>US News &amp; World Reports</a></em>,&lt;ref&gt;{{cite web|url=<a>http://colleges.usnews.rankingsandreviews.com/best-colleges/santa-cruz-ca/ucsc-1321</a>|title=US News.
&lt;ref&gt;  &lt;/ref&gt; In 2009, The Princeton Review (with Gamepro Magazine) ranked UC Santa Cruz's Game Design major among the top 50 in the country.
<p>UC Santa Cruz is also well known for its <a>cannabis</a> culture.
